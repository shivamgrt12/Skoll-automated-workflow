# Memory: Julie Leach

## Personal Profile
Julie is a White American woman with deep Appalachian family roots. Her parents came north from eastern Kentucky for factory work before she was born, and she grew up carrying that working-class discipline into adulthood.

She is quiet, observant, and exacting without being flashy. People often read her calm as reserve until they see her teach, cook for a crowd, or steady a room that is starting to wobble.

- She values effort, competence, reciprocity, and faith that is lived quietly rather than performed.
- She has a dry private humor and a long memory for who shows up when it counts.
- She earned an associate degree in business administration and holds an Ohio cosmetology license.

## Work & Projects
- Julie has been the lead nail technology instructor at Brightwater Academy of Cosmetology for six years.
- Before Brightwater, she spent roughly a decade as a working nail technician at Columbus-area salons after earning her Ohio cosmetology license, building the hands-on foundation she now teaches from.
- She teaches nail fundamentals, advanced nail art, sanitation, safety, and Ohio state board preparation to cohorts of 12 to 18 students, three times per year.
- Her students post a 94 percent state board pass rate against an 82 percent school average.
- She is working toward a cosmetology school director's license and studies nights and weekends with Rodney Beckett.
- Recent practice exams are landing at 78 percent, and she needs 80 percent to pass.
- She coordinates the nail art competition segment of the annual Brightwater student showcase.
- At Grace Community Methodist Church, she serves as an event volunteer coordinator, children's Sunday school teacher, and kitchen lead for major events.

## Health & Wellness
- Julie is generally healthy but carries occupational strain from nail work.
- She has bilateral carpal tunnel, worse in the right hand, wears wrist splints at night, uses ibuprofen 400 mg as needed, and sees Dr. Sarah Mitchell at Holloway Medical for quarterly occupational therapy check-ins.
- Stress can trigger one or two tension headaches per month, and rest plus hydration usually help.
- She keeps an early sleep-and-wake rhythm, and daily devotional time is a mental-health anchor.
