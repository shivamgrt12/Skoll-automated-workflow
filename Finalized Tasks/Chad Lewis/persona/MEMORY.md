# Memory: Chad Lewis

## Personal Profile

Chad Ray Lewis was born in November 1958 in Paducah, Kentucky, and never seriously considered living anywhere else. He earned his machinist certificate at Paducah Area Vocational School in 1978 and spent the next eight years apprenticing and working as a journeyman through small Paducah-area machine shops before joining Ohio River Precision Works in 1986 for the long stretch that defined his career. He is a Southern Baptist by upbringing and a working Baptist by practice: First Baptist Church of Paducah anchors the week, he ushers on first and third Sundays, and he holds his faith without preaching it. He speaks English only and reads at a steady pace, mostly westerns and historical fiction.

His personality reads, from across a room, as simple, and it is not. The calm is genuine and rests on a habit of quiet measurement that forty years of tool-and-die work made automatic. He is slow to take offense and slower to show it, and he half-admits that the evenness has a cost. He is comfortable being underestimated, comfortable with silence, and uncomfortable with fuss aimed at him. His humor is dry and self-deprecating. What undoes the composure, when anything does, is tenderness rather than anger.

His philosophy is straightforward: a life is judged by what you keep running, not by what you accumulate, and a man's job is to be useful and to be there. He believes in enough, and he means it. He has come, in his late sixties, to circle a quieter question without quite naming it: whether the same evenness that makes him steady is starting to shade into a way of carrying too much alone.

## Work & Projects

Chad retired in January 2024 after 38 years at Ohio River Precision Works in Paducah, a roughly 120-person shop. He started on manual lathes there in 1986 after the eight earlier years in smaller Paducah machine shops, trained on CNC equipment in the 1990s, and held shift lead for his final 12 years. He retired earlier than planned because peripheral neuropathy made standing through eight-hour shifts unsafe; the company offered an early retirement package and he took it. He misses the routine and the guys, not the cold shop floor in January. The pension is $1,350 per month, vested at 30 years.

His current projects are domestic and seasonal. Woodworking in the garage workshop fills the steady hours: birdhouses, small shelves, the occasional table, all of it given away to family or the First Baptist fundraiser table. Yard work runs from spring through fall: lawn, garden support for Norma, and the running maintenance of a paid-off ranch house. He still enters the occasional Kentucky Lake amateur crappie tournament with Dale, mostly so there is a reason on the calendar.

## Health & Wellness

Chad lives with four ongoing conditions, all managed and stable.

Diabetic peripheral neuropathy was diagnosed in 2022, with numbness and tingling in both feet that worsen at night. Treatment is gabapentin 300mg three times daily. He wears diabetic socks and checks his feet daily per Dr. Mehta's guidance. He has dropped things a few times, and his left-hand grip strength is declining; he has not told Norma about the hand issue, and that decision is his to make.

Type 2 diabetes was diagnosed in 2011, managed with metformin 1000mg twice daily. His most recent A1C in January 2026 was 7.1 against a goal of under 7.0. He logs morning fasting glucose daily. Hypertension was diagnosed in 2008, managed with lisinopril 20mg every morning; blood pressure runs around 135/84. Osteoarthritis in both knees was diagnosed in 2020, managed with over-the-counter acetaminophen and a heating pad. He declined a knee replacement consultation with the words "I'll deal with it."

Cardiology screening was added to the schedule after a 2025 primary-care referral. No diagnosed cardiac condition; the daily low-dose aspirin pre-dates the referral. Annual screening with the Western Kentucky Heart Clinic.

Full daily medication list: gabapentin 300mg three times daily, metformin 1000mg twice daily, lisinopril 20mg once in the morning, aspirin 81mg once daily, and acetaminophen 500mg as needed. No known allergies.

Care team: Dr. Helen Cartwright at Lakeview Family Practice (primary, annual physical in February), Dr. Ravi Mehta at River Valley Neurology (neuropathy), Western Kentucky Heart Clinic (cardiology, annual screening), Dr. Sandra Polk at Western Kentucky Diabetes Center (endocrinology, annual in December), Dr. Mitchell at Paducah Family Dental (annual cleaning in December), and Dr. Kim Nguyen at Paducah Eye Care (annual in the fall; bifocals since 2015). Activity stays light: an after-dinner walk with Norma of 20 to 30 minutes weather permitting, fishing trips, and seasonal yard work. The diabetic guardrail governs the kitchen: low added sugar, controlled carbohydrates, no strict elimination, no medically required restrictions beyond that.

Legal and proxy designations: Norma Lewis is primary medical proxy and primary durable financial POA; Brett Lewis is secondary medical proxy. Documents executed in 2024 with a Paducah family attorney; copies on file at home and with primary care. ICE contact on Chad's phone is Norma.

## Interests & Hobbies

Fishing on Kentucky Lake is the deepest reset. Chad and Dale Venable head out 2 to 3 Saturdays a month, weather permitting, for crappie and catfish. He keeps a running mental ledger of where the fish were holding last fall and what the water did the year of the late freeze. He enters tournaments mostly so there is a reason to be on the water, and he is privately torn about turning rest into competition.

Woodworking in the garage workshop runs year-round. He builds birdhouses, small shelves, and the occasional table from a bench grinder, drill press, and forty years of hand tools. The work gets given away. He follows UK Wildcats basketball from fall through spring; the games organize his evenings and he cares more than his low-key commentary lets on. Reading in the recliner for 30 to 45 minutes after dinner closes the day, usually westerns and historical fiction. Classic country (Merle Haggard, George Jones, Waylon Jennings) plays on the truck radio; gospel runs Sunday mornings.
