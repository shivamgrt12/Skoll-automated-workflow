Sunday Lunch Recipe Rotation - Outremont
=========================================

Every Sunday: lunch at Simone & Pierre's in Outremont.
We rotate who brings what. These are the hits.

RUTH'S CONTRIBUTIONS
---------------------

Tarte aux pommes (Apple Tart)
  Pâte brisée (homemade, Ruth's recipe from M.Urb. school potlucks)
  5 Cortland apples (from Jean-Talon Market, always Cortland)
  2 tbsp sugar, 1 tsp cinnamon, 1 tbsp lemon juice, 1 tbsp butter
  Arrange sliced apples in concentric circles on pastry.
  Bake 375F / 190C for 40 minutes until golden.
  Simone says it's the best apple tart in Montreal.
  (She's biased but I'll take it.)

Salade composée (Composed Salad)
  Mixed greens from Jean-Talon, shaved radishes, toasted walnuts,
  crumbled chèvre, dried cranberries.
  Dressing: olive oil + red wine vinegar + Dijon + honey + salt.
  Simple but Pierre always has seconds.

Soupe aux légumes (Vegetable Soup)
  Fall/winter contribution. Butternut squash base.
  Squash, carrots, leeks, potatoes, onion, garlic, thyme.
  Simmer 40 min, blend smooth, finish with cream.
  Emile calls this "orange soup" and eats two bowls.

MARC'S CONTRIBUTIONS
--------------------

Pain aux noix (Walnut Bread)
  He bakes this Saturday night. The apartment smells incredible.
  Uses a 24-hour cold ferment. Walnuts from the bulk store.
  Pierre has declared this the official bread of Sunday lunch.

Tarte aux poireaux (Leek Tart)
  His grandmother's recipe from Saguenay.
  Leeks, cream, gruyère, thyme, pâte feuilletée.
  Rich but worth it. Simone requests this at least monthly.

SIMONE'S CLASSICS
-----------------

Poulet rôti (Roast Chicken)
  Her signature. Lemon, garlic, herbs de Provence, butter under skin.
  She's been making this since before Ruth was born.
  Emile eats the drumstick. Always the drumstick.

Tourtière
  Christmas and special occasions only.
  Pork, veal, potato, spices (clove, cinnamon, nutmeg).
  Family recipe from Pierre's mother (Beauce region).
  Ruth tried making it once. "Close but not quite," said Pierre.
  She's accepted she will never match Simone's tourtière.

PIERRE'S ROLE
-------------
  Pierre does not cook.
  Pierre selects the wine.
  Pierre is very good at selecting the wine.
  (Usually a Côtes du Rhône or a Quebec cider in fall.)
  Pierre also does the dishes. This is the deal.

3. Dessert is mandatory.
4. He sits next to Grand-papa Pierre.
