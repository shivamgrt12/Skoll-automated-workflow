# Memory: Benjamin Thomas Marshall

## Personal Profile

- **Full name**: Benjamin Thomas Marshall, she/her, goes by Ben. Birth name from her grandfather Tom and a Bible passage.
- **Heritage**: White American, raised in Baltimore by Grandpa Tom after her mother's addiction took over when Ben was seven.
- **Education**: BA in Social Work from Chesapeake State University, 2007; MSW from Harbor Graduate School of Social Work, 2012.
- **Relationship status**: Divorced from Marcus Marshall since 2020. Not dating. Marshall is her birth name, kept through marriage and divorce.
- **Temperament**: Practiced calm built from being the calmest person in too many rooms; processes the day late, alone, kettle on.
- **Directness**: She does not soften an answer to make a person feel better about a question that did not need asking.
- **Faith**: Private and load-bearing. Grace Community Church on Sunday. Not certainty; endurance. Suspicious of loud or for-sale versions.
- **Philosophy**: Follow-through is the only currency she respects; structure is a form of love; cool anger useful, hot not.
- **Forgiveness stance**: She loves her mother without forgiving her, knows this about herself, and is at peace with that knowledge.

- Coffee: two cups in the morning, cream and sugar, never after noon.
- Wine: one glass on Fridays, usually with Keith. Nothing during the week.
- Tea: strong evening cup with honey. Non-negotiable.
- Shopping: practical. Aldi for groceries, Target for household, Amazon for what she has run out of and cannot drive across town for. No browsing, no impulse buys.
- Gifts: the budget loosens for the small circle. Keith's bourbon, Lily's specific sketchbook, a book Pastor Crawford mentioned and forgot she mentioned. Given quietly.
- Entertainment: literary fiction, crime dramas on TV, folk and Americana playlists. Fantasy series shared with Lily.
- Pet peeves: being interrupted, being talked down to, performance concern, the word "passion" used cheaply, the phrase "circle back," anyone relating a smaller stress as equivalent, any adult speaking to Lily as if Lily were six.
- Sensory: the kitchen radio belongs at one specific volume, and Ben resets it when Lily turns it up. Avoids candles called "fresh linen" and waiting-room cleaning-product smells. Prefers breathable shirts and resoled boots over new ones.
- Social: small loyal perimeter. No parties unless the people are people she already loves. Saying no is information, not rejection. One twenty-minute real conversation over three hours of pleasantries.
- Comfort: a wool blanket on the couch, a hardcover book in her lap, the kettle at 5:50 AM, alley sounds on the patio at dusk.
