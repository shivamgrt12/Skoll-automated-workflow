# Curatorial voice notes

On label copy, third revision in three years

## What I have learned

Visitors do not read full label panels at the rate we used to assume. Closer to forty percent skim, twenty percent stop and read, the rest just look at the object. So the first sentence does almost all the work.

## Voice rules I have settled on

1. Active, not passive
2. Concrete object before abstract idea
3. One date per label, not three
4. Named people get full names the first time, last name after
5. Cite if and only if the visitor has any reason to follow up
6. Read it out loud, if it sounds like a press release rewrite it

## Tone drift to watch for

Over-explaining. We are not the textbook, the object is. I keep cutting the second sentence and the label gets stronger.
