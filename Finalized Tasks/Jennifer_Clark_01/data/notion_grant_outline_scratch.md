Notes toward the Year-Two narrative section. Pulled together fast in early

## Why a Year-Two narrative matters now

The funder reads Year-One as proof of operational capacity. Year-Two is where
they look for evidence that we have a program, not just an exhibition.
Pamela was clear on this in July: the program officer's review committee
weights program continuity heavily, and a single capstone exhibit reads as a
one-off if the Year-Two narrative does not frame it as a launch.

## Year-Two thesis

The Voices of the Past exhibition is the visible piece. The unseen work is
the standing oral history program, the consents apparatus, the community of
practice we are building around the recording sessions, and the catalog
infrastructure that makes the recordings discoverable. Year Two should keep
funding the exhibition operations while shifting weight toward the program.
The program-side budget should grow as a share of the total.

## Section sketch

1. Year-One recap: what got built. Eight stations, forty interviews, opening
   on schedule for Oct 18, 2026. Total spend tracking to the envelope, no
   significant variances at the line-item level worth flagging to the funder.
2. Year-Two ask: standing program funding, two full recording cycles, one
   community partner expansion. First Bethel AME Church board work is the
   anchor partner expansion case study; the genealogy of community
   involvement is uniquely deep there and the board has signaled interest.
3. Year-Three teaser: the touring version of the exhibition, the regional
   partnerships, the archive-side improvements including a public-facing
   reading-room redesign at the museum.

## What I am writing toward

The narrative wants three voices: the curator (institutional accountability),
the program consultant (academic legitimacy through Pamela), and the
community of interviewees (proof that the work earned the trust the recordings
demanded). The third voice is the hardest to render on the page because
those folks are not the ones writing the proposal; the narrative has to
quote them well and frame the consents apparatus in a way that signals
their authority over their own stories.
