Pantry list from Mama, Sept 30 phone call, transcribed at the kitchen table.
She wants the order placed Friday morning before the museum runs.

The call started a little before seven her time. She had her glasses on and the
list pad in her lap, the one with the church directory cover. She walked me
through the kitchen, the pantry, and the pharmacy basket in that order, the way
she always does.

Produce:
- Two bunches collard greens, washed if they have them, the firm leaves not the
  yellowed ones
- Three sweet potatoes, the big ones, not the slim ones
- One bag yellow onions, the three-pound size
- One bag white potatoes, the russets she likes for baking
- Two pounds apples, the firm ones, Fuji if they have Fuji and otherwise
  whatever is firmest
- One bunch bananas, just slightly green, she said
- One head cabbage, the dense kind not the leafy
- One pound carrots, the loose ones not the bagged baby carrots
- One pint blueberries if in season, skip if not
- One bag mixed salad greens, the kind without the dressing pouch
- A small head of cauliflower, she wants to try a roast recipe Mrs. Booker
  gave her at the women's group
- One bunch parsley, flat-leaf, she is finally tired of the curly
- One small ginger root, about a thumb size, she has been making a tea for
  the cold weather

Pantry:
- One jar peanut butter, the natural one, the one that has to be stirred
- One box cornmeal, the yellow not the white
- One bag pinto beans, the one-pound bag
- One bag long-grain rice, the two-pound bag, not the parboiled kind
- One box oatmeal, the kind that does not have sugar, the old-fashioned cut
- One bottle olive oil, the medium size, the extra-virgin
- One box low-sodium chicken broth, the brand the doctor approved
- One small bag whole wheat flour for biscuits
- One small bottle vanilla extract, she finished the last bottle
- One box graham crackers, the plain ones for the kids when they visit
- One small jar local honey, for the tea

Dairy and protein:
- One gallon two percent milk, the date as far out as they have
- One dozen large brown eggs, free range if available
- One pack chicken thighs, bone-in skin-on, three or four pieces is enough
- One pound ground turkey, the lean kind, the ninety-three percent
- One small block sharp cheddar, the Cracker Barrel extra sharp
- One small pack plain Greek yogurt, the no-sugar-added
- One small pack salmon, two filets, she wants to bake them with lemon
- A small piece of country ham for sandwiches mid-week

Pharmacy section, she went slowly through this part:
- Metformin 1000 mg, 90-day refill, she said Dr. Maple put it in already so
  it should be ready at Walgreens on Ninth Avenue
- One small bottle multivitamin, the senior formulation
- One pack glucose test strips, the brand the One Touch meter uses, she
  emphasized brand match because the off-brand strips throw her numbers
- One pack lancets, fine gauge
- One battery for the glucose meter, the little round one, she said the
  display was getting dim
- A new sharps container if they sell them, the small countertop size
- One tube generic hydrocortisone, the one-percent, for a rash on her
  forearm she has been ignoring
- One bottle saline nasal spray for the dry air
- One box adhesive bandages, the fabric kind, the assorted box

Side conversation notes I jotted as we talked:
- She mentioned her A1C came back at 6.9 at the last visit, which is steady
  for her, but she wants to start walking again in the mornings now that the
  weather is breaking
- She asked after Naomi's violin and after Marcus's reading scores; I told
  her about Ms. Diane and the swap she is asking for
- She wanted to know whether we had decided on Pembroke for Naomi yet; I
  said the open house is October eleventh and Derrick and I are going
- She asked whether Derrick's shoulder is any better; I said the Naproxen
  PRN is keeping him moving
- She wants the grandkids to come for lunch on the Sunday after the
  opening, said she will make pound cake and the chicken thighs from this
  order

Notes for the driver and the order itself:
- The collards and the sweet potatoes are the reason the basket is bigger
  this month; she said do not skip them even if the total runs over
- She does not need the cleaning supplies, she still has plenty from the
  September restock
- Tell the driver to leave at the side porch, not the front door; the front
  door bell does not always carry to the back room where she sits
- Tip generously, the driver last time was Mr. Frank Calloway, he is good,
  and if it is him again she said to ask him how his daughter at Tuskegee is
  doing
- If they substitute, she wants a phone call before they swap, not a text;
  her vision on the phone screen is bad enough that she misses texts
- If they are out of the natural peanut butter, do not substitute, she will
  go without

End of transcribed list.
