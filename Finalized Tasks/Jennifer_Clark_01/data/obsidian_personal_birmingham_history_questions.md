# Birmingham history questions I keep coming back to

Not for the exhibition. Just for me.

1. Who organized the first ride share network for the bus boycott solidarity drives in the Birmingham neighborhoods, and how did the route maps actually circulate.

2. The women who ran the lunch counter logistics for the early sit-in waves in Greensboro had counterparts here. Their names are mostly missing from the record I have access to. Where would I look beyond the BPL collections.

3. The role of the small Black-owned print shops, especially the one near Fifth and Tuxedo, in producing the early flyers and mass meeting handbills. Almost no documentation.

4. The transportation arrangements for the children who participated in the May 1963 demonstrations. Whose cars were used and how the network coordinated.

5. The home Bible study groups in the Smithfield neighborhood that hosted strategy meetings after the church gatherings broke up. What records, if any, survive.
