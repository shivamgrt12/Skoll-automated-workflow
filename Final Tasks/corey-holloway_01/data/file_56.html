About

I started in networking, moved through systems, and landed in reliability. The job is part engineering and part judgement: define the problem, isolate the variable, test the fix before calling anything solved. I mentor the engineers coming up behind me and I ride a road bike most mornings to keep my head clear.

Focus Areas

Where I spend my time.

Capacity Planning

Forecasting demand, retuning autoscaling, and keeping headroom honest before traffic finds the gap.

Incident Response

Calm on call practice, sharp runbooks, and post reviews that fix systems rather than assign blame.

Observability

Metrics, logs, and alerts that tell the truth, so a page means something is actually wrong.

Platform Hardening

Upgrades, backup restore drills, and removing the sharp edges that turn a blip into an outage.

Mentoring

Shadow weeks, review sessions, and the patience to explain the why, not just the fix.

Automation

Small reliable scripts that erase toil and let the team spend attention where it matters.

Selected Work

A few things I am proud of.

Availability lift

Took a core platform from three nines to a steady three and a half nines across a year of focused work.

Recovery time

Cut mean time to recovery to roughly eleven minutes through better runbooks and cleaner paging.

Home lab

A quiet three node cluster at home that keeps my reliability instincts sharp and the house automated.
