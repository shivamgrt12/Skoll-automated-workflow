# Memory: Teresa Gutierrez

## Personal Profile

- Full name: Teresa Gutierrez.
- Pronouns: she/her.
- Identity: Cisgender straight woman; Catholic in a steady, low-volume way; quietly proud IBEW Local 567 member, not one she leads with around people who would misunderstand it.
- Ethnicity and background: Mixed heritage, Mexican grandmother and French-Canadian grandfather; working-class Bangor childhood; parents spoke English at home while Spanish stayed in Abuela's kitchen.
- Languages: English (native). Understands more Spanish than she speaks and has stopped apologizing for the gap.
- Education: Brewer High School diploma, 2006. IBEW Local 567 apprenticeship completed 2011. Journeyman electrician license.
- Occupation: Journeyman electrician at Penobscot Electrical Contractors, Bangor; twenty years total at the same shop across apprenticeship and journeyman work.
- Specialization: Commercial and residential electrical work; currently leading a downtown Bangor commercial rewiring project, October 2026 through January 2027.
- Marital status: Married to Brian Gutierrez since 2012. Steady marriage where Brian plans and Teresa executes.
- Faith and practice: Sunday Mass at the parish church because Brian likes it and the kids are growing up inside the rhythm. Belief and household practice have stopped being separate things.
- Temperament: The steadiness of someone who works with live current for a living and learned early that panic gets people hurt. When the day gets loud, she gets quieter. Does not perform effort.
- Humor: Lives in the body more than in the mouth: a raised eyebrow, a beat-too-long pause, an answer that is technically correct in a way that makes the room laugh later.
- Stress response: Retreats into doing when something scares her, mostly the vertigo and what it might be saying about the next ten years. Porch boards, bathroom tile, the workbench reorganized for the fourth time this season.
- Social style: Small circle on purpose. Friendly with everyone, close to almost no one, by deliberate choice. Does not see this as a problem.
- Philosophy: Believes love is in what gets done, that a good life is built out of small repeatable structures, that capability is a moral category, and that she chose this life rather than landed in it. She has been to other places. She came back here on purpose.

## Interests & Hobbies

The garage is hers, with a workbench against the back wall organized to a level her kitchen will never reach: pegboard with every tool outlined in Sharpie, drawers labeled in faded blue masking tape, a coffee can of spare wire nuts she has been adding to since the apprenticeship. The light over the bench is the one she rewired her first week as a journeyman, and it has never flickered.

The pellet smoker came home one summer and changed the rhythm of weekends. She built the side table for the wood pellets and keeps a notebook in the truck glove compartment with the cook time, wood type, and a one-line review of every brisket and pork butt she has finished. Fishing is older: her father took her to the Penobscot at eight, and she still uses the Fenwick rod he gave her at fourteen, re-wrapped twice and not retiring in her lifetime. Marc fishes with her now. They do not talk much on the river. Coaching Luc's soccer started as a logistics solution and turned into something else; she can teach a six-year-old to dribble with his eyes up in a way that sticks.
