# Memory: Chris Johnson

## Personal Profile
Chris is a third-generation Irish-American whose grandparents emigrated from County Cork around 1950. He was raised in Woodlawn, the Bronx, played GAA hurling, danced at feiseanna as a kid, and married Sienna at St. Barnabas. The Irishness is not a costume; it is the wallpaper of his life. Twenty-two years in the FDNY shaped his instincts, his humor, his posture, and the nightmares he does not discuss at dinner. He is quietly devout, with Sunday Mass at St. John the Baptist in Yonkers a fixed point, and his faith stays private and never preachy. He carries controlled intensity: calm in the chaos of a working fire, then pacing the kitchen replaying it, managing his PTSD through therapy and discipline rather than denial.

His education is field-forged. He earned an AAS in Fire Science from SUNY Empire State in 2006 and a BS in Emergency Management from John Jay College of Criminal Justice in 2012, completing the degree while on the job. He is a protective provider who grew up watching his father Pat work three jobs, and he cannot sit still when there are bills to pay and kids to launch.

## Work & Projects
FDNY Lieutenant, Ladder Company 24, 7th Battalion, Midtown Manhattan (W 31st St between 7th and 8th Ave). Appointed 2004, promoted to Lieutenant in 2016 after passing the exam. He supervises a 5-person truck company, leads searches on the fireground, forces entry, and coordinates ventilation and rescue. He is an FDNY Incident Management Team member, deployed to major incidents and multi-alarm fires as a division supervisor.

Shift schedule is 24 hours on and 72 hours off, rotating Group 3, with tours starting at 9:00 AM. Mutual swaps are available with Lt. Danny Kowalski (Ladder 24, Group 1). He is a UFA union delegate for Ladder 24, handling grievances and contract questions for his house. His annual FDNY Bureau of Health Services physical (last full medical March 2026) passed him fit for full duty. He is weighing the Captain's exam, next offered 2027, but has not committed.

Adjunct teaching at John Jay College: the EMT certification course EMS 101, Tuesday and Thursday evenings, fall and spring semesters, under department chair Dr. Angela Torres. He teaches from his own field experience and the students love it. Income: $138,000 FDNY base, roughly $28,000 overtime, and roughly $12,000 adjunct salary.

## Health & Wellness
General: 5 feet 11 inches, 210 lbs, strong but carrying about 15 extra pounds that Dr. Flanagan flags at every visit.

- **Sleep apnea** (diagnosed 2023): ResMed AirSense 11 CPAP, full face mask, nightly use. Compliance tracked via myAir; insurance requires 70% and he hits about 78%, missing some nights after late tours. Sleep study at Westchester Sleep Center under Dr. Maria Santos showed an AHI of 28 events per hour. He hates the mask, hose, and noise, but Sienna confirms he stopped snoring and his energy is better. He brings a travel CPAP for night tours. Next follow-up November 2026.
- **Rotator cuff** (right shoulder, chronic): Partial supraspinatus tear from years of forcible entry. Physical therapy twice weekly, Mondays and Wednesdays 7:30 AM at Yonkers Physical Therapy and Sports Medicine. Orthopedist Dr. James Reilly at HSS recommends arthroscopic repair, which Chris keeps postponing because three months off active duty means desk duty and lost overtime. Managed with ice, ibuprofen 400mg as needed, and resistance-band exercises. Affects fireground overhead work, swinging a hurley, and sleeping on his right side.
- **Tinnitus** (chronic, bilateral, worse in the left ear): From cumulative fire ground noise, PASS alarms, saws, and air horns. No treatment beyond a white noise machine at night. Audiologist Dr. Patricia Dunn at ENT Associates of Westchester found mild high-frequency loss bilaterally at the March 2026 test. Worse after working fires; he treats it as part of the job.
- **High cholesterol** (diagnosed 2021): Rosuvastatin 10mg each evening with dinner. February 2026 lipid panel: total 212, LDL 128, HDL 52, triglycerides 160. Primary care Dr. Sean Flanagan at Westchester Medical Group wants LDL under 100 and pushes him to cut red meat and alcohol, which slips at BBQ competitions and firehouse dinners. Sienna manages it at home with more fish and less red meat.
- **PTSD** (diagnosed 2022, active treatment): Weekly trauma-focused CBT Thursdays 11:00 AM with Dr. Rachel Kovacs, PsyD, a first-responder specialist. Processing a 2016 high-rise fire with a fatality he could not reach in time plus 22 years of cumulative exposure. Symptoms include intermittent insomnia separate from apnea, hypervigilance, and occasional intrusive thoughts and nightmares. No medication now; sertraline tried briefly in 2023 and stopped with Dr. Kovacs's guidance. Sienna joins a quarterly couples session. He is open about therapy at the firehouse and is a trained CISM peer counselor.
