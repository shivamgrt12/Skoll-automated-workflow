--- TURN 1 ---

The special session takes up the consolidation vote on Sage Creek and Pronghorn Valley, and I mean to walk into that room with a case that stands on its own. I have sat on this board since the year they redrew District 3, and I have watched downtown chip at the country schools every budget season since. This time they mean to close them for good. I have been building the answer to that for the better part of a year, and the pieces are scattered across every notebook and account I keep, so pull it all together for me and make it hold weight. I want no soft spots for anybody at that table to kick at, and I decide what the world sees out of it, not you. Start with the four counties I studied, the ones that already went down this road and lived with what it cost them. The spine of my whole argument is what happened to them, so I need those numbers dead true and traceable back to where they came from. The district has since put out its own figures on enrollment and busing, the ones the superintendent keeps waving around, and I have a bad feeling my numbers and theirs do not stand in the same corral. Find where mine and theirs part ways, and tell me plain which side the record actually backs, because that is the ground I will be standing on when they start talking over me. Run the honest bus-ride minutes for the families out past the far fence lines, the little ones who would ride the longest morning and night. The families already answered a survey on the routes, so work what they told us in rather than guessing at it, and show me how you got there. And set the money the district claims it saves against what those counties actually spent once every last bill on that ledger came due and the tally came in honest. I want that arithmetic clean enough to read aloud in a cold room and have it land, no hand-waving, every figure resting on something a person could go check. I need a straight count of where the seven of us stand on this vote. Four of the seven blocks it, and I want to know exactly where we are right now, who is firm, who is soft, and what it might take to move somebody who is still deciding. Folks drift and talk changes, so go back through what has actually been said of late between the chair, the members, and me, and give me the count as it truly sits today. Tell me who I have real business leaning on before the meeting, and what moved them, so I am not spending my breath on a seat that is already lost or already won. Then pull my District 3 families into one working list I can stand behind. Their names are spread across every outreach tool we use, gathered over a year of meetings and mailings and sign-in sheets, and I want a single clean roster pulled out of that mess, one I could actually work from without tripping over myself. Mark the ones who said they would stand up and speak at the session, because I need to know who I can count on being at that table with me and not just cheering from the back. The op-ed for the Cody Enterprise needs polishing before it is fit to run. Lead with the bus, because that is the thing every parent in that room will feel in their gut before they hear a single number. Keep it in my voice, plain and short, the way I talk and not the way a press office talks. And I need the formal board correspondence ready to go alongside it, in the measured register that kind of letter calls for. Hold both of them for me to look over line by line before anything moves an inch past my desk. Nothing goes out over my name until I have read every word of it myself. This is a lot of country to cover on short daylight, and the meeting is closer than I would like. Keep the whole of it tight to what actually wins the vote. When it is all together, walk me through what you found and do not dress it up. Tell me where the district's story and the county record come apart and which one holds. Tell me where the count truly stands and what has been moving it. Tell me what the honest bus minutes and the honest savings come to when you set them side by side. Give me the plain version I can carry into that room, and then I will decide what we do with it.
