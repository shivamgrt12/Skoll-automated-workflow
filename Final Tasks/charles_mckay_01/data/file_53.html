Where this isThe Land

Twenty eight hundred acres of mixed grassland, creek bottom, and timbered hills, twenty five miles south of Cody. Sage Creek runs through the south section, the Absarokas fill the west window, and the original 1923 homestead cabin still stands. The grandparents came over from Edinburgh and homesteaded this ground. The family has ranched it for over a hundred years.

Day to dayThe Work

The 280 head Black Angus herd sold in 2021, but the land stayed and the work never quit. Roy Patterson leases the grazing on twelve hundred acres and four hundred for hay. The rest is fencing, the well and stock water system, road grading, and keeping wildfire off the place.

Forty years of looking upThe Sky

I run a home weather station near the barn, station KWYCODY0142, and log the readings every morning before the day starts. Temperature, pressure, wind, and precipitation, kept by hand and fed to the wider observing network. I read storms by smell and wind shift as much as by the gauge, and I give the occasional talk on it.

In the barnThe Shop

A small woodworking shop in the barn, for furniture and ranch repairs. I built a cradle for each grandchild and I sign and date the underside of every piece. The current job is a low pine bookcase for Hannah, to match her cradle, oiled by hand and ready to travel to Casper for the family dinner.
