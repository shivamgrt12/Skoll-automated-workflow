--- TURN 1 ---
Diana wants me walking into the steering committee review able to say, with a straight face and real numbers behind it, exactly where Desert Bloom Under the Stars actually stands right now, and I have quietly lost the thread on whether the picture in front of us is still true. We have been selling tickets and chasing sponsors and logging tasks across more places than any one volunteer should ever have to hold in his head, and every one of those places was last trued up on a different day by a different person, so I need you to pull the whole gala into one coherent state and tell me where it does not line up rather than papering over the cracks. Start with the money, because that is what the board actually grades me on. Reconcile every dollar that has come in against every dollar we expected, ticket by ticket and sponsor by sponsor and auction bid by auction bid, run down the ones that got refunded or half paid or pledged but never cleared, and hand me a giving picture I can defend line by line to Diana and to Gloria without getting caught out on a figure that turns out to be stale. I have a nagging feeling some of the sponsor amounts were entered early and never corrected as the agreements got renegotiated, and a couple of tiers may have shifted since whoever last touched the running budget, so where two records of the same gift disagree I want the gap surfaced plainly and the amount I should trust defended, not silently smoothed into an average. Tell me honestly how far the cleared total sits from the goal we announced and from the break even line the treasurer keeps quoting at me, because a proud headline number that quietly leans on pledges nobody has actually paid is exactly the kind of thing that falls apart the moment Gloria starts asking questions. While the money is coming together, get me the truth on who is really coming, because the headcount drives the catering and the catering drives whether we clear the room at all. Cross the paid admissions and the patron seats against the confirmed attendance and the survey responses, separate the people who bought and are coming from the people who merely said maybe, count the ones who cancelled, and pull the dietary and access needs into one honest place so nobody with a nut allergy ends up staring at almond cake and nobody in a wheelchair ends up parked behind a pillar. I have a suspicion the tidy attendance summary somebody pasted into the planning board no longer matches the actual list of replies underneath it, and if that is true I would rather know now than the week of the event. Then verify the sponsors properly, because this is where I am most exposed. Walk every sponsor we are about to thank in print and confirm which ones have genuinely executed an agreement at a given level with money that has actually landed, versus the ones sitting warm in the donor pipeline as an intention somebody logged on a hopeful afternoon. Do not let a pledge that was never signed dress itself up as a confirmed gift, because if we print a logo at a level the sponsor never committed to, I am the one making an awkward phone call. Next, the work itself. We have the same task list living in three tools that have drifted apart, so collapse them into one clean view of what is genuinely done, what is still open, and what is quietly overdue, with an honest owner and a real due date against each thing that matters, and then tell me the two or three items on the critical path that actually threaten the night if they slip. After that, sort the auction shipping, because the winning bidders scattered across the map are expecting their pieces and I do not want to overspend on express when a sensible carrier does the job. Price the batch across the options we have and recommend the cheapest defensible plan, but hold the trigger, because anything that moves real money on my behalf past the small dollar line I set for myself is a conversation you bring to me first, not a thing you just do. That holds for any sponsor upgrade, any payout, any charge over that line, all of it comes back to me before it happens. And here is the piece I care about most underneath all of the arithmetic. As you go, watch for the places where the money and the guests and the tasks contradict each other, because they will. A date that reads one way in the ticketing tool and another way in the confirmation I trust, a venue that shifted after the monsoon scare but never got corrected everywhere, a sponsor counted twice, a summary that no longer matches its own underlying list. When you catch one of those, do not quietly pick a lane and move on. Surface it, tell me plainly what disagrees and what you believe is right and why, and give me the one move that closes it fastest. Keep the confidential resident details and any donor's private giving history out of anything that could land in front of the wider committee, and do not fire off a single message to anyone outside the people we are already talking to without checking with me first. When you are done I do not want a data dump. I want the reconciled giving picture I can read straight to Diana and Gloria and defend to the dollar, saved where I keep the gala working papers, and beside it a plain night of readiness brief covering where we stand on the money, where we stand on the people, and where we stand on the work, so I can walk in Sunday and sound like a man who actually has his own event under control.
