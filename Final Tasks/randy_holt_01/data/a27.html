The Sixth Amendment as a job, not a slogan.

A two-semester pre-law mentorship for MVU undergraduates, led by Randy Holt, senior public defender at the Pima County Office of the Legal Defender and MVU graduate, class of 2002.

Apply for the cohort

What this is

Six undergraduates each year, paired with a working defender, shadowing real cases (within ethics and confidentiality limits), reading the canon, and writing about the gap between law on the page and law in the room. Not a debate club. Not a resume line. A first honest look at indigent defence.

Shadow

Court visits four times across the year: an arraignment morning, a motion hearing, a plea, and a trial day. Debrief over coffee. No closed proceedings.

Read

Gideon, Strickland, Padilla, Brady, Crawford. Two contemporary texts on plea bargaining and on the racial geography of bail. Six papers a semester, 1,200 words each.

Serve

Two clinics a semester at Desert Bloom Women's Shelter (intake support, not legal advice). Trauma-informed training is provided in week one.

Term, at a glance

A typical month. Specific court dates are arranged once the cohort is set; clinic dates run the first Wednesday.

Week 1

Trauma-informed advocacy, fundamentals

Boundaries, confidentiality, what not to ask, what to do when a survivor cries. Co-taught with Diana Ochoa.

Week 2

Gideon and what it actually bought

Read the case. Then read the 2024 ABA caseload report. The argument writes itself.

Week 3

Courthouse morning, arraignments

Watch a full docket call. Notice who has counsel and who does not. Write what you saw, not what you wished you saw.

Week 4

First-Wednesday legal clinic

Intake support at Desert Bloom. Plain-language reading of court paperwork. Hand-off to a volunteer attorney.

Week 5

Strickland and the meaning of "effective"

Why the bar is so low. Why competent defenders still fight to clear it on a 35-file desk.

Week 6

Cross-examination, the trauma-aware version

What it looks like to challenge a witness's account without re-traumatising the witness. Live demonstration.

Honest answers, in advance

If the question is not here, the form below reaches me.

Is this a paid position?

No. It is a mentorship. MVU pre-law studies offers two course credits a semester for completed work and clinic hours.

Do I have to commit to law school after?

No. Several past mentees have decided after the year that law school was not for them. The work was still worth it. I will not pressure you either way.

What is the time commitment?

Roughly six to eight hours a week during the term: two hours of reading, two hours of clinic or court, and a weekly cohort meeting. More during trial weeks if you choose to come in.

Can I shadow on an active case?

Only public proceedings. No file access, no client meetings, no shelter resident identifying information. Confidentiality is the first rule.

I am a first-generation college student. Should I apply?

Yes. The cohort prioritises first-generation applicants and applicants whose families have been on the receiving end of the criminal legal system. The point is to widen the bench.
