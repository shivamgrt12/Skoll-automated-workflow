EARL WAYNE WHITAKER
November 3, 1942 - February 14, 2020

Earl Wayne Whitaker, 77, of Harlan, Kentucky, passed away peacefully
at home on February 14, 2020, surrounded by his family.

Born in Harlan County to Wayne and Edith Whitaker, Earl spent 34 years
as a coal miner before retiring in 1998. He was a deacon at Ridgeview
Baptist Church for 25 years and a devoted husband to June for 54 years.

Earl grew up in a company house up Clover Fork, the oldest of four, and
went into the mines the same week he finished school, the way most men
in that hollow did. He worked underground his whole career, first
loading coal by hand and later running a continuous miner, and he wore
the black lung papers he earned like every man who gave the mountain
his lungs. He never once complained about the work. He said it fed his
family and put his boys through school, and that was enough for him.

Before the mines took all his hours, Earl spent two summers braking on
the L and N rail line that hauled Harlan coal down to the Tennessee
yards, and he never lost his love of the railroad. He could tell you
the whistle of every engine that came through the gap. That rail and
freight blood ran on down to his son Floyd, who built a freight
hauling business out of Knoxville, and Earl was proud enough of it to
tell strangers at the feed store about it.

Earl was a fixture at Ridgeview Baptist. He served as deacon for 25
years, sang bass in the choir off key and loud, and never met a church
supper he would skip. He kept a big garden of beans, tomatoes, and
half-runner beans that June put up by the hundred jars. He loved bird
dogs, squirrel hunting up on Pine Mountain, black coffee, the Kentucky
Wildcats, and sitting on the porch of an evening telling the same
stories his boys could recite word for word.

Earl is survived by his wife June Whitaker of Harlan; sons Floyd
Whitaker (Donna) of Knoxville, TN and Darrell "Darl" Whitaker of
Harlan, KY; grandchildren Brandon, Megan, and Cody Whitaker; and
sister Edna Mae Collins of Pikeville, KY.

He was preceded in death by his parents, Wayne and Edith Whitaker, and
by two brothers, Roy Whitaker and Carl Whitaker, both of Harlan County.
He is also survived by many nieces, nephews, cousins, and a church
family that loved him, along with his old bird dog Sadie who waited by
the door for him to the end.

Services were held at Ridgeview Baptist Church on February 18, 2020.
Interment at Resthaven Cemetery, Harlan.

Pastor James Caldwell officiated, with the choir Earl loved singing him
home. Pallbearers were his sons Floyd and Darl, grandsons Brandon and
Cody, nephew Wesley Whitaker, and lifelong friend Estel Middleton. The
family received friends the evening before at the church.

In lieu of flowers, the family asked that memorials be made to the
Ridgeview Baptist Church building fund or to the Harlan County food
pantry, two causes close to Earl's heart. The family wishes to thank
the home health nurses and the good neighbors up Clover Fork who
brought food, sat with June, and never let the family stand alone.

"He was not a rich man by the world's count, but he was rich in the
things that last. He left his boys a good name and a strong back, and
he left this world square with his Maker."
