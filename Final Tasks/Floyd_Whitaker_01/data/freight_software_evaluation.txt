FREIGHT MANAGEMENT SOFTWARE EVALUATION
Floyd Whitaker - September 2026

Purpose: We are outgrowing the spreadsheets and the shoebox of
carrier packets. This is my write-up comparing the two systems we
trialed so we can make a call before the FreightFlow trial runs
out. Keith and Bren and Brand all weighed in.

FREIGHTFLOW PRO (Trial expires October 15, 2026)
- Cloud-based, $89/month for small brokers
- Good load matching, decent carrier database
- DOT compliance module is basic, would need manual supplements
- Mobile app works but is slow on Android
- Customer support has been responsive
- Missing: automated insurance expiry alerts

FreightFlow additional notes:
- Setup was quick, we were entering loads the same afternoon.
- Load board integration pulls in postings automatically.
- Invoicing is clean and it emails the customer a PDF.
- Reports are basic but export to spreadsheet fine.
- No automatic FMCSA pull, we would key SAFER data by hand.
- Two user seats included, extra seats are more per month.

ROUTEPOINT LOGISTICS SUITE
- Desktop + cloud hybrid, $165/month base + $45/month DOT compliance add-on
- Strong DOT compliance integration (pulls FMCSA data automatically)
- Carrier vetting scorecard built in
- Better reporting than FreightFlow
- No mobile app yet (web only)
- More expensive but more complete for our compliance needs

RoutePoint additional notes:
- The compliance add-on is the whole reason to look at it. It pulls
  authority, insurance, and safety ratings straight from FMCSA and
  flags a carrier before you ever tender a load.
- Insurance expiry alerts are automatic, which is exactly the gap
  FreightFlow leaves open. That alone could have caught the
  MountainView insurance lapse before it bit us.
- Carrier scorecard rates each hauler on safety and on-time.
- Reporting is deeper, profit per lane and per customer.
- Learning curve is steeper and the desktop piece has to be
  installed on the office machine.
- No phone app yet, so on the road you are on the web version.

Feature Comparison:
  Monthly cost:        FreightFlow $89 base  |  RoutePoint $165 base
  DOT compliance:      FreightFlow add-on/manual  |  RoutePoint $45 add-on, automatic
  FMCSA data pull:     FreightFlow manual  |  RoutePoint automatic
  Insurance alerts:    FreightFlow none  |  RoutePoint automatic
  Carrier scorecard:   FreightFlow no  |  RoutePoint yes
  Load matching:       FreightFlow strong  |  RoutePoint good
  Reporting:           FreightFlow basic  |  RoutePoint strong
  Mobile app:          FreightFlow yes (slow on Android)  |  RoutePoint web only
  Support:             FreightFlow responsive  |  RoutePoint business hours
  Setup time:          FreightFlow fast  |  RoutePoint slower, more setup

FreightFlow total: $89/month.
RoutePoint total: $165/month base plus the $45/month DOT compliance
add-on, which comes to $210/month all in.

Pros and Cons in plain terms:
- FreightFlow is cheaper and easier and the phone app is handy when
  I am out of the office, but it leaves the compliance work on us
  and it will not warn us when a carrier's insurance is about to
  lapse.
- RoutePoint costs more but it does the compliance heavy lifting,
  and given how tight DOT is getting into 2027, that is worth
  paying for. The lack of a phone app is the main thing I do not
  like.

What the team says:
Keith uses RoutePoint and likes it.
Bren prefers FreightFlow interface but says RoutePoint reports are better.
Brand has not tried either one.

My leaning: RoutePoint, for the compliance and the insurance
alerts, even at the higher price. The extra cost buys us protection
we clearly need. I want Brand to at least log into both before we
sign anything so everybody has hands on it.

Any purchase over our $250 spend approval threshold needs a second
sign-off before we commit, so whichever way we go the monthly
agreement gets reviewed first.

Decision needed by October 15 when FreightFlow trial expires.
