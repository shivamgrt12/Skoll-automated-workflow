Recovering Hanseatic Stories from the North Sea Floor

The North Frisian Medieval Wreck Survey investigates a cluster of 14th-century Hanseatic vessel remains in the shallow waters off the North Frisian Islands, combining multibeam sonar survey, controlled excavation, and close community partnership with Frisian heritage representatives.

Overview

About the Project

The German Bight hides one of the most archaeologically significant concentrations of medieval shipwrecks in northern Europe. Where the Hanseatic trade routes crossed the shallow shelf sea off the North Frisian coast, vessels were lost to storms, navigational hazards, and the harsh conditions of the outer Wattenmeer.

Our project focuses on a cluster of sonar anomalies first identified from archival research. Phase 1 completed systematic MBES coverage. Phase 2 confirmed the presence of a major organic timber deposit at Site NF-7. Phase 3 achieved detailed excavation and recovered 84 individually catalogued finds, including a significant 2.4-metre clinker plank section.

Phase 4 (planned summer 2027) will extend NF-7 documentation and investigate Target T08, a sonar anomaly detected in August 2025 that may represent an adjacent wreck deposit.

History

Project Timeline

2020-2022
Archival Research and Reconnaissance
Systematic review of historical charts, Hanseatic trade records, and existing survey data identified the North Frisian anomaly cluster as a priority investigation target. Community consultation programme initiated with Frisian heritage liaison.
2023 - Phase 1
MBES Survey and Site Selection
Systematic multibeam sonar survey of 12 km2. 47 candidate anomalies identified and classified. NF-7 confirmed as the primary investigation priority on the basis of its H1-class signature and position in the historical navigation channel.
2024 - Phase 2
Test Trenches and Confirmation
Test trenches at NF-7 confirmed substantial organic preservation and established stratigraphic context. 22 finds recovered. Clinker hull construction confirmed. Preliminary dating range 1350-1420 proposed on ceramic and structural evidence.
2025 - Phase 3
Detailed Excavation - 84 Finds
Full photogrammetric mosaic of exposed hull timbers. 84 finds catalogued. NF7-014 plank section (2.4m) recovered. Target T08 identified as possible adjacent wreck. Dendrochronology samples at Caledonia Maritime University for analysis.
2026-2027 - Phase 4
Planning and Phase 4 Field Season
DFG Phase 4 submission due November 2026. Field season planned from Husum base camp, summer 2027. Objectives: extend NF-7 documentation, investigate Target T08, complete sediment coring programme. EU Horizon decision expected Q4 2026.

Updates

Latest News

October 2025
Phase 3 Final Report Submitted to DFG
The Phase 3 final report, including the full finds catalogue and the Phase 4 planning outline, was submitted to the DFG on 07 October 2025. Acknowledgement of receipt received 14 October.
November 2025
Conservation Assessment Complete at Hamburg Maritime Museum
Dr. Frida Moe and the conservation team completed condition assessments for all 84 Phase 3 finds. Iron desalination and PEG timber treatment underway. Exhibition planning discussions commenced.
January 2026
New Publication: Sonar Methods in Nordic Journal of Maritime Archaeology
Gross and Whitfield (2026) published in the Nordic Journal of Maritime Archaeology, presenting the NF-7 sonar methodology and its validation results across three field seasons.
February 2026
EU Horizon Application Submitted
The joint EU Horizon application (reference 101187724) on climate change and submerged heritage was submitted in February 2026. Decision expected by 31 December 2026. Five-year programme if funded.
