Background

About

I am an associate professor in the Department of Archaeology, History, Religious Studies and Theology at Nordmeer Maritime University in Hamburg, where I hold a permanent position since 2010 and was promoted to associate professor in 2016.

My research centres on medieval Hanseatic maritime trade and its material evidence in the shallow-water North Sea environment. I lead the DFG-funded North Frisian Medieval Wreck Survey, now in its third phase following the highly productive 2025 field season at Site NF-7. I am also co-principal investigator on the North Sea Coastal Heritage Mapping project with Hamburg Maritime Museum, and co-leading an EU Horizon application on climate change and submerged heritage with Caledonia Maritime University.

Before joining Nordmeer Maritime University as a postdoctoral researcher in 2006, I completed a B.A. at Schleswig-Holstein University, an M.A. at Leiden Maritime Academy, and a doctorate at Nordmeer Maritime University examining medieval Hanseatic trading routes.

I supervise three doctoral candidates and two master's students. I teach one graduate seminar per semester in Maritime Archaeology Methods.

Active Work

Research Projects

Active - Fieldwork

North Frisian Medieval Wreck Survey

Lead PI on DFG-funded multi-year survey of medieval Hanseatic vessel remains off the North Frisian Islands. Phase 3 (summer 2025) excavated Site NF-7 in detail, recovering 84 catalogued finds. Phase 4 planned for summer 2027 from Husum.

DFG GR 4471/3  |  2023-2027
Active - Year 2

North Sea Coastal Heritage Mapping

Co-PI with Hamburg Maritime Museum on a four-year programme mapping submerged cultural heritage along the Schleswig-Holstein coast. Combines MBES survey with archival research and Frisian community consultation led by Ailo Reimer.

HMM-CHM-22  |  2022-2026
Decision Pending

Climate Change and Submerged Heritage

EU Horizon application submitted February 2026. Joint programme with Prof. James Whitfield at Caledonia Maritime University quantifying climate-driven threat to organic wreck preservation in the North Sea. Decision expected Q4 2026.

EU Horizon 101187724  |  Submitted 13.02.2026
Writing Phase

Monograph: Hanseatic Routes Beneath the German Bight

Comprehensive academic publication of the North Frisian Survey findings, in preparation. Two chapters drafted, three in outline. Target submission to publisher spring 2027. Chapter 5 includes Frisian oral history contribution by Ailo Reimer.

Draft target: spring 2027
Active - Teaching

MARE 6010: Maritime Archaeology Methods

Graduate seminar offered each semester. Covers the full research cycle from archival research and sonar survey to underwater excavation, finds analysis, and publication. Students work on datasets from the North Frisian Survey.
