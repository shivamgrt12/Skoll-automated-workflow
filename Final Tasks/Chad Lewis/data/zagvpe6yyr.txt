NORMA'S WEDNESDAY CHILI
(Copied off the recipe card in the kitchen drawer, in Chad's handwriting
originally so Brett could make it in Louisville - Angela asked for it Aug 2024.)

Feeds four. Or Chad plus leftovers for two lunches.
Total time: about 90 minutes, mostly simmering.
Diabetic-friendly per Dr Cartwright's dietitian - low sodium, no beer, no
brown sugar, drained beans. A1C was 7.1 last check, we're trying to hold that.

------------------------------------------------------------

INGREDIENTS

  1 lb  ground chuck 85/15 (Kroger meat counter, ask Terry to grind fresh
        if it's a Wednesday morning - he does it before lunch rush)
  1     medium yellow onion, diced small
  1     green bell pepper, diced small (Norma's garden Aug-Oct, store the rest of the year)
  3     cloves garlic, minced (Norma minces, Chad forgets and uses too much)
  1     14.5 oz can no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  1     8 oz can no-salt-added tomato sauce
  1     15 oz can kidney beans, drained AND rinsed
  1     15 oz can pinto beans, drained AND rinsed (Chad prefers these, Norma prefers black beans, we alternate)
  1 c   low-sodium beef broth (Kitchen Basics box in the pantry)
  2 T   chili powder (Norma's blend, see below)
  1 t   ground cumin
  1 t   smoked paprika
  1/2 t dried oregano (from Bed 4)
  1/4 t cayenne (Chad wants 1/2, Norma vetoes)
  1     bay leaf, whole
  black pepper to taste
  SALT: none added. Bay leaf and smoked paprika carry it. Dr Cartwright thanks you.

  Optional at the table:
    - shredded sharp cheddar (Chad, small handful)
    - sour cream (Norma, one spoonful)
    - green onion (both, from Bed 3 when in season)
    - saltine crackers (Chad, NO more than 6 - he counts, he means it)

NORMA'S CHILI POWDER BLEND (make a small jar every fall):
  2 T ancho powder
  1 T ground cumin
  1 T smoked paprika
  1 t garlic powder
  1 t onion powder
  1 t Mexican oregano
  1/2 t chipotle powder

------------------------------------------------------------

METHOD

1) Dutch oven medium-high. Brown the beef 6-8 min. Break it small.
   Drain the fat into the metal can Chad keeps under the sink for that
   purpose. Do not pour grease down the drain - remembered the plumbing bill
   from 2019.

2) Beef back to the pot on medium. Add onion, bell pepper, garlic.
   Cook 5-6 min until the onion is see-through.

3) Stir in chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, cayenne, bay leaf,
   black pepper. Toast the spices in the fat for 60 seconds. Norma is firm
   about this step. "Wake the spices up first." Do not skip.

4) Diced tomatoes with juice, tomato sauce, both beans, beef broth. Stir.
   Scrape the bottom of the pot for the good stuff.

5) Bring to a low boil. Cover, reduce to a bare simmer. 45 minutes.
   Stir every 15 min. Chad usually watches the news during this stretch.

6) Uncover, simmer another 15-20 min to thicken.

7) Fish the bay leaf out. Discard. Do not serve to a grandchild by accident.

8) Serve. Toppings at the table.

------------------------------------------------------------

DIABETIC NOTES (from the visit with Dr Cartwright's dietitian, Mar 2023)

  Per 1-1/2 cup serving, no toppings:
    ~320 calories
    ~24g carbs (14g from beans, rest from tomato + veg)
    ~28g protein
    ~9g fat
    ~440mg sodium (all from tomato products + broth, none added)
    ~7g fiber

  With 1 T sharp cheddar + 1 t sour cream + 4 saltines:
    ~410 cal, ~30g carbs, ~32g protein

  Pair with a green salad from Bed 3 (spinach + fall lettuce) for a full
  supper. Norma writes this down every Wednesday for the sugar log:
  pre-supper and 2-hr post-supper reading.
