# Memory: Jenny Wilkins

## Personal Profile
Born in Hartford, Connecticut. B.A. English Literature, Elm Valley College (1978). M.L.S. Library Science, Hartford Graduate Institute (1981). English is native, with reading-level academic French that can navigate a Parisian menu but not a conversation, and Jenny has made peace with that.

Nonbinary, they/them. Came out at 64 (2020). It was not a crisis but a correction, like finally shelving a book in the right section. Spouse Harriet adapted faster than anyone. Most friends and family know. Brother Gordon does not accept it, and they are estranged.

Thirty-two years behind a reference desk taught Jenny that most questions have an answer somewhere, and the trick is knowing which shelf to look on. That instinct runs through everything: the spice rack is alphabetized, the seed packets are sorted by sowing month, the bookshelves follow a private system four decades old. An excellent listener who remembers what you told them and asks the follow-up months later. Quiet, dry, and gently self-mocking, with a stubbornness underneath the gentleness that surprises people when they meet it. A small-group person who speaks in full paragraphs once warmed up, and a gentle contrarian who raises the question nobody asked.

A New Englander in the specific Connecticut sense, where culture is communicated through what is not said: reserve, dry humor, suspicion of self-promotion, and pride in things made well and kept longer than necessary. Anxiety shows up as preparation, managed through routine and planning rather than cured. Jenny believes most things are improved by writing them down, not for productivity but as respect, and that the people you love are the ones you keep showing up for in small ways. They believe in being recognized as oneself and extending that recognition to others, and that the cost of holding that line is sometimes a relationship, but the cost of not holding it is worse.

## Health & Wellness
- **Raynaud's syndrome** (diagnosed 2015): Fingers turn white or blue in cold, sometimes painful. Triggers are cold weather, stress, cold water. Heated gloves October through April. Dr. Farooq monitors, lifestyle management, no medication.
- **GERD** (chronic since 2018): Omeprazole 20mg daily, mornings before food. Elevated bed head, no eating after 6 PM, no eating within 3 hours of lying down. Tums for breakthroughs. Dr. Park monitors.
- **Osteoarthritis (hands, knees)**: Progressive. Fine motor tasks like buttoning, jar lids, and long writing are increasingly hard. Acetaminophen as needed, no NSAIDs (GERD contraindication). Dr. Farooq manages.
- **Hypertension**: Amlodipine 5mg daily. BP stable around 132/78. Dr. Park and Dr. Costello monitor.
- **Medications and supplements**: Omeprazole 20mg (daily, morning before food), amlodipine 5mg (daily, morning), acetaminophen 500mg (as needed), calcium plus vitamin D (daily), glucosamine 1500mg (daily).
- **Dietary restrictions (medical)**: GERD-managed. Avoids tomato sauce, citrus, spicy food, and chocolate after 3 PM. Nothing acidic after noon. Decaf only (caffeine worsens GERD). No food allergies. Misses tomatoes more than anything.
- **Exercise**: Daily 30-minute walk with Harriet, gentle yoga twice weekly (Tue/Thu mornings), gardening March through October. Limited by cold (Raynaud's) and hands and knees (arthritis).
- **Mental health**: Generalized anxiety, not formally diagnosed, managed through routine, planning, and acceptance. Unresolved but non-acute grief about Gordon. Finds gardening and reading therapeutic.

## Interests & Hobbies
The garden is the through-line: a quarter acre edited for nearly forty years. Jenny owns the perennial borders (hostas in dozens of cultivars, a favorite sport called 'June' with chartreuse hearts, echinacea in three colors, naturalized black-eyed Susans, native ferns in the shade) and keeps a graph-paper layout notebook updated each spring. Harriet owns the vegetable beds; Jenny is advisory staff on the tomato question. Reading is the other constant, read for the sentences as much as the plot, with a forty-year commonplace notebook of transcribed passages. Casual birdwatching from the kitchen window with a private yard list, never submitted to eBird. Library volunteering, part habit and part identity. Tried watercoloring, but arthritis made it frustrating.

## Preferences
- **Books**: Literary fiction, garden design, natural history, British mysteries. About 3 to 4 books a month across Kindle, audio, and physical. Reads English mysteries before American ones.
- **Music**: Classical chamber music and piano, folk, and jazz, played quietly. Owns a CD collection and uses Audible rather than streaming much.
- **Food and drink (non-medical taste)**: Gentle flavors. Butter, mild cheeses, soft herbs, roasted root vegetables, peppermint tea late in the day. Makes excellent soup, butternut squash the specialty. Harriet cooks most dinners; Jenny handles breakfast and lunch. Dinner by 6 PM. Alcohol rare, an occasional glass of white wine.
- **Shopping**: Buy well, buy slowly, keep it forever. Local first when the local option is good (Glastonbury Garden Center for plants, Byrd's Books for books, Stop & Shop for groceries, Lambert's Hardware), but will quietly order specialty items online. Loyal to good tools, like a fifteen-year-old pair of Felco shears sent out for sharpening. Gifts are thoughtful and specific, often a book or a divided plant with handwritten care notes. Reviews subscriptions annually with mild suspicion.
- **Clothing and aesthetics**: Comfortable, gender-neutral layers curated over the past four years: oatmeal, navy, and forest cardigans, soft-collar cotton and linen shirts, pressed trousers when called for, clogs and old jeans in the garden. Nothing fussy, nothing that announces. The house is old New England, worn-in and uncrowded, with yellow kitchen walls and copper pots.
- **Travel**: Mostly a New England exercise of foliage day trips, Vermont covered bridges, and coastal overnights, with two England trips in fifteen years for gardens and bookshops. Packs early, always with a book, a notebook, an extra sweater, and heated gloves October through April. Trip purpose matters more than destination; tourism for its own sake feels indecent.
- **Sensory and comfort**: Loves the smell of soil after rain, tomato leaves on the hand, old hardbacks, and the texture of worn linen. Decompresses in the garden or with a comfort re-read (Mary Oliver, Wendell Berry, the Aubrey-Maturin novels, certain Agatha Christies) and cooks to soothe structural worry. Cannot stand leaf blowers, loud restaurant music, or phone chimes. Dislikes surprises, even good ones, and people who do not return borrowed books.
