--- TURN 1 ---
The club's year is closing and I have to stand up in front of everyone at the annual members meeting and give a treasurer's report I can actually defend, so before that happens I need the whole picture trued up and every figure carrying its source, because I have quietly lost confidence that the books, the bank, and the various places money comes in and goes out still agree with one another. Start with the garden club itself. I want a clean accounting of the year built from the ground up, the dues we invoiced against what actually landed, the plant sale proceeds against what got deposited, the donations we were given, and every bill and supply run set against it, and I want the ending cash position to match the bank rather than to match what the books merely assert, because the books are only as honest as the last person who remembered to enter something. Some of the dues came in as checks and cash at meetings and some came in electronically, and I have a nagging feeling the electronic side and the counter side were not both entered the same way, so please chase each member's dues from the invoice through to money in the account and tell me plainly who is genuinely still owed and who only looks owed because of a bookkeeping lag. There is at least one member I keep meaning to follow up with about an outstanding balance and I do not want to accidentally wave that away just because a total happens to come out round. While you are in the dues, I would love a sober read on where the club actually breaks even, because the working figure I have been carrying around for a while may have been built on an old assumption about what we charge per member, and if the rate baked into that old estimate is not the rate we are really billing then the whole break-even story is wrong and the board deserves the corrected version, not the comfortable one. Then do the same discipline for the library friends side, which I keep as a separate set of books, because the used-book sales and the donation drive have their own rhythm and their own account, and the two organizations lean on each other enough that I want them reconciled in parallel rather than blurred together. The book-sale takings and the donation gifts came through more than one channel, and I am fairly sure at least one gift never fully cleared and at least one was partly given back, so anything that failed or reversed must not be sitting in my totals pretending to be income, and I need the net the library can bank on rather than the gross that flatters it. Cross-check the member and supporter rosters against who actually paid, because a name on a mailing list is not the same as a paid member and I would rather present twenty-something real members than a padded count I cannot stand behind. There is also an events picture to square, the fall swap and the sales days, where the registrations and the head counts should line up with the proceeds, and if the money and the attendance disagree I want to know which one to trust before I quote either. What I want to walk away holding is a year-end treasurer's report I can present and the board can trust, laid out so an ordinary member can follow where every dollar came from and went, and alongside it an honest reconciliation memo that lists exactly what did not line up, which source I ultimately believed and which I set aside and why, so that when someone at the meeting asks me a pointed question I have the provenance in hand instead of a shrug. I also want a plain-language summary I can read aloud at the meeting and a set of drafted follow-ups for the loose ends, the members still owing, the bill still open, the small corrections that need a note, all written and ready but sitting as drafts for me to look over rather than anything that goes out on its own, because I am not sending money or messages out into the world on my say-so and the banking stays where it always stays, with Harriet, not with either of us guessing at a portal. Where the evidence is thin or two records simply refuse to agree and I cannot settle which one deserves my trust, do not force a tidy figure to make me feel better, just flag it as open and tell me what you would need to settle it, because I would rather bring one honest unknown to the board than a confident mistake. Take it slowly and get it right, the meeting can wait an hour but the record cannot afford to be wrong, and when you have committed the conclusions somewhere I will actually find them again, give me the short version of what surprised you.
