# Memory: Larry Bates

## Personal Profile

- **Heritage**: Third-generation brewer in the southern Appalachian mountains, where his family has lived four generations and brewed for three.
- **Temperament**: Steady, decisive when needed, and unhurried when he can afford to be. He does not change recipes on impulse.
- **Character**: Loyal in ways that occasionally cost him commercially. Seventeen years of brewing patience shape his approach to the craft.
- **Education**: B.Sc. in Fermentation Science from Oregon State University, 2009. Siebel Institute advanced brewing technology certification, 2014.
- **Training**: Apprenticed under his father Thomas for six years before assuming the head brewer role in 2015.
- **Languages**: English native, German conversational and improving for export markets, basic French from a 2018 Quebec craft exchange.
- **Citizenship**: U.S. citizen born in Asheville, North Carolina. Lived in Corvallis, Oregon during his university years at Oregon State.
- **Cultural identity**: Appalachian by inheritance and conviction with a soft Western North Carolina accent. Protestant in a loose, cultural sense.
- **Philosophy**: Believes brewing is stewardship, not manufacturing. Prioritises continuity over disruption and the next generation over quarterly results.

From Hendersonville, NC. Practical, creative, true partner in life and business. Larry shows love through small steady actions; Sarah has taught him to give words occasionally.
- **Hana Bates**: Born July 11, 2018. Age 7, daughter, second grade at Asheville Municipal Elementary. Cheerful, curious, loves the brewery. Saturday morning walks with Larry are protected.

- **Greg Holloway**: Age 45, head brewer assistant, fifteen years at Bates Brewing Company. Expert grain specialist. Reliable, quiet, indispensable. Larry trusts him completely with the brewhouse.
- **Dave Caldwell**: Age 52, primary barley farmer. Grows specialty malting barley on 200 acres specifically for Bates Brewing Company. Third-generation farming family. The grain relationship is a forty-year partnership predating Larry.
- **Erin Whitfield**: Age 42, primary distributor contact at Blue Ridge Distribution. Ten-year professional relationship. Understands the brand positioning and fights for shelf space. Quarterly meetings.

## Work & Projects

- **Role**: Head brewer and owner of Bates Brewing Company, responsible for all brewing decisions and business operations.
- **Brewing duties**: Covers recipe design, grain selection, mash management, fermentation, conditioning, blending, and barrel programs in full.
- **Business duties**: Manages distribution, finances, marketing alongside Sarah, regulatory compliance, and brewery maintenance and upgrades.
- **Staff**: Five seasonal brewers from November through March and two year-round employees including head assistant Greg Holloway.
- **Production**: Approximately 2,500 barrels annually of barrel-aged imperial stout, farmhouse ale, pale ale, and seasonal limited releases.
- **Ingredients**: Locally grown barley, Appalachian hops, mountain spring water, in-house cultivated yeast, and proprietary fermentation blends sourced regionally.
- **2026 season**: Fourteen successful February batches completed. Experimental barrel-aged imperial stout flagged for Great American Beer Festival competition entry.
- **Export expansion**: Growing exports from 10% to 20% of production with British Craft Imports (UK) and Pacific Craft Distributors (Singapore).
- **Phase 2 renovation**: Fermentation room climate control and tank sensors. Budget $350,000 with Blue Ridge Construction, August through September 2026.
- **2027 experiment**: Planning a barrel-aged imperial stout using 100% locally malted barley at an extended eighteen-month aging timeline.
- **Belgian trip**: Considering a 2027 study pilgrimage to Belgian monasteries, Wallonia farmhouse breweries, and Brussels bars his grandfather visited.

## Preferences

- **News**: *Wall Street Journal* (business daily), *Asheville Citizen-Times* (local), Brewers Association newsletter, *Craft Beer & Brewing*. Focused reader, does not browse aimlessly.
- **Music**: Classic rock (Led Zeppelin, Pink Floyd), blues, Americana, bluegrass. Silence during critical brewing operations.
- **Podcasts**: *Craft Beer Talk*, *NPR Morning Edition*, *Fermentation Lab*, *Business Today*. Drives and off-season mornings.
- **Reading**: Brewing science texts (Ray Daniels, John Palmer). American literature: McCarthy, Hemingway, Steinbeck. History of American craftsmanship.
- **Gifts received**: Artisan ceramics, quality knives, woodworking tools, rare beers from other breweries.
- **Gifts given**: His own beer (always) and Appalachian specialty foods. A specific bottle for an old mentor, a cookbook for Margaret. For Sarah he has slowly learned to do flowers.
- **Food**: American home cooking, seasonal emphasis. Margaret cooks weekdays; Sarah weekends. Locally milled bread, hearty soups, grilled meats, brewing-season stews, Margaret's pickled vegetables. Regular spots: The Farmhouse Tavern (monthly with Moreland), Blue Ridge Smokehouse (celebrations), Craft Beer Hall (industry networking).
- **Drinks**: His own beer daily with dinner in a small amount. Competitor products tasted regularly as professional practice. Drip coffee throughout the day. Occasional bourbon from a local craft distillery.
- **Sensory anchors**: Smell-anchored. Warm mash, yeast in active fermentation, petrichor on the Asheville hills, cold November air. Sounds: slow bubble of early fermentation, Hana's footsteps on the brewery floor, glycol chiller hum.
- **Comfort and decompression**: The brewhouse stool near the lautering tun in the late-afternoon light. Otherwise, a short ridge hike or a walk through the back fields.
- **Shopping**: Pays full price for brewing equipment, boots, and books. Refuses full price on most else. Gives to a local watershed group, the Brewers Association educational fund, and an Asheville music venue.
- **Travel**: Mostly industry. Leisure once a year, off-season, with Sarah and Hana, to somewhere quieter than Asheville. Prefers driving to flying for any reachable destination. Rented cabins in mountain towns. Coastal North Carolina in October.
- **Dislikes**: Breweries that confuse marketing for craft. Consultants who use "consumer insights" without irony. Distributors who pressure for volume at the expense of quality. Over-hopped beer that buries the malt. Loud restaurants. Cell phones at the dinner table.
