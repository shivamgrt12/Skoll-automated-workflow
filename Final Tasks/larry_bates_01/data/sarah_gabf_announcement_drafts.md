## Draft A — If we place

**Subject line option 1:** A note from the brewhouse  
**Subject line option 2:** What the experiment said  
**Preview text:** Recognition at this year's Great American Beer Festival, and what it means for our spring release.

---

Friends,

There is a beer we have been working on for the better part of two years. We sourced the barley from a back-forty plot on the Caldwell farm, malted small, and we aged a single-origin imperial stout in barrels that have been part of this brewery's program longer than most of you have known us. We entered it this year because we wanted to know whether the patience showed up in the glass.

This year at the Great American Beer Festival, the Experimental Single-Origin Imperial Stout placed in Category 23, Barrel-Aged Strong Stout.

We are grateful. We are also, as Larry would say, the same brewery we were on Tuesday. The beer was good before the medal and it is good now. What the recognition does is tell us that one specific path — a single farm, a single year, a long quiet wait — is a path worth keeping open.

A small allocation of the experimental will release this spring, late May, to the bottle club first and then to the brewery tasting room. There will not be a wide distribution. The barrel program does not run that way and we are not going to start now.

We will say more in the next newsletter. For tonight, thank you for being part of a brewery that has been allowed to take its time.

Larry, Sarah, Greg, and the team at Bates

412 Stoneridge Mill Road, Asheville NC

## Draft B — If we do not place

**Subject line option 1:** A note from the brewhouse  
**Subject line option 2:** What the experiment said  
**Preview text:** A short note on this year's Great American Beer Festival, and what the work was for.

---

Friends,

There is a beer we have been working on for the better part of two years. We sourced the barley from a back-forty plot on the Caldwell farm, malted small, and we aged a single-origin imperial stout in barrels that have been part of this brewery's program longer than most of you have known us. We entered it this year because we wanted to know whether the patience showed up in the glass.

This year at the Great American Beer Festival, the Experimental Single-Origin Imperial Stout did not place in its category.

We are not writing to soften that. The judging at the festival is honest work by serious people, and the beer that won won. The reason we entered was not to come home with a medal. We entered because we wanted the work to be seen by people who would tell us the truth. The truth came back, and we will be reading the notes carefully when they are released.

A small allocation of the experimental will release this spring, late May, to the bottle club first and then to the brewery tasting room. We are still proud of the beer. The barrels are still in the cellar. We are still planning, for the 2027 winter program, to do a longer-aged version with a hundred percent locally malted barley, on a timeline that we will only be able to defend if we keep our heads down.

Thank you for being part of a brewery that has been allowed to take its time.

Larry, Sarah, Greg, and the team at Bates  
Bates Brewing Company, est. 1923  
412 Stoneridge Mill Road, Asheville NC
