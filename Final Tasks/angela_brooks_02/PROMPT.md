I need a straight answer on whether the Lantern Tides demo actually ships for the Showcase, and I need it defensible enough that I can say it out loud to Ravi and to Professor Nakamura without hedging, because I have been telling people for months that there will be something playable on that table and somewhere in the last few passes I lost the thread on how close it really is. The honest problem is that my read on the build comes from three different places that have quietly drifted apart while I was heads down in Aseprite. There is where I log what is broken, there is what the demo itself reports back when it falls over in front of a playtester, and there is what the sessions show people actually doing right before they quit, and I have been treating those as one picture when they are plainly not one picture. Some of what I have marked as handled has been sitting marked as handled for a long stretch and I have no confidence it stayed handled through the chapter three rework, and there are places where players are dropping out that I do not think I ever logged at all, which is the part that actually scares me. So run the whole defect surface down, the entire hundred-odd of it and not the top of the list where the labels are tidy, reconcile it against what the build and the sessions are telling you, and for anything that would matter in front of a room do not take one source's word for it. Ravi has been pushing on the audio side against the same deadline and that work does not always announce itself to me, so his half of the picture counts too. What I want at the end of it is a readiness position I can act on, ranked by what would embarrass me in front of a crowd, with a clean line between what is in the demo, what gets cut and rolled into a later chapter, and what is genuinely still open. I want the open things to stay open rather than get a verdict you had to reach for. If the evidence is thin, say it is thin. I would rather walk in knowing there are two unknowns than walk in with a confident number that falls apart when the first person at the booth finds the crash in ninety seconds. Then the table itself, which I have been avoiding for obvious reasons. The print catalog has grown past what I can hold in my head, the counts I think I have are counts I have not laid eyes on since before the last run of orders went out the door, and prints have been moving through the shop that whole time without me reconciling a single thing. I need to know what I can actually sell rather than what a stock number claims I have, and I need it per design rather than as one comfortable lump, because the big pieces move nothing like the small ones and I am not hauling dead weight across town on a bus for the pleasure of carrying it home again. Off the back of that, tell me what to bring, what to reorder and what to let sell out, work the take I can realistically expect against what the table actually costs me once the fees come out of it, and show me the method rather than a total, because I will check it and you know I will check it. The printer needs lead time and the table has to pay for itself before it pays for anything else, so I want the number in front of me with the reasoning attached rather than a figure I have to take on faith. What I want out of that half is a booth and merch picture I can trust and hand to Suki while she is covering the table and I am stuck demoing, which means it has to make sense to somebody who has not been living inside this for a year. And the room, which is the part I keep pretending is handled. The list of who is actually coming has gotten long enough that I cannot eyeball it anymore, it does not agree with what I have been keeping in my own tracker, and the committee has been shuffling the run of show around without telling anybody who is not on the committee. I want to know who in that room is worth ten of my minutes, which of the people already on the early access list are going to be physically standing there and should hear something before they arrive rather than after, and whether the press side is real or whether it is me posting a clip and hoping. Ravi is flying up for this on his own money, and I would like the coverage to justify that. Draft whatever needs to go out, all of it, and draft it properly rather than as a placeholder I have to rewrite, because the press ones in particular get exactly one read before somebody decides whether Lantern Tides is worth a paragraph. People have been asking to see the build early, and one of them has been asking in a way that makes it obvious they expect a yes. Sequence the whole thing however it actually makes sense, though I do not think the outreach is worth writing until the ship list is fixed, and I do not think the table math is worth doing until the counts are real. I have a well documented habit of solving the small beautiful problem instead of the large ugly one, so if what you find is that the large ugly one is that the demo does not ship, lead with that, and then tell me what a version that does ship looks like.
