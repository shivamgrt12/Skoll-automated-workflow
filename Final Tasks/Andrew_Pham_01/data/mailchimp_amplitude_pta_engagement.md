### Subject-line experiment summary

Six bilingual sends across the window averaged **34.5% open rate and 7.6% CTR**. Six
English-only sends across the same window averaged **29.7% open rate and 5.4% CTR**. The
bilingual lift is real but not uniform. The October 7 bilingual send ("Song Ngu Suy Nghi")
underperformed the English-only baseline for the same month at 28.7% open rate, likely
because the subject line was too abstract. The honest read for Angela is that bilingual
subject lines are landing when they are concrete and welcoming, and they are landing less
well when they are conceptual. Bilingual households appear to be responding to the same
words their parents at home would say to them.

### Blog traffic read

The August spike is normal for a school-year restart. What is quiet but real is that
bilingual posts have carried between 25% and 45% of monthly attention, and September in
particular showed a majority-Vietnamese-language post as the month's top read. This
suggests the community is actively looking for bilingual content, not tolerating it.
