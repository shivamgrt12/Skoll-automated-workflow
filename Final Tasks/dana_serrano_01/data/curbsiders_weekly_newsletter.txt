#4XX - Cardiac Amyloidosis: When to Suspect, How to Work It Up
We sat down with a cardiology-amyloid expert to talk about the red flags you
should not miss on the wards: bilateral carpal tunnel, HFpEF with discordant
voltage, low-flow low-gradient AS, and the pyrophosphate scan. Plus when to send
the light chains before you scan.

Pearls:
  - Think ATTR in the older patient with HFpEF and "thick heart."
  - Always rule out a plasma cell disorder first (SPEP/UPEP + free light chains).
  - Bilateral carpal tunnel can predate heart failure by years.
