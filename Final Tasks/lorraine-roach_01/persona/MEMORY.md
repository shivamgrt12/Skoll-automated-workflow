# Memory: Lorraine Roach

## Personal Profile

- **Full name**: Lorraine Roach, a Black-Hawaiian-Southern woman in marine conservation science on windward Oahu
- **Birthplace**: Born in Kailua-Kona on the Big Island, raised there, schooled in Hilo and Honolulu
- **Heritage**: Father is African-American with family roots in Atlanta; mother is Caucasian-American with Hawai'i ties since the 1940s
- **Citizenship**: U.S. citizen raised in Kailua-Kona before settling on windward Oahu as an adult
- **Undergraduate degree**: B.S. in Marine Biology from Pacific Crest University in Hilo, completed in 2016
- **Graduate degree**: M.S. in Conservation Biology from Seaview Graduate Institute in Honolulu, completed in 2019
- **Career entry**: Joined Coral Futures Institute as Marine Conservation Biologist in 2020, now six years tenure
- **Primary language**: English native speaker with a Hawai'i Pidgin lilt that fades in federal agency settings
- **French**: Conversational level, college-acquired, used occasionally for language-exchange practice
- **Hawaiian**: Basic level, learning patiently through Sam and community cultural contexts on the island
- **Temperament**: Steady and layered, listens before speaking, shaped by reef work where change shows subtly
- **Grief awareness**: Carries quiet ongoing grief about reef decline, processed with a counselor in 2023
- **Political style**: Sharper politically than her quiet manner suggests, patient with slow institutional battles
- **Philosophy**: Views science as stewardship and treats traditional ecological knowledge with equal respect
- **Spiritual life**: Not institutionally religious; her closest sacred practice is dawn on the water before paddling

- **Program expansion**: Expanding the monitoring program to three new sites in 2026, launch planned for September
- **Policy brief**: Contributing author on a Pacific Marine Protected Area brief, submission deadline October 16, 2026
- **Brief details**: Co-authoring with two mainland academics, compiling field evidence from three Hawaiian study sites
- **Weekly schedule**: Desk days Monday, Wednesday, Friday at the Kāne'ohe office; field days Tuesday and Thursday

## Preferences

- **Food and drink**: pescatarian, locally caught fish when possible, strong sustainability lens. Regular spots: Tide Pool Kitchen (weekly poke), Sunrise Café (morning açaí and coffee), Ember & Salt (date nights with Sam), Aunty Lani's Plate Lunch (occasional). Two cups of coffee daily, pour-over at home with Kona Ridge Coffee Collective beans.
- **Music**: Hawaiian slack-key guitar, neo-soul (Erykah Badu, D'Angelo), indie folk, ocean-sounds focus playlists. Sam has gotten her into reggae.
- **Podcasts**: *Below the Surface*, *Reef Beats*, *The Long Dive*. Listens during paddles and commute.
- **News**: *Kailua Windward Press* (local), *Honolulu Star-Beacon* (statewide), *Atlanta Journal-Constitution* occasionally for Grandma Ruth context. Follows ocean conservation policy, Hawaiian sovereignty issues, and climate science closely.
- **Aesthetic and style**: functional island with Southern roots. Loose linen, athletic wear, dive gear, one good linen dress for conferences. Wears her grandmother's gold chain since high school and a small wooden pendant Sam carved her last birthday. Natural materials, durable construction, objects that survive sand and salt.
- **Sensory world**: temperature- and pressure-anchored. Notices saltwater at depth, wetsuit fit, outdoor-shower cool. Smells stored with precision: saltwater, sunscreen, the kitchen on a Sunday, Grandma's apartment in memory. Comfortable low-volume sound layering at home (slack-key, palms, ocean). True silence makes her slightly uneasy. The exception is thirty feet down.
- **Shopping**: strong values lens. Money is a moral artifact. Pays full price for reef-safe sunscreen, locally caught fish, and the few professional pieces she actually wears. Refuses full price for almost everything else. Quietly disciplined saver toward the Atlanta visit.
- **Gifts she gives**: experiential or locally sourced. Whale-watching tickets for Beth and Marcus, specific Kona coffee for Grandma Ruth, reef-safe sunscreen and a framed photo for Jenna. For Sam on his birthday, handmade.
- **Comfort and decompression**: water first when the day has been heavy. Sam second, who does not try to fix the day but cooks for her and puts on slack-key. Comfort foods include Grandma's collard greens, cornbread, and the specific fried fish Gerald used to make. Cries privately and rarely.
- **Dislikes**: eco-tourism that prices itself authentic while damaging ecosystems. Conference panels about Hawai'i with no Hawai'i speakers. "Marine resources" framing that flattens living systems. Parachute scientists. "Lucky to live in paradise." Tourists who do not respect coral. Loud restaurants. Day-trippers who litter the canoe-launch beach. Anyone using "paradise" without irony.
