# Home studio buildout notes 2024 (archive)

Filed as reference. The buildout was done over Feb 4-18 2024 with breaks. Main contractor: Northside Build Coop. Total spend $14,820 across labor, lumber, electrical, paint. Receipts archived in the 2024 quarterly invoices file.

## Floor plan and structural

- 14ft x 18ft main shoot bay carved out of the back half of the apartment. South wall painted Benjamin Moore Decorator's White (OC-149) eggshell, three coats over Kilz primer. Reads neutral at 5500K with no green cast.
- 12ft ceiling above the bay (pre-war loft). Drop bar with Manfrotto 088 baby pin lifters bolted into the joist line on 24in centers. Load tested to 80lb per pin.
- 9ft cyc curve poured in lightweight microcement against the southwest corner, 4ft radius. Sanded smooth to 220 grit, sealed with two coats Modac matte. Reads pure white at 5500K once gelled.
- 220V dedicated circuit pulled by Lina Electric (S corp, licensed), $1,800. Two 20-amp outlets at the south wall for the Profoto B10X charger plus the strip light.

## Lighting and gel

- Profoto B10X Plus key plus the original B10X as fill. Both stored on a top shelf in the corner closet.
- 3x C-stand century, 1x C-stand turtle base. Avenger A2030D blocks.
- Westcott Rapid Box Switch 36in octa with grid. The grid is the difference for tabletop, kept it.
- Modern Studio Equipment gobo set, 12 pieces, hand-painted shadow patterns for the cookbook hand work shots.
- Rosco gel kit (full set), color correction (CTO, CTB, plus green), neutral density. Stored in the labeled flat file under the workbench.

## Tether and capture

- 15ft Tether Tools puck pro USB-C plus angled C lock. Two tether pucks total, one backup.
- M2 Pro Mac mini at the cart, Capture One Pro on a 1TB external NVMe. The 1TB drive is the daily working drive, gets cleared every Friday to the local 4-bay Thunderbolt enclosure (OWC).
- Eizo ColorEdge CG279X at the cart for tether monitoring. Calibrated monthly with the i1Display Pro.

## Floor and surface

- Lay-flat anti-fatigue mat at the cart position. The 14-hour cookbook days were killing my back in 2023.
- Two 4ft x 8ft Birch ply boards painted matte white as bounce. One painted matte black on the reverse. Stored vertical in the closet.
- Surface board library: 18 boards, 24x36in, stained or painted. Categorized as warm wood, cool wood, marble, concrete, slate, fabric, papers. The catalog is in the Notion vault under Studio/Surfaces.

## Storage and organization

- IKEA Trofast 9-bin frame at the south wall for props. Labeled by category: glassware, ceramics, linen, flatware, garnish, paper, tape, hardware, scratch.
- Floating shelf 6ft above the cart for the lighting accessory kit.
- A small drawer of branded fabric scraps from prior shoots for swatch matching.

## What did not work and was changed mid-buildout

- Originally specced a 12ft x 12ft bay. Felt cramped after the test cookbook overhead rig setup on day 6. Reworked the wall to push the bay to 14ft x 18ft. Cost the extra $1,200 in framing and paint.
- First cyc pour was the wrong product (Ardex SD-T, too rough). Sanded down and re-poured in microcement (Modac) on day 11. Lost two days of work.
- The 220V dedicated circuit was originally specced as 110V. Realized in week 1 the Profoto B10X Plus charger pulls enough that running the kettle on the same circuit was browning the line. Re-pulled to 220V dedicated. $600 change order.

## Open items as of buildout close

- Acoustic treatment for the corner where I do client calls. Currently a hard echo. Plan: 4 ATS Acoustics 24x48in panels, around $360 total. Deferred to Q4 2024 (still deferred).
- Replace the sliding closet door with a curtain track so I can pull props faster. Deferred.
- The cyc edge starts to show at 30-degree key angle if the subject is more than 6ft tall. Limitation, not a fix. Just shoot tighter.
