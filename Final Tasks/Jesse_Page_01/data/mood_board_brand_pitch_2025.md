# Mood board brand pitch 2025 (archive)

Pulled together for the Q4 2024 to Q1 2025 brand outreach cycle. Used in one form or another for the Tangerine pitch, the Estrela cookbook pitch, the Oatly pitch, and the Hatch Beverages cold outreach. Not active anymore. Archived for reference.

## Overall direction

Warm pre-dawn morning light. Bedford Avenue at 6:30 AM in October. Pastel ceramics. Sourdough crumb. The thing where you can see one hand in the frame doing something just outside of focus. Steam off coffee in cold air. The opposite of overhead-flat-lay influencer food. Editorial standard, not Instagram.

## Reference shoots

- Saul Leiter snow-on-windows series, 1957-1960. The way warm interior light reads against blue ambient.
- William Eggleston the Greenwood Mississippi series. Banal subjects, formally rigorous frames.
- Stephen Shore Uncommon Places, the diner counter shots. Composition first, subject second.
- Hannah Whitaker Peer studies. The repetition framing.
- Aaron Wojack New York Times restaurant takeouts, 2021-2023 archive. The way he handles human hands and food without making it cute.
- Brooke Holm Bon Appetit cover work 2022-2024. The natural light interior style I want to push toward.

## Palette

- Warm white plates against medium-toned woods (oak, walnut). Avoid cool-white modern ceramic.
- Linen napkins in oatmeal, terracotta, dusty rose, sage. No primary colors unless the brand asks.
- Single-vase floral, not arrangements. Late autumn dahlias or November chrysanthemums.
- Steam, smoke, condensation. Real motion not gimmick.

## Tone language for the deck

- "Editorial honesty over commercial polish"
- "Composed but not styled"
- "Anchored in the morning"
- "The shot you would write down at the diner"

## Things to avoid in the pitch

- Overhead flat lay reference images. Already saturated.
- Marble countertop references. Tired by 2023.
- The hand-pours-over-bowl trope. Brittany at Hatch literally asked for the opposite of this.
- Reference to any specific competing brand by name. Always describe the visual, never the company.

## Outcome by client

- Tangerine: signed. Spread 1 of the fall issue uses 2 frames from this board (the Bedford morning glass and the sage linen on oak). Q1 2025 invoiced $1,500.
- Estrela: cookbook signed. Mood board referenced in the Salt + Press editor pitch deck Mar 2025. Carried through the year.
- Oatly: signed retainer, 12 months. Mira Castellanos referenced the "warm pre-dawn" language in the brief.
- Hatch Beverages: signed cold from this board. Brittany said the words "this is exactly what we cannot get from agency reels".

## What I would change for the 2026 cycle

- Remove the Stephen Shore reference. Too photo-historic for brand decks. Save it for editorial cold pitches.
- Add Lucy Sparrow's installation work as a left-field reference. The way she handles food as object.
- Replace the dahlia palette section with a winter vegetable monochrome study. Beets, carrots, celery root.
- Drop the "honesty over polish" line. Sounds preachy in 2026. Replace with "the slow shot in a fast kitchen".
