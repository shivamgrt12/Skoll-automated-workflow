# Flushing dim sum dish log

Personal taste log. Running list of dim sum dishes ranked across the Flushing places we eat at. Two-restaurant rotation (Golden Harvest Palace, Asian Jewels Seafood). Updated after each visit.

## Top tier

- Har gow at Golden Harvest. Skin is thin enough to see the pink shrimp through. Asian Jewels version is slightly thicker and overcooked at the seal. Golden Harvest wins.
- Siu mai at Golden Harvest. Pork-shrimp balance is right. Asian Jewels overdoes the shrimp. Golden Harvest wins.
- Cheung fun beef at Golden Harvest, plain (no XO sauce). The beef is hand-chopped not ground, you can see the texture. Best in Flushing for this style.
- Egg tart Portuguese style at Asian Jewels. Caramelized top, custard not too sweet. Golden Harvest version is fine but the crust is too thick.
- Turnip cake pan-fried at Golden Harvest. Crisp edge, dense interior. Sausage inclusions visible. Eat hot.

## Mid tier

- Char siu bao steamed. Both places do this well. Asian Jewels is slightly sweeter. Neither is a destination dish.
- Mango pudding. Both places. Kevin's favorite. Diplomatic order, not a serious dish.
- Spring roll mini. Crunchy at both. Filling is forgettable.
- Chicken feet braised. Mateo's standing order. Asian Jewels does the sauce slightly more aggressively. Golden Harvest is more restrained.
- Stuffed eggplant in black bean. Asian Jewels wins, eggplant holds together better.
- Sticky rice in lotus leaf. Asian Jewels. Golden Harvest version dries out by the time the cart reaches us.

## Skip tier

- Anything with cream cheese filling. Western cross-contamination, also lactose issue for me.
- The fried sesame ball at either place. Always cold by the time it lands.
- The shu mai that uses ground beef instead of pork-shrimp. Once at Asian Jewels in March. Did not order again.
