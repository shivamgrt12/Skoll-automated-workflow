# Saturday Cookout Menu - Brainstorm

Working notes for what I bring to the grill and what I stay out of Mom's way on for
the Saturday backyard cookout in Houston. Coordinate with Mom before I commit. She
has been running Halloween-to-Christmas potlucks since I was in shorts and she does
not like surprises on the sides table.

## The Rule

Mom always overdoes the sides. Last three trips she has made her potato salad by
sunrise and put out corn on the cob, coleslaw, deviled eggs, and a green bean
casserole she claims is new every year. If I bring another potato salad, or if I
bring anything cold that competes with a Linda side, I will hear about it for a
month. So I stay on the protein side of the table.

## What I Bring

Primary: brisket. This is what I do. The Big Green Egg is at home in Kirkwood but Dad
has a decent offset in the backyard shed and I have run it before. I bring the rub
and the injection kit from home and I show up Friday afternoon to trim and prep.

Backup protein: pulled pork. If the brisket cut is questionable at H-E-B, I pivot
to pulled pork. Same overnight cook, easier hold, feeds a bigger yard.

Sauce and finishing: I bring my own sauce base from home and one bottle of the
Smoke and Oak style for the folks who want sweet. Dad likes vinegar. Rachel likes
sweet. Karen keeps me honest on the salt.

## What I Do Not Bring

- Potato salad. Mom owns this. Do not repeat 2024.
- Coleslaw. Mom's is fine and it will already be on the table.
- Corn on the cob. If it is on the table it is Mom's. If it is not, we will not miss it.
- Deviled eggs. See above.
- Any dessert. Rachel usually brings a pie from a place in Dallas near the hospital.

## Kid Menu

- Ethan lives on chicken nuggets. I bring a bag from home for the cooler run. Grill
  a couple of chicken thighs plain in case he decides he is a grown up that day.
- Lily eats anything with rice. Mom always has rice at some point in the weekend.
  Confirm with Mom that Saturday afternoon includes a rice dish or I do a small
  side of jasmine rice on the grill side burner.
- Watermelon slices for both twins. Cold from Mom's second fridge.

## Grocery Support

If Mom needs a run I will hit the H-E-B on the way in from IAH or Friday morning
before the twins are up. Ask her Wednesday what her list looks like so I am not
duplicating a Costco run she has already done.

## Timing Notes

- Prep Friday afternoon at Dad's
- Overnight cook or pre-dawn start Saturday depending on cut size
- Ready to slice around 2 PM Saturday for a mid-afternoon cookout
- Do not race the clock, the goal is Dad in a lawn chair with a beer while I work

## Cross-Check Against Karen

Karen is lactose intolerant. Mom's mashed potatoes and any dairy-heavy side gets
flagged for Karen. She has her own snack rotation for the trip and knows Mom's yard
by heart. She will handle her own line at the sides table. Nothing on my brisket
plate is dairy-adjacent.

## Cross-Check Against the Twins

Nothing spicy on my brisket. Ethan will not touch it if he sees pepper on the crust.
Slice a plain portion for him first, then hit the rest with the pepper finish for
the grown up plates.

## Follow Up After

Text Mom the menu the day before we fly, not the day of. Give her runway to reset if
she wants to add or drop anything. Do not send the trip logistics until Karen and I
sign off on the final plan.
