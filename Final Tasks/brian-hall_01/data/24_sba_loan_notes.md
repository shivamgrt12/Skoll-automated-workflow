# SBA Loan Notes - Lab Buildout Equipment Tranche

Working notes on the SBA-backed equipment loan through Live Oak Bank.
This is Brian's working memory, not a loan document. Everything here is
under exploration and coordination - nothing here commits, releases,
approves, or signs anything on Brian's behalf.

## The vendor and the bank

- Lender: Live Oak Bank. Selected because their SBA lending group came
  in with the best rate on the equipment tranche among quotes reviewed
  earlier this year. Rate: 7.1% (baseline held from vendor comparison,
  final terms locked at closing).
- Approx tranche size: $74,500. Sized to cover the four-piece VALD
  equipment package (see file 12) - force plate, NordBord, AlterG,
  VALD hub - not the buildout construction itself.
- Buildout construction is separate cash - handled through Peak
  Performance PT operating and the dedicated lab buildout reserve
  account, tracked against the $120,000 approved budget (see file 04).

## Why an SBA loan on equipment and not just cash

- The buildout construction alone eats a substantial chunk of the
  $120,000 approved budget. Equipment on top would drain the buildout
  reserve and force a raid on the emergency fund. Not doing that.
- SBA equipment terms (long amortization, competitive rate) match the
  useful life of the equipment. Force plate and AlterG are 7 to 10
  year assets, not 24-month burns.
- Keeps the operating account and personal accounts unencumbered for
  Q4 tax obligations, twins' 529 contributions, and normal household
  and clinic operations.
