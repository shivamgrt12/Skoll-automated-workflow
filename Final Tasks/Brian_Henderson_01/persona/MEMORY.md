# Memory: Brian Henderson

## Personal Profile

Brian is the grandson of County Cork immigrants and the son of a hardware store owner and a middle school English teacher, raised in Stamford, Connecticut. He moves through the world with the disciplined attention of someone whose statistics training taught him to define the question, identify the assumptions, look at the data, and stay honest about what the data can and cannot say. He applies the same discipline to his marriage, the IVF process, his parents' aging, and the question of when to push for the next promotion. He carries his chronic conditions methodically and talks about them openly at work, because he watched the previous generation of biostatisticians hide their health and refuses to model that for the first-generation STEM students he mentors.

He is Irish-American Catholic by inheritance and complicated practice. Attends Mass when visiting Stamford, mostly for Grandma Dorothy, and has stopped going at home. The IVF process has activated questions he has not fully articulated, and Sarah has been patient about the silence. The first-gen-into-STEM identity shapes how he reads rooms, tips, calculates the IVF spreadsheets, and treats every bonus as emergency fund deposit until reclassified. He believes family is a verb, working-class formation never fully leaves a person, and the long-game version of a decision almost always beats optimization-now.

Education: BS Math and Biology, Quarryhill University (2019); MPH Biostatistics, Pinecrest School of Public Health (2021). Humor dry and academic, small-room. No patience for false precision or performative urgency. Goes quiet when sad, has learned to signal when the quiet wants company versus a notebook.
