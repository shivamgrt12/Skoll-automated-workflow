# Memory: Gerald Ellison

## Personal Profile
Gerald Thomas Ellison is the oldest of four in a working-class Anglo-American family with roots in Ohio and Harlan County, Kentucky. He carries quiet golden-child weight: parents lean on him, siblings look up to him, and adults relax around him without him having to ask for it. His public face is steady, cheerful, and gracious; his private side keeps poems, half-finished songs, and the Lakemont question in a corner of the iPhone notes app that family does not see. He is competitive but never bitter, congratulates winners sincerely, then practices twice as hard the next morning. His word is currency he refuses to inflate. When stress hits, his jaw tightens, his Kentucky accent thickens, and he gets quieter rather than louder.

His music is sacred ground. Bluegrass is an inheritance from Grandpa Walter and the Harlan County visits, not a costume or a quaint Appalachian footnote. He has played fiddle since age seven and picked up mandolin at twelve. Family is the bedrock; faith is real rather than performative; the 1969 Camaro Pinterest board is a secret he half-jokes about. He has not yet been on an airplane. The Camry trips to Kentucky are his favorite version of being alive.
