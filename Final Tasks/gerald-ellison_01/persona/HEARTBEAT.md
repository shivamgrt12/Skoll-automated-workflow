# Heartbeat: Gerald Ellison

## Recurring Events

### Daily
- 6:30 AM wakeup on school days, 11 PM lights out on school nights with the window stretching to 12:30 AM during finals and competition weeks.
- Evening fiddle or mandolin practice in his bedroom for at least thirty minutes after homework, longer when an audition or championship date is on the calendar.

### Weekly
- Monday: 6:30 AM morning fiddle practice, out the door 7 AM, school 7:45 AM to 3:15 PM, Ramblers rehearsal 3:30 PM to 5:30 PM in the band room with Mr. Crawford.
- Tuesday: school 7:45 AM to 3:15 PM, leave by 3:30 PM to pick up Emma from daycare by 4 PM while Karen closes Mitchell's Bakehouse, AP English Lit reading block in the evening.
- Wednesday: 6:30 AM morning fiddle practice, out the door 7 AM, school 7:45 AM to 3:15 PM, Ramblers rehearsal 3:30 PM to 5:30 PM, AP Calc AB problem set after dinner.
- Thursday: school 7:45 AM to 3:15 PM, Emma pickup again by 4 PM, evening reserved for college and scholarship work after homework.
- Friday: 6:30 AM morning fiddle practice, out the door 7 AM, school 7:45 AM to 3:15 PM, Ramblers rehearsal 3:30 PM to 5:30 PM, 8 PM weekly review covering the next seven days across school, band, family, and Lakemont.
- Saturday: cash shift help at Mitchell's Bakehouse with Karen for $50 to $80, plus 8 AM press-the-shirt and tune routine into 9 AM to 12 PM ensemble rehearsal in the band room during competition season.
- Sunday: 10 AM First Methodist Columbus service with the family, 11:30 AM biscuits and gravy at Rusty's Diner, 1 PM ET FaceTime with Grandma Dorothy and Grandpa Walter in Harlan County, evening homework reset and lunch prep for the week.

### Monthly
- First of the month: scholarship portal sweep across Heritage Foundation, Westerville Community Fund, and Ridgecrest Group STEM, plus the bluegrass competition calendar review with Mr. Crawford.
- Mid-month iMessage check-in with Grandma Dorothy when no Sunday FaceTime lands.

### Seasonal / Variable
- Competition season Saturday morning rehearsals at the band room from late winter through spring, paired with extra evening practice on the lead piece for the next festival.
- Holiday cooking blocks: brisket and dry rub for Thanksgiving, cornbread and ham for Christmas, and the Father's Day cookout in June.
