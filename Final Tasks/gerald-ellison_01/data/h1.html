Who I Am

Fiddle, mandolin, and a notes app full of unfinished poems.

I'm a senior at Ridgewood High School on Columbus's west side - first fiddle and student director of The Ridgewood Ramblers, oldest of four, and the guy who will happily talk your ear off about Alison Krauss or Cormac McCarthy depending on the hour.

Bluegrass is not a hobby for me. It's an inheritance - handed down from Grandpa Walter in Harlan County, Kentucky, through a hundred Camry trips south on I-75 and a thousand hours of practice in a bunk-bed bedroom. The music comes first.

I also write. Short poems in the iPhone notes app, music essays, half-finished songs. This site is where some of that finds daylight.

The Ridgewood Ramblers
Student Director · First Fiddle

Fourteen-member bluegrass ensemble at Ridgewood High School, Columbus. Three fiddles, two banjos, mandolin, upright bass, two guitars, five vocalists. We rehearse Monday, Wednesday, and Friday - and on Saturday mornings when the competition calendar says so.

Finished second at regionals last year. Coming for first this spring.

Original Compositions
Fiddle, Mandolin, Voice

Original pieces written for the fiddle, a few for mandolin, and a handful with lyrics I keep mostly to myself. Influences run from Alison Krauss's bow work to Wendell Berry's sense of place to the way Grandpa Walter played a G chord when he thought no one was listening.

Select pieces are part of my Lakemont College audition portfolio.

Harlan County Roots
The Inheritance

I've been playing fiddle since I was seven. My grandfather, Walter Ellison, handed me a bow on a Kentucky back porch and said the only rule was not to stop. I haven't. The Camry trips south on I-75 are my favorite version of being alive.

This music is not a costume. It is where I come from.
