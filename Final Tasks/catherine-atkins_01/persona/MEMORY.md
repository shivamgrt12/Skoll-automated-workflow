# Memory: Catherine Atkins

## Personal Profile
Catherine Atkins is the owner of Atkins and Associates Fine Wines, a Napa Valley importer and distributor she built from the ground up. She holds an MBA from Pacific Coast Business School (2007) and an Advanced Sommelier certification from the International Sommelier Society (earned 2005, cert #ISS-2005-0473), and she apprenticed from 2002 to 2004 under retired sommelier William Prescott in Sonoma County. She is sophisticated, business-astute, and sensory-driven, the kind of person who remembers a wine by its taste, its maker, and the story behind the bottle. She is at home in any fine dining room. Beneath the polish she is a devoted family woman who assists with coaching Jack's soccer and drives Emily to horse shows.

## Work & Projects
Catherine founded Atkins and Associates Fine Wines in 2010 and has built it over fifteen years from a single-region importer into the current 14-winery portfolio. The business operates from a converted Victorian house in downtown Napa with an attached temperature-controlled warehouse, open 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM weekdays with evening and weekend events. Staff includes Brian (sales director), three sales reps, Eric (warehouse manager), and Susan (admin and bookkeeper). The current portfolio is 14 California and Oregon wineries: Napa Valley (5), Sonoma (4), Willamette Valley (3), Paso Robles (2). Annual revenue is 3.8 million dollars, growing 12 percent year over year, with roughly 420,000 dollars of inventory in the warehouse at any time on a 60-day turnover.

The Italian expansion project is the major current initiative: scouting Piedmont and Tuscan producers with a target of four to five (Barolo, Barbaresco, Brunello, Chianti Classico). Sofia Marchetti at Tenuta Marchetti is the lead contact. Distribution agreements need legal review by attorney Richard Stein before signing. The agent has already compiled profiles of six top Barolo estates, drafted a client proposal for Solo Filo's fall wine program, and compared temperature-controlled shipping options for Italian imports.

Key accounts: Le Jardin Doré, Valleyvine, Bottega, Vine and Table, Maison Terre, Solo Filo. Shipping runs temperature-controlled through Domaine Transport under an exclusive agreement. Licensing is current on both the TTB federal importer license (Basic Permit #CA-I-21055) and the California ABC license (Type 17/20, #555-0412). Insurance is a Hartford business policy. The business was profiled in the March issue of Vine and Cellar Magazine. An open concern is tariff uncertainty on European wines, which she monitors closely.
