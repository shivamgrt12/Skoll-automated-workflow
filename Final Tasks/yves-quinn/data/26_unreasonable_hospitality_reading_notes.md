# Unreasonable Hospitality -- Will Guidara

Reading notes, kept while going through in October 2026. Not a review, more of a working document for the catering business.

## Chapter 1 -- Welcome
- The premise: hospitality is not the food, it is the feeling.
- Guidara at 11 Madison Park -- the guest who wanted a hot dog. The story is the point.
- For Cuisine du Nord: what is our "hot dog moment" at a corporate lunch? Something we can do for one person in the room that they will still be talking about a year later.

## Chapter 3 -- Being present
- The idea of the reservation as a story that starts before the guest arrives.
- Notes-forward hospitality. The concierge muscle at Hawthorne Grand already trains this in me.
- Apply to corporate clients: what do we know about the room's decision-maker that we can honor without being creepy?

## Chapter 5 -- Feedback culture
- After-service reviews without blame.
- I need to introduce a five minute post-event debrief for Emile, Sienna, and Luc. Not a critique, a check.

## Chapter 7 -- The one percent
- Constant search for the small addition that transforms.
- For farmer's market days: what is the one percent thing? Warm the cretons crackers before service? Bring a proper flower for the table?

## Chapter 8 -- Legendary service
- The line: unreasonable in the pursuit of the guest's happiness.
- Applied to Cuisine du Nord: the aesthetic is warmth and abundance, not polish. Unreasonable in our idiom is another course of tourtiere sent to the kitchen crew at the end of the wedding, not a truffle shaving.
