# Memory: Darren Cash

## Personal Profile

Darren grew up in Wilmington with salt water in his veins and the steady pull of two cultures on his shoulders. His mother's side, the Mitchells, are English-American with deep roots on the North Carolina coast going back generations. His father's side, the Cashes, are Scots-Irish American descended from Appalachian settlers who migrated to the coast. He speaks English with a soft Carolina drawl, peppered with local fishing and boating vernacular he picked up from the Mitchell side.

He has the calm steadiness of someone who has spent thousands of hours underwater, where panic kills and patience saves. That translates to surface life, which makes him the person his crew and his family turn to in a crisis, a gift and a trap. He processes emotion through physical movement first. If he is upset, he surfs. If he is anxious, he swims. If he is grieving, he sits on the rocks at Carolina Beach.

He carries a quiet environmental grief he cannot fully articulate. He has watched coral bleaching accelerate in real time during his dives, and the gap between what scientists know and what is being done makes him angry in a way that has nowhere productive to go yet. With close friends he is surprisingly goofy and playful. The serious marine bio student is real, and so is the guy doing terrible impressions of his professors.

He graduated New Hanover High School in 2024, spent a year at Northbridge College in Raleigh (2024 to 2025), then transferred to Oakridge University's Wilmington campus in Fall 2025 to be closer to home and the Cape Fear coast's ecosystems. He is currently a rising junior pursuing a B.S. in Marine Biology with a 3.4 GPA, expected completion Spring 2028. His operating philosophy is patience and observation. He acts when conditions are right, not when impatience pushes.

## Work & Projects

- **Employer**: Atlantic Reef Dive Co., affiliated with Verdant Solutions, on the Wilmington waterfront.
- **Role**: Part-time PADI dive instructor. Leads Open Water and Advanced Open Water certification courses, guides reef dives for tourists, assists with equipment maintenance and tank fills.
- **Schedule**: Wednesday, Friday, and Saturday mornings, 6:30 AM to 12:30 PM, with occasional Sunday mornings during peak visitor windows. Adjusts to weather, ocean conditions, and academic demands.
- **Pay**: $18 per hour plus tips. Averages $950 to $1,200 per month, season dependent. Tips run better in winter visitor stretches and during the summer tourism rush, with tighter shoulder months in between.
- **Manager**: Brett Dawson. Flexible, fair, respects academic priorities, allows shift swaps for research or exams.
- **Tension**: Peak visitor windows mean more hours but more demanding clients. Off-peak months tighten finances. Some tourists treat the reef and coastal ecology like a theme park, which Darren finds exhausting to manage diplomatically.
- **Research**: Undergraduate research project under Professor Whitfield studying coral recovery rates in Wrightsville Sound post-bleaching events. Weekly dive surveys, data collection, photo documentation, and lab analysis. Could become his honors thesis.
- **Career trajectory**: Wants to work in marine conservation, ideally on the Carolina coast. Graduate school is the next step. Torn between staying local (limited programs) and going elsewhere (better programs, leaving home).
