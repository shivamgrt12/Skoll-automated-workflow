# Memory: Karen Bell

## Personal Profile
Karen Marie Bell, born and raised in Fort Lauderdale. Daughter of Rosemary Bell (living, devout Catholic, fiercely independent) and the late James Bell (passed 2014); older sister of Philip. BSN University of Florida (2000), MSN/PMHNP Barry University (2007), Post-Master's Certificate in Psychopharmacology Nova Southeastern (2012). Board-certified PMHNP-BC, prescribing PMHNP since 2011 after a 2007-to-2011 RN-to-PMHNP credentialing transition.

Culturally identifies as Southern first, Black American, Floridian, Catholic. Grew up watching her community treat mental illness with silence and prayer, and decided early to bridge clinical science and cultural understanding. Philosophy: suffering is communal before individual, language is medicine, the privatization of pain is a great quiet violence of American life. Representation is not the goal but a condition of it.

Measured before warm; once warmth arrives it stays. Stubborn about what she will not be told about herself or her people. More comfortable with others' vulnerability than her own; humor dry, sideways, lands without warning. Serious cook (kitchen as the room that fixes things), plays piano (standards, bossa nova) for herself, paints watercolor on the lanai, gardens because her brain needs hands in dirt before it can talk. Does not gossip, does not fill silences.
