## 1. Purpose and Scope

The annual staffing freeze is a Maplewood School Board governance mechanism that
pauses all new external hiring from the effective date through March 31 of the
following year. Its purpose is to stabilize the workforce complement during the
budget reconciliation window and to prevent new salary commitments from landing
against an unclosed fiscal year.

The freeze applies to:
- All new external postings (certificated and support staff)
- Any posting that has not reached a signed employment offer by the effective date
- Internal postings that would create a backfill vacancy requiring an external hire

The freeze does NOT apply to:
- Emergency supply teacher and occasional staff bookings (daily or short-term)
- Long-term occasional (LTO) assignments already contracted before the effective date
- Internal lateral transfers that do not change total headcount
- Student placements, co-op positions, and volunteer roles

## 2. Operational Procedure

### 2.1 Pre-freeze preparation (two weeks before effective date)
- The principal reviews all active postings in the hiring pipeline (Greenhouse)
  and flags any at risk of missing the freeze line.
- The vice-principal confirms candidate interview schedules against the countdown.
- HR receives a signed freeze-readiness attestation from each school.

### 2.2 Freeze activation
- On the effective date, the superintendent's office issues a board-wide notice.
- ServiceNow carries the authoritative change record with the confirmed date.
- Any posting without a signed offer is moved to "frozen" status in Greenhouse.
- The Confluence runbook page is updated (though it may lag; see note above).

### 2.3 During the freeze
- No new external postings may be created without superintendent approval.
- Existing frozen postings are reviewed monthly for continued relevance.
- Emergency staffing needs (e.g., sudden medical leave) are handled through the
  supply pool or internal redistribution, not new hires.
- The principal may request a freeze exception through a written submission to
  the superintendent, citing student-safety or legal-compliance grounds.

### 2.4 Freeze lift
- The freeze lifts automatically on April 1 unless the superintendent extends it.
- Frozen postings are reviewed and either reactivated or closed.
- The board HR office sends the lift notice through the same ServiceNow channel.

## 3. Exception Process

A freeze exception requires:
1. A written request from the principal to the superintendent.
2. Justification grounded in one of: student safety, legal compliance, or
   board-mandated program delivery.
3. Confirmation that the position cannot be covered by internal redistribution
   or the supply pool.
4. Superintendent sign-off recorded in ServiceNow.

Exceptions are rare and are not granted for convenience, workload balancing,
or program enhancement alone.
