## Equity Researcher Reading List

*Mary's rolling reading list for the equity leadership work. Sources she has*
*cited before in board and community talks. Not exhaustive; representative.*

Maintained in the reflective vault. Titles and quotes recorded here are the
ones Mary has quoted publicly in the last three years or has flagged for
possible use in the upcoming keynote. Original language preserved where the
researcher wrote in a language other than English.

---

### 1. Rosa Maria Torres (Ecuador)

- Trajectory: Ecuadorian educator, essayist, and former Minister of Education.
  Longtime contributor to the Latin American critical education tradition,
  editor of the OtrAeducacion blog, published across UNESCO, CREFAL, and IIEP.
- Areas: educational equity, popular education, right to learn framing, adult
  and lifelong education.

Quotes Mary has drawn on previously (original Spanish preserved, English
translation to be prepared before delivery):

> "La educacion es un derecho de toda persona a lo largo de toda su vida, no
> un privilegio de la infancia ni un producto que se consume en un aula."
> — Rosa Maria Torres, OtrAeducacion, 2017

> "No se trata de que las escuelas sean mas culturalmente sensibles. Se trata
> de que reconozcan al estudiante como sujeto de saber, no como recipiente."
> — Rosa Maria Torres, Conference address CREFAL, 2019

Context Mary would use this in: opening argument for treating equity as a
structural right rather than a program.

---

### 2. Sylvia Schmelkes (Mexico)

- Trajectory: Mexican sociologist and educator, former head of the Instituto
  Nacional para la Evaluacion de la Educacion, longtime leader in intercultural
  bilingual education in Mexico, currently at the Universidad Iberoamericana.
- Areas: intercultural education, indigenous language rights in schooling,
  equity through curriculum, teacher preparation.

Quotes Mary has drawn on previously:

> "La interculturalidad no se decreta desde arriba. Se construye en el
> encuentro cotidiano entre culturas que se reconocen mutuamente como
> legitimas."
> — Sylvia Schmelkes, "La interculturalidad en la educacion basica," 2013

> "Cuando la escuela solo valora un tipo de conocimiento, esta enseñando a
> todos los niños que solo hay una forma legitima de ser humano."
> — Sylvia Schmelkes, Universidad Iberoamericana lecture, 2020

Context Mary would use this in: the section on why culturally responsive
pedagogy is not an add-on but a reordering of what counts as knowledge.

---

### 3. Paulo Freire (Brazil)

- Trajectory: Brazilian educator, philosopher, and author of Pedagogy of the
  Oppressed. Foundational figure in critical pedagogy globally.
- Areas: critical consciousness, dialogic education, oppression and liberation
  in schooling.

Quotes Mary has drawn on previously (Freire wrote in Portuguese; the English
translations here are the standard published ones and are considered canonical):

> "The teacher is of course an artist, but being an artist does not mean that
> he or she can make the profile, can shape the students. What the educator
> does in teaching is to make it possible for the students to become themselves."
> — Paulo Freire, Pedagogy of Freedom, 1998

> "No pedagogy which is truly liberating can remain distant from the oppressed
> by treating them as unfortunates and by presenting for their emulation models
> from among the oppressors."
> — Paulo Freire, Pedagogy of the Oppressed, 1968

Context Mary would use this in: the closing turn about who the equity work is
actually for.

---

### 4. Bettina L. Love (United States)

- Trajectory: American education researcher, professor at Teachers College
  Columbia, author of We Want to Do More Than Survive.
- Areas: abolitionist teaching, Black educational thought, joy as pedagogy.

Quotes Mary has drawn on previously:

> "We want to do more than survive. We want to thrive with dignity, joy, and
> the freedom to be full human beings."
> — Bettina L. Love, We Want to Do More Than Survive, 2019

Context Mary would use this in: the mentorship arc section.

---

### 5. Verna Kirkness and Jo-ann Archibald (Canada)

- Trajectory: Cree educator (Kirkness) and Sto:lo educator and scholar
  (Archibald), foundational contributors to Indigenous education scholarship
  in Canada.
- Areas: Indigenous ways of knowing in the curriculum, respectful research
  methodologies, storywork.

Reading list only; Mary would not quote these researchers directly in a talk
without first inviting an Indigenous colleague to co-frame the reference, per
the protocol she agreed with the Boreal Indigenous Education Council.

---

### Notes on Use

The Spanish language quotes above are stored in the original because Mary
prefers to deliver them from the original where the sentence is short enough
that the audience can follow the English translation immediately after. For
the keynote delivery she will read the English translation only. The English
rendering must be prepared with care because the standard published English
translations of Torres and Schmelkes are uneven and Mary has been burned once
by a machine translation that turned "sujeto de saber" into "subject of
knowing" when the sense was closer to "one who bears knowledge."

Reading circle members who have signed out titles from this list are tracked
separately in the reading circle log. This list is not the reading circle
roster.
