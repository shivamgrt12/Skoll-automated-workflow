# Ridgewood Ravens - December 2026 Newsletter

*A message from Principal Vasquez to the Ridgewood community*

---

Dear Ridgewood Families,

As we approach the end of another semester, I want to take a moment to celebrate
the energy and growth I have seen across our school this fall. This newsletter is
a community communication and holds no operational figures for the board review.

## Term Highlights

### Winter Concert (December 12)
The winter concert was a full house — standing room only in the gymnasium. Thank
you to Mr. Patterson and the entire music department for the months of rehearsal
that made it possible. The jazz ensemble's arrangement of "Feliz Navidad" brought
the crowd to its feet, and the grade 9 choir showed us what the next three years
will sound like. We are proud of every student who performed.

### Equity and Inclusion
The Student Equity Council hosted a well-attended lunch discussion series this
term, with sessions on identity, belonging, and allyship. Over 120 students
participated across six sessions. Year Two of our equity plan is well underway,
and we look forward to sharing more in the new year.

### Grade 9 Transition Program
Our peer mentorship pilot wrapped its first-semester cycle with 48 mentorship
pairs meeting regularly. Grade 9 students reported feeling more connected to the
school, and our mentors — all senior students — told us the experience sharpened
their own leadership. We will expand the model in semester two.

### Staff Wellness
Our staff wellness initiative continued with flexible PD opportunities and
workload audits. I am grateful to the teachers and support staff who have engaged
with these resources and to the wellness committee for keeping the conversation
real rather than performative.

### Athletics
The Ridgewood Ravens had a strong fall season. The senior girls' volleyball team
reached the regional semifinals, the cross-country team placed third at the
board championships, and the junior boys' basketball team is undefeated heading
into the holiday break. Go Ravens!

### Arts
The visual arts department mounted a stunning student gallery in the main hallway,
and the drama program's fall production of *The Crucible* sold out three
performances. Our arts community continues to be one of Ridgewood's great
strengths.
