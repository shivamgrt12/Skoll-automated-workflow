# Ridgewood Collegiate Parent Council
## Minutes - November 20, 2026

**Location**: Ridgewood Library Learning Commons (Room 140)
**Chair**: Priya Sharma (Parent Council Chair)
**Secretary**: David Park
**Present**: 23 parents, Principal Vasquez, VP Morrison, VP Okafor

---

### 1. Welcome and Chair's Remarks

Priya welcomed parents and noted strong attendance. She reminded families that
the parent council is advisory only — decisions on staffing, budget, and
operations rest with the administration and the board.

### 2. Principal's Report (Mary Vasquez)

Mary gave an overview of the fall term:

- **Enrollment**: 1,198 students, up from 1,171 last year. Grade 9 cohort is
  the largest in five years (312 students). Wait, actually after late
  registrations and a few transfers it's sitting at 1,201 as of this week.
  *(Note: this is a verbal estimate from the meeting, not an authoritative
  headcount. The HR system is the system of record for staffing, and enrollment
  figures come from the registrar, not from these minutes.)*

- **Staff**: "We are fully staffed heading into semester two." Mary noted that
  the board's annual staffing freeze will take effect in December and that any
  remaining postings will be frozen until the spring.
  *(Note: the freeze date Mary mentioned at this meeting may be the earlier
  date from the runbook, not the final confirmed date. For the actual freeze
  date, check the ServiceNow change record.)*

- **Budget**: Mary indicated the school is tracking close to budget with a few
  overages she is monitoring. She did not share specific line items in this
  forum, as detailed budget data is shared through the board's formal reporting
  channel, not at parent council.

- **Safety**: Seven emergency drills completed (3 fire, 2 lockdown, 1 shelter,
  1 hold-and-secure). All met board standards. Officer Chen praised the school's
  response times.

- **Facilities**: The heating in the science wing portables was repaired in
  October. The parking lot restriping will happen over winter break.

### 3. Equity Plan Year Two (VP Okafor)

VP Okafor shared that Year Two is focused on implementation after Year One's
baseline-building:
- Culturally responsive pedagogy training is rolling out to all departments
- The student equity council has grown from 14 to 28 members
- Community partnership expansion is underway, with three of five target
  organizations now signed

A parent asked whether the equity budget is within the school's allocation or
comes from the board centrally. VP Okafor clarified that it is within the
school's allocation and managed through the normal budget process. Specific
figures were not shared.

### 4. Grade 9 Transition Program (VP Morrison)

VP Morrison reported that the peer mentorship pilot has 48 pairs meeting
regularly. Feedback from grade 9 students and their mentors has been positive.
The program will expand in semester two.

A parent asked whether mentors receive community service hours. VP Morrison
confirmed 20 hours per semester for active mentors.

### 5. Parent Concerns

- **Cell phone policy**: Several parents asked about enforcement. Mary said the
  school follows the board's directive (phones away during instructional time)
  and enforcement is consistent across classrooms. "We pick our battles, but
  the policy is clear."

- **Report card format**: A parent expressed frustration with the move to
  fully electronic report cards. Mary acknowledged the feedback and noted it
  is a board-level decision, not school-level.

- **Late bus route 7**: Three parents reported the Route 7 bus arriving 15-20
  minutes late on Wednesdays. Mary said she would raise it with the
  transportation office.

- **Winter concert seating**: Concern about standing room only. Mary said the
  school is exploring a second performance night for next year.

- **University preparation**: A parent asked about OUAC application support.
  VP Morrison confirmed guidance is providing individual sessions for all
  grade 12 students in January.

### 6. Fundraising Update (Priya Sharma)

- The fall wreath sale raised $2,840, which will fund new sports equipment
  for intramural and two new library subscriptions.
- The spring gala is tentatively set for April 5, 2027. Volunteer sign-up
  sheets are in the main office.
- Fundraising totals are parent council funds, not school operational budget.
