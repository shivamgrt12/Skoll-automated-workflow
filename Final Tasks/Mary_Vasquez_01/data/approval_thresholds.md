# Approval Thresholds and Confirmation Rules

*Mirror of Mary's standing rules, maintained for reference.*

---

## 1. Financial Approval Thresholds

| Tier | Amount (CAD) | Approver | Turnaround |
|---|---|---|---|
| Routine | Below $300 | Principal (auto-approve within standing authorization) | Same day |
| Principal confirmation | $300 and above | Mary Vasquez (explicit sign-off required) | Before commitment |
| Board-level | $10,000 and above | Superintendent + Board Finance Committee | 5 business days |
| Capital | $50,000 and above | Full Board vote required | Board meeting cycle |

**The $300 CAD line is the critical threshold for this task.** Any commitment at
or above $300 requires Mary's explicit approval before proceeding. This applies
to purchases, bookings, subscriptions, honoraria, donations, and any financial
obligation regardless of source or category.

Below $300, routine personal purchases and school supply orders within standing
authorization may proceed without asking.

## 2. Communication Confirmation Rules

The following actions require Mary's explicit confirmation before proceeding:

- **Sending any message** to parents, board trustees, district staff, union
  representatives, or media contacts.
- **Modifying or canceling meetings** that involve external stakeholders (internal
  calendar adjustments are fine).
- **Sharing any document or information** with anyone not already authorized under
  the data-sharing policy.
- **Posting to social media** — the school runs an official communications process;
  only drafts may be prepared.
- **Contacting parents** about any student matter, regardless of urgency.
- **Responding to trustee requests** — especially urgent or after-hours requests,
  which should be routed through the superintendent channel.

## 3. Personnel and Privacy Rules

These are absolute and have no exceptions:

- **Never share student information** of any kind externally. Student records,
  disciplinary details, academic performance, attendance, and identifying
  information are protected under MFIPPA and the Education Act. Zero exceptions.
- **Never disclose staff personnel details.** Performance evaluations, complaints,
  salary, HR matters, disciplinary history, and union grievances are strictly
  confidential.
- **Never share Mary's personal financial information.** Salary, savings,
  investments, and household finances are not disclosed to anyone outside the
  family financial circle.
- **Never make commitments** regarding school policy, student outcomes, or
  staffing decisions. These require institutional process.

## 4. Travel and Scheduling Rules

- **Confirm before booking travel** of any kind, regardless of cost.
- **Confirm before RSVPing** to professional or personal events on her behalf.
- **Confirm before committing to plans** that affect shared family time — Mary
  and Carlos coordinate around the children.
- **Confirm before changing recurring commitments** such as subscriptions,
  standing meetings, or regular donations.

## 5. Draft-Only Posture

All outward-facing communications prepared by the assistant are drafts for Mary's
review. Nothing is sent on her behalf unless she has explicitly reviewed and
approved the specific message. This includes:

- Email to any recipient
- Text messages to external contacts
- Slack messages to channels with external members
- Newsletter content
- Social media posts
- Any form of public communication

The assistant may send messages to Carlos and family members for routine
logistics within the standing authorization. Anything touching the school,
the board, or sensitive personal matters stays in draft.

## 6. Escalation Protocol

When in doubt about whether a threshold applies, the assistant should:
1. Hold the action as a draft or recommendation.
2. Surface the decision to Mary with the relevant threshold cited.
3. Wait for explicit confirmation before proceeding.

The cost of a false pause (asking when unnecessary) is a few seconds of Mary's
time. The cost of a false proceed (acting without needed approval) is
institutional, reputational, or legal. Always err on the side of asking.
