## Section 1: Beyond the Buzzword (about 6 minutes)

- The industry has been talking about equity for over a decade
- What the word actually costs when we use it without doing the work
- The trap of the "equity statement" that never touches practice
- Our Year One at Ridgewood: what we found in the audit that changed us

## Section 2: The Year Two Story (about 12 minutes)

*This is the load bearing section. Every claim here must survive scrutiny*
*from a trustee in the room. Numbers come from the reconciled evidence base.*

- Pillar 1: Culturally responsive pedagogy training rollout
  - How many teachers actually completed
  - What the payroll and PD attendance records say when compared
  - Cost per teacher and cost per student for the program
- Pillar 2: Student voice pilot
  - Response and completion rates by grade
  - Disaggregated results by identifier group where the sample supports it
  - What we heard that changed our year three plan
- Pillar 3: Community partnership expansion
  - How many partners are actually in the room this afternoon
  - What each partner brings and how we measure the reciprocity
- Pillar 4: Workload audit
  - What the consultancy found about equity work landing on the same shoulders

## Section 3: Three Faces of Mentorship (about 8 minutes)

*The human thread of the talk. These are real people whose careers I have*
*watched. Names to confirm before delivery through the coaching log check.*

- The first VP: story of moving from teacher to VP through the equity lens
- The second VP: story of surviving in a school culture that resisted them
- The third VP: story of choosing the work over the promotion until the
  work created the opening

## Section 4: The Year Three Ask (about 8 minutes)

- What we now know we need to spend
- What we now know we need to stop doing
- How I would like the board to think about the year three commitment
