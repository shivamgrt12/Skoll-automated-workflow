# Rosa's Pozole Rojo

Unrelated to work. Saved here by accident from a family chat. This is Rosa's
recipe passed down from Abuela Consuelo in Guadalajara. Mary transcribed it
from Rosa's handwritten card (faded and splattered) in November 2024.

---

## Ingredients (serves 8-10)

### For the broth
- Pork shoulder, bone-in, 2.5 lb (cut into 2-inch chunks)
- Pork neck bones, 1 lb (for richer broth)
- Hominy (posole), 2 large cans (29 oz each), drained and rinsed
- White onion, 1 whole (peeled, halved)
- Garlic, 6 cloves (whole)
- Bay leaves, 3
- Mexican oregano, 1 tbsp (dried)
- Salt, 2 tsp (adjust at the end)
- Water, enough to cover by 3 inches (about 12 cups)

### For the chile sauce
- Guajillo chiles, 8 (stems and seeds removed)
- Ancho chiles, 3 (stems and seeds removed)
- Chile de árbol, 2 (optional, for heat — Carlos likes it, kids do not)
- Garlic, 4 cloves
- White onion, half
- Cumin, 1 tsp
- Mexican oregano, 1 tsp
- Water from soaking, about 1 cup

### Toppings (serve in small bowls at the table)
- Thinly sliced radishes
- Shredded green cabbage
- Diced white onion
- Chopped cilantro
- Lime wedges (lots — Miguel uses three per bowl)
- Dried oregano
- Crushed chile de árbol flakes
- Tostadas (store-bought are fine, Rosa says homemade are better)
- Sliced avocado

---

## Method

### Day before (optional but better)
1. Toast the dried chiles in a dry comal or cast-iron skillet until fragrant
   and slightly darkened, about 30 seconds per side. Do not burn them.
2. Soak the toasted chiles in hot water for 30 minutes until soft.
3. Blend the soaked chiles with the garlic, onion, cumin, oregano, and about
   1 cup of the soaking water until very smooth.
4. Strain the sauce through a fine-mesh sieve, pressing with a spoon. Discard
   the solids. Refrigerate overnight.

### Day of
1. Place the pork shoulder and neck bones in a large stockpot (the big blue
   Le Creuset, not the small one). Add the whole onion, whole garlic cloves,
   bay leaves, oregano, and salt. Cover with water by 3 inches.
2. Bring to a boil over high heat. Skim the grey foam that rises in the first
   15 minutes — Rosa is very particular about this step.
3. Reduce to a gentle simmer. Cook uncovered for 2 to 2.5 hours, until the
   pork is fall-apart tender. Add water if the level drops below the meat.
4. Remove the pork and shred it into bite-sized pieces. Discard the neck
   bones, bay leaves, onion, and garlic. Strain the broth if needed.
5. Return the shredded pork to the pot. Add the drained hominy and the chile
   sauce. Stir well.
6. Simmer for another 30 to 45 minutes so the flavors marry. Taste and
   adjust salt.
7. Serve hot in deep bowls with all the toppings on the table.

## Notes from Rosa

- "Mija, do not rush the chile step. If you rush, the sauce is bitter and
  then you have to add sugar and that is not pozole, that is candy."
- "The pork must be shoulder, not loin. Loin has no flavor. Your father tried
  loin once in 1997 and we do not talk about it."
- "Always make double. Pozole is better the next day and Eduardo will want
  a container for the week."
- "The hominy must be rinsed until the water runs clear or the broth gets
  cloudy and Consuelo would not approve."

This recipe feeds the whole family with leftovers. Make it on Saturday, eat
it Saturday night, and bring Eduardo a container on Sunday after Mass.
