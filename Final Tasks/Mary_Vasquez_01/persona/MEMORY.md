# Memory: Mary Vasquez

## Personal Profile
Mary was born in Guadalajara, Mexico, and immigrated to Toronto with her family at age 8 in 1989, old enough to remember Guadalajara and young enough to absorb Canada down to her vowels. She is a Canadian citizen. She holds a B.Ed. from Lakeshore University of Education, Toronto (2003) and an M.Ed. in Educational Leadership from Crestmont University (2009). She is a member in good standing of the Ontario College of Teachers (OCT registration number 478312) and has completed the Principal's Qualification Program (Parts 1 and 2) through the Ontario Principals' Council, the standard certification path for an Ontario principal.

She is Catholic in a way that is private, structural, and deep. She crosses herself before difficult meetings without thinking about it, and her faith is quieter and more durable than performance. She holds Mexican cultural identity through food, family obligation, music, and the church, and she watches carefully that her children keep what she had: Spanish at home, abuela every day, Sunday meals as gravity.

She speaks English (the language of her career and the inside of her own head), Spanish (fluent: the language of her mother's kitchen, family, and prayer), and French (conversational, functional for school-board communications), thinking in all three at slightly different times of day.

Her temperament is steady, warm in a way that is real and selective, and built on diplomatic steel she came to before she came to diplomacy. She listens with her whole face and remembers what people said years ago. She believes representation matters and is more complicated than the slogans suggest, and that mentorship is a long transaction measured in a decade rather than a year. She believes every teenager deserves an adult in the building fighting for them, which is why she has stayed in public education despite better-paying offers.

## Work & Projects
Mary is principal of Ridgewood Collegiate Institute, a public high school of roughly 1,200 students (grades 9 to 12) under the Maplewood School Board, with a diverse student body, strong academic and arts programs, and some socioeconomic challenges. She was appointed in August 2022 and is in her fourth year. Before that she was vice-principal at Brookstone Academy from August 2017 to July 2022 (five years), and before that she taught secondary English and history at Lakeside Secondary School from September 2004 to June 2017 (thirteen years, the first year as an LTO and the rest on a permanent contract). The 2003 to 2004 stretch between graduating with her B.Ed. and the Lakeside appointment was a year of supply teaching across Maplewood schools while she finished her OCT registration.

Current projects:
- **Equity and Inclusion Strategic Plan**: leading the school's three-year equity plan. Year 2 focus is culturally responsive pedagogy training for all staff, a student voice advisory council, and community partnership expansion. Year 3 strategy is under development for fall 2027.
- **Grade 9 Transition Program Redesign**: overhauling orientation and support for incoming grade 9 students, piloting a peer mentorship model in October 2026.
- **Staff Wellness Initiative**: responding to post-pandemic teacher burnout with flexible PD days, mental health resources, and workload audits.

She is at school Monday to Friday, roughly 7:30 AM to 5:00 PM and often later, with some evenings for parent council, board meetings, and school events. She will present "Leading for Equity: Beyond the Buzzword" at the Maplewood Educational Leadership Conference on October 22, 2026, and is considering an Ed.D. starting 2028, comparing part-time executive programs at Crestmont University and Baywood College.
