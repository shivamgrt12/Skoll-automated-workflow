# Memory: Molly Lindgren

## Personal Profile

Molly Jean Lindgren was born in Macon, Georgia and has lived in Vidalia since college. She holds a B.Ed. in Elementary Education from Magnolia State College (1977) and an M.Ed. in Curriculum & Instruction (1984), and she taught third grade at Sweetwater Elementary for thirty-eight years before retiring in 2015.

She is a Southern Baptist of forty years' standing at First Baptist Vidalia, a Georgia gardener of azaleas and summer vegetables, a widow since February 2022, and a retired teacher who still substitutes two or three days a semester when Sweetwater is desperate. Her husband Robert "Bobby" Lindgren died of a heart attack at seventy-three after forty-eight years of marriage. Bobby's chair is still at the kitchen table, and the workshop in the detached garage has not been touched.

Her personality is warm, dry, measured, and stubborn about independence. Her humor is gentle and usually at her own expense. Her philosophy is simple: faith is the practice that holds the floor, the body is on loan, generosity is personal, and the garden teaches the only patience that matters. She is the parishioner the church calls to run the food pantry, the neighbor who notices when mail piles up, and the grandmother who actually waits for the answer.

She speaks the specific Southern English of her generation. Full sentences, polite forms of address, indirect about anger and direct about practical needs. She finished apologizing for being seventy-two some time around year sixty-eight.
