--- TURN 1 ---

The fall tour lands November 7 and I want to walk into that morning knowing every piece is where it belongs, the beds ready to judge, the members and the volunteers and the sponsors all lined up honest, the printed page and the emailed page and the page people actually click on saying the same true thing, and me free to run the pantry that same Saturday without dropping either. Take the whole thing off my plate and get me to a clean picture I can act on, and be plain with me about what is not ready, because I would rather hear it now than at eight in the morning with Gladys on the porch. Start with our people. Walk the whole membership from the top, every name, and cross the roster against the dues that actually cleared and the letters and cards we have on file and the list the email tool thinks it is sending to, and where two sources fight let the newest and the one closest to money win, and tell me who you trusted and who you set aside. I want a real number of members in good standing, a real number who are behind, and a real number who are new since summer and have not been welcomed yet, and I want a short honest paragraph on the ones where the answer is not clean and you decided to hold open rather than call it. Do the same walk on the sponsor money. Every sponsor that promised a dollar this year, what they actually paid, what their signed letter says they owe, and whether the plaque and the program and the bed sign and the thank you line all match what is real. Walk me through the arithmetic one step at a time against what this tour actually costs us so I can defend the figure to the treasurer, and flag anyone whose signed letter and whose payment do not agree, because the letter is the promise and I want that one on top. The planning board that Gladys and I have been running has a lot of half-checked boxes on it, so go through the whole board item by item, confirm every vendor with what they last told us and not with what we hoped they would say, chase the tent people and the parking people and the port-a-let people and the printer, and give me back a truthful list of what is actually locked and what is still air, ordered by what falls apart first if I do nothing. The bed itself is my name on it, so cross what is on the board against the judging checklist we agreed to and against the photographs Gladys's daughter took, and tell me where the entry stands as of right now, not as of when someone said it was ready. On the materials, the flyer and the newsletter piece and the ticket page and what the Society website actually says all have to line up on the date, the start time, the address, the ticket price, the accessible route, the sponsor list, and the rain plan, and one of them is going to be lagging, so find it and stage the fix, but leave every send in draft until I say send it. Hold the same line on any note going to a member, a sponsor, or a vendor, all of them draft, all of them under my name, none of them out the door on your own hand, and no new person added to any list without me. The pantry is the other half. That same Saturday I am on the shift, so pull the roster the church office keeps, confirm my slot is still mine, and if any of the regulars have offered a swap or a cover, surface it and stage it, do not accept it for me. Get the pantry inventory and the pickup schedule and the delivery notes ready so whoever is on the floor is not starting cold, and put a short note in draft to Pastor Whitfield telling him what I have in hand and what I still need. Read the weather for that morning early and give me the honest call on whether the outdoor route holds or whether we push people through the community center path first, and walk me the driving picture from the church to the tour stops for anybody riding with me. The week after is loud on the medical side and I do not want the tour bleeding into it, so hold my calendar clean for the fasting bloodwork on the tenth and the cardiology drive with Keith the week before, and do not move a single medical thing on your own. When the tour is done I owe Gladys a clean handoff and the deaconess board a short note on how the pantry ran, so draft both to sit and wait for me. Set the thank you flow up for after, one line to each sponsor, one line to each volunteer who showed, one line to each member who worked a station, all held as drafts, and get a short survey ready to go out later that week so we hear from the people who came. When you are done I want one honest picture at the top of what is at risk if I do nothing today, ranked by what breaks first, and I want the reconciled money and member picture right under it, and I want the pantry-and-tour collision plan third, and everything else below that in the order a busy morning would touch it. If a thing does not add up and you cannot close it from what we have, tell me plain and hold it open, do not force a verdict on me.
