Barbara's recipe

Macaroni and Cheese

Serves 8, or 4 with leftovers fought over on Monday

You will need

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni
  • 4 tablespoons butter and 4 tablespoons flour
  • 3 cups warm whole milk
  • 1 pound sharp cheddar plus a handful of grated gruyere
  • 1 teaspoon dry mustard, salt, a grate of nutmeg
  • Buttered breadcrumbs for the top

Method

Cook the macaroni two minutes short of the box. Melt the butter, whisk in the flour and cook one minute, then pour in the warm milk slowly. Off the heat, add the cheddar and gruyere, the dry mustard, salt, and nutmeg. Fold in the pasta, top with buttered breadcrumbs, and bake at 375 for thirty minutes until the edges blister.

Sunday main

Sunday Pot Roast

Three pounds of chuck, salted the night before

You will need

  • 3 pound chuck roast, salted overnight
  • Onions, carrots, celery, garlic
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cups beef stock and a splash of red wine

Method

Sear the roast hard in a Dutch oven, then build the braise on the onions, carrots, celery, garlic, bay leaf, stock, and wine. Lid on, 300 degrees, three hours, until a fork twists out clean. Rest before slicing against the grain.

Weeknight main

Meatloaf, the honest version

Do not overwork the meat or it turns to rubber

You will need

  • 2 pounds ground chuck
  • 1 egg and three quarters cup breadcrumbs soaked in milk
  • Grated onion, Worcestershire, salt
  • Glaze of ketchup, brown sugar, and a little cider vinegar

Method

Mix gently, shape, and glaze. Bake at 350 for one hour. The glaze should set to a lacquer and the loaf should slice clean without falling apart.

Saturday morning

Buttermilk Pancakes

Tyler gets chocolate chips, Emily insists on none touching

You will need

  • 2 cups flour, 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder, half teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups buttermilk, 2 eggs
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter

Method

Whisk the dry, then the wet, and bring them together with a light hand. Rest the batter ten minutes. Ladle onto a medium griddle and flip when the bubbles hold.
