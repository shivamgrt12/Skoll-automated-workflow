The studio

Standards that are not negotiable

Trained at the Arden Conservatory of the Arts, I teach about sixty dancers across six levels and stage the winter and spring showcases. The work is the same every day: slow everything down, build the line, and trust that the music will arrive.

Method

Breakthroughs are earned in the boring repetitions. I push hard and I celebrate every real one, in a personal note after every performance. Charm over cruelty, but never a sugarcoat.

Featured production

Appalachian Spring

A company of thirty-two dancers, ages twelve to eighteen, spent eleven weeks learning that the hardest thing a young dancer can do is wait. Martha Graham first danced this on a bare stage in 1944. We kept that spareness on purpose. No scrim, no projection, one bench.

  • I. Variations on a Shaker ThemeLevels 5 and 6
  • II. The Bride and Her Intendedpas de deux
  • III. The Revivalist and His Flockfull company
  • IV. Day's Endadagio, full company
