# Memory: Jason Cooper

## Personal Profile

Jason is a ballet instructor and choreographer at Parkside Dance Academy in Philadelphia, with a BFA from the Arden Conservatory of the Arts. He grew up the son of a Juilliard-trained ballet corps member, and his identity carries that lineage forward: disciplined, perfectionist, reserved socially, and fiercely loyal to the people who let him do serious work. He lives in an 1890s Fairmount rowhouse with his wife Sarah and their two kids, where the finished basement has been converted to a private practice space with a sprung floor, a barre, and mirrors. He reads Fitzgerald the way other people read scripture, attends Liberty City Ballet and the Arch Street Playhouse as a season subscriber, and cooks Sunday comfort food classics from his mother's recipes. He is moved by beauty in music, architecture, and the small everyday gestures of his students. He dislikes small talk, runs a tight schedule, and treats the studio as a place where standards are non-negotiable and praise is earned.

## Work & Projects

Parkside Dance Academy runs classes 9:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily. Jason teaches 10:00 AM to 1:00 PM and 4:00 PM to 6:00 PM most days, with Wednesday afternoons reserved for choreography studio time. PDA has 180 students enrolled, and Jason teaches about 60 directly across six levels. The faculty meeting is the first Tuesday of every month at 9:00 AM.

Current production cycle: the spring showcase "Appalachian Spring" wrapped May 23 to 25 at the Academy Theater, with a cast of 32 dancers ages 12 to 18. Costume designer Lisa Thompson worked to a $4,800 budget. Music was a licensed Copland arrangement at a $1,200 fee, paid. Lighting designer Dave Marshall handled tech. Winter showcase repertoire proposals are due to Patricia by October 1, and performances are set for mid-December at the Academy Theater.

PDA also runs a small online store for branded gear and dancewear, plus a website and ticketing surface maintained by a volunteer IT contractor; Jason keeps an eye on both during showcase ticket launches. Other professional commitments include the American Youth Ballet Competition regional support for PDA students, NCB guest masterclasses with artists like Amanda Brooks when scheduling permits, and the Fall Dance Educators Symposium in New York October 17 to 18.
