Commissioned Work

Commission a Painting

Every commission begins with a conversation.

I work from observation, memory, and reference images you share with me. Before I begin, I send a sketch and a color study for your approval so there are no surprises when the final piece arrives.

Subjects I work well with: storefronts and buildings with character, interiors in natural light, garden and floral arrangements, landscape views, and domestic scenes. I do not paint portraits with faces.

Commission slots open each season. Delivery is typically six to ten weeks from deposit, depending on complexity.

Instruction

Watercolor Classes

Classes focus on building honest technique from direct observation. Students learn to see light before they learn to paint it. All levels welcome; advanced students work on florals and portraiture.

Saturday Group Class

Open to all skill levels. Structured sessions covering technique, composition, and color theory. Wet-on-wet, glazing, lifting, dry brush. Critique and individual feedback each session.

When: Saturdays, 2:00 to 4:00 PM Where: Dearborn Community Arts Center, Studio B Tuition: $40 per month Enrollment: Open - limited spots

Tuesday Private Class

Small group meeting in the home studio on Pelham Road. Individualized pace and subject matter. Students work in series across a semester; advanced work in portraits and complex landscapes.

When: Tuesdays, 7:00 to 8:30 PM Where: Home studio, Dearborn Tuition: Donated - no fee Enrollment: Waitlist - inquire below

About

Maria Sandoval

I learned to paint from my grandmother, who worked in oils and whose eyes measured color in a way I am still trying to understand. I came back to painting seriously in my early thirties, after dental school and residency, and found that it did something nothing else did: it made me pay attention to exactly one thing.

Watercolor is unforgiving about this. You cannot overwork it and expect it to improve. The best paintings I have made are the ones where I stopped before I thought I was finished. That is a lesson I keep relearning.

"Overworked paintings are always worse than ones left alone too soon."

I teach because explaining something forces you to understand it better. My students range from 16 to 65 and arrive from every direction; the ones who progress fastest are the ones who learn to slow down and really look before the brush touches paper.
