# Memory: Maria Sandoval

## Personal Profile

Maria Sandoval is a Mexican-American pediatric dentist, small business owner, and watercolor painting teacher rooted in Dearborn, Michigan. Her Mexican-American identity is lived in her kitchen, her calendar, and the particular way her family argues: loudly, with food involved, and with the expectation that showing up is not optional. She does not reach for it as a professional label and does not minimize it either. Her accent surfaces when talking to her mother and disappears when talking to an insurance company.

Her relationship with faith is evolving. She grew up in a tradition that offered certainty and has arrived at a version of belief that is less certain but more honestly hers. She sits on the church board because she believes in the community it sustains, not because every doctrinal position aligns with where she has landed.

She is disciplined in the way of someone who built a practice from the ground up: early mornings feel natural, procrastination feels physically uncomfortable, and lists are kept not because she forgets but because crossing items off delivers a satisfaction she cannot replicate. Her discipline has a hard limit; when she hits it, she stops completely. A fully unproductive weekend is a circuit breaker, not laziness. She pushes herself hard and extends grace to everyone else, a double standard she recognizes and has not dismantled. She holds a private, deadpan humor that people earn access to slowly, and uses it as a bridge and as a test.

### Watercolor Painting Teaching

- Weekly group class at the Dearborn Community Arts Center (Saturday 2:00 to 4:00 PM).
- Private beginner class at her home (Tuesday 7:00 to 8:30 PM).
- Approximately 15 regular students across both classes, ages 16 to 65.
- Focus: landscapes for beginners, florals and portraits for advanced students; includes art history, composition, and color theory.
- Income: $40 per student per month for group class; $200 per month stipend from the Arts Center; private class time is donated.
- Commissions: $200 to $800 per piece depending on complexity. Commission proposals include pricing, timeline, material options, and design sketches; Maria reviews before sending.
