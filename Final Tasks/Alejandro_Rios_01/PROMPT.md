--- TURN 1 ---
I want a financial picture I can actually trust going into the coming year, and I want it built from what is real rather than from the tidy numbers I carry in my head. The honest trouble is that my spending has scattered itself across the inbox and every receipt rail a vendor has ever used to bill me, so there is no one place I can open and believe, and the household total I keep quoting myself is a guess wearing a suit. Start by pulling the whole trailing year of charges into a single clean census, every recurring line and every vendor that bills me on a cycle, and go wide across the mail, the payment processor receipts, and the shipment confirmations rather than leaning on any one of them, because the same charge often lands in two places and I would rather you catch the duplicate than let it quietly inflate the total. I already suspect a few of these have crept. A price I agreed to a year back is not the price I am paying on at least one of them, and where the receipts disagree I want you to say plainly which figure you trusted and which you set aside rather than folding it in without a word. Do the same for the pet supply autoship and the odd gear order that come through the carriers, since the autoship runs on its own cadence and the amounts never hold still from one shipment to the next. What I am genuinely clearing each month is the figure I actually need, because if the real number sits below what I think I am setting aside then the whole plan bends and I would rather learn that from the receipts than from an empty account in the spring. From there I want a month by month cash flow across the whole year ahead that folds in the obligations already on the horizon, the spring dental cleaning, the quarterly skin visits, the yearly workup for Maple, the June days back in Kalamazoo with my parents, the insurance renewals, and the ordinary weight of rent and loans and the phone bill underneath all of it, and I want it built so the months where two of those costs stack do not quietly put me underwater. The two things I am steering toward are the emergency fund and the student loans. The fund is short of where I need it to sit before I will feel steady, and I want a defensible path to that mark that does not starve a month a real expense lands in, and I want the same plain honesty on the loan balance, whether it is worth pushing past the income based minimum with whatever surplus the reconciliation actually frees, or whether the fund has to come first and the loan waits. Walk me through the arithmetic rather than handing me a verdict, show me the glide path and the month the fund reaches target under a couple of reasonable paces, run the loan payoff the same way, and if the surplus turns out too thin to call cleanly then hold that question open instead of forcing it, because I would rather sit with a real tradeoff than act on a comfortable one. The lease is the last piece and it has a clock on it. The renewal packet is in front of me and the rent printed on it is not the rent I have been quoting myself, so work the plan off what is real, and lay the renew or move decision out clean, because that call is mine to make once the numbers say what they say. Draft me the cancellation notes for whatever subscriptions read as dead weight, ready for me to look over and send once I am satisfied. My sister will likely ask how I am set up when we next talk, and this is mine to keep close. What I want to be holding at the end is two things I can lean on. One is that recurring spend census, a single honest list of every cycle charge with the duplicates merged, the crept prices corrected, the dead weight flagged, and a short line on each place you trusted one record over another. The other is the cash flow itself, the month by month picture with the fund path and the loan tradeoff laid side by side, built to survive the actual year rather than to look neat in a screenshot. Have both ready before the first of January, when I sit down for the usual budget pass, since that is the moment I want to walk into already knowing the shape of the year instead of rebuilding it from a drawer of receipts at the kitchen table. Go slow enough to be right. If a charge is ambiguous, if a receipt could be a renewal or a one off, if a source is too thin to trust, say so and hold it rather than shoving it into a bucket to make the total tidy. I would rather the picture come back honest and a little open in two places than clean and quietly wrong.
