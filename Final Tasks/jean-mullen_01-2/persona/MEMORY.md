# Memory: Jean Mullen

## Personal Profile

- **Origin**: Born in Cork, Ireland. Irish citizen, long-term visa holder for India. Moved to Chennai in 2005; over twenty years resident.
- **Education**: MA Performing Arts, UCC (2004). Munster-tradition training under Margaret O'Brien from age 6. IDTA certification (2003). Kalakshetra Foundation short course (2007).
- **Languages**: English (native), Irish (conversational), Tamil (intermediate), Hindi (basic).
- **Temperament**: Calm, exacting, patient with honest effort. Forgives nerves; does not forgive carelessness with tradition. Trust earned slowly, held durably.
- **Cultural identity**: Irish sharpened by distance. Cork in her speech when tired, cooking when homesick. Twenty Chennai years: Tamil learned earnestly, vegetarianism settled, local and Irish calendars held equally.
- **Philosophy**: Art is not decoration. Tradition is living, not preserved. Community is currency. Family is the quiet axis.
- **Social style**: Selective, not shy. Commands a hall, goes home to people who require no performance. Circle runs deep, not wide.

## Work & Projects

- **Academy**: Céilí Mór Academy of Dance, founded 2012. Rented first-floor studio, Mylapore. 35 students, ages 6 to adult. Three levels. Annual Day performance at Kalaivanar Auditorium each October. Founder, artistic director, primary advanced instructor.
- **Career**: 2004 MA at UCC. 2005 moved to Chennai. 2005 to 2012 independent practice, workshops, guest collaborations. 2012 to present academy founder and artistic director, fourteen continuous years.
- **Five Elements**: Full-length contemporary Irish dance work, five aspects of nature. Chennai Arts Festival premiere, winter season opening slot. Most ambitious work to date.
- **Emma Walsh Solo Debut**: Senior student (17), Kalaivanar Auditorium. Reflects on Jean as teacher. Intensive private coaching ongoing.
- **Metro Arts Commission**: Dance-inspired installation for Mylapore Metro station inauguration. Blends traditional movement with digital projection.
- **Riverside solo recital**: Off-season series slot at Riverside Arts Hall, full programme. Extends the six-year performance relationship with Dr. Richardson beyond the December season.
- **Professional development**: Visiting artist, Academy of Performing Arts (annual). Lecture-demonstration on storytelling in choreography, Chennai Arts Festival conference.
- **Operating posture**: Performance season peaks November through January.

## Interests & Hobbies

- **Balcony garden**: Herbs, jasmine, rosemary. Pinching rosemary at dawn resets a mind wound tight by rehearsal.
- **Journaling**: Early dark, before the day makes demands. Reflection, planning, or simply holding the page while thoughts settle.
- **Archival project**: Digitizing old VHS recordings of Margaret O'Brien's performances, coordinated remotely with Margaret's family in Cork. Closer to a devotional act than logistics.
- **Reading**: Scholarly dance treatises (slowly, often more than once), Irish literature (Colm Tóibín, Sebastian Barry), spiritual texts (Celtic prayers, Yeats), mythology retellings (Juliet Marillier).
- **Listening**: Traditional Irish and Celtic (The Chieftains, Planxty, Martin Hayes for contemporary voice). Van Morrison. Distinguishes between music she studies and music she simply needs.
- **Cooking as decompression**: Colcannon, soda bread, vegetable stew, potato soup. Irish comfort food translated through local ingredients.

## Preferences

- **News**: *The Irish Times* every morning, arts and culture coverage closely. *Irish Dancing Magazine* cover to cover.
- **Podcasts**: *Dublin Stories* and *Celtic Arts Conversations*, played on car rides.
- **Gifts**: Meaningful over expensive. Handwoven scarves, books on art or spirituality, Celtic jewelry, concert tickets, shared exhibitions. Prefers giving experiences over objects.
- **Aesthetic in dress**: Comfortable, made for movement; stage and occasion dressing is deliberate. Prefers one well-made scarf to several disposable ones.
- **Likes**: The hour before the world wakes. Strong filter coffee at dawn. South Indian filter coffee, embraced late and defended with the conviction of a convert. Jasmine on the evening balcony. Celtic knotwork. The look on a student's face when a step they have fought for clicks. Mythology and old stories.
- **Dislikes**: Carelessness dressed up as spontaneity. Empty promises. Being rushed through a creative decision. Being managed. Condescension toward the audience. Noise without purpose. Interruption during composition.
- **Sensory world**: Lives in her body. Loves jasmine in evening air, rosemary on her hands, feet on a wooden floor. Sensitive to harsh light, clutter, and overheated rooms. Childhood foods pull her to Cork; idli and dosa now belong too. The studio early, floor hers alone, is the version of her art closest to why she began.
- **Travel posture**: Two kinds. Going home to Cork is a re-rooting, where comfort and family matter more than novelty. Everywhere else is approached with a working artist's practicality. Given a free afternoon she seeks a performance, a quiet church, a market, or a long walk.
- **Comfort rituals**: Kitchen (colcannon, vegetable stew). Balcony garden. Traditional and Celtic recordings. Morning meditation, coffee, journaling. Long call with Fiona or Margaret when something has rattled her. Chamomile at night.
