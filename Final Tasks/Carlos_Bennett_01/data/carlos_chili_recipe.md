# Carlos Chili - slow-cooked, never a shortcut

Four hours minimum, better the second day. Feeds a dock crowd.

## Ingredients
| Item | Amount | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Chuck roast | 3 lb | hand-cut cubes, not ground |
| Miller's Smokehouse sausage | 1 lb | sliced on the bias |
| Yellow onion | 2 large | diced |
| Garlic | 6 cloves | minced |
| Fire-roasted tomatoes | 28 oz | with juice |
| Kidney + pinto beans | 15 oz each | rinsed |
| Ancho + guajillo | 2 each | soaked, blended |
| Cumin, smoked paprika | to taste | toast the cumin |
| Black coffee | 1/2 cup | the secret |
| Yuengling | 1 bottle | in the pot, one for the cook |

## Method
1. Brown the chuck in the cast iron in batches, do not crowd.
2. Render the sausage, pull it, soften the onion and garlic in the fat.
3. Deglaze with the beer and coffee, scrape the bottom.
4. Back in the meat, the chile paste, tomatoes, spices. Low and slow four hours.
5. Beans in the last hour so they hold shape.

## Serve
Cornbread on the side, no exceptions. Sharp cheddar, raw onion, a little hot sauce for the ones who ask.
