# Chloe - Activities

Chloe is nine, fourth grade at Holy Name. Two standing things outside school - art and Girl Scouts. She is the easy one on paper, but the routines matter to her more than she lets on, so I keep them off the negotiating table.

## Art
- Wednesday afternoons at half past three
- Runs at the Roslindale Community Center, walkable-ish but usually a drive
- About eighty a month
- Instructor is Ms. Delgado, she runs the elementary studio block, kind and very patient with the shy ones
- The mediums rotate by unit - fall was watercolor and collage, winter moves into clay and printmaking, so the smock comes home messier after the holidays
- Chloe keeps a folder of her pieces in her room, do not recycle the loose paper on her desk, that pile is the portfolio
- Her big art show was the end of October in the school gym, that one already happened
- The winter studio show is at the Roslindale Community Center in December, date still to confirm with Ms. Delgado, it goes on the board once I have it

## Girl Scouts
- Alternating Saturday mornings at half past eight
- Troop 60412, meets in the Holy Name parish hall basement, sometimes a field trip instead
- Troop dues about twenty five, I need the receipt saved for the flex spending paperwork
- She is working a badge and needs her sash, the new badges have to get sewn on, that is a weekend project I keep pushing
- Cookie season paperwork starts creeping in after the new year, the troop leader is Mrs. Okafor and she emails the parent list, watch for it
- Snack duty rotates, when it is our Saturday I send nut-free only because of the boys' table habits, easiest to just keep the whole troop snack nut-free
- Fall calendar had the bridging ceremony and a nature walk at the Arboretum, both done

## Coverage notes
- On my Wednesday shift weeks she is coming off a school day so the sitter or Colleen handles the art drop
- Saturday scouts only collide with hockey on game weekends, watch those, the 0830 scouts start and a late-morning Liam game can be run back to back if the driver is one person, but it is tight
- No phone for her yet, everything routes through me or whoever has her
- She gets carsick on the highway if she reads in the car, so no screens for the Quincy drive, snacks up front
- Bedtime is a soft eight thirty on school nights, she will read past it and that is fine

She is easy and independent for nine, but the Wednesday art slot is sacred to her, so it goes on the coverage grid every week. If something has to give in a crunch week, it is not the art.
