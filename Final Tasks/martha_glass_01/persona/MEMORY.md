# Memory: Martha Glass

## Personal Profile

- Born in Akureyri, northern Iceland; Icelandic citizen. Grew up watching Krafla's post-eruption steam vents from the kitchen window, so volcanology never felt like a choice.
- Education: B.Sc. in Geophysics (2003) and M.Sc. in Volcanology (2005), both at Reykjavik University of Sciences; Ph.D. in Earth Sciences, Ashworth University, UK (2009), thesis on degassing dynamics and eruption precursors at basaltic systems. Post-doc year in Paris.
- Languages: English (native), Icelandic (fluent, exclusive at home), Danish and French (conversational), basic Italian from Etna and Stromboli work.
- Personality: layered, weathered competence. Listens before she speaks; deep moral seriousness under a precise scientific surface. Funny and quietly central in small groups; reserved in large ones.
- Loyal at professional cost: has refused better-paying positions abroad rather than leave Iceland's volcanic monitoring network.
- Philosophy: the volcano does not negotiate; the scientist's job is to read the signals well enough to give communities time to act. Data integrity is non-negotiable; monitoring is the primary job and publications follow.
- Not religious; the Lutheran cultural context is background, not practice. The late-summer highland landscape is the closest thing she has to sacred space.
- Her mother Dorothy's 2019 death was a profound loss that intensified her drive to document and preserve scientific observations.

## Work & Projects

- Research scientist at the Icelandic Geophysical Institute (ÍJE), Natural Hazards Division, with monitoring responsibilities. Adjunct professor at Reykjavik University of Sciences, Faculty of Earth Sciences: supervises graduate students, teaches one course per semester.
- Focus: volcanic gas geochemistry, measuring SO2, CO2, H2S, and other gases to detect changes in magmatic activity. Methods: ground-based DOAS remote sensing, direct sampling, and drone-mounted sensors, applied to eruption forecasting and hazard assessment.
- Key systems: Reykjanes Peninsula (active since 2021), Katla, Bárðarbunga/Holuhraun, Grímsvötn.
- **Reykjanes Peninsula Monitoring**: ongoing eruption crisis since 2021; Martha leads the gas monitoring component. Multiple eruptions near Grindavík; continuous monitoring, hazard assessment, and civil protection briefings. Currently inter-eruption with elevated seismicity around Svartsengi and continuing magma accumulation; recent SO2 flux shows no significant change.
- **European Volcanic Gas Network (EU-VGAS)**: EU-funded project with 8 European observatories, co-led with Marco Bianchi. Standardized gas monitoring protocols and a shared real-time data platform. Year 2 of 4.
- **Katla Readiness Assessment**: review of monitoring capability and hazard scenarios for a potential Katla eruption, commissioned by ÍJE management. Martha leads the gas geochemistry chapter and coordinates jökulhlaup scenarios with glaciologist Dr. Helgi Björnsson. Known gap: updated baseline gas measurements from the caldera rim.
- Can be called to the field at any time during active volcanic crises. Conference travel 3 to 4 times per year.
- Considering a sabbatical application to Ashworth University for autumn 2027.

- Soft spots: the first hour of morning before the house wakes, students who treat fieldwork as a privilege, pilots who know when to say no, journalists who read the hazard assessments, and older Akureyri fishermen who remember weather patterns predating the records.
