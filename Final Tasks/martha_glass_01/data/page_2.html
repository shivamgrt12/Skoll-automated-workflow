Reading the Mountain

A volcano is a long sentence with most of the words missing. This is the work of reading the quiet between eruptions.

Written by Martha Glass, volcanologist, Reykjavik
Follow the writing
What this is

The years of quiet, not the dramatic morning

Most people imagine a volcano as a single event. Those of us who watch them know them as decades of small changes in gas and ground that, read carefully enough, give a town the hours it needs. This book is about that unglamorous truth, written for readers who have never held a spectrometer.
