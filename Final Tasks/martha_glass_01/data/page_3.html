Eruption forecasting and hazard communication

Reading volcanic gas to give communities time to act

We measure the gases that magma releases, sulphur dioxide, carbon dioxide, and hydrogen sulphide, to detect changes in activity across Iceland's restless systems and the wider European network.

What we do

Three lines of measurement

Our methods are chosen to work where volcanoes actually are, on glaciers, in highland weather, and across plumes that rarely sit still.

Remote scanning

Ground based scanning measures sulphur dioxide flux in any plume that clears the terrain, day to day and through unrest.

Direct sampling

Field instruments capture gas species ratios at fumaroles and vents during the short windows the weather allows.

Drone surveys

Sensor payloads reach plumes and craters that are unsafe or impossible to sample on foot, extending coverage safely.
