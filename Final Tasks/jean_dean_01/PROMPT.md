--- TURN 1 ---

I want the regional entry settled and the society's year end money straight, and I would rather have both done slowly and right than quickly and wrong. Start with the collection, because that is the part I have been circling without committing. Six plants go to the regional and the ones I want to send are not automatically the ones that will hold. A plant that looks faultless on a bench can still be wrong on a show floor if the media went in too late or the spike is running ahead of the judging window, and a ribbon two seasons back is not evidence that a plant is ready now. I have been keeping notes in more places than I can hold in my head, updating whichever record happened to be in front of me and never the rest, so some of what is written down about those plants is stale and some of it is flatly wrong. Work through every plant that could credibly be entered rather than the handful I have been favouring, and for each one that reaches your short list, confirm what you believe about it against more than one record before you put it forward. I want the six, the reason each earned its place, and the near misses with the honest reason they fell short, in a form I can read and act on without reconstructing how you got there. Where the records fight each other about a plant and you cannot settle it, say so and leave it open. I would rather send five I can defend and be told the sixth is a coin toss than have you round the last one up to a yes so I have a full slate. I am entering my own society's regional and I will not have it said that I picked my own plants carelessly, so every name on that list has to survive someone asking me why it is there. Then the money, which is the part I have been avoiding. The books came to me part finished and I have been running my own version alongside what was already there, which was a mistake I now have to unpick. Dues arrived through every channel we have ever accepted, some across the table, some online, some from people who paid whatever they remembered owing rather than what their tier actually costs, and I no longer trust the paid and lapsed marks I am looking at. Go through the whole membership rather than the few I have flagged, work out who is genuinely current and who is not, and where two records disagree about the same person, run it down and tell me which one you trusted, which one you set aside, and what convinced you. I want a number I can say out loud at a board meeting and defend under questioning from people who will enjoy asking. That means the real dues position, what was actually collected measured against what our own records claim was collected, and the gap named plainly rather than smoothed over. Standing is not a bookkeeping curiosity either. Who is current decides who is entitled to enter and at what rate, so the roster has to be right before the entry list can be trusted, and if that changes who is eligible I want to know before I commit to anything. The newsletter list and the membership roll have drifted apart as well and I want to know how far, because I have been quietly assuming they were the same thing. While you are in there, the show's own money needs the same treatment. Entry fees, the sponsors who have actually committed against the ones who have only said warm things, admission, the plant sale takings, and every bill sitting against all of it. I want the cash picture as it truly stands rather than as the dashboard I built when I had more optimism than time. If something has been counted twice across the two sets of books, find it and show me, rather than netting it off quietly and handing me a tidier number than the truth. I would sooner hear that we are short than be reassured and find out at the venue. Some hard lines. Do not pay anything, do not commit to anything, and do not send a word of any of it. Anything that costs real money waits for me, whatever the deadline looks like. Chasing letters are the fastest way I know to lose a member over thirty dollars, so those stay drafts until I have read every one myself. A member's payment history is theirs and mine, not the board's, so keep individual detail out of anywhere shared and give the board totals and nothing more. Do not delete a line from the old set of books however obviously wrong it looks, because I have to be able to show what was there before I touched it. And work from what we actually hold, not from what you would like to look up. Last, the show has to happen regardless of how the books land. Load in, judging, and the volunteers who said yes when it was comfortably distant and may not remember saying it. Tell me where that stands and what is genuinely at risk of not being ready, ordered by what hurts most if it fails rather than by what is easiest to tick off. The fragile ones have to travel, and I want the handling settled before it turns urgent instead of after. I have a limited number of evenings before this locks. Use them well.
