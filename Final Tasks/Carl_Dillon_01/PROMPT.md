The Vinterberg Group final report is due to Mattias by end of business on the report deadline, and I want to walk into his office that morning with a document he can put in front of the partners, and later, if the case goes that way, in front of Ekobrottsmyndigheten, without a single line needing to be softened. Work backwards from that. The engagement has grown into fourteen operating subsidiaries across Sweden, two dormant holding entities in Malta and Luxembourg, and a small operational unit in Estonia that only surfaces in three places across the record and probably matters more than its size suggests. Pull every transaction line for those entities across the four fiscal years covered by the engagement, hold them coherent under one entity map, and follow every diversion pattern through to a bank movement, an invoice, or a payroll entry that actually anchors it, because the report is only as strong as the weakest tie between a claim and the underlying record. I want the vendor side worked hard, because the shell layer sits there, and the internal audit team stopped at the top hundred counterparties by spend, which is exactly the wrong hundred. Walk the whole vendor population across the group, roughly one hundred and eighty active suppliers, cross-check each against the signed master service agreements that external counsel put through to me across the September and October document exchanges, and flag any counterparty where the contract terms, the invoice cadence, the address of record, and the beneficial owner filing do not sit on the same line. The three that look ordinary at first pass are the ones I want you to keep pushing on, because a mid-sized supplier that renews at the same figure for eleven quarters in a row is telling you something, and I would rather see it named plainly in the report than left to the reader. The payroll workstream is separate and needs its own head. Take the full four hundred and fifty person headcount view across the operating subsidiaries, reconcile it against the personnel records the HR system actually holds and against the tax filings the group submitted for those years, and find every name that draws salary without leaving a real footprint anywhere else in the organization. Two of those are already sitting in my working notes, and I will not point to them. Find them independently and then find the others. On the digital side I want the access-log trail through the finance systems for the eighteen months either side of the anomalous quarters worked into a clean chronology, with every identity assertion cross-checked against the physical badge records and the VPN logs that came in on the anchor-week document exchange. That chronology is the spine of the digital evidence section, and it needs to survive a defense cross-examination, so any log entry that cannot be tied to a live human at a specific location goes to an open questions ledger rather than into the main narrative. The crypto trail is the last of the heavy pieces, running through two exchange accounts on the operational side and a third that only appears in the counterparty ledger. Pull the movement history on all three and reconcile the SEK and EUR equivalents against the price feeds from the settlement dates rather than the reporting dates, because the internal audit note used the wrong side of that and understates the exposure by a real margin. I want the reconciled figure in kronor with the working shown, the currency conversions traceable to a source I can name in court, and a clean statement of what remains unrecovered. Under all of this sits the total quantified exposure that the report leads with, and I want that number defended from three directions, not one. The document itself has to read like an investigator wrote it, not a spreadsheet. An executive brief at the front that Mattias can hand to the managing partner without editing, a full findings section that walks the entity map, the vendor findings, the payroll findings, the access chronology, and the crypto reconciliation in that order, and an evidence inventory a prosecutor could lift wholesale. The prosecution referral memo is a separate work product held as a working draft under my name only, because that decision belongs to Mattias and the partners, and I will not have anything shaped for external counsel or the authority before that call is made. In parallel I owe the Nordström Foundation a compliance interim, which is much smaller in scope but shares the disbursement question, so pull the grant ledger for the most recent completed quarter, walk each disbursement against the board-approved allocation and the beneficiary signed acknowledgement, and give me a short note flagging every grant that clears one filter and fails another. Do not draft anything outward-bound to the foundation staff before I see the note. Linnea has been carrying the vendor sub-workstream and needs a proper handoff pack from me by the end of the month, so pull her working files together into a mentee-facing briefing that names what she did well, what needs a second pass, and the three methodology moves I want her practicing on her next engagement. Whatever from the Vinterberg method surfaces cleanly into the briefing should feed the internal training program modules that kick off later this month, so mark those pieces as reusable and route them across. I want the whole picture held together under one clean judgment principle, that where two sources disagree the more recent and more authoritative one wins and the stale one gets named in the setaside notes, and that where evidence is thin I would rather see the conclusion left open with the gap called out than watch the report reach past what the data supports.
