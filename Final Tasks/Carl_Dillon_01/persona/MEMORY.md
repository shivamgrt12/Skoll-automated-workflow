# Memory: Carl Dillon

## Personal Profile

- Born in Uppsala; his family moved to Stockholm when he was 12. He carries a slight outsider's perspective on the capital and notices the architecture of Swedish social class with the same attention he brings to financial structures.
- Education: B.Sc. in Business Administration, Stockholm University (2009); M.Sc. in Accounting and Financial Management, Stockholm School of Economics (2011); Certified Fraud Examiner, Association of Certified Fraud Examiners, since 2016.
- Languages: native Swedish, fluent English in daily professional use, intermediate German (good enough for meetings, not for jokes).
- Calm and methodical on the surface with a restlessness underneath. The investigative reflex never switches off: he audits restaurant bills, news logic, and sales pitches by instinct, and finds it more useful than annoying.
- Slower to speak than expected. He listens for the structure of an argument and arrives at his point fully formed, which people sometimes mistake for disengagement.
- Holds order and ambiguity together without tension: an immaculate budget alongside music about loss and longing.
- Hard on himself in ways invisible to others. He processes through activity, a long run or a reorganized shelf, rather than conversation.
- Private without being secretive: shares when asked, does not volunteer. He considers this economy of communication respectful.
- Core values: getting things exactly right out of respect for the truth beneath surfaces; consistency over intensity in every domain; fierce self-reliance paired with a slow recalibration, in recent years, about whether a fully self-contained life is the same as a well-lived one. He believes in institutions while seeing clearly how they fail people, and sees his role as making them work better from the inside.
- Swedish identity operates as background logic: lagom, punctuality, institutional fairness, discomfort with conspicuous display. He metabolizes winter darkness contentedly and finds summer's social pressure to be outdoors mildly exhausting.
- Small circle by choice. Observer in groups until he has something substantive, then precise enough to command attention. He shows care through actions and logistics, not declarations.

## Preferences

- **News**: The New York Times every morning, The Economist for international perspective, and Revisorsinspektionen updates for Swedish financial regulation.
- **Music**: Americana and indie rock first, jazz for deep analytical work, classical for quiet Sunday mornings. Playlists organized by mood and activity with a specificity that surprises people who know him as a numbers person.
- **Podcasts**: Planet Money, Forensic Focus, Radiolab. Listened to on runs and commutes.
- **Reading**: Crime fiction and thrillers, financial regulation texts for work, literary fiction. Twenty to forty minutes in bed ends every day, read to a natural stopping point rather than a page count.
- **Food**: Balanced whole foods with fish three to four times a week (salmon, herring, cod). Favorites: smoked salmon bagels, grilled steak with roasted vegetables, his mother's fish soup recipe, cinnamon rolls from his favorite bakery.
- **Regular spots**: Pelikan for classic Swedish food, Tak Stockholm for special occasions, Café Pascal for morning coffee and work sessions.
- **Coffee**: Three cups daily, strong filter, beans from Johan & Nyström. He experiences it as a complete sensory event, optimized at every step.
- **Style**: Understated, well-fitted, monochromatic: dark blues, greys, blacks. Quality basics over statement pieces, with footwear as the one visible exception. Scandinavian design instincts: clean lines, function over ornament.
- **Travel**: Purposeful and light. Carry-on only under five days, aisle seats, headphones and a book, no sleeping on planes. Drawn to walkable cities with strong food culture and rewards in the side streets: Copenhagen, Edinburgh, Vienna. Gothenburg trips run looser, with room for Elsa to redirect everything.
- **Shopping**: Deliberate to the point of algorithmic. He spends freely on food, running gear, and coffee, replaces running shoes on a mileage schedule, and runs a 24-hour rule on non-essential purchases. One regret: the limited-edition National vinyl he passed on.
- **Gifts**: Practical and high quality; for family, thoughtful and personal, never generic. Good kitchen tools, running gear, and bookshop gift cards land well with him.
- **Decompression**: A deliberate evening transition (shoes off, music on, fifteen minutes of nothing), protected post-long-run Saturday calm, and restoration through solitude rather than socializing. The Maja dinners are the energizing exception.
- **Sensory**: Sound organizes his day and silence is a protected default. Tactile quality drives purchases: fabric weight, wood finish, a well-bound book. Winter prompts enclosure and warm light; summer prompts movement and longer evenings.
- **Pet peeves**: Imprecision passed off as casualness, agenda-free meetings, correlation-causation confusion in financial journalism, unlabeled shellfish in dishes, listings that omit the avgift, podcast hosts who interrupt guests, and software that auto-updates mid-work.
