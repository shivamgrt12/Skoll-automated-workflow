--- TURN 1 ---
Okay, I need you to help me get my freelance books actually honest before Frank comes asking for them the way he does every cycle, because every single time he sits me down I hand him a mess and feel like a kid who lost his permission slip, and I would rather walk in with something clean for once and look like a person who takes his own work seriously. The thing is I have let the record-keeping drift. Money for the art lands from a bunch of different places, the album covers I do for Benny, the poster work for the venue over in Hillsboro, the odd gig poster a musician sends my way, the zine and print sales at the fest and the makers market, the trickle of print orders that go out through the shop listings, cash in the box at the table, cards tapped on the reader at the table, plain transfers, the payout thing that dumps into checking a few days after a sale, and somewhere in the middle of all of it I stopped trusting that what I have written down matches what actually showed up. I need you to go through the whole back-catalog of jobs and sales, every invoice I ever cut and every dollar that supposedly came in against it, and walk each one from the moment I billed it to the moment it actually landed somewhere I can point at, cross-checking the money I recorded against the money that truly cleared each way it comes in, and where the story does not line up I need you to run it down and tell me plainly which version is real. Do not just average the two or quietly pick the friendlier number, because a couple of these are stale from back when I was sloppier and I have lost all confidence they still square with reality. Some I am scared are marked paid that never actually paid, and there is at least one table weekend where the total I logged and the total that came across the readers flat out do not agree and I honestly cannot remember which one is right anymore. On the other side of the ledger I need the spending trued up the same careful way, the supplies, the ink and boards and nibs, the software I keep threatening to cancel, my half of the studio, the transit I run back and forth on, the printing runs for the zines, all of it sorted into what is genuinely a cost of making the art and what is just me living my life, because Frank only cares about the first kind and I am the one who always blurs the two. Keep the art business its own clean thing, just the illustration and the zines and the prints, and let anything that is not truly part of that work stay out of the picture, because it muddies everything if it gets folded in where it does not belong. Once it is all reconciled what I am really after is a picture I can actually trust, the real income set against the real costs, what the art genuinely cleared for the stretch, every number traced back to where it came from so that when Frank raises an eyebrow I can put my finger on the line instead of shrugging and going red, with the open ends called out too, whatever is still owed to me and has not landed, because the venue poster is sitting out there unpaid and I have a bad feeling it is not the only one I told myself was fine when it was not. On top of that I need the whole thing packaged the way Frank actually reads it, the totals grouped the way an accountant wants them, clean enough that he can pick it up and run without a dozen follow-up questions, plus the rough amount I ought to be setting aside for what I will owe, worked out in a way that holds up rather than a number I pulled out of the air, because every cycle I lowball that and it comes back to bite me. Here is the hard line though, none of this leaves my hands until I have read it myself. Draft it, save it, put the note to Frank together if you want it ready, but do not send a thing to him or to anyone else, this stays between the two of us until I say go, and be careful about where any of it even sits, because my money situation is not a group conversation and I do not want it surfacing anywhere the studio crowd or anyone can stumble onto it. Where the evidence is thin, where a sale left almost no trail or a figure only exists in one spot and I cannot back it against anything else, do not force it into a tidy total to make the page look finished, just flag it as soft and tell me so, so I know where I am standing on solid ground and where I am guessing. I know this is a slog and I know I should have kept up with it all along, so spare me the lecture on that part, just help me walk in with my head up for once holding something that looks like how a real working artist keeps his affairs, instead of whatever this drawer full of half-remembered receipts currently is.
