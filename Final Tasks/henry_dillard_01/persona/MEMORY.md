# Memory: Henry Dillard

## Personal Profile
- **Full name**: Henry James Dillard. **Pronouns**: he/him.
- **Ethnicity / background**: White American, quietly working-class Southern; grew up between two households after his parents divorced when he was 14, librarian mother in Murfreesboro, software-engineer father in Franklin, TN. Uses *y'all* without thinking, knows his way around a smoker, proud of Nashville as a real city, not the tourist version.
- **Occupation**: Barista at Copper Kettle Coffee (part-time, ~30 hrs/week) + freelance illustrator + aspiring graphic novelist.
- **Education**: Completed one semester at Thornfield University School of Fine Arts (2021) before dropping out; mostly self-taught, with online courses through Ashvale Institute Continuing Ed.
- **Temperament**: Runs quieter than the people around him, listens more than he talks, watches more than he asks, often arrives at the most precise read ten minutes after the table has moved on. Gentle and self-effacing, uncomfortable as the center of attention, widely described as one of the kindest people his friends know. The kindness is real and also a form of self-protection.
- **Inner life**: A long, low-grade anxiety managed with daily Lexapro and standing therapy, plus a perfectionism that can collapse a productive day into a 2 AM deletion spiral, and a chronic awareness that his career is precarious in a family of stable professionals. Better at naming these patterns than preventing them. What balances it is durable creative seriousness; deep at night with the right music and pen, he is steady in a way the rest of his life rarely matches.
- **Philosophy**: Craft, slow, and quiet. The work is the work whether or not it is recognized or profitable; the quality of attention you bring to a panel or a cortado is itself a moral category; small repeated acts of care beat grand gestures. Suspicious of cynicism and absolutism; left of his extended family in a careful, unspoken way. Not formally religious, a residual Southern Protestantism set down without animosity, with the sacred running through his art and his people rather than any institution.
- **Cultural identity in work**: Inside the small-press indie-comics tradition (Tomine, Walden, Lemire, Brubaker) with a strong Southern literary vein that shapes *The Liminal Market* more than any comics influence. More confident about his cultural identity in his work than in his life.

- **Music while drawing**: lo-fi hip hop, Sturgill Simpson, Tyler Childers, Hovvdy, ambient Brian Eno; "Henry's Ink Session" on Spotify.
- **Reading**: Graphic novels (Tomine, Walden, Lemire, Brubaker), sci-fi paperbacks, poetry (Jack Gilbert, Richard Siken). Uses the library, swaps with Jake, takes hand-me-down art books from Prof. Hargrove; buys zines and small-press comics to support other makers.
- **Coffee & food**: Light-roast single-origin pour-over at home (Chemex), oat-milk cortado at work, 3-4 cups/day. Loves ramen, tacos, Nashville hot chicken, food-truck burgers; Sunday sourdough loaf (starter "Gloop"); comfort food is Grandma Dot's brisket and Mom's mac and cheese. Derek cooks weeknights (Southern + Tex-Mex), Henry does the dishes.
- **Streaming & news**: Netflix (shared with Derek), Crunchyroll (currently "Dungeon Meshi"); follows The Meridian Post and local Nashville alt-weeklies, mostly gets news from social media.
- **Aesthetic & style**: Thrift-store practical with art-school inflection, flannels, soft jeans, work boots, vintage band tees, same canvas tote three years running. Beard most winters. His art aesthetic is precise: clean linework, careful spotting of blacks, restrained Copic palettes, patient hand-lettering. Shops Parnassus Books, thrift stores, Blick, supply swap meets.
- **Travel**: Travels little (money and work). Small trips matter, Franklin for Grandma Dot's BBQ, comics events in Atlanta/Asheville, the Small Press Expo trip to Maryland. Slow itineraries; trains over planes. Dreams of Tokyo, Mexico City, Edinburgh during the Fringe.
- **Comfort & decompression**: Drawing is the primary reset (aware the same desk is where the anxiety lives); therapy is the deeper maintenance; cooking evenings with Derek (NPR low on the speaker) are reliably restorative; so are sundown bike rides along the Cumberland River and Saturday afternoons at the studio while Jake prints.
- **Dislikes & pet peeves**: Allergy to bigness, big talkers, big rooms, networking events he leaves tired. Dislikes the implication that comics or freelance is less serious work, interruption while he draws on break, being asked when the book will be done, telephones, weddings. Bothered by AI illustration replacing craft and baristas who don't care about coffee.
- **Spending**: Careful and reluctant; thrifts almost all clothing, supports indie makers, and sustains a small economy of handmade gifts within his community.
