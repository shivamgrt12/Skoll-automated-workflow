# Memory: Kristen Moore

## Personal Profile

Kristen Marie Moore was born in Memphis and has never lived anywhere else. Memphis is in her vowels, her music, and her sense of what an afternoon should feel like. She does not romanticize the South; she knows it as it is. Her ancestors are buried in this dirt, her mother is twenty minutes away in Whitehaven, and the man at the gas station on Germantown Parkway knows her by name. She is a Black Southern woman whose cultural inheritance runs through Sunday service at Grace Tabernacle, her grandmother's piano, and the peach cobbler recipe on a notecard taped together at the corners. She is conscious of what she kept from her mother's generation (the cobbler, the church, the hospitality) and what she put down (the silence about therapy, the idea that strong women do not need help).

She holds a B.S. in Psychology (2004) and an M.Ed. in School Counseling (2008), both from the University of Memphis. Native English speaker, no other languages. Her path was built on twenty years counseling teenagers through college applications, family crises, and identity spirals, and she refuses to forget the people who taught her how to listen. The refusal lives in small ongoing choices: the Sunday visit to Loretta, the casserole, the remembered birthday, the way she stays late on a Wednesday for a senior whose application is unraveling.

She believes you owe your people your honesty before you owe them your comfort. She believes grief is a room you learn to live in, not a problem to be solved. She believes boundaries are an act of love and that she is still learning to hold them. She believes God is a real presence in her life without being in a hurry to define what that means. She believes in showing up small and consistent rather than grand and occasional: the casserole, the phone call, the remembered birthday.

She runs warm and a little loud when she is happy, and her laugh has a way of pulling other people in. She is stubborn under the warmth, deliberate when a decision matters, and has been told she is "intense" and "a lot" by people who meant it as criticism. She is mostly fine with that. What she will not tolerate is being condescended to. She second-guesses herself in private more than she ever shows, especially decisions about Jaylen. She is widowed; Marcus Moore was killed in a car accident in February 2022, and grief reshaped her rather than broke her. She is not currently dating and is not interested in being asked about it.
