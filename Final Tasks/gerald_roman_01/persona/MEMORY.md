# Memory: Gerald Roman

## Personal Profile

- Grew up in northeast Baltimore; her family has been in the area for generations, with close ties to extended family and community across the Baltimore to DC corridor.
- Education: B.S. in Biology, Westbrook University (2013); M.P.H. in Epidemiology, Thornfield University School of Public Health (2015); Ph.D. in Epidemiology, Kensington University (2022). Dissertation: "Socioeconomic Determinants of Respiratory Illness Burden in Mid-Atlantic Urban Populations."
- Rigorous and musical in equal measure. She holds multiple threads at once whether the material is surveillance data or a fingerstyle arrangement.
- Warmth arrives gradually but completely. Strangers sometimes misread the initial stillness as coldness; she refuses to perform instant warmth.
- Intellectually fearless. Her teacher father raised her to value truth over hierarchy, so she questions senior researchers' methodology politely and without backing down, accepting that some colleagues read her as difficult.
- A Black woman in academic epidemiology who has declined the expectation of performed enthusiasm. Socially selective by design: a few substantive conversations over a long mixer, a small circle with total reliability inside it.
- Dry, understated humor that rewards patience. Her quiet observations are often the sharpest thing said all evening.
- Rooted in a Baltimore older and more complicated than outsiders know. She chose to stay, and the choosing shapes her work.
- Raised Methodist by her devout mother. She attends church on major holidays and carries the practical ethics without claiming the faith outright.
- The fingerstyle tradition inherited from her grandmother Dorothy is the deepest strand of her identity: specific tunes and fingerings, a line she carries forward seriously. The grief surfaces in the fingers, not in conversation.
- Philosophy: the data has to be honest, the interpretation careful, and the communication mindful of whose life it lands on. Public health is a moral discipline disguised as a technical one. She is skeptical of institutions that celebrate inclusion without changing how they decide, and she believes you do not get to keep what you do not pass on.

## Key Relationships

- **Grace Roman (mother, 62)**: Registered nurse at Cedarbrook Medical Center, night shifts 7 PM to 7 AM, three nights a week on rotation. Strong, practical, devout Methodist; attends church Sunday mornings when not working. Gerald's closest confidante; they talk weekly and see each other biweekly.
- **Robert Roman (father, 65)**: Retired after 20+ years as a Baltimore high school teacher, then an educational aide. Quiet, intellectual, proud; reads voraciously, tends a small garden, home most days. His health is declining.
- **Rachel Roman (sister, 27)**: Second-year medical student at Ashvale State University School of Medicine in Philadelphia. Brilliant but overwhelmed. Gerald mentors her heavily, knows she over-mentors, and intends to keep doing it. They text daily and talk two or three times a week; Rachel comes to Baltimore one or two weekends a month.
- **Dorothy Roman (grandmother, deceased 2018)**: Folk and fingerpicking guitarist who taught Gerald the instrument. Her passing deepened Gerald's commitment to the guitar as a way of keeping the connection open.
- **Dr. Eleanor Voss (supervisor and mentor, 52)**: Senior Research Director at CIPH and the person who recruited Gerald. Brilliant epidemiologist, demanding but fair, and an advocate for Gerald's career advancement.
- **Dr. Marcus Hayes (research collaborator, 40)**: Biostatistician at CIPH who works closely with Gerald on data analysis. Mutual respect; occasional lunch companion.
- **Chris Nyberg (close friend, 38)**: Elementary school teacher in Silver Spring, Maryland, and fellow guitarist in the Chesapeake Acoustic Collective. They drive to performances together and share a deep musical bond.
- **Derek Palmer (community friend, 41)**: Owns a small specialty grocery store in northeast Baltimore. A community connector who hosts gatherings where Gerald sometimes plays guitar.
- **Nathan Cole (ex-boyfriend, 36)**: Two-year relationship that ended in early 2025 over incompatible priorities; he wanted San Francisco and a health tech startup, she is committed to Baltimore and community-centered public health. They do not communicate. Not ugly, just sad. She is single, not actively looking, but not closed off.
- **Yemi Adeyemi and Sarah Kim**: Research assistants on Gerald's team at CIPH.

## Work & Projects

- **Chesapeake Institute of Public Health (CIPH)**: research institute affiliated with several Baltimore-area universities, focused on urban public health, infectious disease, and health equity. Gerald is four years in as a Research Scientist.
- Focus: infectious disease surveillance and respiratory illness epidemiology in urban populations. Principal investigator on one study, co-investigator on two others. Six peer-reviewed publications, two as first author.
- **Current major project**: the Urban Respiratory Illness Surveillance Network (URISN), a multi-year study tracking respiratory infection patterns across Baltimore neighborhoods and how socioeconomic factors shape disease burden and healthcare access. Funded by a three-year grant from the Meridian Public Health Foundation.
- Team: Dr. Eleanor Voss (supervisor), Dr. Marcus Hayes (biostatistician), and research assistants Yemi Adeyemi and Sarah Kim.
- Often works evenings on manuscripts and analysis from home during deadline stretches.
- Career goals: establish herself as a leading voice in urban infectious disease epidemiology, win an independent R01-equivalent grant, and eventually hold a faculty position training public health researchers who look like the communities they study.
- Professional development: presenting at the National Conference of Epidemiologists, reviewing for two journals, and informally mentoring a graduate student.

- Sends $150 to $200 a month to her parents to help with expenses, willingly given.

- Goals: pay the loans down aggressively once possible, build the emergency fund to six months of expenses, and eventually help Rachel with medical school costs.

## Health & Wellness

- Generally healthy and physically active. Occasional tension headaches from screen-heavy analysis work, managed with breaks and hydration.
- Sleep: about seven hours most nights, dropping to five or six for weeks during intense publication deadlines.
- Running: 15 to 20 miles a week. Completed a half marathon in 2025 and is loosely training toward a full marathon.
- Mild anxiety, not clinically diagnosed, that shows up as over-preparation and difficulty delegating. She manages it with running, guitar, and structured routines; she has considered therapy but has not started.
- Robert: type 2 diabetes managed with metformin and diet, chronic fatigue, and mild neuropathy in his feet. Gerald monitors his medication compliance and accompanies him to appointments at Greenleaf Medical Associates.
- Grace: generally healthy but overworked; night shift nursing brings sleep disruption and back pain. Gerald worries about her burnout.
- Rachel: stress-related insomnia during medical school exam periods. Gerald nudges her to take breaks.

## Interests & Hobbies

- Fingerstyle acoustic guitar: technically demanding and expressive, learned from Dorothy and continued under a local instructor. Folk, Americana, and instrumental compositions. She performs at local venues, acoustic festivals, and community events, and occasionally teaches workshops. Evening practice is closer to meditation than skill-building.
- Singing: a strong, clear voice, with harmonies alongside her playing in the Chesapeake Acoustic Collective, an informal ensemble that performs two or three times a season. She is more relaxed at those rehearsals than almost anywhere else.
- Running: mornings around Druid Hill Park or the Charles Village streets. She runs for the brain that arrives at her desk afterward, not for pace.
- Reading: public health policy, epidemiology journals, contemporary literary fiction, and poetry, especially Mary Oliver and Lucille Clifton.
- Container gardening on the balcony: basil, thyme, cilantro, cherry tomatoes, and a stubborn habanero experiment. Checking the leaves before the first coffee is a protected ritual.
- Cooking the family canon: pot roast, chicken and dumplings, cornbread, collard greens, fried chicken, and hearty stews, made the way her mother and grandmother made them, plus experiments from cookbooks. Half practice, half memory.

## Preferences

- Music: the fingerstyle masters (Tommy Emmanuel, Andy McKee, Kaki King), folk and Americana (Iron & Wine, Fleet Foxes), jazz (Miles Davis, John Coltrane; she hears parallels between improvisation and fingerstyle), and Bach, whose mathematical precision appeals to her.
- Entertainment: public health podcasts (This Week in Virology, The Public Health Dispatch), contemporary fiction, long-form journalism (The Lakeshore Review, ProPublica), and the occasional documentary. Not a TV person; she would rather read or play.
- Food: omnivore anchored in home-style American comfort food, cooking three or four nights a week and reaching for comfort dishes when stressed. Eats out at Southern soul food spots, local seafood places (crab cakes are the favorite), and the occasional nice restaurant as a treat. Dislikes loud restaurants.
- Drink: tea through the day, herbal blends or English breakfast, with one morning coffee with milk. Not a big drinker; an occasional glass of wine with dinner or a craft beer at community events.
- Shopping: buys slowly and keeps things long. Sustainable and ethical brands when the budget allows, thrift stores and Target otherwise, the local running store for gear, and groceries from Whole Foods plus Derek's specialty shop. Uncomfortable with conspicuous consumption; she would rather host a small dinner at home.
- Gifts: researched, specific, and attentive. The noticing is the gift.
- Social media: Twitter for academic public health discussion and LinkedIn for networking. No Instagram, TikTok, or Facebook of her own.
- Travel: one carry-on and the same packing list every time. Prefers trains over planes, the Amtrak to Philadelphia especially, and vacations toward quiet, water-adjacent places with good light and food; the North Carolina coast trip is the model. Guesthouses and lived-in neighborhoods over hotels.
- Comfort and decompression: twenty minutes of warm-up fingerpicking when pressure builds, Grace's kitchen on a Sunday for the gentler kind, and the reading chair, a saved long-form piece, a record, and the parlor guitar within reach on heavier nights.
- Sensory world: she notices sound first in any room and chose the apartment partly for its acoustics. Loves new-string texture under her fingertips, the cedar smell of Dorothy's guitar case, and the protected early morning hour of city quiet and coffee. Cannot stand fluorescent buzz, synthetic air freshener, or a fork scraping a plate.
- Pet peeves: performed concern and uncited expertise, meetings that should have been emails, academic gossip dressed as professional concern, and anyone treating Baltimore as a punchline.
