The housing gradient

What stands out

  • + Each 10-point drop in housing quality tracks a 14 percent rise in respiratory illness.
  • + The gap between the highest and lowest sites is more than two to one.
  • + The pattern holds after adjusting for age, income, insurance, and smoking.
  • + Adults 55 and older carry the steepest part of the gradient.

About this dashboard

How to read these numbers
The Housing Quality Index combines city inspection records with resident self-report on a scale of 0 to 100, where lower scores mean worse conditions. Incidence counts symptom-confirmed respiratory illness episodes for every 1,000 person-months of follow-up, so neighborhoods of different sizes can be compared fairly. This is a public summary. Individual records stay confidential and never leave the secure study systems.
