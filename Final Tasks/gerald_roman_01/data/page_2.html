About

Evidence in service of a place

I am a research scientist at the Chesapeake Institute of Public Health and the principal investigator of the Urban Respiratory Illness Surveillance Network. My work pairs careful statistics with a stubborn belief that the numbers describe real families on real blocks. When I am not at the institute, I play fingerstyle guitar around the mid-Atlantic.

Research

What I work on

Surveillance design

Building community-embedded systems that follow respiratory illness at the neighborhood level, not just the city average.

Housing and health

Measuring how structural housing quality shapes who gets sick, and how often, across a socioeconomic gradient.

Health equity

Turning findings into recommendations that local housing and public health policy can actually use.
