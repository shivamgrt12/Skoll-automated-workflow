1978 Ford F-150 - Engine Rebuild Running Notes
==============================================
Engine: 351M (Modified), 2-barrel carb, C6 automatic
Mileage at teardown: 142,387 (unknown if original)

GOAL: Get her running reliably for weekend drives, not a show truck.
      Keep it period-correct where possible.

--- COMPRESSION TEST (before teardown, May 2025) ---
Cylinder 1: 125 psi
Cylinder 2: 130 psi
Cylinder 3: 118 psi  <-- low
Cylinder 4: 128 psi
Cylinder 5: 132 psi
Cylinder 6: 125 psi
Cylinder 7: 115 psi  <-- low
Cylinder 8: 130 psi

Conclusion: #3 and #7 are low. Wet test showed improvement = rings.
Decision: Full rebuild rather than just rings. She's earned it.

--- TEARDOWN NOTES (June 2025) ---
- Removed engine with Ben's help (he drove up from Shiprock, good weekend)
- Engine hoist borrowed from Eddie Tsosie
- Found the #3 exhaust valve was slightly burned — explains the low compression
- #7 cylinder wall had some scoring, nothing a .030 overbore won't fix
- Crank looks good, no scoring, will get it checked at machine shop
- All rod bearings show wear but no failure
- Cam lobes look okay but replacing anyway — going with Comp Cams 260H
- Timing chain was stretched, guides worn

--- MACHINE SHOP (July-August 2025) ---
- Took block and heads to Southwest Engine in Albuquerque
- Block: .030 overbore, decked .005, hot tanked
- Heads: New valve seats (#3 and #7 exhaust), valve job all 16, resurfaced
- Crank: Polished, within spec, no grinding needed — saved $200
- Total machine shop bill: $1,340 (ouch but worth it)

--- ASSEMBLY (September-October 2025) ---
- Painted block Ford blue, looks sharp
- Assembly lube everything, torque specs from the Ford shop manual
- Main caps: 95-105 ft-lbs (used 100)
- Rod caps: 40-45 ft-lbs (used 42)
- Head bolts: 105-112 ft-lbs (used 108), torque sequence per manual
- Set valve lash hot: .014 intake, .016 exhaust (hydraulic lifter prelim)
- Degree'd the cam: 4° advanced from spec, left it — slight low-end torque gain

--- FIRST START (October 19, 2025) ---
- Primed oil pump with drill before cranking
- Cranked 10 seconds, nothing
- Realized I left the distributor 180° out. Typical.
- Corrected, she fired on the second crank. Ran rough for 30 sec then smoothed out.
- Held at 2000 RPM for 20 min for cam break-in
- No leaks. No smoke. Oil pressure 55 psi at 2000 RPM.
- Ben called right as she fired — he heard it through the phone and cheered

--- BREAK-IN AND TUNING (November 2025 - ongoing) ---
- Changed oil at 20 miles (full of metal flake, normal)
- Changed again at 500 miles — clean
- Set timing to 10° BTDC, seems to like it
- Idle is a touch rough below 650 RPM, adjusted to 700
- Cruising at 55 mph on I-40, she's happy around 2200 RPM
- Gas mileage: about 12 mpg. It's a 351M, I knew what I was signing up for.
