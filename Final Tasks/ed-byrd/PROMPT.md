I want the whole stretch into October 24, 2026 run end to end, so that when I walk into the gala planning and sit down with the building committee I am holding nothing but the final calls. Take the whole run as one job, fan out across everything we are connected to, and bring it back to me in a shape I can act on without hunting for it.

Square the church campaign first, because that is the one that will not wait. Tell me plainly where the tally really stands against the one point eight million we set out to raise, and I want the number that has actually cleared the account, not the rounded figure folks have been passing hand to hand since the summer drive. Reconcile every pledge across the whole rolls, mark for me what is paid, what is still owed, and what has quietly lapsed, and where the cleared receipts and an older committee sheet disagree, the receipts win. Harold Bingham has been telling half the deacons he is paid in full, but go to what actually cleared and set his pledge straight, paid against promised, so I am not caught out on it later. Draft the congregation update off the true number and bring it right to the edge of going out, but hold it. That tally does not leave this house until Pastor Whitfield and I have squared it between us, so put no donor amount and no total in front of anyone but the two of us.

Run both properties the same disciplined way. Close the books on Sunrise Lodge and on the Lakeview Motor Inn, and walk the occupancy and the average daily rate against what the ledgers actually show this quarter, not against last year's printed rate sheet, which is stale and will lead you wrong. Where the current book and the old sheet fight, carry the current book and tell me which one you trusted and which one you set aside. Catch the payroll before it runs wrong, because there is a run sitting in there that has not gone through clean, and I would far rather flag it now than claw it back after the checks have cut. Get the Lakeview pilot to a decision I can defend out loud. Cost it out, recommend the split, one step at a time so the whole thing holds up when the committee pushes on it, but commit nothing and pay nothing until I say go.

Keep Dale Hutchins honest on the fellowship hall. Hold his change orders against the line the committee actually approved and not a dollar past it, count what he has run cumulative through his latest order, and set that against the approved contingency, not against some older set of minutes that shows a smaller number. Get the fixtures and materials tracked to the site so I know what has shipped and what is still in transit to the motels and the church build. Settle the gala side. Set the endocrinology appointment and the committee dates around them, never over them.

Hand it all back to me saved as four things. A priority-ranked readiness brief across the campaign, both motels, the renovation pilot, the fellowship-hall build, and the gala, with every deadline called out plainly so nothing sneaks up on me. A cash-flow and funding doc that carries the campaign gap and the Lakeview room math with a split I can take straight to the committee. A log of every place two sources disagreed, naming for each the one you trusted and the one you set aside. And a held queue of everything you have prepared but are holding for my word, the congregation update, anything over my line, any contract wanting a signature, any change to a rate or a vendor, and any word bound for the committee or the family, because none of it goes out until I say go.
