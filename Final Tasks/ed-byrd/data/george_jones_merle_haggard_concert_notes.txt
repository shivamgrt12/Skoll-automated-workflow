Concert Memories & Music Notes - Ed Byrd
==========================================

GEORGE JONES - Tennessee Theatre, Knoxville - March 1998
---------------------------------------------------------
Jolene and I drove up on a Friday night. Tyler was with Mom (Dorothy Mae
drove down from Galax for the weekend). Third row center. George came out
in a suit, no flash, just him and the band. Opened with "White Lightning"
and the whole place was on their feet.

When he sang "He Stopped Loving Her Today" I looked over and Jolene was
crying. I was too but I'll deny it. That voice -- there's nothing like it
in country music, before or since. He did a 90-minute set, no intermission,
barely talked between songs. Just one after another. "The Grand Tour,"
"She Thinks I Still Care," "Who's Gonna Fill Their Shoes."

After the show we got pie at Shoney's on the way home. Best night out we
ever had. I've still got the ticket stub in my Bible.

MERLE HAGGARD - Bijou Theatre, Knoxville - November 2005
---------------------------------------------------------
Bob and I went to this one, "guys night." Merle was 68 and sounded like
he was 30. That Bakersfield sound -- the Telecasters, the steel guitar, the
shuffle beat. He played for two hours. "Mama Tried," "Working Man Blues,"
"Tonight the Bottle Let Me Down," "Sing Me Back Home."

He told stories between songs -- about Bakersfield, about Johnny Cash
visiting him in prison, about his mama. You could hear a pin drop. Then
he'd kick into another song and the whole place was alive.

Merle passed in 2016. Same day as Buck Owens' birthday. I played "Silver
Wings" on repeat that whole week. Jolene didn't say a word about it.

HANK WILLIAMS (tribute show) - Civic Auditorium, Maryville - July 2019
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Local tribute band, "The Drifting Cowboys Revival." Not the real thing
but they were respectful and talented. The lead singer had that high
lonesome sound pretty close. Played all the hits -- "Your Cheatin' Heart,"
"Cold Cold Heart," "I'm So Lonesome I Could Cry," "I Saw the Light."

Brought Tyler and Shelby. Tyler tolerated it. Shelby actually loved it --
she said Hank's lyrics read like poetry. She's not wrong. Hank Williams
was the original singer-songwriter. Everything since is a footnote.
