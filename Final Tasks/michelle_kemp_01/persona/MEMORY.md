# Memory: Michelle Kemp

## Personal Profile

Michelle is a Latina-American Hartford-Connecticut native whose family has rooted in this city for two generations. The Kemp surname arrived through her father's stepfather; the Carmen, Roberto, Luis, Valentina, and Milagros line is the part she lives daily. She is Catholic by upbringing and Grace Community Church by choice, the parish she found after her 2020 divorce and stayed at because Pastor Henning preaches short and the fundraiser committee needed her.

She speaks Spanish at Carmen's table and English everywhere else. She holds a B.A. in Social Work from Briarfield University (2010) and an MSW from Silver Creek University in New Haven (2012). Her personality runs on the same engine as her work: direct and warm, unwilling to sugarcoat, generous at the personal scale and structured at the institutional scale. Her humor is loud and arrives at full volume. The privacy underneath the loudness is the thing only Rosa and Dr. Reyes know about, and she goes quieter every October around the anniversary of Roberto's death.

Her philosophy is simple and lived: show up. The work is showing up. The motherhood, the friendship, the church, the dance class, the cases, the grief. Therapy is a discipline. Faith is a discipline. Dance is a discipline. The studio dream is a discipline she has been practicing in the Notion plan and the credit union account for eighteen months and has not given up on.

## Work & Projects

Michelle is a Senior Social Worker in the Child Welfare Division at Elm City Family Services, where she has been on staff for fourteen years. Her caseload runs eighteen to twenty-two active families and she specializes in family reunification. Her base hours are Monday through Friday, 8:30 AM to 5:00 PM, with occasional evening home visits on Mondays and Wednesdays. Salary is $68,500 a year with state pension benefits through SERS and employer health insurance.

She holds an LCSW license and owes fifteen hours of continuing education a year, due in late January. She is actively considering the clinical supervisor certification, which would mean a raise, a reduced caseload, and a path toward less direct casework. She has compared Baystate and Silver Creek certification programs.

In parallel, she is eighteen months into planning her own dance studio in Hartford. The Notion business plan, the QuickBooks shadow ledger, and the Marcus Savings dream fund at Nutmeg Federal Credit Union are all active. She has toured spaces in Parkville and Frog Hollow and estimated $18,000 in first-year startup costs. No lease signed.
