--- TURN 1 ---

I have been circling the studio decision for eighteen months and I am done circling, so I need the honest version of where this thing actually stands before I let myself walk into a lease conversation and sign something I cannot defend. The dream fund has been sitting quiet and separate while I kept telling myself the numbers were close enough, and I have lost faith that close enough is still true, because I have been keeping my class money in more than one set of books to work out which one I will trust when this goes real, and the two of them have drifted apart in ways I stopped tracking at some point. I want every dollar my dance work has actually brought in pulled into one reconciled picture I can stand behind, the class packs and the drop ins and the privates and the workshops and the ticket money from the shows, with each final figure defended and a clear line on which record you trusted and which one you set aside wherever they disagree. Some of what I have written down for pricing is aspirational and some of it is what people actually handed over, and I genuinely cannot tell you anymore which is which without you running it down against what truly landed, so find where the story I have been telling myself and the money that moved part company and show me.

The private side is its own knot, because I have a long book of one on one bookings stretching back through the year that does not cleanly line up with what I logged as earned, and I need that squared honestly and not hand waved past. Some of those students paid me in cash after class, some ran it through a reader, and a few I know I never wrote up at all because they are friends, or friends of my mother, and I let it slide the way you do. Half of that book lives in a notebook and half of it lives in my head, and the honest total is in a place I have been a little afraid to look. That is exactly the soft spot I need you to be hard about. Once the income is solid, rebuild my monthly model from the floor up, the break even head count and the projected net, on figures that actually survive the reconciliation rather than the ones I penciled in when I was feeling optimistic, because the whole plan leans on a break even I set early and I no longer believe the assumptions holding it up.

Rent is the piece that scares me most, so before you let any break even stand, go pull the real market picture for the two neighborhoods I have been eyeing, dozens of comparable spaces if that is what it takes, and check it hard against the rent I baked into the plan, because if a room like the one I want costs more than I guessed then the whole model tips and I would rather hear that from you than from a landlord across a table. I want a clear eyed read on what the fall showcase actually nets me once the ticket platform and the card processing take their cut, and what that puts toward the reserve I swore I would have before I sign a thing, and I want the gap between where the fund sits and where it has to sit said out loud so I stop flattering myself about the runway. Then pull all of it into a decision I can act on, a straight go or no go with the reasoning laid bare and the risks ranked, the kind of read I could sit with once and know whether I am ready or whether I am still a season short.

Keep the trued up books and the decision as two separate things I can hold, one that shows the real money with where every number came from and one that hands me the recommendation, because I will want to sit alone with the figures before I let any verdict push me one way. There is a showcase note I want drafted and waiting for the class list so folks have the date and the details in front of them, but it stays a draft in my hands until I say go. I have watched people in this scene light themselves on fire by moving too fast, announcing a room before the ink was dry, telling the crowd a thing was real when it was still a maybe, and I am not doing that to myself. Hold the whole thing close. This is my money, my risk, and my name on the door. I have carried it quietly for eighteen months and I can carry it a little further, right up until the picture is finally honest enough to stand on.
