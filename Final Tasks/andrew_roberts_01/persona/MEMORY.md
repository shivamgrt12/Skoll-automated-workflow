# Memory: Andrew Roberts

## Personal Profile

Andrew Roberts is a retired mechanical engineer and the man people instinctively hand the map to. Forty years of figuring out why machines failed left him with a temperament that meets any problem by taking it apart, a method he trusts past its usefulness, and he knows it; he corrects for it unevenly. He runs calm on the surface and busier underneath than he lets on, patient to a fault with others and oddly impatient with himself. What surprises people who peg him as the reserved retired engineer is how curious he stays and how readily he laughs, usually at himself first.

He is Midwestern in the marrow, Chicago-built and Naperville-settled: understated, suspicious of anyone who talks too big, raised in a world where a man's worth was tied to what he could fix and provide. Retirement quietly retired the very thing that framework told him he was for, and he has spent these years renegotiating what a useful life looks like when the work is gone. His working creed is that you are measured by whether the thing actually works when someone leans on it, and he applies that standard to bridges, to friendships, and most ruthlessly to himself. He believes a person should keep learning until they stop breathing, and equally that there is dignity in accepting limits gracefully, and he is living daily inside the gap between those two beliefs.

He holds a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from the Lakeshore Institute of Technology (Chicago, 1977). English is his native language. He is widowed: his wife Carol passed in October 2022 after 44 years of marriage, and the grief is integrated now, permanent rather than acute.

## Work & Projects

- **Career**: After his 1977 degree he spent his early career in design and project engineering at two Chicago-area industrial firms through the late 1970s and 1980s, then joined Prairieview Manufacturing (industrial equipment, Naperville) in 1992. He spent 28 years there, rising to Senior Mechanical Engineer, and retired at 65 in 2020 on good terms.
- **Volunteering**: DuPage County Historical Society, Thursday mornings 9 to 12. He identifies with the unmonumented labor that built a place.
- **Learning**: Reads extensively and takes community courses, including a 6-week writing workshop at the Naperville Public Library that left him privately thrilled and privately mortified.
- **Genealogy**: Considering a family genealogy project tracing the Roberts line, circling it with the wariness of a man who suspects it could become consuming.

## Health & Wellness

- **Gout**: Diagnosed 2019. Managed with allopurinol 300 mg each morning. Flares 3 to 4 times a year, usually from dietary lapses, dehydration, or stress, treated at onset with colchicine 0.6 mg plus NSAIDs. Last flare February 2026 (right big toe, 5 days). Dr. Singh monitors uric acid quarterly.
- **Prediabetes**: Diagnosed 2024 (A1c 5.8%). Managed with diet and exercise, no medication. A1c checked every 6 months; last check October 2026 was 5.7% (improved), with Dr. Ivers saying to stay the course.
- **Mild osteoarthritis**: Both knees. Managed with activity modification and occasional acetaminophen; no running.
- **Providers**: Dr. Robert Ivers (primary care, every 6 months, next April 2027); Dr. Anita Singh (rheumatology, every 4 months); Dr. Helen Park (dentist, Naperville Dental Care, every 6 months, next April 2027).
- **Medications and supplements**: Allopurinol 300 mg daily, colchicine 0.6 mg as needed, acetaminophen 500 mg as needed, Vitamin D3 2000 IU daily, omega-3 fish oil 1000 mg daily, and a daily multivitamin.

## Preferences

- **Food and dining**: Competent but basic cooking (good scrambled eggs, reasonable chicken, decent pasta); Carol was the cook and he has been learning since. Favorite spots are The Station Grill (grilled chicken sandwich), Pho Viet (#3 chicken pho), and Il Forno (the date spot with Janet, grilled fish special). One cup of black coffee each morning, cut from three after the prediabetes diagnosis, which he misses. Snacks on almonds, celery and hummus, cheese in moderation, and a little dark chocolate. An occasional glass of red wine with Janet, no beer (a gout trigger) and no spirits.
- **Reading and listening**: History, biography, and literary fiction, 2 to 3 books a month across print and Audible, plus history podcasts on walks.
- **Style**: Quiet, durable, particular. Well-kept oxford shirts, a few good sweaters, shoes resoled rather than replaced; he would rather own three good things than a drawer of disposable ones.
- **Shopping**: Researches before buying, repairs before replacing, prefers the local hardware store and the actual conversation. Constitutionally incapable of an impulse purchase for himself, but spends freely and almost guiltily on the grandkids and on quietly picking up the check.
- **Travel**: Modest ambitions, unhurried and close to the bone. Would like to take Janet somewhere eventually, perhaps Door County in summer or a train through the Southwest, held loosely rather than pinned to a date.
- **Sensory world**: Keyed to texture and sound; the smell of machine oil at the workbench, the layered ticking of clocks he has revived, radio voices low in the kitchen, and the bite of cold morning air on the early walk.
