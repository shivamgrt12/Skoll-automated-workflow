# Memory: Carlos Whitfield

## Personal Profile

Carlos grew up in a working-class, mixed Sacramento neighborhood where Spanish drifting over the back fences became a second language he half-owns: enough for warmth and basic conversation, not enough to read anything official. He carries the dignity-of-labor ethic of that world without sentimentalizing it. Respect is earned by what you do and how you treat people; titles count for little. That value system traveled intact through a long mechanical-engineering career into a Pacific Northwest retirement.

He is a churchgoer the way many men of his generation are, present more than fervent. His marriage to June, who came from a close Mexican-American family (the Garcias), blended traditions he never fully sorted into "his" and "hers." Grief taught him that meaning is mostly made in the ordinary keeping-on.

- **Education**: B.S. Mechanical Engineering, Willamette Polytechnic Institute, Salem, OR (1977). A.A.S. Drafting Technology, Valley Technical College, Sacramento (1975).
- **Languages**: English (native). Conversational Spanish, picked up from the Garcias.
- **Marital status**: Widowed since November 14, 2023.
- **Military service**: None. Vietnam-era draft lottery (1972) did not call his number; student deferment 1973-1977. Not a veteran, not eligible for VA benefits.
- **Faith**: First Presbyterian Beaverton, member since 1997. Pastor Rev. Ellen Marston. Once a month or so.
- **End-of-life designations** (updated June 2024 with Cedar Hills Estate Law):
  - **ICE**: Karen Whitfield-Park (daughter). Alternate: Kevin Whitfield (son).
