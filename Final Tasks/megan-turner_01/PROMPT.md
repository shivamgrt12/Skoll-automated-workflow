--- TURN 1 ---
I want my whole year of money trued up and sitting in one clean picture I can hand the preparer without apologizing for it, because I kept books for fifteen years and I will not put my name to a figure I cannot stand behind. I want every last line the bank has on me for the full twelve months run down, not a spot check but the whole of it, and sorted the way a return actually wants it, the property tax and the oil heat and the insurance and the groceries and the medicine and the small gifts and the giving, so that what went out sits under its right category and nothing is left in a lump I have to explain to a stranger later. The fixed money that comes in every month I thought I knew cold, right down to the dollar, so check the deposits that actually arrived against what I carry in my head, because I have a suspicion the amount did not hold steady clean across the twelve months and I would rather find the step myself than miss it and hand the preparer a total that is quietly wrong. The giving is the piece I care most about getting right, the church envelope and the online gifts and the library fund, and I want every dollar of it defended with something I could put my finger on, because that is the line the preparer leans on and the one I would be mortified to overstate. Pull the giving together from wherever it actually cleared and set it against the account, and where the two do not agree do not just take the friendlier number, run it down until you know which one is telling the truth and can tell me why, because some of these figures are stale from receipts I filed months apart and I have lost my confidence that they still line up with what came out of the bank. The medicine is the other heavy one, the pharmacy and the mail-order refills and the copays and the Medicare and the supplement premiums, and I want the out-of-pocket totaled honestly across all of it, the same care, one source held up against another before you trust it, and a plain note wherever a receipt and the bank charge for the same fill disagree so that I can decide it rather than guess. The small retirement account has its quarterly paper and a year-end summary, and I want the interest and the dividends off it captured for the preparer as well, and if a quarter statement and the year-end summary tell me different stories I want to know which one governs and be able to explain the gap between them. With all of it gathered, give me a picture I can actually act on, income by source, spending by category, the giving total and the medical total each carried on its own with the provenance sitting right behind it, and beside that a short honest tally of everything that would not reconcile and how you settled each one, the way I used to keep the exceptions page at the hardware store so the owner always knew where the soft spots were. I also want to know, plainly, whether the cushion I think I have is really there once the actuals are in, whether the little surplus I assume each month held up over the year, and whether the safety money is still whole, because I run a careful house and I would like the truth of it and not the comfortable story I tell myself. Now the fences, and I mean them. This is a reading job and not a moving job, so you work from what is already in front of you and you change nothing, because the doing of it stays in my own hands. If you turn up a bill I have let slip, flag it for me. If a transfer to one of the grandchildren belongs on the list, it counts like any other line and I want it in the picture. Anything you draft for me, a question for David or a cover note for the preparer, stays a draft until I have read it with my own eyes and said the word out loud. What I share and with whom is my call alone, so you put none of this in front of anyone until I have said so plainly and by name. Where the paper is thin or a total simply will not settle, I would far rather you tell me it is open and say what is missing than force a tidy number to make the page look finished, because a wrong figure that looks certain is worse to me than an honest gap I can go chase. I have a good eye for this and I will be checking behind you, so I want each account and each pile of receipts given its due and the whole of it brought into one honest picture I can stand behind.
