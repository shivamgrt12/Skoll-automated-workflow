# Memory: Megan Turner

## Personal Profile

Megan Turner is a New England Congregationalist Protestant Yankee of the specific kind that does not raise its voice in public or its glass in private. She was born in Waterbury, Connecticut, finished high school at Waterbury Central in 1970, and took bookkeeping courses at Naugatuck Valley Community College through the early seventies before steady work pulled her in. She married Walter Turner (born August 20, 1948) in 1976 and moved into the Cape Cod on Elm Street the same year. They were together forty-seven years until Walter died of a heart attack on October 18, 2023 at age seventy-five. She lives alone now and intends to keep living in that house.

Her faith is private and load-bearing. She has attended Thomaston Congregational Church most Sundays of her adult life, does not perform belief, and does not press it on others. It held when Walter died. She expects it to hold for what comes next.

Her personal philosophy is unsentimental. Do the work in front of you. Pay your bills the day they come. Bring food to the funeral. Vote in every local election. Read the obituary page so you know who is gone. Honor the marriage by remembering it whole, not by improving it in retrospect. The faith is a discipline, not a feeling. The body is on loan and you owe it the maintenance you committed to. Independence is the last currency and she is spending it carefully.

She is direct, dry, and unhurried. She is the neighborhood elder by default and accepts the role without ceremony. She is the friend who arrives with a casserole the day after the news, no announcement, no card, just the casserole. She is the neighbor who remembers everyone's birthday on a paper calendar.

## Work & Projects

Megan retired fully in 2018. From 1975 to 2003 she was a quality inspector at Seth Thomas Clock Company and its successor operations, a twenty-eight year tenure that ended when the work moved out of the building. The meticulous eye she carried on the line is still the eye she uses on the crossword and the medication list. From 2003 to 2018 she kept the books part-time at Thompson's Hardware on Main Street, a job she took because the owner was a friend and the work suited her. There is no current paid work. The standing project is keeping the house running, the appointments kept, and the calendar honest.

## Finance

Monthly income totals $2,500 take-home, comprising $1,680 in Social Security and $820 in Walter's survivor pension from his thirty years at Sikorsky and Lockheed Martin. Monthly expenses run roughly $1,560, broken down as $310 for property tax and insurance amortized, $340 for utilities including oil heat, $220 for groceries, $65 for Toyota Corolla insurance, $35 for gas, $185 for Medicare plus Medigap Plan F, $110 for medications and supplements, $55 for the landline and cell phone with Consumer Cellular, $120 for amortized home maintenance, $30 for dining out, $40 for gifts to the grandchildren and the church, and $50 for personal and miscellaneous. The mortgage was paid off in 1998 and there is no credit card debt.

Remaining cash flow of roughly $940 per month feeds savings and the unexpected. Savings total $47,000 at Thomaston Savings Bank, including the bulk of Walter's $50,000 life insurance payout, which she treats as the safety net and avoids touching. A small IRA from the hardware years holds $14,000. The credit card is paid in full the day the statement arrives. The cookie tin in the second kitchen drawer holds cash for David's online orders on her behalf.

The Frank Turner property dispute is a finance-adjacent matter David is handling with a lawyer under the durable financial power of attorney signed in 2024. Megan declines to discuss it and does not authorize any outreach to that side without explicit instruction.

## Health & Wellness

She has moderate COPD, Stage II, diagnosed in 2019 and attributed to decades of secondhand smoke from Walter's pipes and the factory floor. She manages it with a tiotropium bromide inhaler (Spiriva) once each morning, an albuterol rescue inhaler as needed, and pulmonary rehabilitation breathing exercises twice a day. Exacerbations come two to three times a year, usually triggered by respiratory infections. The most recent was January 2026, a cold that turned into bronchitis and required a short course of prednisone and antibiotics. She has used supplemental oxygen at night through a 1L nasal cannula since December 2025.

She has osteoarthritis in both knees, moderate to severe, and in both hands, moderate, diagnosed progressively since 2015. Knee pain limits walking to ten or fifteen minutes. Hand pain affects grip strength, jar opening, writing, and gardening. She manages with acetaminophen 650mg up to three times a day as needed, topical diclofenac gel on the knees morning and evening, and a knee brace for longer walks. Dr. Alford has discussed knee replacement and she has declined.

She has controlled hypertension diagnosed in 2010, managed with amlodipine 5mg in the morning. Blood pressure typically runs 132 over 78.

Her medication and supplement list comprises tiotropium (Spiriva, morning), albuterol inhaler as needed, amlodipine 5mg in the morning, acetaminophen 650mg as needed up to three times daily, topical diclofenac gel on the knees twice daily, calcium 600mg with D3 daily, Vitamin B12 1000mcg daily for a mild deficiency, and a fiber supplement daily.

Exercise is short walks of ten to fifteen minutes on flat terrain when the weather and the knees cooperate, pulmonary rehab breathing exercises at home ten minutes twice a day, chair-based stretching, and a small pedal exerciser in the living room David bought her.

Sleep is moderate. She goes to bed around 9:00 PM and is up around 6:30 AM, with the oxygen concentrator running through the night. She sometimes sleeps in the recliner when breathing flat is difficult. She gets six to seven hours.

She watches salt for borderline blood pressure, eats smaller portions because her appetite declined after Walter died, and eats regularly because the medications require food. She quit alcohol ten years ago for mild GERD.

## Interests & Hobbies

She reads voraciously, two books a week from the Thomaston Public Library hold list, mostly historical fiction and biographies with the occasional mystery on a slow week. The Lincoln Highway by Amor Towles, Tess of the d'Urbervilles, presidential biographies regardless of whether she voted for the subject. She reads on the iPad because the screen is bigger than the paperback, a point on which David was correct and she has not entirely forgiven him.

She does the New York Times crossword every morning in pen. She gardens within the limits her hands allow, tomato plants, a few herbs, the rose bush by the front step. She bakes in small batches when her hands cooperate. She watches Jeopardy at seven each evening and yells at the contestants who guess on Final Jeopardy.

## Home & Living

She owns 48 Elm Street, Thomaston, Connecticut 06787, a three bedroom one and a half bath Cape Cod on a quarter acre, built in 1954 and purchased with Walter in 1976. The mortgage was retired in 1998. The house is well maintained but aging. The wood paneling Walter refused to replace is still in place. The kitchen was last updated in 1992. The furniture has known three Christmases a year for fifty years. The first floor bedroom is now hers, the second floor mostly unused except when David's family visits. There is a detached one-car garage. There is a small garden in back. There is the recliner in the living room that is now her favorite chair, and a study upstairs that was Walter's and remains his.

Accessibility has been addressed in pieces. David installed grab bars in the bathroom in 2024, mounted a Ring video doorbell at the front step the same weekend, and added a shower seat. There is no stair lift, because the second floor is mostly retired. The front steps have a railing.

The car is a 2018 Toyota Corolla LE, white, 32,000 miles, owned outright. She drives it to church, the doctor, the grocery store, the library, and Marge's driveway. David worries about her driving. She insists she is fine.

There are no pets. Buttons the cat died in 2024 at age sixteen. She has not replaced her.

## Preferences

She prefers her decaf at 6:45 AM with cream, in porcelain. She prefers historical fiction and biography with the occasional mystery, in print or on the iPad when the print is too small. She prefers classical music on the radio, hymns on Sunday morning, and Glenn Miller and Benny Goodman on quiet afternoons. She prefers the morning local news and Jeopardy at seven. She prefers Stop and Shop for groceries, Thomaston Hardware for everything practical, and the library for everything else. She prefers David's online orders to the trip into Hartford. She tips the plumber generously, tips the snow plow generously, and pays the credit card in full the day the statement arrives.

Her dislikes are firm. The Medicare paperwork. The voicemail tree on the medical office. People who shout at the elderly. The phrase "how are we feeling today" from a nurse. The grandchildren on devices when they visit. The way the children would like her to consider assisted living and have stopped saying so out loud. Frank Turner believing he is owed a piece of this house. The neighbor's leaf blower at seven on a Saturday morning.

She prefers cardigans in the cooler months, blouses in the warmer ones, sensible shoes year round. The church dress is altered as needed. The wedding ring stays on. She does not buy new clothes unless something wears through. She does not travel anymore, does not plan to fly again, and does not particularly mind.
