--- TURN 1 ---
Our seventy-fifth anniversary at Greater Hope is bearing down on us in December, and I have been carrying too much of this in my head to keep doing so alone. Pastor Gaines put me on the committee for the setup work I know how to do, and the folks running the commemorative book project quietly handed me the storefronts after none of them could tell me straight what we had actually sold, so between the two hats I am the one who has to know where every piece of this stands before the next committee call. What I need from you is an honest, defensible picture of the whole affair from where it sits right now, walked all the way through to a set of things I can put in front of Pastor and the committee without hedging or hand-waving, and I want it done with the same care I would give a communion tray. Begin with the commemorative book, because that is what has been costing me sleep. We have the same title running through more than one storefront the committee stood up, and every one of them has been touched by different people at different points with different numbers, so the counts on hand, the fulfilled orders, and the money that actually cleared do not agree with each other and have not agreed for a good while. I need a real running total for what has been sold across the whole run, what has been paid for, what is still sitting on the shelf in the fellowship hall closet, and where any stray copies have wandered off to, and I want the write-up to name the source you trusted for each figure and the source you set aside, because I would rather explain a defended number than defend a tidy one. From there I want the committee list of members, elders, and the wider congregation walked end to end and cleaned up, because our last mailing attempt bounced back on a couple of hundred addresses and Sister Franklin's granddaughter had to spend the better part of one evening resetting our sending standing for us, and I refuse to walk into that again. A draft of the anniversary letter itself needs to sit ready for me to read, written in the register the congregation actually uses, warm enough for the elders and honest enough for the younger families who have watched us struggle. I want the pastoral pieces of it left to Pastor Gaines, and the letter should read like a deacon wrote it and not like a marketing pitch. The anniversary landing page and the weekly announcement post need to be trued up against what the committee last approved, because I have caught more than one place where the service order and the guest preacher lineup on the page disagree with the printed program that already went to the elders' committee, and I want you to tell me which one is right, why, and what has to change to make the whole thing sing the same tune. The online giving that has been coming in for the anniversary fund needs to be reconciled against what the church books show has been deposited and what the committee treasurer thinks we have raised, because I know some of the weekly totals in one place have not been posted in the other for a couple of pay cycles running, and I want a clean number I can set next to the anniversary budget line and say we are here, we need this much more, and here is where the shortfall is likely to come from. While you are in the neighborhood, the guest preacher recordings we have been putting up for the members who cannot make it in person need the titles fixed and the missing services filled in, because I have been telling folks for months they can find their favorite visiting minister up there and half the times I check the title is a placeholder or the file is not tagged with a date they can find. The planning committee board and the running document the committee has been keeping in parallel disagree on who owns what for setup, and since I am the one who unlocks the building, sets the communion, and stages the commemorative-book table, I need one clean assignment sheet that names every task, the deacon or committee member on it, the time it happens, and the room it happens in, so nobody stands around waiting for me at seven in the morning on the day itself. Underneath all of that I need you to keep the ordinary business of the day held tight, because Miss Eunice is still expecting me at her door at the same time she always is, and Dorothy has already told me the family is coming for the meal after service like they do each week and she wants a headcount well before she starts the greens so she knows how much to put on. If a signage proof, a survey answer, an RSVP roster, or a letter of agreement is sitting somewhere waiting on me, surface it for my review and get the ones that need a reply drafted up so I can read them side by side and sign what I can sign, but nothing goes out and nothing gets committed without my say-so, because I have not made it seventy years signing my name to things I have not read. Where the numbers do not add up, tell me plainly, name what you trusted and what you set aside, and hold an open question rather than force a tidy answer, because Pastor will ask me and I will not lie to him.
