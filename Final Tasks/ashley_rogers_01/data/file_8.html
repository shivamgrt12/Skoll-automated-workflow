Thirty one years at the wheel, and still on the road

Retired bus operator. Church deacon. Food bank hand. Chess partner. A short page about the work I still do and the people I do it for.

About

I drove the 53 Woodward line for thirty one years. You learn a city from the front of a bus, and you learn the people more. Retirement did not mean stopping. It meant choosing where the hours go. They go to the church, the food bank, the table, and the family.

What I do now

DeaconFood bankChess table

Deacon, Greater Hope Baptist

Open the building Sunday morning, set communion, read the sick list, and keep the visitation rotation moving. I drive an elder to worship every Sunday because she cannot get there on her own.

Food bank, three mornings a week

Stocking, packing, and a delivery run to seniors who cannot come in. I help close out the till at the fundraiser and keep the donor list current.

Chess and dominoes

Fifteen years at the community center, two afternoons a week. I do not let anyone win. I bring my grandson when he can come, and I listen more than I talk.

The road, in years

1974
Finished high school in Detroit. First steady jobs, learned a city by walking it.
1978
Earned the commercial license. First morning on the 53 Woodward line, in the dark, hands shaking.
2009
Retired after 31 years at the wheel. Same line, same city, a lot of regulars.
2008 to today
Deacon at Greater Hope Baptist. Setup, communion, visitation, and the Sunday ride for an elder.
today
Three mornings a week at the food bank, two afternoons at the chess table, and a garden out back.
