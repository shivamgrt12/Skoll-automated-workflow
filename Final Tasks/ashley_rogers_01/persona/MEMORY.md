# Memory: Ashley Rogers

## Personal Profile

Ashley is a retired DDOT bus driver and long-serving Baptist deacon living in the Brightmoor neighborhood of northwest Detroit with his wife Dorothy of 46 years. He was born and raised in Detroit and has watched the city burn, rebuild, and burn again across seven decades, and his pride in it is earned and unsentimental.

He holds a high school diploma from Chadsey High School (1974), a Commercial Driver's License issued by the Michigan Secretary of State (1978), and various Greater Hope Baptist deacon-training certificates. He speaks English natively. He is American, with deep east-side roots and four generations of family on the same side of town.

His faith is the floor under everything, not the ceiling. He has served three pastors over the course of his deacon tenure; Pastor Raymond Gaines is the one he respects most. He opens the building Sunday mornings, sets up communion, prays over the sick list, and drives Miss Eunice Palmer to and from service because her macular degeneration has taken her driving. His faith is structural and unperformed; he does not need anyone to share it, and he does not mock anyone who does not.

He carries the weight of community concern: food insecurity in Brightmoor, the grandkids' safety as the older ones grow into adolescence, Dorothy's declining mobility, the food bank's funding gap. He processes through faith, his 6:30 AM walks, his Tuesday and Thursday chess sessions with Clarence, and on rare occasions his sons-in-law. He has never considered therapy and probably will not.

He stopped drinking 35 years ago. He does not explain or moralize about it. He keeps stories like architecture: each one carries a lesson, a warning, or a piece of grace he intends to hand down. If you interrupt one, he will start again at the beginning.
