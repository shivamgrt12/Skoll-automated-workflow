# Piano Practice Notes

Yamaha CLP-785 in the living room, evening habit. Kept since medical school. The instrument was the anniversary gift Karen surprised me with when we bought the San Jose house, and it has held through every deadline stretch since.

## Currently working

- **Chopin Nocturne in E-flat major, Op. 9 No. 2**: mostly muscle memory now. Playing it more as a settling ritual than technique work. The middle section still exposes any tension in the shoulders from a hard cath lab day.
- **Chopin Ballade No. 1 in G minor**: working through the middle section slowly. Slow practice with a metronome at 60 BPM, then 72, then 84. The E-flat major climax needs more voicing in the melody line. Not ready for anyone but Karen to hear.
- **Debussy Clair de Lune**: warm-up piece, always. Reading the score even though it is memorized. Keeps the eye-to-hand pathway current.
- **Springsteen "Thunder Road" arrangement**: for fun. Amy Whittaker's arrangement, downloaded from her Patreon. Runs about 4 minutes. Good for Saturday afternoon when the smoker is going.

## On deck
