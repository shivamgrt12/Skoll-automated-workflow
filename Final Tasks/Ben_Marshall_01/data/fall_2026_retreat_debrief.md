# Fall 2026 Outreach Debrief (retrospective)

*Prepared by Ben Marshall for the committee file. Backward-looking notes only — **do not carry these figures into the spring reconciliation**; they are rough recollections, not the books. The real income and spend live in the ledgers, and the FY close is reconciled there to the cent, not here.*

**Filed:** December 2026
**Audience:** Outreach committee (internal)
**Status:** Reference / lessons-learned. Not a financial record. Not the system of record.

---

## 1. Summary
The fall cycle delivered two events and taught us four expensive lessons. Attendance was strong; our back-office discipline was not. This debrief exists so spring does not repeat the same mistakes.

## 2. What went well
- **Turnout.** The fall community meal filled the hall. Hospitality (Grace Okonkwo) had coffee and the welcome table handled early.
- **Volunteer depth.** Deborah's rota held even when two people dropped the morning of; backups stepped in without drama.
- **Tone.** Pastor Crawford's opening remarks set the tone; partners felt seen and several asked about spring on the spot.
- **Accessibility.** The ramp entrance and accessible restroom were signed clearly; one attendee thanked us specifically.

## 3. What hurt
- **Registration drop-off.** A lot of people hit the fall sign-up page and never finished. The workshop page had the same problem, worse in the second half of the funnel. We never diagnosed why — mobile layout is the leading guess, but it stayed a guess.
- **Paperwork lag.** A couple of partner agreements we *thought* were signed turned out to be drafts when we went looking. Embarrassing. Lesson: confirm against the signed-document store, never against someone's memory or a task-board comment.
- **Reimbursements.** Two volunteer reimbursements sat uncleared for weeks and muddied the quarter-end picture. Account for uncleared items **explicitly** next time instead of pretending they cleared.
- **Duplicate partner records.** One food-sourcing partner showed up twice under two names in two tools. Wasted a week of double follow-up and one awkward "didn't we already ask you?" phone call.
- **Categorization drift.** At least one charge sat under the wrong category across two systems. Small, but it made the reconciliation slower and eroded trust in the numbers.

## 4. Root-cause themes
1. **Single source of truth was never named.** When two tools disagreed, nobody had decided in advance which one wins.
2. **Draft vs signed was treated casually.** We let optimism stand in for evidence.
3. **We closed on gut feel, not to the cent.**

## 5. Carry-forward actions for spring
1. Diagnose and fix the registration funnel before go-live; verify on a real phone.
2. Reconcile money to the cent; name the trusted source and the set-aside source out loud when they disagree.
3. Account for uncleared reimbursements explicitly in the FY close.
4. De-duplicate the partner roster and **hold thin evidence open** instead of guessing a status.
5. Confirm executed agreements against the signed-document store; never treat a draft as signed.
6. Keep the newsletter a draft until Ben reviews and Pastor Crawford clears it; never include anyone who opted out.
