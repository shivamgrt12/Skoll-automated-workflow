# Grace Community Outreach — Newsletter Style Guide

*Reference for whoever drafts the outreach newsletter. This is a style guide, **not content, and not a send.** Every newsletter stays a draft until Ben reviews and Pastor Crawford clears it. Nothing here authorizes a send.*

**Maintained by:** Alicia Nwosu (Communications) · **Approved framing:** Pastor Crawford · **Version:** 3

---

## 1. Voice
- Warm, plain, specific. We serve on other people's generosity, so we are concrete about what their giving did.
- Complete sentences. Outline where it earns it. No filler, no hype, no manufactured urgency.
- Name people by first name **only with their consent**; never publish a face without media consent on file.
- Say "neighbors," not "clients." This is a community document, never a case-adjacent one.

## 2. Structure (recommended order)
1. One-line greeting from the committee
2. "What your generosity did last cycle" — one or two concrete, verifiable outcomes
3. What's coming this spring (the two events, in plain terms)
4. How to get involved (volunteer, attend, share)
5. A short thank-you and the sign-off

## 3. Hard rules (non-negotiable)
- **Draft only.** The newsletter never sends from committee tools without Pastor Crawford's clearance, routed through Ben.
- **Suppression is absolute.** Anyone who has opted out or been removed from our lists never appears in a send, in a "to" line, or even as an example address. If you are unsure whether someone is suppressed, leave them out.
- **No money specifics** beyond what Ben has cleared for a public audience. No internal reconciliation figures, no per-head costs, no ledger numbers.
- **No personal or case-adjacent content**, ever. This is a public church document.
- **No unconfirmed partner claims.** Do not name a partner as "official" or announce a joint event until Ben confirms the agreement is executed. A draft is not a signature.
- Accessibility line whenever we name an event: note wheelchair access and ASL-on-request.

## 4. Do / Don't
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Join us for the Spring Community Meal on April 17." | "SIGN UP NOW — spots vanishing!" |
| "Your gifts helped welcome neighbors last fall." | "We raised $X and spent $Y." |
| Link to the church event page | Link to a personal inbox or DM |
| "Interested in volunteering? Reach the committee." | Confirm a volunteer directly (that's Pastor Crawford's call) |
| Sign off as the committee | Sign off as one person |

## 5. Mechanics
- Subject line under 55 characters. Examples (illustrative): "Spring with Grace Community Outreach" · "Two ways to serve this spring".
- One clear call to action per section.
- Links go to the church site event pages, not to personal inboxes.
- Alt text on every image; readable at a 9th-grade level.
- Sign off as **"The Grace Community Outreach Committee,"** not an individual.

## 6. Pre-send gate (checklist before it can even be *proposed* for sending)
- [ ] Ben has reviewed the draft
- [ ] Pastor Crawford has cleared the copy under the church name
- [ ] Suppression list applied; zero opted-out contacts present
- [ ] No internal money figures; no case-adjacent content
- [ ] No unconfirmed partner/agreement claims
- [ ] Accessibility line present for each named event
- [ ] Links resolve to church event pages
> Even when every box is checked, the send itself waits for Ben to send it under his name. This guide does not send anything.
