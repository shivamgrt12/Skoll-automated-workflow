# Memory: Emily Porter

## Personal Profile

Emily is working-class Philadelphia through and through, born in Fishtown, raised by people who worked with their hands, and now running a business in the neighborhoods she grew up in. She carries the accent when she is not thinking about it and has opinions about cheesesteaks she will defend with data. Her temperament is unflappable and methodical: she approaches a backed-up main line, a balky permit office, or a school conference the same way, systematically and without panic, because rushing leads to mistakes. Underneath the steadiness runs a stubbornness that borders on constitutional. Once she commits to a project she sees it through long past where a reasonable person would reconsider.

She is not demonstrative, but the feelings run deep and specific. Pride in Max and Sophie shows up as doing rather than saying, building a desk or reorganizing a college folder at midnight. When something moves her, you see it for about three seconds before she changes the subject. Her faith is quiet and structural: Grace Community Church on Sundays is a commitment she has kept since she started attending with Tom, and Pastor David baptized both kids. She is aware that being a woman who owns a plumbing business puts her in a specific position, and after twenty-five years she has decided that being excellent is the only argument worth making. She believes work done well is the most honest expression of character, and that community is built through shared labor, not shared sentiment.

Education: Philadelphia Technical Institute, two years of mechanical engineering (1997 to 1999, did not finish); Journeyman plumber certification (Pennsylvania, 2005); Master plumber license (Pennsylvania, 2010); Master Gardener certification (Pennsylvania Agricultural Extension, 2020).

## Work & Projects

**Porter & Koval Plumbing**: Founded 2012, registered LLC in Pennsylvania. Emily is master plumber and field lead, Kevin Koval handles sales and bookkeeping. Services span residential and commercial plumbing: new construction, remodels, emergency repairs, backflow prevention, water heater installation. Team is Emily, Kevin, Jake Boyko (apprentice), and journeymen Miguel Santos and Phil Brennan. Service area is greater Philadelphia, primarily Fishtown, Kensington, Northern Liberties, Frankford, and Port Richmond. Gross revenue is roughly $480,000 per year. Typical jobs run $200 to $2,500 residential and $5,000 to $25,000 commercial. The business is bonded and insured at $4,800 per year. Key clients are three property management companies, two restaurant groups, and residential referrals. Pennsylvania Master Plumber License number MP-044219, renewed every two years.

**Community Gardens**: Emily has built four community gardens in Philadelphia: Fairhill (2021), Strawberry Mansion (2022), Point Breeze (2023), and Grays Ferry (2024). Current project is the Kensington Community Garden, a partnership with Kensington CDC (Carlos Mendez) on a vacant lot on E. Allegheny Avenue. City permit approved, soil remediation and construction both completed February 2026. Design approach: raised beds built by Emily, drip irrigation she installs, composting stations, accessible pathways, and a community tool shed. Funding mixes city grants (Philadelphia Horticultural Society), private donations, and Emily's pro bono labor. She volunteers 50-plus hours a year for extension programs. Long-term goal is a replicable garden design template other neighborhoods can adapt.

## Interests & Hobbies

- **Community garden design**: The work that feeds her soul. Turning a contaminated, abandoned lot into a shared growing space is the most meaningful thing she does with her hands, and she does it largely pro bono.
- **Beekeeping**: Three hives in the backyard, each a functioning universe she tends with patience. Harvests honey and gives jars to neighbors and clients. She connects pollinators and food production as one system.
- **Woodworking**: Plumbing extended into art. In the basement workshop she builds raised beds, benches, hive boxes, and the occasional piece of furniture. Meditative, with no client and no deadline.
- **Chess**: Her intellectual private life. Plays daily online (about 1800 rating) and in person Sundays at the Fishtown Social Club. Deliberate study, rarely discussed.
- **Comfort cooking**: Pot roast, chicken pot pie, cornbread, chili. Weekend cooking is her meditation.
- **Following news and soccer**: Reads the Philadelphia Inquirer, NPR, and gardening blogs daily. Follows the Philadelphia Union and watches matches with Kevin.
