# Porter & Koval Plumbing LLC - New Hire Onboarding

**Revision:** October 2026  
**Author:** Emily Porter, Owner  
**Applies to:** All new field and office hires  
**Shop address:** 1842 East Susquehanna Avenue, Fishtown, Philadelphia PA 19125

---

## Pre-Start (before Day 1)

- [ ] Offer letter signed and filed
- [ ] Background check cleared through BambooHR (Kevin initiates)
- [ ] Drug screening completed at LabCorp Frankford Avenue
- [ ] Order uniform shirts (2 navy polos, 1 winter fleece) from Independence Sign Works
- [ ] Provision company email at Finthesiss.ai domain
- [ ] Create Slack account and add to #field-ops, #general, and #safety channels
- [ ] Add to Airtable job-tracking workspace as viewer
- [ ] Prepare tool belt and basic hand-tool kit from truck inventory

---

## Week 1 — Orientation and Shop Tour

- [ ] Complete W-4 and I-9 with Emily on Day 1
- [ ] Review employee handbook cover to cover (see employee_handbook.pdf)
- [ ] Tour Fishtown shop: tool room, parts shelves, truck bay, conference table, break area
- [ ] Walk the truck inventory with Kevin (see porter_koval_truck_inventory_2026-10.xlsx)
- [ ] Shadow Kevin on a residential service call — observe customer greeting, job scoping, and invoice handoff
- [ ] Get fitted for PPE: safety glasses, hard hat, steel-toe boots, nitrile gloves, hearing protection
- [ ] Set up company email and confirm Slack access works on personal phone
- [ ] Review the safety data sheets index for the top 10 chemicals in the shop (see safety_data_sheets_index.xlsx)

## Week 2 — Safety and Technical Foundations

- [ ] Complete OSHA 10 online module — must finish by end of Day 10
- [ ] Ride along on two commercial jobs: one drain, one fixture install
- [ ] Review lead-safe work practices (EPA RRP Rule) — Emily walks through the protocol on site
- [ ] Learn the QuickBooks invoice workflow: Emily demos how to create, send, and follow up on an invoice
- [ ] Practice copper sweat soldering on the test bench in the shop — Kevin evaluates joint quality
- [ ] Review PA plumbing code chapters 1-4 (residential provisions)
- [ ] Tour one active job site and identify the permit board, shutoff locations, and waste containment setup

## Week 3 — Supervised Field Work

- [ ] First solo residential drain clearing under Kevin phone supervision
- [ ] Complete first aid and CPR certification at Red Cross Frankford (2-hour class, shop pays)
- [ ] Learn the Airtable job tracking system: how to update status, log hours, and attach photos
- [ ] Review the safety data sheets index in detail — quiz from Kevin on correct PPE per chemical
- [ ] Attend the monthly crew safety meeting (first Monday of the month at 07:00)
- [ ] Practice water heater flush and anode rod inspection on the shop demo unit
- [ ] Review the emergency callout rate schedule (see porter_koval_emergency_callout_rates_2026.json)

## Week 4 — Increasing Independence

- [ ] Solo on two residential calls with phone check-in to Emily or Kevin after each
- [ ] Review customer communication standards: tone, follow-up, complaint escalation
- [ ] Complete the 30-day evaluation form with Emily — covers safety awareness, technical skills, customer manner
- [ ] Set 90-day skill development goals with Kevin as sponsor
- [ ] Learn the Intercom customer lookup workflow for finding job history on return calls
- [ ] If apprentice track: confirm PA apprentice registration paperwork with the state board

---

## Required Documents (within 30 days of start)

- [ ] Valid PA driver license (CDL not required but preferred for the Transit van)
- [ ] Social Security card or passport for I-9
- [ ] OSHA 10 completion card
- [ ] First aid / CPR card
- [ ] Signed acknowledgment page from employee handbook
- [ ] Signed drug-free workplace agreement
- [ ] Emergency contact form filed with Emily

---

## 90-Day Checkpoint

At the 90-day mark Emily and Kevin meet with the new hire for a formal review. Topics include:

1. Field readiness — can the hire run a standard residential call solo?
2. Safety compliance — any near-misses or safety concerns logged?
3. Customer feedback — any survey results or complaints?
4. Tool and truck responsibility — is the assigned truck kit complete and maintained?
5. Next training milestone — what skill station is next on the apprentice curriculum?
