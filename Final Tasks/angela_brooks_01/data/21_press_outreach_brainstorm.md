# Press outreach - brainstorm (unstructured)

I started a press contact list weeks ago and have not touched it. It is probably full of stale
emails and people who are ALSO already on the Lantern Tides mailing list, which would be
embarrassing to double-contact. Clean it, dedupe it against the mailing list, then draft a plan.
None of this is a send list yet - it is raw thinking.

## Ideas for segments
- Segment A: local Seattle indie/press who cover student showcases. These are the ones who might
  actually walk the Showcase floor, so a short "come find booth 47" note fits them.
- Segment B: narrative-game / cozy-game newsletters. Lantern Tides is a slow, warm,
  magical-realism story game, so the cozy/narrative angle is the honest pitch here.
- Segment C: podcasters who did jam coverage before. Lower priority, longer lead time.
- Segment D: personal contacts from the 2025 jam (warm intros only). These are people who already
  know me - no cold-pitch energy, just a friendly heads-up.

## The dedupe problem
The mailing list already has ~121 subscribers, and I know some press folks are on it. If I blast
outreach without cross-checking, I will land in someone's inbox twice looking like I did not do my
homework. So step one is always: reconcile the press list against the mailing list export and pull
the overlaps out of the cold-outreach batch.

One name I keep flip-flopping on: Ren Kobayashi (Indiepocalypse). I am pretty sure Ren is on the
mailing list already AND in my press notes - possibly listed twice in the press export itself - so
that is exactly the kind of duplicate I need caught before anything drafts.

## Cadence thinking (rough, not final)
Stage it in small batches across the run-up to the Showcase, most-relevant segments first, nothing
same-day. Every row stays "staged / pending" until I say go.

## Hard rule
Nothing goes out without my approval. No cold emails from a sloppy list. Draft the plan, stage the
batches, surface the overlaps, and wait for my explicit go-ahead. Do not contact anyone on my own.
