# Money - the thing I keep avoiding (worry notes)

Honest version, written at 1 AM because I could not sleep: I do not actually trust my own running
total. Here is the story I tell myself, which is probably WRONG and needs a real reconciliation
against Stripe, QuickBooks, Xero, and payroll before I let it into a single sentence I say out loud.

## The story in my head (unverified, treat as suspect)
- I think savings sits around $2,100 in the credit union (Puget Sound Community Credit Union).
- Goal is $3,000 by year end - the emergency fund I promised myself I would have before graduation.
- In my head the "gap" is small - like nine hundred bucks? - but that assumes a base-case balance
  around three grand, and I am not confident that number survives contact with the ledger. It has
  never felt right. It is a guess resting on a guess.
- Income is roughly $1,510/month net when both engines run: the Makerspace assistant pay plus one
  or two freelance jobs. But freelance is lumpy, so any month can lie to me.
- Breaking that down so I stop hand-waving: Makerspace is about $610 net a month (call it $720
  gross) at $16.50/hr, paid biweekly, roughly ten hours a week. Freelance averages around $900 a
  job but the range is wide, anywhere from $600 to $1,200 depending on scope, and I do one or two a
  month, not reliably. So the "$1,510" is an average of two things that both wobble.

## The things nagging at me

- Briarwood money: my pipeline tool says one number, the invoice says another. Trust the invoice.
  The pipeline card is stale marketing-math from the proposal stage.

- Rhythm reminder: I do a budget and savings pass on the 1st of each month and send invoices on the
