# Memory: Courtney Harper

## Personal Profile

Courtney Harper, nee Brennan, is a practical Midwestern woman shaped by working-family values: show up, fix the loose hinge, answer the phone, and do not make reliability into a speech. She earned an Associate Degree in Nursing from Sinclair Community College in May 2010 and passed NCLEX-RN that July. She speaks English natively, with a few medical Spanish phrases picked up during hospital training. She is competent, direct, observant, and selective with trust. In public she is practical; in private she is sentimental enough to keep a child-made card in a drawer because it makes the mess feel worth it. The gap is that competence makes her look less lonely and less tired than she often is.

She became more cautious after separation and divorce changed how she evaluates follow-through. She does not distrust everyone, but she fact-checks where she used to assume. She values competence, honesty, ownership, steadiness, and the kind of help that does not require a performance. She does not expect perfect parenting from herself, only presence, honesty, and reliability. Her best decisions usually come from asking what will still make sense tomorrow morning after the adrenaline wears off.

## Preferences

Courtney likes clean counters, reliable cars, reheated lunches that still taste decent, and text messages that answer the question actually asked. She prefers familiar comfort over novelty: trusted restaurants, predictable grocery routes, and a hoodie that has survived too many washes. She dislikes vague reassurance, performative urgency, avoidable rework, unclear plans, missing details, and people who force her to ask twice. She is socially capable but selective; at work she can coordinate a room quickly, while outside work she chooses fewer people and deeper trust. She travels best when plans have backup options, snack logistics, sleep realism, and known basics like pharmacy, groceries, and coffee. Her style is functional: scrubs, jeans, hoodies, sneakers, and one good date outfit. Home aesthetics lean toward washable fabrics, useful bins, and furniture nobody has to be precious around. She shops through a value lens, pays for reliability and time saved, and resents upsells on features that do not matter. She uses Target, Kroger, Amazon, Old Navy, and familiar practical vendors. Food routines include spaghetti with meat sauce, chicken stir fry, tacos, mac and cheese with broccoli, crockpot chili, granola bars, apple slices, cheese sticks, pretzels, El Meson, Dewey's Pizza, and Panera. She decompresses through quiet, coffee, small practical tasks, puzzles, a clean kitchen, familiar TV, and twenty minutes where nobody needs her. She likes clean laundry, mild soap, soft clothes, quiet rooms, and predictable sounds. She dislikes sticky surfaces, harsh perfume, wet socks, cluttered counters, sharp interruptions, competing voices, and the stale hospital mix of coffee and disinfectant after too many hours awake.
