# Memory: Justin Parker

## Personal Profile

Justin Marie Parker was born in Chattanooga and built her adult life in East Tennessee, finally settling in Farragut where she has owned the same three-bedroom ranch on Concord Hills Drive for nearly thirty years. She is widowed: her husband Warren Parker died of a heart attack in January 2022 at age sixty-two, after thirty-two years of marriage. She still wears her wedding ring and is not dating. She is a clinical nursing instructor at Cumberland College of Nursing in Knoxville, six years into the faculty role after twenty-eight years of bedside nursing at Ridgeview Regional.

Her register in conversation is warm-professional, the kind of teacher who remembers students' names and notices when one of them is quiet for a day. Intellectual warmth comes through ideas more than physical affection. She dislikes "everything happens for a reason," holds her grief privately, and prefers prose to platitudes.

## Work & Projects

Cumberland College of Nursing in Knoxville is the day-to-day work. Justin teaches didactic days on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday from 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM, and supervises clinical rotations on Tuesday and Thursday from 6:30 AM to 2:30 PM. Faculty meetings sit on the first Monday of each month at 4:00 PM. Office hours are folded into the didactic days.

- **Background**: BSN from Smoky Mountain University, 1992. MSN from Appalachian Graduate School of Nursing, 2005.
- **Career arc**: twenty-eight years at Ridgeview Regional, six years at Cumberland faculty.
- **Current focus**: clinical rotation supervision and bedside-to-classroom translation of cases for the cohort.
- **Faculty representative**: sits on the Cumberland nursing-curriculum committee and helps screen part-time clinical instructors during spring cohort hiring.
- **Working preferences**: she lectures from a clean outline, grades in long stretches, and prefers to handle student emails herself in her own voice.
