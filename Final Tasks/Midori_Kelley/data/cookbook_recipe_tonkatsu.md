# Tonkatsu - mom's recipe but lazier on weeknights

Serves 2. ~30 minutes if I have everything thawed.

## Ingredients

- 2 boneless pork loin chops, about 3/4 inch (about 320 g total)
- Salt + black pepper
- 2 tbsp flour (or potato starch if I'm out)
- 1 egg, beaten with 1 tsp water
- ~1 cup panko (the bag from Pono Market is best)
- Neutral oil for shallow-fry (~1 inch in a cast iron)

## Sauce - cheat version

- 3 tbsp ketchup
- 2 tbsp Worcestershire
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tsp Dijon
- pinch sugar

Stir until smooth. The real one from Bull-Dog is better but I keep forgetting.

## Method

1. Pat the chops dry. Pound them a bit with the bottom of a glass to
   tenderize and so they cook evenly. Season both sides.
2. Dredge flour -> egg -> panko, pressing the panko in.
3. Heat oil to 165 C (use the wood-chopstick test). Lay the chops in
   away from you.
4. Fry 3-4 min per side until deep golden.
5. Drain on a rack (NOT paper, paper gets soggy).
6. Slice across the grain into 3/4 inch strips.

## Plate

- Shredded green cabbage with a squeeze of lemon
- Hot rice
- Sauce on the side
- Miso soup if I'm being good

## Notes

- Last time I tried beef tonkatsu cuts for variety - not bad with
  the same panko coat but use a thinner cut.
- Kaze gets the trim bits raw, no breading.
