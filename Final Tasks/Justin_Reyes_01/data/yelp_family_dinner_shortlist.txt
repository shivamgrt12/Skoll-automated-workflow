## 1. La Casita Mexicana
    Address: 4030 East Gage Ave, Bell CA 90201
    Rating: 4.4 stars (1,847 reviews)
    Price: $$$
    Cuisine: Regional Mexican (Oaxaca-focused)
    Private room: Yes, up to 24, no room fee if $800+ food/beverage minimum
    Tequila list: 68 tequilas, 42 mezcales, family-owned distributor relationships
    Distance from Torrance: 24 minutes (405N to 710N)
    Notes: Papa Roberto ate here in 2023 for his cousin Luis' 60th birthday and
        loved the mole negro. Chef Ramiro Arvizu has been on Bourdain. Confirmed
        the private room availability for Sunday January 17, 2027 by phone.
    Pros: Papa's own approval from 2023, mole negro is elite, tequila selection
        is genuinely deep, family-run so they get what a milestone birthday is
    Cons: Bell is a 24-minute drive without traffic, closer to 45 with traffic
        on a Sunday evening; Mom Maria does not love long drives after dark

## 2. Milpa Grille
    Address: 235 East Broadway, Long Beach CA 90802
    Rating: 4.6 stars (923 reviews)
    Price: $$
    Cuisine: Mexican, seasonal/farm-to-table lean
    Private room: No private room, has semi-private back area seating 14
    Tequila list: 34 tequilas, 18 mezcales, curated not comprehensive
    Distance from Torrance: 18 minutes (405S)
    Notes: opened 2022, chef Gilberto Cetina Jr from a Yucatecan family in
        Merida. Menu changes weekly. Excellent cochinita pibil, house-made
        tortillas, Papa Roberto has been talking about a real cochinita since
        our family Cabo trip in 2019.
    Pros: chef pedigree, Long Beach is easy for Mom Maria, cochinita pibil is
        the move, price point is not aggressive, Sunday-night vibe is warm
        without being loud
    Cons: no true private room, back area is behind a partial wall not a
        door, could be loud on a Sunday evening, does not take reservations
        more than 60 days out (need to book November 18 to lock January 17)

## 3. Broken Spanish
    Address: 1050 South Flower St, Los Angeles CA 90015
    Rating: 4.3 stars (2,410 reviews)
    Price: $$$$
    Cuisine: Modern Mexican, tasting-menu tilt
    Private room: Yes, "Cazuelas" room up to 16, room fee $500 plus
        $1,200 food/beverage minimum
    Tequila list: 55 tequilas, 30 mezcales, cocktail-forward
    Distance from Torrance: 32 minutes (110N)
    Notes: chef Ray Garcia, formerly at BS/MXN. This is the "special-occasion"
        splurge option. Everyone will remember the meal but the bill will be
        real. Sofia and I ate here for a Reyes family gathering in 2024 and
        it was genuinely great.
    Pros: memorable food, adult-restaurant setting, this is what a 65th feels
        like when you go big, room has actual doors not curtains
    Cons: bill will run $180 per head with drinks (Mom Maria will be
        uncomfortable with that number even if we cover it), 32-minute drive
        is at the outer edge of Maria's willingness, this is not a "family"
        restaurant in the way Papa might want

## Recommendation for Sofia

Milpa Grille (option 2) is the best fit on the balance of Roberto-happiness,
Maria-comfort, distance, and cost. Book the semi-private back area for 12 by
mid-November. La Casita Mexicana (option 1) is the sentimental favorite but
the Bell drive kills it for Mom. Broken Spanish (option 3) is the splurge
if we all agree to go big but Sofia and I both know Mom will insist on
paying half and that number will make her tight for weeks.
