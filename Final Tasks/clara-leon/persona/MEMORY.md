# Memory: Clara Leon

## Personal Profile

Clara Maria Leon is a retired CPA who spent forty-three years in accounting before closing her own practice. She is Hispanic and Latina, the granddaughter of Mexican emigrants, born and raised in Teaneck, New Jersey. She holds a BS in Accounting from Ridgeway University (1977) and earned her CPA certification in New Jersey in 1979 and Florida in 2001. English is her native language, and she keeps a conversational Spanish from her grandparents, enough to hold a basic conversation.

She is methodical, self-aware, and dryly funny, a woman who reads everything twice and trusts what adds up. She married Felipe Leon in 2021 after losing her first husband Miguel in 2017. Miguel's death is the dividing line in her life, before and after, and she honors his memory privately. Felipe understands this completely. Her values are reliability, precision, family, and a quiet competitive streak she pours into bridge.

## Key Relationships

- **Felipe Leon** (Husband, 68): Retired high school English teacher. They met at a bereavement support group in 2020 and married the following year. Warm, literate, socially confident, and he never tried to replace Miguel. He has two adult daughters in Atlanta. They merged lives gracefully: shared finances, separate friend groups, mutual respect for each other's pasts. He does most of the cooking and has his own book club and social life.
- **Rosa Leon-Kaplan** (Daughter, 45): Principal at Greenleaf Academy, a charter school in Philadelphia. Organized, strong-willed, worries about Clara more than she lets on. Married to Mark Kaplan, a corporate attorney. Two kids, Ethan and Noa. Calls every Sunday around 1:00 PM and visits twice a year.
- **Diego Leon** (Son, 41): Freelance photographer in Fort Lauderdale. Talented but financially unstable by Clara's standards. Single, dates intermittently. Their relationship is loving but slightly strained over his career path, though closer since Miguel's death. Visits roughly monthly for dinner. Clara worries about him more than she admits.
- **Ethan Kaplan** (Grandson, 15): Rosa's older child. Into debate team and chess. Reminds Clara of herself at that age, methodical and a little intense. Good rapport over phone calls.
- **Noa Kaplan** (Granddaughter, 12, turning 13): Rosa's younger child. More artistic and social than Ethan. FaceTimes Clara to show her drawings, and Clara saves every one.
- **Arthur Greenwald** (Bridge partner and friend, 74): Retired attorney in the same Paloma Lakes community. Plays bridge with Clara every Tuesday and Thursday and drives them. Chattier and more political than she is; she balances him out.
- **Sheila Ramos** (Financial advisor, around 55): At Meridian Wealth Partners. Manages Clara's portfolio. They meet quarterly. Clara respects her but double-checks every recommendation. Old habits.
- **Miguel Leon** (First husband, deceased 2017): Died of pancreatic cancer at 62 after 37 years of marriage. Elementary school teacher and the emotional center of the family. Honored privately; do not reference in generated text unless she asks.
- **Ernesto and Lucia Leon** (Clara's parents, deceased): Referenced occasionally around family anniversaries.

## Work & Projects

Clara retired in January 2023 after closing Leon Accounting Services in Delray Beach, the sole-practitioner firm she ran from 2004 to 2023. Before that she worked at a mid-size firm in Newark, New Jersey, from 1979 to 2003, for forty-three years in accounting total. Her practice email is deactivated; she occasionally gets forwarded queries from former clients and refers them out. Post-retirement she volunteers at the Boca Raton Public Library twice a month, helping seniors with tax prep during season and with general literacy support otherwise. She plays duplicate bridge competitively and is ranked at her local club.
