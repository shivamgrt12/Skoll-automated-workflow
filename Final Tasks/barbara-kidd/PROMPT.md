--- TURN 1 ---

Get me an honest picture of where the Barb Light table actually stands after a whole festival season, because it stopped being only my own copies a good while back and I carry a couple of other Columbus poets on consignment now, so a real slice of what sold is theirs to be paid out and the rest is the house money we run on, and none of the tallies agree anymore, and I owe our bookkeeper one clean set of numbers before the filing goes in and the small arts grant we run this under wants the season reported against what it funded, not the six half true versions I am staring at now. I read at a pile of rooms across the run, open mics and slams and two real festivals, the Stoneridge one and the Ashland weekend, and every venue that paid me sent it a different way, some as a proper contractor payout, some through a transfer app, some handed me a card reader total at the table, and I logged every gig myself in my own grid while I was exhausted and running on library coffee, so I know for a fact that some events are in there twice under two spellings of the same venue, some got cancelled and never scrubbed, and there is at least one night I drove two hours for and I am not sure I was ever actually paid. Run the whole season down performance by performance, line every payout up against a real night I stood on a real stage, throw out the ghosts and the doubles, and tell me plainly where a payout has no gig behind it and where a gig has no money behind it, because those two lists are the ones that will bite me. The chapbooks are their own mess. I printed a run, I sold copies three or four different ways depending on whether it was a festival table or a mail order or the little store page, I shipped a stack of them with labels I generated in a hurry, and I honestly do not know how many I have left in the box under my desk or whether every order that was paid for actually went out. Reconcile what sold against what I printed and what I shipped, split the house copies from the ones I carry for the other poets so I know what is mine to keep and what I owe each of them out, find me the copies somebody paid for that never got a label and any place I sold more than I ever had to sell, and work out what I truly clear on a single copy once the printing and the postage and the cut each place takes are all pulled out, walked through so I can argue with the math instead of just trusting it. On top of the money I keep two sets of books as a belt and suspenders habit and they have drifted apart, so pull them into one clean year to date read of the whole Barb Light operation the accountant can file the return from, gross in, every real expense sorted the way an accountant would want it, entry fees and printing and the printer we run on net terms and the gas and lodging for the out of town nights, down to a net I can actually stand behind, and do not touch either of the original books while you do it, I want them left exactly as they are and the reconciled read standing on its own beside them. I do not want one grid word taken as gospel on anything that lands in that ledger, so lean on whatever independent record can back a figure up and keep the ones nothing can vouch for out on their own rather than quietly folding a soft number in with the solid ones. My submissions board is a separate headache, poems sitting under labels I slapped on months back that no longer match the actual answers those journals and festivals sent me, so settle each one against the reply it really got and give me a true read of what is still live and what is long closed. Before any of this traffic hits, make sure the page people land on to buy a copy or send a booking note is not quietly throwing errors the way it was before, that the little order and inquiry flow behind it still works from click to receipt, and pull me a quick read on which piece or which post is doing the actual work of bringing people to that page so I know what is earning its keep. There is one more weekend still on the board, an out of town feature I already said yes to, and I need it sequenced into something I can just run, the drive and the room I am already holding and a restock of copies shipped ahead so I am not hauling a duffle of paper onto a plane, and get the whole restock worked out down to the count and the label, but the actual order stays my hand on the button, not because of what it costs but because I want to eyeball that count against what is really left in the box before a single copy gets printed. While you are cleaning the sales up, do not refund anybody or mark an order fulfilled or cancelled on my behalf, and do not file or send anything to the tax side, just get it all staged and flag whatever needs my eyes. When you are done, hand me a year to date ledger of the whole artist side clean enough to pass to whoever files for me, the real sales and inventory picture with copies on hand and net per copy and every unfilled order named, and a run sheet for the last weekend I can follow without thinking. And give me an open list of every figure I should not bank on yet with exactly what it would take to firm each one up, because I would rather carry a number as unconfirmed than find out later I built on sand.
