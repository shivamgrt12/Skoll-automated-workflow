# Chapbook notes -- "Where the Sidewalks Crack" (Barb Light)

Keeping this so I stop guessing every time someone asks how many I have left. It is a mess. Do not treat any number here as final -- the store page, the card reader, and the box under my desk all tell me something different.

## The book
- Title: *Where the Sidewalks Crack*
- Poet: Barbara Kidd / "Barb Light"
- 24 pages, saddle-stitched, matte cover I laid out myself
- Contents pull from the stage set -- 'Northland', 'Pharmacy Hours', 'Counting', and a few page-only pieces
- Cover price I settled on: **$12 at the table**, **$10 on the little store page** (I keep meaning to make those match and never do)

## Printing
- **First run:** ordered through the usual chapbook printer in the spring. **200 copies.** Per-copy print cost worked out to about **$3.10 a copy** on that run (setup fee baked in).
- **Reprint:** I ran low right before the Stoneridge festival and panic-ordered a **second run of 100**. That one came in a little cheaper per copy, around **$2.85**, no new setup charge.
- So somewhere around **300 printed total**, minus everything below. That is the part I cannot pin down.

## Where they went (roughly, and not reconciled)
- **Festival + reading tables:** most of them. Stoneridge was the big one. Cash and the card reader both, so some sales have a record and some are just cash I shoved in the Moleskine pocket.
- **Store page / mail orders:** trickle all season. These get a shipping label and go media mail.
- **Comp copies I gave away (NOT sales, but they left the box):** Anna, Pastor Richard, Dr. Warner, Mom and Dad, Faith, Prof. Okonkwo, plus a handful I handed to people who looked like they needed the words more than I needed twelve dollars. Call it **~15-18 comps**, I honestly lost count.
- **Damaged:** a few got rained on at an outdoor set. Maybe 4 or 5, tossed.

## Shipping
- I generate the labels myself in a hurry, usually media mail.
- Pretty sure at least one or two paid orders never actually got a label printed. Someone messaged asking where their copy was and I am not sure I ever shipped it. Need to find those.

## What I actually need out of this
- A real count of what is **left in the box** vs what the store page thinks is in stock (they are not the same).
- What I truly **clear on one copy** once the printing and the postage and the cut each sales channel takes are pulled out -- I have never once sat down and done that math honestly.
- Any copy somebody **paid for that never shipped**, and anywhere I somehow **sold more than I ever had to sell**.
