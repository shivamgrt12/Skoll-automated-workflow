# Memory: Amanda Martinez

## Personal Profile
Amanda Martinez is a Nigerian-American who lives at the intersection of two worlds most people think do not overlap: cybersecurity and music production. By day he thinks like an attacker to defend systems; in his studio he builds layered afrobeats that pull from Lagos club culture, Atlanta trap, and London grime. Both demand pattern recognition and reward patience. He is Igbo, born and raised in Houston to parents who emigrated from Enugu in 1980, and he code-switches between American professional English, Nigerian pidgin with cousins, and Igbo with his parents, letting each version of himself coexist rather than reconciling them.

He holds a diploma from Clearfield High School in Houston (2003) and a BS in Computer Science from Thornfield University in Atlanta (2007), followed by his CISSP (2012), CEH (2014), and continuing certifications in cloud security and threat intelligence. He is intellectually confident to the point of occasional arrogance, voracious in his reading, and genuinely curious enough to change his mind with better information. He mentors younger professionals in both fields through challenge rather than comfort, remembering what it was to be the only Black man in the infosec room and the only Nigerian in the studio. He carries the weight of his parents' investment in him as both motivation and pressure, and he struggles with vulnerability in close relationships in a way he recognizes but has not fully addressed.

## Work & Projects
Ironclad Security Consulting LLC is a sole proprietorship registered in North Carolina, offering penetration testing, vulnerability assessments, security audits, SOC 2 compliance consulting, incident-response planning, and security-awareness training. He keeps 4 to 6 active clients, a mix of mid-size companies, small tech firms, and the occasional healthcare or financial organization. Pricing runs $150 to $200 an hour, project engagements $8,000 to $15,000, and retainer clients $3,000 to $5,000 a month, for roughly $145,000 to $165,000 gross a year. His toolkit is Kali Linux, Burp Suite, Metasploit, Nessus, custom scripts, and various cloud-security platforms.

Key current clients: Ashdale Technologies (Durham), quarterly security audit at $12,000 per engagement over three years; Greenleaf Health Systems (Raleigh), HIPAA compliance on a $4,500 monthly retainer; Stonewick Financial (Charlotte), annual penetration test at $15,000; and Brightmoor SaaS (Austin, remote), a $10,000 SOC 2 project.

As AmandaXO he produces afrobeats, Afro-fusion, and Afro-electronic music in a treated home studio running Ableton Live, a Universal Audio Apollo Twin interface, Yamaha HS8 monitors, and assorted synths and drum machines. He has released two EPs ("First Generation," 2023, and "Lagos to Greensboro," 2025) and eight singles, distributed through DistroKid, with around 12,000 monthly Spotify listeners strongest in Nigeria, the UK, and the US. Music income is about $3,000 to $5,000 a year, mostly reinvested into equipment and marketing. Current projects: a three-track EP with Yemi for summer release, a beat pack for licensing, and a remix commission for a UK afrobeats artist.

## Finance
Consulting grosses about $155,000 a year against roughly $18,000 in business expenses, for about $137,000 before taxes; music adds about $4,000, reinvested. He makes estimated quarterly tax payments at an effective rate near 28 percent. Fixed costs include rent of $1,450 a month on the two-bedroom Lindley Park apartment, a $520 car payment with three years remaining on the BMW, $155 car insurance, $130 utilities, $75 phone, $90 fiber internet, about $221 in software and security-tool subscriptions, $65 gym, and roughly $400 groceries. Student loans were paid off in 2019.

Savings sit at $42,000 at Piedmont Federal Credit Union, $8,500 in checking, $85,000 in Vanguard index funds for retirement, and a $15,000 business reserve account. He is a disciplined earner and saver who spends on quality but does not overspend, and he sends his parents $500 a month as both cultural obligation and genuine gratitude.

## Health & Wellness
Amanda is in excellent physical shape and disciplined about it. He is 6 feet 1 inch and about 195 pounds with an athletic build, lifts four mornings a week (Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, Friday) at Piedmont Iron Gym, boxes on Wednesday and Saturday, and runs when the weather is good, tracking macros loosely. He has mild tinnitus in the left ear from years of studio monitoring and now uses ear protection and reference headphones. His last physical was November 2025 with Dr. James Okonkwo at Piedmont Medical Associates; dental is twice yearly at Lindley Park Dental Care; vision is 20/20.

Mentally he is functional but tends to intellectualize emotions rather than feel them. He is still processing the end of his relationship with Amara and knows he needs to work on emotional availability, approaching it like a problem to solve. He manages stress through exercise, music, and routine, has considered therapy without starting, and gets occasional insomnia during heavy client engagements. He targets 11 PM to 6 AM of sleep but often runs later during studio sessions, and uses blue-light glasses at night.

Diet: no food allergies, eats anything, but is intentional about nutrition for his training. Weekday meals are high-protein and meal-prepped (grilled chicken and rice, salmon, eggs, protein shakes); breakfast is a shake or eggs with toast and avocado, plus black coffee. He drinks little, maybe two or three craft beers a week, with palm wine at family gatherings.

Drive times: 25 minutes to Durham, about 80 to Raleigh, 90 to Charlotte, 5 hours to Atlanta, and he flies the roughly 20-hour distance to Houston.
