Research corner

This month we are featuring summer research from one of our co-chairs, who spent the summer studying small-city zoning reform in two New England cities. The short version: when a mid-size city loosens density rules without pairing tenant protections, displacement risk appears to tick up, and even light protections seem to dampen the effect. The work continues as a senior thesis this year.

If you are looking for a thesis topic that is both data-friendly and genuinely consequential, zoning and land use is an underrated place to start. Come to the grad school panel in November to hear more.
