Selected research

Senior thesis, 2027 to 2028

Zoning and displacement in two New England cities

A corridor-level panel for Worcester and New Haven, 2018 to 2025, with a matched difference-in-differences design measuring how upzoning shifts tenant turnover and rent burden.

Whitfield Fellowship, Summer 2027

A displacement index for data-poor cities

A three-component screening index of turnover, rent burden change, and net market-rate permits, built so a planning office without a data team can triage where to look.

Data Visualization II, Fall 2027

Urban heat and zoning overlap

Pairs public satellite heat data with the thesis zoning corridors to ask whether the hottest corridors are also the most exposed to displacement. They overlap, but not one to one.

Mean displacement index by corridor type. Upzoned corridors without tenant protections score highest. Even light protections move the index meaningfully toward the comparison group. Descriptive means with wide standard errors, the thesis tests whether the gap survives a proper comparison design.
