> This is the 2001 baseline that formalized the decommission workflow when we retired the first batch of 1980s Case machines. It was superseded by the 2013 revision after we started tagging ECM data. Kept here for archival cross-check only.

## 1. Scope

Applies to any Mesa Verde-owned wheel loader, motor grader, backhoe, or compactor that is being removed from active service for disposal, resale, or scrap.

## 2. Prerequisites

- Equipment Manager written approval (paper form).
- Unit on the yard, keys pulled, DEF/fluid supplies stocked for drain.
- Two-person crew minimum.

## 3. Procedure

### 3.1 Fluid drain

Drain engine oil, hydraulic oil, coolant, and fuel into labeled waste drums. Log volumes on the paper worksheet.

### 3.2 Filter and hose removal

Pull all serviceable filters. Cap open hydraulic lines with the yellow plastic plugs. Do NOT reuse filters even if visually clean.

### 3.3 Battery disconnect

Disconnect and remove batteries. Battery cores to the recycler within 30 days.

### 3.4 DOT plate removal

Remove DOT plate and any state-issued serial tags. Photograph the machine's cast serial number pad before removal for the disposal file.

### 3.5 Final worksheet

Complete the paper Decommission Worksheet (form MV-EQ-DW-2001). Foreman signs. File original in the yard office binder.

## 4. Records retention

- Paper worksheet: 7 years in yard office binder.
- Photocopy: mailed to HQ Finance for depreciation writedown.

## 5. Threshold rule

No hard hr-meter threshold in this baseline. Decommission triggered by Equipment Manager judgment (major failure, insurance write-off, or planned fleet refresh).
