Mesa Verde Paving and Gravel - Equipment Decommission Procedure canon history
Plain-text summary, filed 2026-10-19 by Sophia Rivera for the engagement file.

The reason there are three of these procedure documents on the Confluence side
is that we have thirty-plus years of Mesa Verde disposals and only two moments
where the procedure got a proper stamp. A third moment is in draft and is not
operative. I want to say the history in one place so nobody in the deal team
mistakes the third document for the operative one.

Baseline: MV-PROC-DECOMM-v2001
--------------------------------------
The 2001 baseline was the first time we wrote down what had been an oral
tradition for twenty years. R. Alvarado was Shop Foreman. He stamped it on
March 14, 2001, and P. Cortez the Equipment Manager (retired in 2006, passed
away 2011) countersigned. I witnessed. It was a paper-only document: a
Decommission Worksheet, a foreman signature, and a copy to HQ for the
depreciation writedown. There was no hr-meter threshold in it. Decommission
happened when the Equipment Manager decided it happened, on the basis of major
failure or a planned fleet refresh. That was fine for the era. The 1990s line
was still relatively young.

Revised: MV-PROC-DECOMM-v2013
--------------------------------------
J. Villareal took over as Equipment Manager after Cortez retired in 2006 and
by 2013 he wanted a proper procedure with ECM data pulls, hydraulic pressure
logs, and a formal hr-meter threshold. He drafted it, R. Alvarado countersigned
as Shop Foreman, and I witnessed on August 2, 2013. From that day forward
every disposal on the yard has run the v2013 procedure. When Jira became the
equipment-maintenance system of record in January 2022, we hard-coded the
procedure_ref field on the ticket schema to accept v2013 (and v2001 for legacy
back-fill). Every post-2013 ticket in the MV-EQ project references v2013.
Every one. The threshold rule that lives on the disposal readiness criteria
Confluence page (12,000 hr for wheel loader and grader classes on the 1990s
line) comes out of the v2013 revision.

Draft: MV-PROC-DECOMM-v2024-draft (not operative)
--------------------------------------
M. Chen from Compliance opened a draft in May 2024 after an internal audit
flagged proposed regulatory changes on hydraulic-fluid disposal and heavy-metal
recovery. The draft would lower the threshold to 10,000 hr for wheel loaders
and 11,000 hr for graders, add a heavy-metal reclaim step, and add a Compliance
countersignature line. Nothing about the draft has been ratified. R. Alvarado
asked in June 2024 for audit citations before he would countersign the
threshold change. M. Chen said in August 2024 that they were waiting on
external counsel. The thread has been quiet since February 2025. No Jira ticket
references the draft. No signoff. Confluence page labels are draft,
compliance_review, not_adopted.

The history is filed here so whoever reads the brief can see how the three
versions relate, which ones were actually signed and adopted, and which was
only ever opened as a draft. The call on which one governed the recent service
cycles is mine to make against the tickets, and this is the background for it.
