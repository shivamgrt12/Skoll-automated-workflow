## 0. Draft rationale (per M. Chen)

Compliance opened this draft after a 2024 internal audit flagged potential regulatory changes to hydraulic-fluid disposal and heavy-metal recovery. Draft proposes:

1. Adjusting the hr-meter threshold rule.
2. Adding a heavy-metal recovery step for batteries and hydraulic reservoirs.
3. Adding a Compliance signoff line on the disposal worksheet.

Nothing in this draft has been ratified by Equipment Maintenance leadership or by the Shop Foreman.

## 1. Scope

(Same scope as v2013; carried forward for review.)

Applies to any Mesa Verde-owned wheel loader, motor grader, backhoe, or compactor that is being removed from active service.

## 2. Prerequisites

(Same as v2013.)

## 3. Procedure (proposed)

### 3.1 Hr-meter capture and photograph

Unchanged from v2013.

### 3.2 ECM data pull

Unchanged from v2013.

### 3.3 Fluid drain

Same drain procedure. **Proposed addition:** hydraulic fluid to be tested for heavy-metal content and disposed via a certified reclaim vendor, not the standard waste drum. (Proposal - unratified.)

### 3.4 Battery core handling

**Proposed change:** battery cores must go to the certified reclaim vendor within 14 days (v2013 allows 30 days). (Proposal - unratified.)

### 3.5 Compliance signoff line

**Proposed addition:** Compliance countersignature required on disposal worksheet before Jira ticket close. (Proposal - unratified.)

## 5. Threshold rule (proposed change)

**Proposed:** Change threshold from 12,000 hr to 10,000 hr for wheel loader class and 11,000 hr for grader class. **Not ratified.** Any equipment currently between the proposed thresholds and the existing v2013 threshold remains in-service under the v2013 rule.
