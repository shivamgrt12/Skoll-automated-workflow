--- TURN 1 ---

The Council sits down with me and Pastor David is going to ask me point blank whether the Holiday Celebration is truly ready or whether we are running on good intentions and guesswork, so I need the honest answer before I walk in. Take the whole thing off my plate and come back with where we actually stand, not where the Events Committee believes we stand, because those two pictures have been drifting apart and I want to know by exactly how much and why. Start with the money. Gifts and pledges have been coming in through every door for months and nobody has matched what people promised against what actually landed. Some gave through one online channel, some through another, some tapped a card at the welcome table, some pressed folded bills or a sealed envelope into Rosa's hand after service, and some made a pledge in a passing message that may or may not ever have turned into real dollars. I need every commitment walked against every actual payment across every one of those channels, and against the paper cash ledger and the deposit envelopes Rosa keeps, so the true picture of what we collected sits beside what we were told to expect, with every gap named with a name and an amount so I can see who is still outstanding. Some of these figures are stale from earlier counting and I have lost confidence they still line up with what has actually cleared, so run the discrepancies down and make every number defensible before I repeat it to anyone. On the spending side the committee has been steering by a working budget, but our vendors have been agreeing to costs in conversations and confirmations that I doubt ever made it back into that budget, and I have a bad feeling what they are quoting now runs higher than what we still have written down. Go through the meal, the flowers, the music, the tables and chairs, the sound, and every smaller line, comparing the latest word from each vendor against what we first shook hands on and against what the budget still carries, and flag every place a price or a quantity or a delivery term quietly moved. Then give me one honest total of everything we truly owe set against the real donation picture, so I can see whether the math closes or whether we are leaning on money that has not arrived to cover things we already promised. The volunteer situation is the piece that wakes me at night. We have somewhere north of fifty souls who raised a hand at one point, between the sign-up forms and the group threads and the written replies and the ones who told me in person after prayer group, but I do not know how many are real confirmed shifts versus names on a list who nodded once and went quiet. Walk the whole roster against what each person actually confirmed and where and how they said it, and where the list calls someone confirmed but nothing in any thread or reply backs it up, treat that as unproven and tell me so plainly. Sort the solid from the maybes from the ones who have gone silent, lay that coverage against what a gathering this size needs role by role, and show me where we are thin, especially on the food service line and the children's area and parking and cleanup, so I know whether I have the people or whether I need to stand up after service and ask again. The wellness side of the celebration is mine to own and it has gotten away from me. The herbal preparations, the screening supplies, the healers I have been coordinating with and the small honorariums I promised them, the remedies I am pulling from my own stock, all of it lives scattered across private notes and separate conversations and small receipts, and nothing has been drawn into one picture. Figure out what I have committed to provide, what I still need to acquire, and what the real cost lands at against what the committee set aside for that piece, because I suspect those two numbers are nowhere near each other, and if any supply price has drifted since the quotes I first gathered, flag it before I agree to anything, and keep every name from my private practice off anything that could touch a church page. And the registration has been open to the whole neighborhood, not only the congregation, through more than one signup path plus the paper sheet Rosa keeps by the hall, and I do not believe anyone has checked whether the same households are sitting on two lists at once or whether what we planned for truly scales to the crowd coming. Reconcile those lists into a real head count, weigh it against the chairs and the food and the paper goods and the parking and the booth we can deliver, and tell me precisely where it breaks and what closing that gap would cost. When it is done I want two things I can carry into that room. A council-ready report that lays out plainly the true financial position, the volunteer coverage with every gap named, the vendor status, the wellness logistics, and every open decision still waiting on someone. And a reconciled budget that shows every dollar in and every dollar committed out with the distance between the committee's working numbers and reality called out in the open, so we stop deciding against a number that is not real. Do not send a word to anyone, do not commit to any purchase or any deposit, and do not move anything on my schedule. Draft it and hold it for my hand. And where the records are thin or you cannot get a clear answer, say so plainly rather than filling the hole with a guess, because I would sooner walk in with an honest gap than a confident wrong number.
