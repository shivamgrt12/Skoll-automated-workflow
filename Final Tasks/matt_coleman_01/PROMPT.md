--- TURN 1 ---

The Church Council meets December 8, and Pastor David is going to ask me point blank whether the Holiday Celebration is ready or whether we are running on good intentions and guesswork, so I need the honest answer before I walk into that room. Take the whole thing off my plate and come back with where we actually stand, not where the Events Committee believes we stand, because those two pictures have been drifting apart and I want to know by how much. Start with the money. Donations and pledges have been coming in through different doors for months and nobody has sat down to match what was promised against what actually landed. Some people gave through one channel, some through another, some handed cash to Rosa or to me after service, and some made a pledge in an email or a message that may or may not have turned into real dollars. I need every commitment matched against every actual payment, the full picture of what we collected versus what we were told to expect, and every gap named with a name attached. On the spending side, the committee has been tracking a working budget, but vendor commitments and supply costs have been agreed to in conversations and confirmations that I am not confident ever made it back into that budget. Reconcile both sides and give me one honest number for where we are, and when two sources disagree on an amount, the one closest to the actual transaction is the one I trust. Tell me which figure you used and which you set aside. The volunteer situation is the piece that wakes me up. We have somewhere around fifty people who raised a hand at one point or another, between the sign-up forms and the group chats and the ones who told me in person after prayer group, but I do not know how many of those are real confirmed shifts versus names on a list who never followed through. Walk the full roster against what each person actually confirmed and when they said they could be there, sort the confirmed from the maybes from the ones who went quiet, and lay that against what a gathering this size actually needs covered. I want to see where we are short, especially on the food service line and the children's area and parking and cleanup, and I need a straight answer about whether we have the people or whether I need to stand up after service and ask again. The wellness part of the celebration is mine to own but it has gotten away from me. The herbal preparations, the screening supplies, the two healers from the community I have been coordinating with, the remedies I am putting together from my own stock, all of it lives in different notes and conversations and nothing has been pulled into one picture. Figure out what I have committed to provide, what I still need to acquire, and what the cost looks like against what the committee budgeted for that piece, because I suspect those numbers are not close. If any supply costs have moved since the quotes I originally gathered, flag that before I agree to anything. The vendors for the meal and the decorations and the music have been confirming through messages and emails but I have not gone back to check whether every confirmation still matches what we originally agreed on price and quantity and delivery. Go through each one, compare the latest confirmation against the original commitment, and flag anything that shifted. I also need the total obligation across all vendors set against the donation picture so I can see whether the math closes or whether we are counting on money that has not arrived to cover things we already promised. Registration has been open to the whole community, not just the congregation, and the interest is bigger than anything we have done before. I do not think anyone has checked whether what we planned for, the food quantities, the volunteer coverage, the supplies, the space, actually scales to the number of people who signed up or said they are coming. Size the real demand against what we can actually deliver on every front and tell me where it breaks. I want two things when this is done. A council-ready report that gives Pastor David and the rest of them the true financial position, the volunteer coverage with the gaps named, the vendor status, the wellness logistics, and every open item that still needs a decision, written so that a room full of people who care about this community can see exactly where things are and what needs to happen before December 12. And a reconciled budget that shows every dollar in and every dollar committed out with the distance between the committee's working numbers and reality called out, so we stop making decisions against a number that is not real. Do not send anything to anyone, do not commit to any purchase, and do not move anything on my schedule. Draft and hold everything for me. And where the evidence is thin or you cannot get a clear answer from the records, say so plainly rather than filling in a guess, because I would rather walk into that council meeting with an honest gap than a confident wrong number.
