# Memory: Matt Coleman

## Personal Profile

Matt Coleman is a Mexican-American man born and raised on the Eastside of El Paso, Texas. He is a registered nurse by training and thirty-four years of practice at the Eastside Community Clinic, retired in 2022 with a Texas RN license still active. He is a curandero in the older sense, trained from age twelve by his grandmother in herbal medicine, energy work, and therapeutic massage. He is a practicing Christian at the small ecumenical Grace Community Church, Catholic by upbringing, and a man of daily prayer. He is gay, and was married to Arturo Coleman for thirty-six years until Arturo died in April 2021 of COVID-19 complications. ADN from Rio Grande Health Sciences College (1988), BSN from Greenmantle College (1995).

His values are simple and constant: feed people, sit with them when they are grieving, tell them the truth when they need it and the kindness they need the rest of the time. He believes faith is the practice and not the slogan, that healing is the slow work of paying attention to a body in a room, and that community is the only retirement plan that has ever worked.

He has the warmth of a man around whom rooms reorganize themselves without anyone deciding to. He is gentle and he is steel, and the steel comes out at a city council meeting about clinic funding or when a hospital administrator gets in the way of a migrant patient. He laughs at himself first and teases his grandchildren relentlessly. He carries Arturo's old wedding band on a chain under his shirt collar. Sofia spotted it once and pretended not to.

## Work & Projects

Matt retired from the Eastside Community Clinic in 2022 after thirty-four years of nursing, the last twenty as lead community health nurse. He specialized in diabetes education, preventive screenings, prenatal care referrals, and immunization drives. Former patients still bring him flowers and food. He continues to volunteer at the clinic two mornings a week, Tuesday and Thursday, 8:30 AM to noon, running diabetes education sessions, BP screenings, and health navigation for elderly patients. In January 2026 he drafted a letter to the clinic director proposing expanded diabetes education hours for the summer program and is following up through the volunteer coordinator.

His wellness practice runs out of the small consultation room off his hallway. He sees three to five community members per week by word of mouth for energy work with herbs and aromatherapy, herbal consultations, and therapeutic massage. He does not charge; he accepts gifts of food, candles, or small offerings. His grandmother passed in 2010 at ninety-one. He sources specialty herbs from a supplier in Las Cruces and online, including dried damiana located in early 2026.

At Grace Community Church he serves on the Council and the Events Committee, and he coordinates the annual Holiday Celebration each December. He attends the daily 7:00 AM service when his knees and the schedule allow, and the Sunday 10:00 AM service without fail.
