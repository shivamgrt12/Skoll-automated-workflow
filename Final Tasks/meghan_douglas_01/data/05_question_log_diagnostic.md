## What This Says

**Solid enough to bank on.** Peripheral nerve, tendon, and splinting-orthotic-fabrication all sit above 80 percent with adequate volume. Anatomy sits at 78 percent with the largest volume, meaning it is not a lucky sample. Those four domains are 49 percent of the blueprint weight and they are working.

**Soft.** Fractures and dislocations (76.9), arthritis (74.1), and wounds and burns (71.4) are within reach but not there yet. These three are 29 percent of the blueprint weight. Every additional point in accuracy in this cluster is worth more than an additional point in the strong cluster because volume is already there.

**Where you have been quietly avoiding.** Amputation and prosthetics (18 questions, 50 percent accuracy) and congenital and pediatric hand conditions (15 questions, 46.7 percent accuracy). Together they are 11 percent of the blueprint weight and you are effectively coin-flipping on both. The volume tells the story: 33 questions across two full domains in six months of studying is not a sample, it is avoidance. The accuracy tells the second half of the story.

**The pain-science cluster** (complex regional pain, edema) is a lower-priority soft spot. 66.7 percent is not disqualifying but 39 questions is thin. That domain benefits most from focused reading rather than more question drill.
