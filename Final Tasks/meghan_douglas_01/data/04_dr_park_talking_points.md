# Dr. Park Standing-Weekly Talking Points

For the Thursday 4 p.m. check-in. Prep block sits at 3:30. This is a working readout, not a confession. Bring one number, one question, one small ask.

## The Opener

"I did a line-by-line audit of the ledger back to September 2023. The corrected eligible total is 2,634.5 hours, not the twenty eight hundred I had been carrying in my head. The delta is duplicates and a category cluster that the newest instructor eligibility memo says does not count anymore. I wanted you to have the real number before we talk about the January sitting."

That is the opening line. It is 55 seconds. It lands the correction, sources the correction, and does not editorialize.

## The One Question

"On the supervised-versus-solo call, I have four rows in my last twelve months where you were in the building but not in the room. Under our convention, do those log as supervised or as solo? I want to be consistent both ways going forward."

That is the question that matters. It is a supervision-convention question, not a "how am I doing" question. It gives Dr. Park a specific, small, factual thing to answer. She will answer it. It resolves rows 4 and 6 on the reclassification queue and, more importantly, tells you how to log the next twelve weeks of hours cleanly.

## The Small Ask

"The current pace projects me to about 2,900 hours by January 9. I am going to think about whether that means shifting the sitting. Can I bring you a proposal at next Thursday's check-in?"

That is the ask. It signals the honest read without dropping the whole conversation into January-9-or-not right now. It buys you a week to decide with the readiness brief in hand. Dr. Park is not going to say no to that.

## What Not to Say

- Do not open with "so I have some bad news."
- Do not walk her through the reclassification queue. That is your workbook, not the meeting.
- Do not offer a decision on the January sitting in this check-in. You do not have one yet.
- Nothing about salary, loans, the emergency fund, or the Hashimoto's protocol comes up. Those are not conversations with Dr. Park.
- Nothing about specific patient sessions or the Airtable session codes crosses into this meeting.

## Clinical-Wall Note

Nothing in this talk track carries a patient identifier or chart language. Session codes stay on the ledger, and the ledger stays where it lives.

## After the Check-in

Log her supervision-convention answer in a fresh row on the Notion question log under a new tag `supervision-convention`. Apply the answer to rows 4 and 6 in the reclassification queue and to every forward-looking Airtable entry.
