# Memory: Darius Barker

## Personal Profile
Darius Marcus Barker is African American and a native English speaker with some conversational French left over from college courses he sampled but never completed. He is a Baptist Christian who attends Sunday worship and Wednesday Bible study, is active in the men's ministry at Riverside Baptist Church, and observes Lent and the major Christian holidays with devotion. He attended high school in Memphis, Tennessee, has no formal college degree, and completed a food safety and restaurant management certificate at James River Culinary Institute in 2021. He is self-taught in business accounting.

He grew up in Memphis in a large extended family rooted in the Bluff City for generations. His father Earl was a mechanic and his mother Dorothy was a celebrated home cook whose recipes form the backbone of Memphis Soul Kitchen's menu. He moved to Richmond in 2009, worked various jobs, and drove a taxi for seven years while saving to open the restaurant.

He carries Memphis in his bones and chose Richmond as the place to build a new chapter while staying connected to generations of Southern Black resilience. His hospitality is rooted in how Dorothy raised him: if someone is hungry, they eat. He is patient to a degree that unnerves impatient people, deeply musical in a way that structures his planning, and carries a quiet pride that never tips into arrogance. He struggles with delegation because he built everything himself. He has a booming laugh and uses humor as a bridge across generations and across awkward silences. He is fiercely protective of his children's connection to their roots while genuinely wanting them to thrive in modern America, and has made peace with the tension between those.

## Work & Projects
**Memphis Soul Kitchen** is a 42-seat Southern soul food restaurant in Richmond's Church Hill neighborhood. Open since October 2022 and now in its fourth year of operation. Open Tuesday through Sunday, 11:00 AM to 9:00 PM (10:00 PM on Friday and Saturday), closed Mondays. Menu features Southern staples including smoked ribs, gumbo, fried catfish, collard greens, mac and cheese, and fried plantains, plus seasonal specials and a small but growing catering business. Darius is hands-on in the kitchen six days a week. Margins are razor-thin.

**Staff**: Darius (owner and head chef), Devon Carter (sous chef), Megan Callahan (front-of-house manager), two part-time line cooks (Terrence and Marcus Jr.), one dishwasher (DeShawn), and two part-time servers.

**Catering** is the growing side of the business: corporate lunches, community events, weddings. Darius wants to expand it but needs capital. Recent active lead is a 75-person Dominion Financial Group corporate lunch. Memphis Soul Kitchen also runs a DoorDash storefront for delivery orders, monitored daily by Darius and paused when the kitchen is slammed.

**Riverside Groove Collective**: not a job but a real income stream. Six-member jazz-funk band. Rehearses Thursday evenings at the Northside Community Center. Performs two to three times monthly. Typical earnings of $200 to $400 per performance split among six members, $150 to $200 per school gig, $300 to $600 per jazz festival.

**Long-term project**: buy the building from Frank Donovan when the lease renews in 2027. Currently building emergency fund and exploring small business lending options.

## Finance
Monthly income (approximate): Memphis Soul Kitchen net revenue $4,500 to $5,500 after food costs, staff, rent, and utilities; catering gigs $600 to $1,000; Monique's home health aide income $1,800; Darius's band performance share $100 to $200. Total household: roughly $7,000 to $8,500 per month.

Monthly household expenses (approximate, excluding restaurant operating costs already netted out of the revenue line above): home rent (3-bedroom Church Hill apartment) $1,950; groceries $550; support to Earl in Memphis $400; 2019 Honda CR-V payment $310; car and restaurant insurance $280; utilities (home and restaurant) $350; Jaylen's school supplies and activities $120; ACA marketplace health insurance $580; two phone lines $110; savings $200 to $400 when possible.

Notes: disciplined but the restaurant eats most of the income. Paying off a $15,000 small business loan from River City Community Credit Union with 18 months remaining. Dreams of buying the building from Frank Donovan. Emergency fund approximately $4,000.

## Interests & Hobbies

- **Community organizing**: helps families navigate challenges, connects them with services, mentors young men through the church youth program.

## Preferences
- **Music**: blues, jazz, funk, and neo-soul. B.B. King, Miles Davis, Earth Wind & Fire, D'Angelo. Marvin Gaye plays in the restaurant during dinner service.
- **Reading**: news from Memphis via The Commercial Appeal online. Restaurant industry magazines including The Independent Plate. Jaylen got him hooked on a fictional graphic novel series called The Sankofa Chronicles.
- **Clothing**: Western casual during the week and his Sunday best suit for church and community events.
- **Shopping**: specialty butcher on Broad Street for premium cuts. Southside Wholesale Market for restaurant bulk orders. Monique handles most household shopping.
- **Entertainment**: basketball on TV (NBA on weeknights, college ball on weekends). Occasional family movie night with the kids choosing.
- **Food and drink**: loves bold, slow-cooked flavors: smoked ribs, collard greens, gumbo, fried catfish, smothered pork chops. Drinks sweet tea and fresh-squeezed lemonade daily. Moderate alcohol: enjoys a bourbon on rare occasions but mostly abstains. Enjoys experimenting with local Virginia ingredients (Chesapeake blue crab, Shenandoah apples, Virginia peanuts) adapted to Southern techniques.
- **Pet peeves**: food waste (will not tolerate it in his kitchen or his home), people who mispronounce Barker as Parker without correcting themselves, the assumption that all Southern food is the same.
