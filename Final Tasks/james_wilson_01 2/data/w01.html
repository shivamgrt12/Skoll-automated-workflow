The Studio

Made on Capitol Street

Wilson Ceramics is a one-person studio on Capitol Street in Charleston, West Virginia. Every piece is wheel-thrown or hand-built by James Wilson, fired to cone 6 in an electric kiln, and glazed with formulas developed and tested in the studio over years of practice.

The work is functional: mugs that hold coffee the way a mug should, bowls that sit right in the hands, plates that age well with use. The glazes are earth tones and satin mattes - warm amber, linen, stone grey, sage - that come from the same Appalachian landscape James has worked in since moving to Charleston after graduate school.

James holds an MFA in Studio Art and teaches ceramics at the University of Charleston in the spring and fall. The studio is open for community classes four sessions a week. Commissions are accepted for sets and one-of-a-kind pieces by inquiry.

How It Gets Made

From clay to your table

01

Wedging and centering

Stoneware clay is hand-wedged to remove air and align the grain before throwing. Centering is the first and hardest part of every pot.

02

Throwing or hand-building

Mugs, bowls, and vases are thrown on one of two Brent pottery wheels. Slab and coil methods are used for larger forms and hand-built work.

03

Drying and trimming

Pieces dry slowly to leather-hard, then are returned to the wheel for foot trimming. Handles are pulled and attached by hand.

04

Bisque firing

Bone-dry pieces load into the Skutt electric kiln for a bisque fire to cone 06 - hard enough to glaze, porous enough to accept it.

05

Glazing

Glazes are mixed in the studio from raw materials - silica, feldspar, kaolin, and oxides. Each batch is tested on tiles before use.

06

Glaze firing

Final firing to cone 6, around 2232 degrees. The kiln runs overnight. The smart controller monitors remotely so the firing is never left unwatched.

Custom Work

Commission a piece

Commissions are accepted for dinnerware sets, single statement pieces, and gift orders. Allow 6 to 10 weeks from contract to delivery.

  • 1

    Inquiry

    Send a message with your idea, a quantity estimate, and a general timeline. A quote follows within a week.

  • 2

    Glaze consult

    A tile sample of the proposed glaze or glazes is fired and reviewed before work begins.

  • 3

    Contract and deposit

    A simple contract is signed digitally. A 50 percent deposit is collected before clay is touched.

  • 4

    Production and firing

    Pieces are thrown, dried, bisqued, glazed, and glaze-fired. Progress photos are shared at key stages.

  • 5

    Delivery or studio pickup

    Completed work is available for pickup at the studio or can be shipped. Balance is due before release.

Common Questions

Before you reach out

Is your work dishwasher and microwave safe? +
Yes. All Wilson Ceramics functional ware is fired to cone 6 in an electric kiln, which produces a fully vitrified, food-safe, dishwasher-safe, and microwave-safe result. The exception is any piece with a metallic luster glaze - those are hand-wash only and labeled as such.
How long does a commission take? +
Standard commissions run 6 to 10 weeks from signed contract to delivery. This includes a glaze sample review before production begins, two kiln firings, and drying time between stages. Holiday gift commissions placed after October 1 may not be deliverable before December.
Do I need prior experience to take a class? +
No. All four class sessions welcome complete beginners. The Monday and Tuesday wheel sections are the most popular entry point. If you have some experience and want to know whether you would fit in a given group, reach out before registering - that is an easy conversation.
Do you ship? +
Yes. Etsy orders ship via UPS with insurance and protective packaging. Commission pieces are individually wrapped and boxed. Shipping on large commission sets is quoted separately and added to the final invoice.
Can I visit the studio? +
Studio visits are available by appointment for commission consultations and prospective commission clients. The studio is not a walk-in retail space, but the display area in the studio is available to view during scheduled visits. Book through the Calendly link on the contact page.
Where can I buy your work outside of Etsy? +
Wilson Ceramics work is stocked seasonally at Tamarack Marketplace in Beckley, the Capitol Market Gift Shop in Charleston, and the Mountain Stage Gift Shop in Charleston. Availability varies by season. The most reliable selection is on Etsy, with the studio website reflecting current inventory.
