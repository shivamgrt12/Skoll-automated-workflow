# Memory: Jason Porter

## Personal Profile

- **DOB**: November 12, 1998
- **Roots**: Raised in Tulsa and chose to stay, building career and craft in eastern Oklahoma rather than chasing a coastal market.
- **Heritage**: Southern roots through both sides of the family, grandmother's pottery as spiritual inheritance, father's environmental work as professional.
- **Mindset**: Processes the world through environmental law's analytical rigor and pottery's tactile intuition as one unified attention.
- **Temperament**: Runs warm with people and cold with systems, patience with individuals surprises those who see him in court.
- **Under stress**: Gets quieter and more deliberate, pottery output doubles into measured repetitive coil-building that organizes his thinking.
- **Principle**: Will not cut corners on cases even when shortcuts would cost nothing anyone else would notice.
- **Conviction**: Believes environmental law builds structural protection for things that cannot advocate for themselves.
- **Undergraduate**: B.A. Environmental Studies and Political Science, Tallgrass State University (Tulsa), conferred May 2020.
- **Law degree**: J.D. with environmental law concentration, Blackwood School of Law, conferred May 2023.
- **Clerkship**: Clerked for Judge Patricia Stonebridge, Eastern District of Oklahoma, August 2023 through July 2024.
- **Bar admission**: Admitted to the Oklahoma Bar in October 2023, member in good standing.

## Key Relationships

- **Noah Crawford (girlfriend)**: 29, born April 17, 1997. History teacher at Brookside High School in Tulsa. Eight-month relationship that began at a local arts festival. Thoughtful, kind, shares his love of the outdoors. Regular evening and weekend time together, hiking with Cedar and sharing meals.
- **Robert Porter (father)**: 54, born March 3, 1972. Environmental Programs Manager for the City of Tulsa. Jason's role model for environmental work. Close family bond centered around shared dinners and environmental values.
- **Susan Porter (mother), née Miller**: 52, born March 30, 1974. English teacher at Creekside High School in Tulsa. Cooks the Sunday dinners; the Porter-Miller Southern recipes flow through her.

- **Elaine Porter "Grandma Elaine"**: 78, born February 9, 1948. Traditional potter in Tahlequah, matriarch of the Porter family. Taught Jason coil-building starting at age ten. Jason drives to Tahlequah for pottery sessions and dinner with her.
- **Lily Bridges (best friend)**: 28, born March 17, 1998. Social worker at Tulsa Family Services. Known since middle school. They do yoga together and brunch at Stonehorse Cafe.
- **Daniel Redmond (work colleague)**: 35, senior attorney at Redstone Legal Collective and Jason's first call on the Triton case. Co-counsel on most expert prep.
- **Elena Vasquez (firm managing partner)**: 48, founder of Redstone Legal Collective and Jason's mentor. Fierce litigator. Final say on case strategy.
- **Tom Whitfield (pottery community friend)**: 62, retired art teacher who runs the community ceramics studio in Tulsa where Jason fires his pieces and teaches the coil-building workshop.
- **Councilwoman Sarah Windsor**: 45, Tulsa City Council, environmental committee. Works closely with Robert Porter and is a regulatory contact for the alliance.

## Work & Projects

- **Role**: Environmental Attorney at Redstone Legal Collective. Joined August 2024 after the Stonebridge clerkship; approaching two years tenure as of mid-2026.
- **Mentor and supervisor**: Elena Vasquez, Managing Partner.
- **Key case**: Greenfield Watershed Alliance v. Triton Energy Partners. Challenges illegal wastewater discharge into Arkansas River tributaries. Expert depositions in progress.
- **Career goal**: Senior Attorney within three years. Eventually a practice centered on environmental conservation litigation in the region.
- **Pro bono and community**: regular research support for the Greenfield Watershed Alliance and occasional consults arranged through Calendly.

## Interests & Hobbies

- **Traditional pottery**: coil-building and hand-forming techniques learned from Grandma Elaine starting at age ten. Home studio, traditional clay prep, both traditional and contemporary pieces. The studio is where he thinks most clearly.
- **Environmental advocacy**: beyond legal work, volunteers with the Greenfield Watershed Alliance on river cleanup days and quarterly water quality monitoring.
- **Hiking**: Turkey Mountain, Keystone State Park, and weekend trips to the Ozarks. Cedar in front, Noah often alongside.
- **Reading**: environmental law journals, contemporary fiction (Barbara Kingsolver, Richard Powers, Ann Patchett, Brandon Hobson), mysteries for unwinding (Tana French, Louise Penny).
- **Gardening**: small raised-bed garden on the patio. Tomatoes, peppers, herbs, heirloom squash seeds from Grandma Elaine.
- **Cooking**: Southern comfort traditions. Grandma Elaine's cornbread and apple dumplings, his mother's fried okra and pecan pie, weeknight grain bowls and soups.
- **Yoga at Stillwater Yoga Studio.**
- **Art markets**: sells pottery at regional markets four to five times a year. Riverbank Arts Festival in October is the biggest.
