# Pottery ledger

This spreadsheet is my hand-kept Q3 ledger for PorterClayWorks. I try to update it every
Saturday after market or after an Etsy batch clears, but I fell behind after mid August
because Triton trial prep ate the second half of the month and I never came back to it.

July and August are penciled in from memory and paper receipts. September is completely
blank because I never entered a single row. The quarter total here reads about 3505 dollars
net, which I know is wrong and low. Every year I underestimate September because the fall
markets push volume up right before Riverbank.

## Where the real numbers live
The live truth is spread across:
- Etsy shop receipts for online orders
- Stripe and PayPal for the online payout processors
- Square for the market booth card reader
- Ally checking through Plaid for the deposits that actually landed
- FedEx and UPS labels for the shipping cost side
- QuickBooks and Xero for the sole-prop bookkeeping

Reconcile against those and treat this file as a starting point only.

## Rules for this reconciliation
- The live sales feed is truth. This ledger is stale.
- Give every mismatch over fifty dollars its own line.
- Tie each shop payout back to its bank deposit in the pottery checking account.
- Keep income, sales, and bank figures inside the one summary. Do not send them anywhere.
- Do not cite the 3505 number as the true Q3 total. It is the starting drift, not the answer.

## Follow up
Once the close is done, note the corrected Q3 net back into QuickBooks and reconcile with
Xero so the quarterly self employment estimate the accountant runs lines up with reality.
The Q4 sleeve for taxes is already thin because Riverbank is next Saturday.
