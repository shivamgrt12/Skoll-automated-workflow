I keep circling this publisher decision and I need to get my head around it instead of going with my gut, because the conversation with the Ember Gate side is coming back around once we have real demo numbers to point at, Pixel North still wants to see where we have landed before they say anything concrete, and Tyler is quietly convinced we should just self publish and keep it all ours, so what I have is three strong opinions and no answer. What I want out of you is the whole picture pulled together and defended, so that when I sit down to make the call I know which path leaves Moonstone in the strongest position and why, not just which one sounds most flattering across the table. The problem is that half the numbers I have been quoting are stale and I have lost track of what is still true. I have been showing the same wishlist and conversion figures in the pitch for a long stretch and I honestly do not know if they still hold, the burn and runway math I carry in my head is from an older version of the budget before Alex's next batch of tracks came due and the insurance moved and the booth money got real, and the deal terms I remember from the early back and forth may not match whatever actually got put in writing later, so I need every figure that goes into this run down against everything we have gathered since and made genuinely defensible before I lean on it, and I want to be clear about which numbers I should have stopped trusting. Do not force a clean verdict where the evidence is thin either. If the real conversion story is messier than the one headline number I like to quote, tell me that and show me the range instead of pretending it is tidy, because I would rather walk in honest than get caught out on my own data. Beyond truing up the numbers I need you to actually work the economics of each path, because an advance means nothing to me until you put it next to what we would owe back out of revenue and what it does to how many months of runway we are actually sitting on, and self publishing is not free the moment you count what we would have to spend ourselves to buy the reach a publisher brings for us. So model the three options honestly against where our money really is and how long it lasts under each, and give me a runway picture I can trust for every one of them rather than a single rosy line. Then there is the state of the actual game, which is the part I cannot fake in front of anyone. I have a mountain of open work sitting in the backlog, bugs and half finished level and boss and interface pieces all tangled together, and I need the whole pile gone through and split into what genuinely has to be done before we could stand behind an Early Access build and what is just nice to have, because the honest launch timeline that falls out of that is the single biggest thing that changes what any of these deals is worth and whether I can credibly promise a date to a publisher without lying to them. Fold in what the beta testers have actually been telling us across the sessions too, since the loudest recurring complaints are either a real threat to how the launch lands or they are noise I can safely park, and I would rather know which before I let a publisher's timeline pressure shove us out the door half baked. I also want a read on the room outside the studio, where the community sentiment sits and which way the wider indie conversation is drifting, because that colors how much leverage we really have and whether the exposure and the press a publisher can open up is worth giving away a cut of everything for. Pull all of that into one honest recommendation I can act on, a clear case for whichever path wins with the tradeoffs of the other two spelled out plainly so nothing blindsides me later, and put the trued up set of numbers behind it out where I can see exactly what changed from the figures I have been repeating and lean on as the truth from here. Now the careful part, because this is where I get nervous. Nothing goes out to Sam or to anyone on the Pixel North side off the back of this, no matter how finished a reply looks, because I make first contact on the deal talk myself and I decide when we re engage, so keep everything to drafts and workings I can look over. Same goes for anything sensitive leaving the studio, our internal budget and the runway math and the design and build internals stay between me and Tyler and do not get attached to anything pointed at a publisher even when it would obviously make the pitch land harder, and my own savings and personal side of the finances are not part of the studio story at all. Do not commit us to any real money on your own either, the booth and anything in that range waits on me saying yes. Tyler should be able to see the studio side of all of this, the build readiness and the shared finances and the timeline, but keep the parts that are only mine out of whatever you would hand to him. I know this is a lot knotted together and some of it only works in order, the deal math is meaningless until the numbers underneath it are solid and the recommendation is meaningless until the launch timeline is honest, so take it in whatever sequence actually makes it hold together and just get me to something I can stand behind.
