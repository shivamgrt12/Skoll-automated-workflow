# Memory: Jessica Wright

## Personal Profile
Jessica Wright (née Khoury) is a principal interior designer and small-business owner who grew up in suburban Arizona, moved east for design school, and built her life in Washington, DC. She holds a BFA in Interior Design from Ellsworth School of the Arts and Design (2002) and is NCIDQ-certified. She is quietly rooted but widely curious, drawing on Mediterranean tile work, Japanese minimalism, and Scandinavian light without feeling scattered by the range. She treats beauty as structural rather than decorative, composing rooms with a logic of light and material. She is flexible about style and unyielding about quality, and clients sometimes mistake that standard for rigidity. Her perfectionism is both her greatest asset and her likeliest path to burnout, and she knows it. The hostess gene runs deep: her mother taught her a guest should never want for anything, and her dinner parties are warm, considered, and effortful behind an effortless surface.

## Work & Projects
Principal designer and owner of Wright Interiors, a sole proprietorship registered in DC and roughly three years old as an independent firm, founded 2023. After earning her BFA in 2002 she worked as a junior then associate designer at Lowell and Park Interiors in DC from 2002 to 2013, then spent ten years as a senior designer at Hargrove Design Associates from 2013 to 2023 before going independent, a continuous timeline with no gaps. No physical studio; she works from a dedicated second-floor home office plus client sites. Full-service residential interior design: concept, sourcing, vendor management, and installation oversight. She typically juggles three to five active projects, with client meetings usually Tuesday and Thursday and site visits as needed. The flagship active project is the Pemberton Georgetown townhouse renovation, now in its Phase 2 design reveal cycle. She drives a 2022 Volvo XC60 to client sites and vendor showrooms.

## Health & Wellness
Jessica is generally healthy, with annual physicals and up-to-date screenings. She gets mild tension headaches during high-stress project phases, managed with hydration and reduced screen time. She walks three to four times a week in Rock Creek Park, about 45 minutes at a brisk pace, and takes a Saturday 9:00 AM Pilates class at Core Balance Studio. She sleeps a solid seven hours most nights, occasionally disrupted by worry about her parents. No formal therapy; she processes through Rania, long walks, and her work. ## Home & Living
A four-bedroom colonial on Calvert Street NW in Woodley Park, about 2,800 square feet, purchased 2014 with a mortgage through 2044. Hardwood floors throughout and restored moldings. Jessica renovated the kitchen herself in 2022 and the primary bathroom in 2024, treating the home as a portfolio piece. The open-concept kitchen has custom cabinetry, marble counters, and a professional-grade range, and is her favorite room. Her second-floor home office holds a large drafting desk, material-library shelves, and a backyard view. The backyard has mature trees and a small patio with string lights for hosting. Two cars: her 2022 Volvo XC60 and Greg's 2020 BMW 3 Series, with garage plus street parking.
