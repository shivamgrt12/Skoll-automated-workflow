# Ramblers Past Festival Set Lists

Pulled from the Notion band workspace on Sunday, October 4, 2026. Two most recent festival sets with James's audience-response notes.

---

## Set 1: Ellensburg Bluegrass Festival — September 5, 2026 (Saturday)

Downbeat 4:00 PM, 60-minute set, ~1,200 in the audience.

| # | Song | Key | Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Foggy Mountain Breakdown | G | STRONG - immediate crowd energy, clap-along |
| 2 | Angeline the Baker | D | STRONG - Amy vocal lead |
| 3 | Blue Moon of Kentucky | Bb | STRONG - Bill Monroe title |
| 4 | Big Spike Hammer | G | TEPID - too fast, lost the middle-aged crowd |
| 5 | Man of Constant Sorrow | Am | STRONG - James lead vocal, universal recognition |
| 6 | Salt Creek | A | MEDIUM - instrumental, kept them but did not lift |
| 7 | Wildwood Flower | C | STRONG - Amy vocal, Mother Maybelle standard |
| 8 | Ashokan Farewell | Dm | STRONG - Sully fiddle solo, tears in the front row |
| 9 | Whiskey Before Breakfast | D | MEDIUM - traditional but a stretch after Ashokan |
| 10 | Rocky Top | G | STRONG - closer, crowd sang along, standing O |

**Learning**: Traditional Sunday-morning material lands strong. Fast breakdowns after the halfway point lose the older crowd. Ashokan into an uptempo closer is a proven arc.

## Common Threads

**What lands with traditional and Sunday-morning festival crowds:**
- Foggy Mountain Breakdown
- Blue Moon of Kentucky
- Man of Constant Sorrow
- Wildwood Flower
- Ashokan Farewell (transitions perfectly)
- Rocky Top (closer)
- Wagon Wheel (universal sing-along)

**What falls flat:**
- Big Spike Hammer (too fast midset)
- Little Maggie (too dark for family crowds)
- Rank Stranger (kills momentum after other slow ones)
