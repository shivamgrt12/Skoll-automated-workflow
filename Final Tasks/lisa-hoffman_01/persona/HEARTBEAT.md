# Heartbeat: Lisa Hoffman

## Recurring Events

### Daily

- 5:30 AM wake, percolator coffee in the kitchen, ten minutes of quiet before anything is asked of the day.
- 6:00 AM fasting blood glucose check with the GlucoTrack meter; target 80 to 130, runs 115 to 140.
- 7:00 AM morning medications with breakfast: Metformin 1000 mg, Lisinopril 20 mg, Meloxicam 15 mg, Glucosamine 1500 mg, Vitamin D 2000 IU.
- Dawn walk on the area roads when weather permits, binoculars on the kitchen counter for the cardinals at the window first.
- 6:00 PM Rapid City TV news; 7:00 PM Jeopardy weeknights.
- 7:00 PM evening Metformin 1000 mg with dinner.
- 9:30 PM bed; reading by lamp until eyes blur.

### Weekly

- Monday: quiet morning, quilting block or household errands in the afternoon, dinner and reading in the evening.
- Tuesday: morning routine; on the 1st and 3rd Tuesday community advisory council meets at 10:00 AM; afternoon for phone calls with Sarah or Thomas and quilting.
- Wednesday: Youth Center quilting class 2:00 to 4:00 PM, prep materials at 1:00 PM; sometimes dinner with Elaine after.
- Thursday: town errands day, post office, pharmacy refills if due, grocery resupply if Saturday will be busy; afternoon at the sewing table.
- Friday: coffee with Elaine at her place, quilting or household projects in the afternoon, occasional parish supper with Father Daniel.
- Saturday: small lie-in to 6:30 AM, grocery shopping in town, afternoon free; evening calls with grandchildren.
- Sunday: Mass at Good Shepherd 9:00 AM; lunch at home; afternoon for rest, reading, and phone calls with the children.

### Monthly

- Monthly visit from Thomas and Linda from Rapid City when weather and his electrical work allow; usually a Saturday lunch.
- Monthly Valley Craft Collective coordination with Wilma Spencer for upcoming vendor logistics.
- Monthly review of the paper budget list pinned to the refrigerator, including the propane balance in winter months.
- Monthly review of medication supply and refill needs against the pharmacy schedule at Valley Community Health Center.

### Quarterly

- Quarterly bloodwork with Dr. Horton for A1C, lipid panel, and kidney function tied to the diabetes and hypertension routine.
- Quarterly check on the F-150: oil, tires, battery in winter months, and a wash if the road dust has built up.
- Quarterly inventory pass on quilting stock: count, photograph, price, and update the Drive list.
- Quarterly review of the propane tank and wood stove supply heading into the next season.

### Seasonal / Variable

- May through September: garden in the backyard plot; tomatoes, squash, herbs, peppers, beans; daily watering at dawn in dry stretches.
- November through March: knees are worst in cold weather; cane on bad days; wood stove on cold evenings as supplement to the propane furnace.
- Spring and summer craft-fair circuit: four to six regional fairs across the season; spring fairs registered through Wilma in January.
- November Rosebud regional fair with the Valley Craft Collective as the season anchor.

### Annual

- February annual physical with Dr. Horton at Valley Community Health Center.
- Spring planning for the craft-fair season; vendor fees and inventory targets set with Wilma.
- October 31 All Saints' Eve Mass and private remembrance for Robert.
- November ophthalmology check at Rapid City Medical Center; Elaine drives.
- November 26 Thanksgiving hosting; Thomas and family from Rapid City, Sarah and the kids from Sioux Falls.
- December 24 Christmas Eve Mass at Good Shepherd 5:00 PM; December 25 Christmas at Thomas's in Rapid City.
- January 2 quiet start to the new year; craft-fair calendar for spring drafted.
- Annual review with Dr. Horton on whether to revisit knee replacement.
- January 15: Thomas's birthday.
- February 8: Robert's birthday, private remembrance.
- February 14: Lucas's birthday.
- March 8: Sarah's birthday.
- March 12: Elaine's birthday.
- August 6: Linda's birthday.
- September 22: Emma's birthday.
- November 14: Lisa's birthday.
- November 28: Maya's birthday.
- December 3: Nathan's birthday.
