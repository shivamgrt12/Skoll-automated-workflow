Sorting, Shelving, a Carrier's Second Route

Two and a half hours a shift, the second and fourth Saturday of the month, mostly sorting donations and shelving by section. Thirty years walking Route 106 turned out to transfer cleanly to reading a sorting table. The work is unglamorous, useful, and finite, which is most of what a good morning needs to be.

Twice a month

Second and fourth Saturdays, coordinated by M. Reyes, with D. Khoury on the off weeks.

The annual sale

An open-to-close shift the last Saturday of its month, the busiest day on the calendar.

Built small on purpose

Enough to be useful and known, not so much that a flare week breaks a promise to other people.

The one big day

The Annual Book Sale

Open to close

The last Saturday of its month, an all-day floor shift, blocked out well ahead and worked around no appointment.

From sort to crate

The weeks before shift from ordinary shelving to pricing and crating; the roster goes around in draft and is revised twice before final.

What it funds

Proceeds keep the large-print collection stocked and the children's room supplied, which closes a personal loop.
