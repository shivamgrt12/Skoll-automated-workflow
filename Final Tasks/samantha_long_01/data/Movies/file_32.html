The Growing Season at Plot 14

Six raised beds, three blocks from a second-floor apartment on Narragansett Boulevard, worked Monday and Friday mornings under a hat and long sleeves. Tomatoes carry the season, the zucchini overproduces on purpose, and whatever the apartment cannot eat leaves the gate for Frank and for Beth's family.

Nine packets

The same seed list each year, about $24 from a mail catalogue, ordered in early February.

Two mornings a week

Monday and Friday before the heat, with the cane along on the bad neuropathy days.

Three households fed

The apartment first, then Frank on Fridays and Beth's table on the alternate Sundays.

Zone 6b timing

The Season, Step by Step

Cranston's last reliable frost lands in early to mid May, which sets everything else. Warm crops start indoors six weeks ahead; greens go in three weeks before the transplants.

What the beds return

Yield Across the Season

Pounds harvested, by crop

Roughly 91 pounds across the season from six beds, most of it tomato and zucchini.

The honest line

In grocery terms the plot pays for itself several times over. Counted honestly it pays for the mornings: two hours bent over a tomato row is two hours the body is busy and the mind is quiet.
