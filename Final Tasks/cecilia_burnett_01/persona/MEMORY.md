# Memory: Cecilia Burnett

## Personal Profile
Cecilia is a White American of Anglo-Irish and Scottish heritage who built Golden Prairie Nails from nothing after a difficult divorce in her twenties, and she carries the quiet authority of someone who has earned everything she has. She rarely talks about that history unless someone has earned the story. She holds an Associate's degree in Business Administration from Cedarbrook Community College (2000) and a cosmetology license from Westgrove Technical Institute (2001). She is deeply superstitious about routines and lucky numbers despite seeing herself as a rational businesswoman, and she will reschedule a lease signing if a date feels wrong. She reads self-help and business books obsessively but trusts her gut over any framework when a decision actually matters. She is privately anxious about whether her children will carry forward family traditions or let them dissolve into separate adult lives.

- **Megan Burnett (daughter, born February 8, 2004)**: Senior at Thornfield University studying communications. Independent and a little rebellious. Cecilia wants her in the business but tries not to push too hard.
- **Tyler Burnett (son, born December 19, 2008)**: Junior at Westfield Preparatory Academy, robotics enthusiast, quiet and focused. Cecilia worries he is too introverted.
- **Denise Caldwell (sister, born October 3, 1984)**: Registered nurse at Greenleaf Medical Center, lives in Aurora. Cecilia's closest confidant and occasional business sounding board.
- **Sharon Henley (best friend, born November 22, 1979)**: Owns a bakery on Main Street, friends since childhood, brutal honesty between them.
- **Amanda Keller (Main Street manager, 34)**: Reliable and detail-oriented, six years with Cecilia.
- **Brittany Schafer (Orchard Plaza manager, 29)**: Energetic and good with younger clients, sometimes cuts corners on inventory tracking.
- **Ryan Novak (Wheaton manager, 38)**: Former nail tech turned manager, calm under pressure, handles upset clients well.
- **Patricia Holm (Main Street landlord, 60)**: Owns several commercial properties in Naperville, businesslike but firm on rent increases.
- **Mark Sullivan (accountant, 50)**: CPA at Ridgepoint Financial Services, has handled salon finances for eight years; Cecilia trusts his judgment completely.

## Work & Projects
- Worked as a nail technician at salons in the western suburbs from 2001 to 2007, building her client base and savings after the divorce.
- Opened the Main Street flagship of Golden Prairie Nails in 2007 and grew it into a full-service salon over the following years.
- Opened the Orchard Plaza location near Aurora in 2016 and the newer Wheaton location in 2023.
- Owns and operates Golden Prairie Nails across all three locations.
- Employs 28 nail technicians plus three managers (Amanda, Brittany, Ryan) and two front-desk staff, alongside her mother Dorothy, who works the Main Street front desk part-time (three days a week).
- Visits the Main Street and Orchard Plaza locations daily and the Wheaton location two to three times a week, spending the most time at the Main Street flagship.
- Negotiating the Main Street lease renewal: landlord Patricia Holm wants a 15% increase, Cecilia is countering at 7%.
- Exploring adding waxing and eyelash-extension services at Wheaton, which needs additional esthetician licensing.
- Nominated for the Naperville Small Business Alliance community-leadership award.
- Revenue is seasonal: prom season (April to May) and the holiday season (November to December) are peak.
- Staffing is a constant challenge; two Orchard Plaza technicians recently left and she is interviewing replacements.
- Weighing a customer loyalty app against a simple punch-card system, unsure about development cost.
- Active in the Naperville Small Business Alliance.

## Finance
- Combined gross revenue across the three salons is approximately $1,200,000 per year.
- Personal take-home after expenses, payroll, rent, and supplies is roughly $145,000 per year.
- Mortgage on the family home is $2,800 per month, owing approximately $310,000.
- Monthly rent across the three salon locations totals approximately $14,500.
- Maintains a $40,000 business emergency fund at Ridgecrest Community Bank.
- Personal savings of approximately $85,000, with $18,000 per year remaining toward Megan's tuition.
- Saving toward the Ireland and Scotland family trip with a target of $15,000 by mid-2027, mostly through a conservative monthly index-ETF auto-invest Craig set up, alongside a small crypto slice he watches; any move at or above the $500 threshold needs Cecilia's approval.
- Craig's salary from the bookkeeping role is $52,000 per year.
- Business credit-card debt of $4,200 from recent Wheaton equipment purchases (nail lamps and pedicure chairs).
- Cash flow across three locations is a constant stress, and payroll weeks are particularly tight.
