About

Where the cooking comes from

I grew up in a food-obsessed Italian-American kitchen in Bayonne, learning to cook from scratch at eight, standing on a stool next to my grandmother. A culinary diploma, stages in Portland, and a long climb from line cook to sous chef followed, but the binder of handwritten recipes is still where everything starts.

  • Runs the line, manages prep, trains junior cooks
  • Sources direct from Hudson Valley farms and the fish market
  • Quality control on every plate that leaves the pass
  • Builds the business plan on nights off
"Food is the most honest thing humans do." My working philosophy

The long game

Rootstock

The idea

A 40-seat seasonal American restaurant with an open kitchen, an a la carte room, and an optional five-course tasting menu.

The sourcing

Roughly 70 percent of food spend going direct to growers and producers. The supplier relationships exist today.

The plan

Lease and financing targeted for early 2027, buildout to follow, doors open later that year. Slow on purpose.
