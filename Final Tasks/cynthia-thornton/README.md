# cynthia-thornton-rootstock-pitch-window — Cynthia Thornton

Professional/Prosumer. Cynthia is a sous chef at Harborview Kitchen (Jersey City) who is quietly building toward opening her own seasonal-American 40-seat restaurant Rootstock, and inside the two-week window that runs from Frank Benedetto's introduced-investor dinner through the Elm Street Hoboken lease walk-through her assistant must true up every load-bearing figure in the pitch package (vendor pricing, menu-to-sourcing coherence, restaurant fund and runway, projected food cost, and lease unit economics) so nothing collapses under Frank's contact pushing on it, while leaving direct investor contact, the fund and business-plan detail, Grandma Rose's recipes, any vendor commitment or purchase above the spend threshold, and any pre-noon scheduling untouched without her explicit approval.

- Rootstock restaurant fund balance: the live Harbor Savings bank feed at $28,845 wins over the Rootstock hub savings-tracker snapshot at $28,000 that was last synced weeks before the focal window.
- Elm Street Hoboken commercial space square footage: the draft LOI at 1,720 sq ft wins over the broker-pipeline listing metadata at 1,850 sq ft.
- Rootstock projected food-cost percentage: the bottom-up recompute at ~36.5% (from reconciled per-unit vendor prices × the current menu × the projected cover mix) wins over the 32% assumption baked into the accounting projection model.
- Greenfield Farm Market fall delivery window: the peer-flagged mid-season Tuesday-cutoff shift surfaced in the chefs'-collective thread wins over the old delivery-day column still sitting in the vendor database.
