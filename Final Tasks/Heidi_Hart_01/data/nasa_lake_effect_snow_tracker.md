## Meteorological drivers this season

- **Lake Michigan surface temperature:** Running approximately 3 degrees F above the 5-year average, delaying the first lake-effect band by 5-7 days relative to a colder season. The warmer surface holds more moisture and, when the first cold air outbreak arrives (currently modeled for the November 8-12 window), the resulting temperature differential is projected to produce heavier initial bands than in 2025.
- **Wind fetch orientation:** WNW favoring interior lake-effect bands (fetch of 90+ miles across Lake Michigan into Michigan interior counties including the Springfield corridor)
- **Upper air pattern:** Polar jet displaced northward through mid-October, then southward migration accelerating in early November per the NCEP CFS ensemble
- **ENSO state:** Weak La Niña favors slightly more variable Great Lakes winter with above-average lake-effect activity

## Fuel demand elasticity model

During the first 48 hours of a heavy band (Grade 2+ NWS classification, 6+ inches accumulated, 15+ mph wind gusts):

- **Gasoline volume spike (regular 87):** +8 to +12 percent above the trailing 4-week baseline
- **Gasoline volume spike (premium 93):** +2 to +4 percent (less elastic)
- **Diesel volume spike:** +5 to +9 percent
- **Rock salt sales:** 3-5x baseline (from ~$4K weekly baseline across three stores to $12K-$22K peak days)
- **Ice melt sales:** 2-3x baseline
- **Coffee/hot beverage:** +18 percent at Greenfield (highest deli traffic increase)
- **Bread/milk pre-storm rush:** +25 percent 24 hours before forecast band
