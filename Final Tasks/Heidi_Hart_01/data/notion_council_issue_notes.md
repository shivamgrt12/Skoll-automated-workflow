## 3. Monroe Avenue Precedent - Reference File

**Source:** Springfield DOT Engineering Traffic Impact Study 2024-TE-Monroe
**Publication date:** June 15, 2024
**Principal Investigator:** R. J. Sokolowski, PE, PTOE (License MI-8842-C)

- **Corridor studied:** Monroe Avenue between 32nd Street and Beltline Road, District 4
- **Baseline period:** October 2024, pre-buildout
- **Post-buildout period:** Q1 2025, six months after Speedway-anchored retail buildout at 3800 Monroe Avenue opened November 2024
- **Baseline weekday count (Monroe / 34th Street, primary reference):** 4,120 vehicle trips per weekday
- **Post-buildout weekday count (Monroe / 34th Street):** 5,438 vehicle trips per weekday
- **Empirical finding:** **32 percent cross-traffic increase within 6 months** at primary count location
- **Applicant projection (pre-buildout Nov 2023):** 'no adverse traffic impact' - materially inconsistent with observed
- **Pedestrian-crossing incidents baseline:** 2 near-miss, 0 vehicle-pedestrian contact
- **Pedestrian-crossing incidents post-buildout:** 5 near-miss, 3 vehicle-pedestrian contact (2 minor injury)
- **Study conclusion:** parallel C-2 to C-3 rezoning applications along matching corridors should be assessed against Monroe empirical baseline

**Diana Kowalski margin note (transferred from chair copy):** "the Monroe precedent is our best answer to the Aegis no-impact claim; make sure Heidi carries it into the room."
