## 1. Base Proposal

Authorize an annual $250,000 Small Business Development Fund distributed to Springfield small businesses (fewer than 25 FTE, gross revenue under $3M annually per applicant) as capital-improvement grants, matching-fund awards, and technical-assistance credits. Fund is administered by the Springfield Chamber of Commerce under Council oversight, replenished from general fund reserves at the start of each fiscal year, and reports quarterly to the SBED Committee.

Fund awards are competitive; typical award range $2,500-$25,000. Fund is not intended to substitute for state or federal small business assistance; matching-fund awards may be paired with Michigan Small Business Development grants (Chamber Q4 grant deadlines list).

## 2. Amendment 1 - Marchetti (D6) - Per-Award Cap at $10,000

**Sponsor:** Councilman Marchetti (D6)
**Filed:** September 28, 2026
**Text:** "Amend Section 3 to cap the maximum per-award amount at $10,000 gross, inclusive of any matching-fund component, notwithstanding the language of the base proposal."

**Stated rationale (Marchetti):** ensures broader distribution across a larger applicant pool; guards against a small number of awards absorbing majority of fund.

**Analysis:**
- Base proposal typical award range $2,500-$25,000; a $10K cap eliminates the upper half of the typical range
- Prior year FY 2025 comparable dev fund distributed 18 grants averaging $11K each with 4 grants at $18K-$22K to capital-improvement projects
- Capping at $10K likely prevents fund from supporting mid-size capital projects (refrigeration retrofits, EV charging install, tank replacement)

**Position:** Neutral, with **counter-proposal to raise cap to $25,000** which preserves the upper half of the base range while acknowledging Marchetti's distribution concern. This lands as a compromise position that keeps Marchetti in the vote.

---

## 3. Amendment 2 - Klein (D1) - 30 Percent Downtown Set-Aside

**Sponsor:** Councilwoman Klein (D1)
**Filed:** September 30, 2026
**Text:** "Amend Section 5 to reserve 30 percent of the annual fund for applicants whose principal place of business is located within the Springfield Downtown Development Authority boundary."

**Stated rationale (Klein):** downtown storefronts are disproportionately impacted by ongoing streetscape construction and should receive priority.

**Analysis:**
- The Downtown DDA boundary excludes all three Hart-owned stores (District 4)
- The Downtown DDA boundary also excludes 84 percent of D4 small businesses per prior year mapping
- Precedent argued: SBED-2026-08 downtown storefront renovation matching grant, $75K annual, already funds downtown-only assistance
- A 30 percent set-aside creates a district-boundary distortion that penalizes non-downtown applicants (D4 constituents) who already fund the general reserves that source this fund

**Position:** **Oppose.** The downtown-specific need is already addressed by SBED-2026-08 storefront grant. Layering a downtown set-aside on top of a general small-business fund creates a double-priority for the same geography and undermines the D4 constituent case for this fund.

---

## 4. Amendment 3 - Torres (D5) - Quarterly Reporting Requirement

**Sponsor:** Councilman Torres (D5)
**Filed:** September 29, 2026
**Text:** "Amend Section 7 to require quarterly reporting to the Small Business & Economic Development Committee, including applicant demographics, award amounts, and rejection reasons, with the first report due 90 days after fund activation."

**Stated rationale (Torres):** transparency; enables Committee to identify distribution patterns and adjust criteria.

**Analysis:**
- Torres consistently files quarterly-reporting amendments across dockets; language is standard boilerplate
- Reporting burden on the Chamber administrator is modest (~4 hours per quarter)
- Amendment strengthens Council oversight; supports evidence-based reauthorization at next FY budget cycle

**Position:** **Support.** No downside; strengthens fund accountability.

## 5. Recommended Position (for Diana's Monday circulation)

**Support base proposal + Amendment 3 (Torres quarterly reporting); Oppose Amendment 2 (Klein 30 percent downtown set-aside); Neutral on Amendment 1 (Marchetti $10K cap) with counter-proposal to raise cap to $25,000.**

Reasoning:
- Base proposal serves the D4 small-business constituency for which Heidi was elected
- Torres reporting amendment strengthens accountability without harming the fund's operating characteristics
- Klein downtown set-aside creates unjustified geographic distortion; downtown need is already served by SBED-2026-08
- Marchetti cap concern is legitimate on distribution grounds but $10K is too restrictive; $25K compromise preserves upper range while addressing distribution
