## 2. Applicant's Central Claim

The Applicant's Section 3 traffic impact statement states that the proposed convenience and fuel service will produce **no adverse traffic impact** on the Warren Road corridor or on adjacent cross-streets. Projected trip generation is 810 vehicle trips per weekday, of which the Applicant claims approximately 62 percent are 'pass-by' trips already present on Warren Road under existing conditions.

The Applicant explicitly does not consent to conditions requiring pedestrian-crossing improvements, corridor traffic-signal upgrades, or right-turn dedicated lane construction as a condition of rezoning approval.

The Applicant states: "The Applicant is not aware of any parallel-corridor precedent that would contravene this assessment."

---

## 3. Position To Take

The Applicant's claim of no adverse traffic impact is **empirically inconsistent with the Monroe Avenue corridor precedent**, which is the operative same-block precedent for District 4 unsignalized-cross-street corridors with a single retail anchor above 3,500 gross square feet within one-quarter mile of a residential school district boundary and an existing unsignalized pedestrian crossing. Warren Road at 2400 satisfies all four conditions.

Recommended position: **Oppose the rezoning as filed**, or in the alternative, condition approval on (a) pedestrian-crossing signalization at Warren Road / 26th Street, (b) right-turn dedicated lane construction on the westbound approach, and (c) a Springfield DOT-controlled post-buildout monitoring window with automatic corridor traffic-signal upgrade trigger at cross-traffic increase above 20 percent.
