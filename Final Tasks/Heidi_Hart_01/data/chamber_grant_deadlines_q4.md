## Active Q4 grant windows — overview

Six active or upcoming grant windows carry Q4 2026 deadlines relevant to Springfield small storefront operators. Three are directly applicable to Heidi's three-store portfolio individually. Two are downtown-corridor only and do not apply. One is a rolling-window state tax credit that aligns with the tax relief position paper.

## Grant 1 — Michigan Small Business Development Grant (state level)

- **Program name:** Michigan Small Business Development Grant (MSBDG)
- **Administering body:** Michigan Economic Development Corporation (MEDC)
- **Application window:** October 15 — November 30, 2026
- **Award ceiling:** $25,000 per applicant entity
- **Match requirement:** 25 percent applicant cash or in-kind contribution
- **Eligibility ceiling:** Storefronts under $3 million annual gross revenue (individual entity, not consolidated ownership)
- **Eligible uses:** Capital improvements (new refrigeration, POS systems, safety upgrades, ADA improvements, energy efficiency), staff training, marketing spend
- **Ineligible uses:** Operating expense (rent, payroll, inventory), owner distributions, debt service
- **Application portal:** MEDC business gateway (medc-grants.example.gov/msbdg)
- **Contact:** MEDC Small Business Advocate, Ellen Reyes, ellen.reyes@medc.example.gov, (517) 555-0128
- **Notes for Bell portfolio:** Greenfield Fuel and Market at $1.8M individual revenue eligible; Schaefer Quick Stop at $950K eligible; Warren Road Express at $1.1M eligible. All three qualify individually since MEDC counts by store entity not combined ownership. Recommended focus for a single application in this cycle: Greenfield POS refresh (existing terminals are 6 years old, warranty expired Q2 2026) or Schaefer walk-in cooler replacement (compressor failure log shows 4 events in 12 months). Sam Abbott estimated Greenfield POS refresh at $18K in his Q3 ops review; walk-in cooler replacement quoted at $22K by two vendors.

## Grant 2 — Springfield Downtown Renaissance Grant (city level)

- **Program name:** Springfield Downtown Renaissance Grant (SDRG)
- **Administering body:** City of Springfield Economic Development Office
- **Application window:** November 1 — December 15, 2026
- **Award ceiling:** $10,000 per applicant
- **Match requirement:** 50 percent applicant contribution
- **Eligibility:** Downtown corridor businesses only, defined as parcels within the Downtown Special Assessment District (SAD) map
- **Bell portfolio applicability:** None. None of the three Bell stores fall within the SAD boundary. Greenfield is in the Northeast Commercial Corridor, Schaefer in the Southwest Highway Zone, Warren Road in the East Central Neighborhood Commercial Overlay.
- **Notes:** Include in the tax relief position paper as evidence that downtown-only programs leave the neighborhood corridor stores without matched grant access, which strengthens the tax relief case for smaller stores.

## Grant 3 — Michigan Retail Modernization Credit (state tax credit)

- **Program name:** Michigan Retail Modernization Credit (MRMC)
- **Administering body:** Michigan Department of Treasury
- **Application window:** Rolling through end of fiscal year (state FY closes September 30)
- **Award structure:** Tax credit up to 15 percent of qualified capital improvements, non-refundable but carries forward two tax years
- **Eligibility:** Any Michigan retail entity with a documented Michigan sales tax license and physical storefront under $5M annual revenue
- **Eligible improvements:** Refrigeration, HVAC, fuel dispensing equipment, POS/payment technology, security systems, energy efficiency upgrades
- **Cap:** $150,000 in qualified improvements per entity per tax year (yielding max credit of $22,500)
- **Application:** Filed with annual state tax return as Form MI-4700 Schedule R
- **Notes for tax relief paper:** MRMC is the model Springfield could adapt at the municipal level. The 15 percent capital improvement credit line in the FY 2027 tax relief proposal (TR-03 in airtable_tax_relief_supporting_data) is directly modeled on this state precedent. Chamber has endorsed the concept in principle. Fold into the position paper as: "Springfield's proposed capital improvement credit mirrors Michigan's existing MRMC framework, providing local reinforcement of a policy already in state law."

## Grant 4 — Michigan Rural Grocer Support Fund

- **Program name:** Michigan Rural Grocer Support Fund
- **Administering body:** MEDC in partnership with Michigan Food Retailer Association
- **Application window:** December 1, 2026 — January 31, 2027
- **Award ceiling:** $50,000 per applicant
- **Eligibility:** Grocery or convenience stores serving USDA-designated food desert census tracts
- **Bell portfolio applicability:** Warren Road Express partially eligible (serves census tract 8412.06 which meets Low Income Low Access threshold). Greenfield and Schaefer not eligible.
- **Notes:** Worth exploring for Warren Road only. Would require food desert designation attestation letter from MEDC's rural food access office. Not a T0 item.

## Grant 5 — Springfield Small Business Recovery Reserve

- **Program name:** Springfield Small Business Recovery Reserve
- **Administering body:** City of Springfield Small Business Office (Directed by Council SBED committee)
- **Application window:** Currently paused pending Council approval of FY 2027 budget line
- **Award ceiling:** $5,000 per applicant
- **Eligibility:** Any Springfield storefront regardless of corridor
- **Notes:** This is the fund line item Heidi's dev fund vote on October 6 renews at $250,000. The eight thousand dollar reserve grants for FY 2026 were fully distributed by August. Reserve reopens post-dev fund vote if approved. Position paper reference: "The Recovery Reserve is a demonstrated fit for stores like the Warren Road corridor, where 18 grants averaging $11,000 were distributed in FY 2025 with an 83 percent retention rate at 12 months."

## Grant 6 — Michigan Employer Training Reimbursement

- **Program name:** Michigan Employer Training Reimbursement (METR)
- **Administering body:** Michigan Department of Labor and Economic Opportunity
- **Application window:** Continuous, quarterly reimbursement cycle
- **Award structure:** 50 percent reimbursement of eligible staff training costs, up to $5,000 per employee per year
- **Eligibility:** Any Michigan employer with a documented training plan
- **Notes for Bell portfolio:** Applies to Sam Abbott's ongoing Greenfield staff development. Quarterly reimbursement claim already filed Q2 2026 for $2,400 (approved). Q3 claim to file by October 31 for approximately $1,850. Nathan handles the filing.
