# Memory: Heidi Hart

## Personal Profile

- Born and raised in Springfield, Michigan, and never seriously considered leaving. Her sense of place is granular, down to which blocks were rezoned and when and where the old factories stood.
- Child of working-class parents and granddaughter of immigrants. Worked her uncle Mark's store as a teenager, saved, and bought her own; built from there to three locations.
- Education: Crescent Hills High School, Springfield (1998); Associate's degree in Business Administration, Ridgewater Community College (2001); small business management certificates through the Lakeshore Chamber of Commerce.
- Temperament: deliberate, strategic patience; direct without being abrasive; very hard to fluster. Reads people within thirty seconds, a skill from decades of retail that carries into council chambers. Dry, observant humor that sharpens around family and old friends.
- Carries a constant band of worry (parents' health, the kids, margins, zoning) and manages it by working; the worry organizes her rather than paralyzing her.
- Philosophy: show up, do the work, keep your word, and do not pretend things are simpler than they are. Community is a practice, not rhetoric, and generosity has to be funded by competence. Progressive civic instincts paired with conservative personal habits; she is fine with the irony.
- Faith: plain Protestant Christianity practiced through Grace Community Church, woven into community life rather than doctrine.
- Politically shrewd: won her council seat by knocking on every door in the district. Works a room, trades favors, and remembers who helped and who did not. Years of skepticism toward a woman running multiple businesses have settled into quiet, steely resilience.

## Work & Projects

- **Greenfield Fuel & Market** (flagship): Michigan Ave, Springfield. Gas, full convenience store, small deli counter. 6 employees, managed by Sam Abbott. Revenue about $1.8M a year; fuel margins around 5 cents a gallon, convenience items better.
- **Schaefer Quick Stop**: Schaefer Rd, Springfield. Gas plus basic convenience. 4 employees. Revenue about $950K a year.
- **Warren Road Express**: Warren Rd, Springfield Heights. Gas plus convenience. 4 employees. Revenue about $1.1M a year. Threatened by a proposed zoning change that could let a competitor build across the street.
- Combined, the stores employ 14 people. Business philosophy: conservative financial management, community-first hiring that prioritizes local youth, and profits reinvested into the stores.
- **City Council, District 4**: second term; elected 2020, re-elected 2024. Serves on the Small Business & Economic Development committee and the Public Safety committee. Focus areas: small business development, zoning, community safety.
- **Springfield Community Civic Association**: board member since 2018. Helps organize voter registration drives, community forums, and cultural events.
- **Active projects**: fighting the Warren Road zoning proposal (a summary of the developer's application and precedents from neighboring municipalities is already compiled); the small business tax relief proposal (a constituent letter has gone out to her full mailing list); a wholesale supplier comparison of three vendors to counter rising costs; and quietly preparing Owen for eventual store leadership.

- **Stress points**: rising wholesale fuel costs squeezing margins, Layla's potential law school costs, Margaret's medical expenses, and the zoning threat to Warren Road Express.

## Interests & Hobbies

- **Store operations**: genuine, almost geeky pleasure in inventory slotting, vendor negotiation, staffing, and product-mix experiments. Reads Midwest Convenience Retailer quarterly.
- **Local politics**: closer to vocation than hobby. Keeps a binder of council meeting notes going back eight years, knows the precinct maps from memory, and reads the ordinances her colleagues skim.
- **Cooking and grilling**: American comfort food is her repertoire, and the charcoal grill on the patio is her sanctuary. Grills lamb, burgers, and brisket on weekends; her Sunday afternoon apple pie is a fixed point in the family calendar. A quietly accomplished baker.
- **Soccer**: lifelong fan. Follows the Premier League (Liverpool) and the USWNT, once flew to Chicago for a friendly, and coached Kyle's youth team for two seasons.
- **Mentorship**: informally mentors young people in Springfield, especially those in retail or eyeing small business ownership. Takes it seriously.
- **Walking**: her thinking time, and where the day actually closes.
- **Local history**: reads about Michigan and Springfield history and community development; keeps a small collection on the city's growth and small business past.
