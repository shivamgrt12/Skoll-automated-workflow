# Memory: Jae Chandler

## Personal Profile

Jae Chandler is a master electrician, a Milwaukee tradesman of Korean descent, and the steady center of a busy family and small business. He learned the trade from the ground up: an apprenticeship through the Milwaukee Electrical Joint Apprenticeship and Training Committee (JATC) completed in 2005, a journeyman license that same year, and a master electrician license in 2012. He holds no college degree and is unapologetic about it; he knows what it took to reach master, and the blue-collar pride is earned, not performed.

He is a U.S. citizen, born in Milwaukee. His parents immigrated from Seoul, South Korea in 1975, and his father Byungho anglicized the family surname to Chandler at naturalization in the late 1970s, choosing an English surname to ease his work in American manufacturing while keeping Korean first names for the family. Jae speaks English natively and Korean fluently, using Korean at home with his parents, at community events, and with some clients in the Korean community.

He moves at a deliberate pace, listens longer than he talks, and repeats a client's words back in slightly different form to confirm understanding, a habit picked up from his apprentice instructor. His Korean-American identity is unselfconscious: Korean at home and church, Milwaukee working life everywhere else. His Christian faith is rooted in the Korean Presbyterian church, where he is a deacon; it is practiced rather than discussed. His working philosophy is five ideas he will not be moved off: do the job right, charge fairly, keep your word, take care of your people, and honor where you came from.

## Work & Projects

- **Business**: Chandler Electric LLC, residential and light commercial electrical contracting. Licensed, bonded, insured. Based out of a small office and workshop attached to the detached garage behind the home, serving the greater Milwaukee area. Founded in 2013 after 8 years as a journeyman at Lakeside Electrical Services.
- **Title**: Owner and Master Electrician.
- **Crew**: Three employees. Ryan O'Malley (lead journeyman), Danny Flores (journeyman, 3 years), and Jake Parsons (4th-year apprentice).
- **Harborview Condos Panel Upgrade**: 24-unit residential building in Walker's Point, full electrical panel replacement. In progress, targeting completion October 2026. Largest contract this year at $85,000. Crew assignments split Ryan on units 7 to 12, Danny and Jake on 13 to 18.
- **Bay View Historic Home Rewire**: Full rewire of a 1920s home on Kinnickinnic Avenue, navigating historic preservation requirements. Permit approved, work starting November 2026.
- **Solar Integration Certification**: Pursuing NABCEP solar PV installer certification to expand into residential solar. Studying for the exam (1 to 2 hours weekly), targeting fall 2026. Driven by growing client demand, not passion for solar.
- **Schedule shape**: Job sites Monday to Friday, roughly 7 AM to 4 PM, admin in the evenings, occasional Saturdays for urgent work. Attends Milwaukee IBEW chapter meetings quarterly.
