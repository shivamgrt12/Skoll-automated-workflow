Who I Am

Angela M. Peterson, PhD
Marine Ecologist

Marine Biologist, Research Scientist, Ocean Advocate

I am a research scientist at the Pacific Reef Research Institute in Honolulu, Hawaii, where I lead the Phase III Long-Term Coral Reef Health Monitoring Program. My work centers on quantifying coral cover, bleaching dynamics, and benthic community change at three Oahu monitoring sites over multi-year time scales.

I earned my PhD in Marine Ecology from Bayside Graduate Institute in 2020, where my dissertation examined coral recovery trajectories following thermal bleaching events in Hawaii's nearshore reef ecosystems. My committee was chaired by Dr. Keanu Palani, my current PI.

Beyond the research vessel and data sheets, I believe reef science has to connect to the communities that live alongside these ecosystems. I lead PRRI's public outreach program, which reached over 1,200 community members in 2025 through aquarium events, school workshops, and coastal walks.

Hanauma Bay — Stations A, B, C

Healthy fringing reef ecosystem with live coral cover ranging 38–44% across three stations. Low bleaching (under 2%) and strong seasonal recovery pattern observed through 2025–2026 survey cycle. Station C shows the highest cover at 44%.

Status: Healthy

Waikiki Reef — Station C Bleaching Alert

Alert issued November 6, 2025. Bleaching reached 5.1%, crossing the Phase III 5% threshold — the first threshold exceedance in our monitoring period. Rising trend: 6.2% (January 2026), 7.2% (February 2026). Monitoring frequency increased to bi-monthly. Urban stressors compound thermal stress vulnerability.

⚠ ALERT ACTIVE

Lanai Lookout — Baseline Established

New Phase III monitoring site added December 2025. Baseline live coral cover 37.5% averaged across two transects at Station A. Low bleaching (2.3%), excellent visibility (24 m), and stable substrate conditions indicate a relatively undisturbed reef environment.

Status: Baseline Active

Water Temperature & Bleaching Dynamics

Peak sea surface temperatures of 27.4–27.5°C observed September–October 2025 correlate with elevated bleaching at Waikiki Reef and Hanauma Bay Station B. Temperature decline to 24°C+ facilitated recovery at Hanauma Bay, while Waikiki Reef Station C shows continued stress accumulation despite cooler water.

Grant: CCF-2023-HI-0047
