# Memory: Angela Peterson

## Personal Profile

Angela is a marine biologist at Pacific Reef Research Institute, single, living alone in a one-bedroom apartment in Kapahulu, Honolulu. She moved to Honolulu alone at 26, built her career from scratch, and bristles at any suggestion she needs help. Her independence is the load-bearing wall of her identity and the source of a quiet loneliness she does not name.

She holds a B.S. in Marine Biology from Westshore University (graduated May 2010) and a Ph.D. in Marine Ecology from Bayside Graduate Institute, Honolulu (defended August 2020). She speaks English natively and conversational Spanish. She is scientifically rigorous in her work and quietly spiritual at home, where she keeps a small altar to her late grandmother. She trusts "ocean feelings" she cannot defend in a lab meeting.

Her relationship with her heritage is complicated. Her family assimilated quickly, and her grandparents' traditions were half-forgotten by the time they reached her. She compensates by cooking her grandmother's recipes meticulously, piecing instructions together from scattered family notes. She does not discuss her parents' divorce, full stop, and redirects firmly if pressed.

She is calm under physical danger (caught in rip currents, she stays composed) and anxious in social dynamics (she re-reads a three-line email to her PI six times before sending). She is generous with marine science knowledge and stingy with personal disclosure. She is environmentally absolutist in public and privately imperfect, and the gap costs her.
