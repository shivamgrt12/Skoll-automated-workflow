# Memory: Katie Lee

## Personal Profile

Born Lee Ka-eun in Seoul. Immigrated to the United States at twelve with her mother Jung-hee after her father's death. Settled first in northern New Jersey, then to Boston for college. Holds a BS in Biology from Wellesley College and an MD from Tufts University School of Medicine. Completed OB/GYN residency at Brigham and Women's Hospital and joined the attending staff after a year as chief resident.

Carries the steadiness of someone who has spent years catching babies in the dark hours of the morning. She lowers her voice when a room goes wrong. She is precise about everything, from a sneaker knot to a one-sentence answer to a hard question. She is gentler than her training suggests, and her bedside manner is the actual sound of her listening, not a technique. She is more comfortable being useful than being known, and David has learned to ask twice when he wants the real answer.

A quiet, lifelong Korean Presbyterian. Attends First Korean Presbyterian Church of Cambridge with Jung-hee when the call schedule allows. Fasts during Lent. Prays privately. Faith is personal, not performative.

Code-switches fluidly. Clinical English at work. Korean at home with her mother. A mixed dialect with David and the kids. She thinks of herself as Korean and American in the order the room requires.

Believes the body is not a metaphor, that filial duty is a load-bearing wall, that teaching the residents is the same work as caring for patients, and that perfectionism has cost her more than she meant to spend.
