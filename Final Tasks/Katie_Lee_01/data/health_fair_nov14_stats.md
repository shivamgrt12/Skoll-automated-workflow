## Counseling one-on-ones - follow-up outcomes

Four private counseling conversations at the station. All four with Korean-language preference. Two resulted in follow-up scheduling at Brigham OB for December 2026 (Dr. Lee panel).

## Volunteer feedback for next year's setup

Three structural notes from the volunteers who staffed the booth with me:

1. Handout table was placed too close to the walk-through aisle; visitors could not linger without blocking traffic. Move the handout table two feet back next year.
2. Korean-language signage was smaller than English signage; some elderly visitors did not identify the booth as Korean-friendly on first pass. Match font sizes next year.
3. Counseling station curtain was translucent under booth lighting. Silhouettes were visible from three feet away. Replace with fully opaque curtain material.

## What did not happen (honest note)

- The prenatal counseling station had lower foot traffic than the 2025 fair (13 conversations that year vs 4 this year). Two possible explanations, no way to disambiguate from the data alone: (a) the curtain visibility above may have discouraged privacy-sensitive visitors, or (b) the community has become more comfortable scheduling directly at Brigham through the Korean-language patient portal launched in May 2026, reducing the fair-driven demand.

- The IUD counseling flyer we had planned for the booth did not arrive from the print vendor in time. We did not distribute a substitute; visitors who asked were routed to the Korean-language patient portal for a phone consult.
