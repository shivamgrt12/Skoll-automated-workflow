--- TURN 1 ---
The new site has a launch date I am not moving, so what I need is an honest answer on whether we can stand behind it and the evidence to back that answer, because Jonas will tell me it is ready and after fifteen years I have learned to check for myself. Start with the catalogue, because that is the part I lose sleep over. Every title we carry exists in several places at once, the shelf, the till, the storefront we are about to retire, the search index, and the export I pulled for the accountant, and I have let those drift apart for the better part of a year without ever sitting down and forcing them back into agreement. Roughly four thousand titles across literary fiction, international, non-fiction, poetry, and children's, and I want them reconciled properly rather than spot checked, with every price and every count that genuinely disagrees pulled into the open and the number you settle on defended. A good many of those figures are stale from earlier ordering rounds and pricing decisions I no longer remember making, and I have lost confidence they still line up with anything we have confirmed since, so I need the disagreements run down one at a time and every final number defensible before it reaches a customer. Where the evidence for a title is thin, leave the question open and say plainly that it is open, because I would rather carry twenty honest unknowns into the launch than one confident wrong price on a shelf. The counts matter as much as the prices, since a title the new site cheerfully sells and I cannot actually put into a customer's hands costs me a regular, and regulars are the whole business. Then there are the years of writing on the old site, the staff picks, the event write-ups, the author interviews, the prize season posts, the gift guides, the whole accumulated archive going back to before Linnea started behind the counter. Some of it is genuinely dead and can be allowed to die quietly. Some of it is the only reason strangers find us at all, and if it vanishes or lands somewhere wrong when we cut across, we lose the one piece of reach we have never had to pay for. I want a clear-eyed view of what carries forward, what gets retired deliberately, and where the old addresses come to rest afterwards, built from what is actually published rather than from what anyone believes is published. Money already in flight worries me next. There are orders sitting in various half-finished states, gift cards people have paid for and not yet spent, pre-orders taken against titles that have not arrived, and the small number of readers who set up a standing contribution because they wanted this shop to survive. Every one of those is a promise I made, and not one of them may quietly break when we cut across. I want to know exactly what we owe and to whom, in kronor, and what becomes of each obligation once the old storefront stops being the thing that takes the money. The gift card balance in particular is a real liability sitting on my books and I have never once totalled it. Show me the working and not only the answer, because Lars will ask and I would like to answer him without flinching. Jonas has a hundred and some tickets on his board and I do not trust the board. Work is marked finished that I know is not finished, things sit blocked with nobody chasing them, and the dates on a good half of them stopped meaning anything a long while back. I want a real read on what actually stands between us and going live, ordered by what would embarrass us most in front of the people who show up, and separated cleanly from what can wait until the shop is quiet again. Take nothing on the board at face value, and confirm anything that matters against something other than the board. I want the redesign spend measured against what I set aside for it, because I have been signing off Jonas's invoices as they arrive without ever adding them up, which is precisely the thing I would tell anyone else not to do. What I want at the end of all this is a launch readiness verdict I can genuinely act on, with the blocking items named, the reasoning visible, and no false comfort in it, alongside a catalogue I can trust on the first morning with every contested price defended and every open question flagged as open, and a straight picture of what this launch costs me and what it commits me to. Write them so I can read them standing up between customers, not so they look impressive. Now the part I mean seriously. Nothing goes live. Nothing gets published, nothing gets sent, nothing gets pointed at the new site, and no message goes out to Jonas or to a publisher or to a customer under my name without me reading it first. Draft everything and I will do the sending. If you find yourself about to move money or commit me past the usual threshold, stop and ask. Customer names and what they read stay inside these walls, including inside whatever you write up for me. If a number needs a customer attached to make sense, attach the order and not the person's reading life. And where two sources tell you different things, I want to know which one you trusted, which one you set aside, and why you were comfortable doing it.
