# Memory: Lucia Weaver

## Personal Profile

Lucia was born in Portland, Oregon, in 1983 to Dorothy, a music teacher, and Robert, a small-press editor who died in 2018 of a sudden heart attack. She arrived in Sweden at 19 in 2002 on a literature exchange and never left, and she was naturalised as a Swedish citizen in 2008 while keeping her American citizenship. Her identity sits comfortably in both places: American by temperament in directness and humour, Swedish by daily life and seasonal sensibility.

She holds an M.A. in Comparative Literature from Uppsala University (2008), spent a 2005 exchange term in Edinburgh, and completed the Företagsrådgivarna small-business owner course, a 12-week evening programme in Uppsala, in 2010 before opening the shop. She reads in English, Swedish, German, and slow French. She is privately reserved, not shy, with a dry American humour she lets surface only when the room is right. She processed her father's death over a year of therapy in 2018 and 2019 and manages well now, though early summer can still feel heavy.

She is unmarried, no children, no romantic partner at present, and content with that arrangement. She owns one good dress, one good coat, and very little else she does not use weekly.

**Physical**: About 168 cm (5'6"), lean build from daily walking and cycling. Ash-brown hair worn shoulder length, often pinned up at the shop. Grey-blue eyes. Small silver reading glasses on a thin leather cord. A faint ink stain on the left hand on journal weekdays. Dresses in dark wool roll-necks and trousers in winter, linen blouses and dark skirts in summer, a single navy wool coat, leather lace-up boots year round, and the canvas shoulder bag she has carried since her master's years.

**Big Five (working read)**: Openness 8/10 (reads widely across languages, curates with nuance), Conscientiousness 8/10 (fifteen years of solo operation with careful records), Extraversion 4/10 (reserved by default, warm with regulars and close circle), Agreeableness 6/10 (warm but holds clear lines on suppliers and customers), Neuroticism 4/10 (steady after the 2018 and 2019 therapy work, early summer still carries weight).

## Work & Projects

Lucia owns and runs **Weaver & Book**, an independent literary bookshop on Svartbäcksgatan in central Uppsala. She opened it in 2011 with savings, a small inheritance, and an unused business line of credit at Handelsbanken she has never had to draw. The shop holds roughly 4,000 titles across literary fiction, contemporary fiction, poetry, and curated non-fiction in English, Swedish, German, and a small French shelf. Hours are Tuesday to Saturday, 10:00 to 18:00, extended to 20:00 on event evenings. Closed Sunday and Monday.

The shop rent is 18,000 kr per month on a 5-year lease renewed in 2024, basement storage included. Annual gross revenue is roughly 2.4 million kr. Lucia draws about 45,000 kr per month net. Strong sales September to December (university term, prize season, Christmas), weak June to August (summer quiet).

She works with 8 to 10 Swedish and international distributors. Publishing relationships are personal, built on fifteen years of careful ordering and event partnerships. Spring and autumn publisher catalogues drive the two main ordering windows.

Current projects in motion: the new website with a freelance designer (Webflow, Stripe, target launch October 16, 2026); a cultural fund grant application for an author event series (deadline October 2, 2026); the 15th anniversary celebration on October 17, 2026 with an author panel and local press; a quiet evaluation of a monthly bookshop newsletter under the Weaver & Book name. Long-running side practice: translating a German novella into English at roughly a paragraph a week, kept private, not for publication.

Customer-facing initiatives Lucia protects: the lending shelf for regulars, student discounts, careful small-press pricing, and co-hosted events with the Uppsala University literature department and the municipal library.
