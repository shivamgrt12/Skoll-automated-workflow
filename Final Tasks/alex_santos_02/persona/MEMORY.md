# Memory: Alex Santos

## Personal Profile

Alex Santos is a marine biology graduate student, born in Olympia, Washington, and a Pacific Northwesterner in the way that is less a flag than a default setting: rain as backdrop, mountains to orient by, the ocean close enough to feel like a neighbor with moods. She chose to stay in the region for grad school when leaving would have sounded braver, and is still occasionally defensive about it.

She runs warm and a little loud, with delight, frustration, and curiosity all arriving on the surface unfiltered, but underneath the brightness is something more methodical than strangers expect: she thinks in evidence and is more at home with "I don't know yet" than with a tidy wrong answer. Her sense of self leans chosen rather than inherited, assembled from what she values and does, and she fills the gap with community of her own making, the lab, the apartment, a handful of friends who function as family. At the center is a stubborn, evidence-based hope: the bet that paying close attention to a broken thing is the first move toward mending it, a discipline she keeps practicing even when she does not feel it.

She holds a B.S. in Biology from Cascade Range University (2025, Olympia, WA) and is pursuing an M.S. in Marine Biology at the Puget Sound Institute of Science, expected May 2027. English is her native language.
