The shoreline is full. You just have to look closer.

A community outreach project of the Coastal Ecology Lab, helping the Salish Sea community read its own rocky shores and watch how a warming ocean is changing them.

What we do

We turn one lab's intertidal survey work into something a family at the visitor center can hold onto. No degree required, just curiosity and a low tide.

🌏

Guided shore walks

Free low tide walks at local beaches where you meet the mussels, sea stars, and snails that hold the rock together.

📊

Citizen counts

Simple, repeatable quadrat counts that anyone can learn, feeding real observations back to the survey record.

🌍

Plain language science

Talks and species cards that explain what ocean warming is doing to the shore, without the jargon wall.
