About

The work

Graduate research assistant in the Coastal Ecology Lab under Dr. Sarah Lindstrom, twenty hours a week of survey collection, lab processing, and analysis on the ocean warming project.

The question

Does summer water temperature shift which invertebrates live where on the rocks? My thesis follows mussel beds, barnacles, snails, and sea stars across a thermal gradient.

The place

A Pacific Northwesterner by default setting. I stayed in the region for graduate school because the questions I care about are all within a tide window of home.

Research

Thesis

Effects of ocean warming on intertidal invertebrate community composition in Puget Sound. Field data complete, currently in analysis and writing, expected May 2027.

Key result

Across three focal shores, mean mussel cover fell from sixty one percent at the coolest visit to forty four at the warmest. Temperature explained about a fifth of community variation.

Methods and code

Fixed transect quadrat surveys, diversity indices, and ordination, all reproducible in R. Analysis scripts and the survey pipeline live on my GitHub.
