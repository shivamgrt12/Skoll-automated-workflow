--- TURN 1 ---
The whole stretch from Thanksgiving hosting through the quiet at my own place after the tree comes down is going to eat me alive if we do not sit and lay it out honestly in one place, because turning thirty is folded into Grandma's table for the whole Nelson side, into the weekend I finally spend in Boston with Dad and Aunt Nancy, into Christmas Eve on Wethersfield, into the calm at home the morning after, into the tail of the run training I still have to see through, and the last stretch I tried to carry in my head I ended up double booked against my own therapy hour and Ryan's shift landed on the wrong side of a family thing. I want a single trustworthy picture of the whole season, one I can open in a quiet hour and know where I stand across the hosting, the travel, the gifts, the money, the small parts that belong only to me, and the two health windows I am not allowed to move. Start with the Thanksgiving table because that is the one that will get away from us first, and Grandma has already begun her rotation debate over roast versus turkey without asking anyone. Everyone across the Nelson side is coming, plus the handful of Grace folks Grandma quietly adds to the list without telling us, plus people driving in from Boston, from New Haven, from a friend of Ryan's stranded off shift, and a couple of the little cousins whose parents will forget to mention allergies unless we ask twice. Hold the shape of the day for me, meaning who is bringing which dish and in which pan, what carload is landing at what hour, what will actually fit in Grandma's small oven and what has to route through mine, what Diane is willing to carry and what she should not be carrying because her CNA week has already been brutal, what Ryan can grab on his way in from East Hartford, and where Aunt Laura lands between the salon closing and dessert, and treat Grandma's kitchen as the small kitchen it actually is. The Boston weekend with Dad and Aunt Nancy has to be built the way I want it to feel and not the way it is easiest to book, meaning a place with a real kitchen in a walkable neighborhood near them, a schedule that lets Dad and me cook one long meal from Aunt Nancy's book without the two days getting sliced into short visits, and a return that gets me back home for one calm night before the workweek starts again, and his side has been quiet on a couple of the details, and the freshest word from Aunt Nancy shifted the day I thought we were cooking, so please trust the newer line and tell me clearly what you had to set aside. Threaded through all of it is the gift and card list, which has grown well past what I can hold in my head, so I want a real ledger of every person I owe something to across immediate family, the extended cousins on Diane's side and on Dad's side, the Grace circle around Grandma, Camille, my four providers who each get something small, the bookstore owner on Park who remembers my order, the bakery owner who knows my order without asking, and the handful of people I still send a card to even when we have drifted, with a picked idea for each name, a source that is a small independent shop when I can find one, a defensible cap per person so no single gift eats the season, a running total against a whole season number I will not let myself go over, and a ship by date that gets everything landed before the wrapping night. On the money side I need the whole stretch laid honestly against what I actually have, meaning the Thanksgiving groceries plus the Boston travel plus the lodging plus the gifts plus the Christmas food plus the small thing I might want to do for turning thirty plus the annual Roth housekeeping I always mean to do and always push down the road, all summed against the surplus I run in a normal month and against the emergency line I have been climbing toward and refuse to break through, with a clear number I can spend and a clear number I cannot fall below, and if it does not add up I want to see it not add up in writing rather than by feel. The two health windows sit inside that same stretch and they are not moving, so build the plan so they hold their ground and everything else routes around them, and please keep those dates off any note they do not need to sit on. Where two pieces of information about the same person or the same date disagree, tell me the one you kept and the one you set aside, because I have been juggling this in three places at once and I have lost confidence in the older lines. Bring all of it home as a picture I can open once and trust, meaning a single household plan for the whole season that carries the hosting and the travel and the wrapping and the calmer days together, a gift and card ledger built for the people and not for the totals, and an honest cash picture through the whole stretch, and hold a quieter thread for me at the edge of it about turning thirty that I can look at when I am ready and not before, with room for whatever I may want to put on paper for Grandma or for Dad or for the eight year old version of me that is still around here somewhere. Nothing goes out under my name until I have seen it, and please treat every booking and every purchase as a draft until I say the word.
