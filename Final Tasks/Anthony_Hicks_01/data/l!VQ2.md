# Ridgeline Driver Blog — Safety Bulletins Archive

Chronological archive of safety bulletins posted to the internal driver blog by the
Ridgeline safety team. Bulletins are the operational signal drivers are expected to
absorb between wiki updates. When a bulletin conflicts with a newer wiki entry, the
wiki wins.

---

### July 6, 2026 — Bulletin 2026-07: Summer Tire Pressure Watch

Hot pavement in Southeast corridors is throwing tire pressure readings high. Bleed to
the truck's cold-set spec. Do not leave a rig sitting on the sun side of a lot for
more than four hours without a walk-around.

### July 22, 2026 — Bulletin 2026-08: Rolling Restart Guidance

Reminder that adverse driving conditions permit up to a rolling three-hour extension
of driving time. Document conditions in the tablet at the time of extension.
*(Note: this bulletin has been superseded by the November 12, 2026 wiki update. The
current standard is up to two additional hours, thirteen hours total driving in a
shift. Do not apply the three-hour rule.)*

### August 15, 2026 — Bulletin 2026-09: I-40 Dispatch Message Lag

Drivers on the I-40 corridor between Little Rock and Nashville have reported dispatch
message lag of 15 to 25 minutes. IT is working the queue. In the meantime confirm any
time-sensitive dispatch by phone.

### September 3, 2026 — Bulletin 2026-10: HOS Clock Sync After Fuel Stop

Drivers should verify HOS clock state after any fuel stop over 30 minutes. If the
clock has reset, relog before pulling away. IT has patched the pattern once and a
second patch is in test.

### September 22, 2026 — Bulletin 2026-11: Independent Stops on I-65 South

Two independent stops on I-65 south of Birmingham have been flagged for security
incidents this quarter. Drivers should not overnight at TS006 or TS015 pending safety
team review.

### October 4, 2026 — Bulletin 2026-12: Fuel Card Scanner Freeze

Winter operations reminder. Fuel-card scanner has failed at a handful of pumps in
cold weather. If the scanner will not read, wait for the pump vendor to reset before
paying. Do not use personal card. *(Note: this bulletin has been superseded by the
October 28, 2026 wiki update, which now instructs personal-card fallback with
reimbursement up to $200 per event. Follow the wiki.)*

### October 18, 2026 — Bulletin 2026-13: Cold-Start Tablet Reboots

Tablet reboot pattern below 20F cab temperature is confirmed and firmware fix is in
progress. In the meantime, keep the cab climate warm the last hour before driver
handoff. Report every incident so IT can size the fix.

### November 1, 2026 — Bulletin 2026-14: I-75 Northbound Icing Watch

Icing forecast on the I-75 corridor across Tennessee and Kentucky through mid-February
2027. Fuel to full before pushing north of Knoxville. Anti-gel additive available at
approved chain pumps only.

### November 20, 2026 — Bulletin 2026-15: New Driver Assignment Rules

Reminder to dispatchers that drivers in their first 90 days may not be assigned to
independent overnight lots without terminal sign-off. See the corridor operating rules
in the driver policy wiki.

### December 8, 2026 — Bulletin 2026-16: I-40 Dispatch Lag Not Fixed

Cold snap loaded the dispatch broker again and the 20-minute lag pattern returned on
I-40. Redesign has slipped. Confirm time-sensitive dispatch by phone through end of
February 2027.

### December 22, 2026 — Bulletin 2026-17: Winter Fuel Fallback Reminder

Restating the October 28, 2026 wiki update. If the tablet fuel-card scanner fails,
pay out of pocket and submit reimbursement within 14 days up to $200 per event. Do
not park unfueled.

### January 5, 2027 — Bulletin 2027-01: Cold-Start Reboot Firmware ETA

Firmware fix for the cold-start reboot pattern has slipped to late January 2027.
Corridor most affected is I-40 through Tennessee and Arkansas. Report every incident.
