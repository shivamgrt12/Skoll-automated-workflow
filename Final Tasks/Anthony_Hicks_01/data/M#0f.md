# Ridgeline Freight Solutions — Driver Policy Wiki

Internal policy reference. Applies to all Ridgeline drivers, terminal staff, and
dispatch personnel. This wiki is the newest authoritative source for driver operating
rules. When a bulletin or an older memo conflicts with this wiki, the wiki wins.

---

## Hours of Service

**Last updated: November 12, 2026**

- Standard driving window: 11 hours after 10 consecutive hours off duty.
- On-duty window: 14 consecutive hours after 10 consecutive hours off duty.
- 30-minute break required within the first 8 hours of driving.
- **Adverse driving conditions extension: up to 2 additional hours of driving time, not
  to exceed 13 hours total driving in a shift, and only when weather, visibility, or
  road conditions could not reasonably have been known at the start of duty.**
  (Superseded prior guidance that allowed a rolling 3-hour extension. That prior
  guidance is no longer in effect and drivers who apply it will fail HOS audit.)
- 34-hour restart resets the weekly cycle. Rolling 7-day maximum: 60 hours. Rolling
  8-day maximum: 70 hours.
- Split sleeper-berth provision: 7 plus 3, or 8 plus 2, permitted only for drivers
  cleared by the safety team through the split-berth memo.

## Winter Fuel Guidance

**Last updated: October 28, 2026**

- Fuel to at least three-quarters tank before any overnight park in a corridor segment
  with forecast temperatures below 20F. Idle fuel burn under winter blend can move a
  tank read by ten percent overnight.
- Winter-blend diesel required in Midwest corridors November through March. Southeast
  corridors operate on standard diesel year round unless a forecast cold snap is
  posted on the driver blog.
- Anti-gel additive is Ridgeline-approved at Pilot, Love's, and TA branded pumps.
  Independent stops are not approved for anti-gel additive without terminal sign-off.
- **Fuel-card fallback under freeze: if the tablet fuel-card scanner fails at a pump,
  drivers pay out of pocket and submit reimbursement within 14 days, up to a $200 cap
  per event. Do not park unfueled.**
  (Supersedes the older "wait for scanner replacement" guidance that led to two frozen
  starts in February 2026.)

## Corridor Operating Rules

**Last updated: December 3, 2026**

- Recommended overnight corridors: I-65, I-20, I-75, I-40 mainline. Detours off
  mainline for overnight park require dispatcher acknowledgment.
- Prohibited overnight lots: any lot flagged safety or security by the safety team in
  the current stop atlas. Lots may not be used regardless of driver preference or
  personal ratings.
- New driver assignments: dispatch may not assign a driver in their first 90 days to
  an independent lot without terminal sign-off.
- Rest area versus truck stop: rest areas are permitted for the 30-minute break but
  not for full overnight park in the mainline corridors above unless no truck stop
  parking is available within a 45-minute reroute.

## Escalation

**Last updated: September 18, 2026**

- Roadside mechanical: dispatch first, then Ridgeline roadside contract line.
- Medical event on route: dispatch first, then 911 if warranted.
- Weather hold: dispatcher decides based on the safety team's live corridor watch. A
  driver may unilaterally hold for weather and dispatch will support that decision if
  documented within four hours.
- Suspected security threat at a stop: exit immediately, dispatch within the hour,
  and file a stop-incident tag in the fleet log.
