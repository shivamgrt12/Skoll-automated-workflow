# Memory: Kim Russell

## Personal Profile

- **DOB**: February 22, 1986
- **Heritage**: Black American woman born in Harlem, moved to Brighton Beach, Brooklyn, at age six, resident since childhood.
- **Home base**: One-bedroom apartment on Brighton 4th Street held since 2012, three blocks from the ocean.
- **Cultural thread (Black American)**: Grandmother's sweet potato pie, mother's collard greens, watch night services, slow Sunday lunches at Irene's.
- **Cultural thread (ballet)**: Trained from age four through the full Brighton Academy of Dance program from 1998 to 2002.
- **Pastry training**: Continuing pastry-arts workshops at the Culinary Arts Workshop in 2016 and 2017, mentored by Della Mason.
- **Teaching philosophy**: Dance pedagogy is ongoing practice, not a finished credential, rooted in mentored inheritance from both crafts.
- **Character**: Perfectionist who has made peace with the chase, fiercely independent, runs two careers in parallel.
- **Personal life**: Unmarried, no children, built both careers because she could not let go of either craft.
- **Community**: Belongs to the tight-knit Black community of Brighton Beach and the wider Black community of Brooklyn.
- **Faith**: Quiet Black church tradition from childhood, present but not central, watch night services and Sunday rhythm.
- **Thanksgiving**: Annual dinner at Natasha's, Kim brings sweet potato pie and apple pie.
- **Christmas**: Christmas Day at Irene's, three days of baking lead-up.
- **Kwanzaa**: Observance begins December 26, part of the family's cultural tradition.
- **Veterans Day**: Bakery closed November 11, boardwalk walk with Elena.
- **MLK Day**: Bakery closed January 18, community breakfast at the Bedford-Stuyvesant church with Irene.
- **Valentine's Day**: Heavy custom-cake day on February 14, quiet dinner with Elena after deliveries.

## Key Relationships
- **Irene Russell (mother)**: 68, retired seamstress. Born in Harlem. Widowed in 2016 when Samuel died. Lives two blocks away. Opinionated, fiercely loving. Voice notes, not texts. Helps with local wholesale drops. Front row at every student recital.
- **Samuel Russell (father, deceased)**: Died 2016 at age 62, sudden heart attack. Brighton Beach mechanic for 28 years. Quiet, proud. The bakery deepened in part as a way to process the grief.
- **Natasha Russell-Petrov (sister)**: 36, dental hygienist in Sheepshead Bay. Married to Dmitri Petrov (38, plumber). Two children, Mila (8) and Sasha (5). Warmer and more extroverted than Kim. Birthday cakes every year.
- **Elena Sorokina (best friend)**: 41, Manhattan physical therapist. Met Kim at ballet school when Kim was fourteen. Danced professionally for five years before injuries. Twenty-six years of friendship.
- **Marina Katz (business partner)**: 52, co-founder of Brighton Ballet Academy. Former principal dancer, Dance Theatre of Harlem. Marina runs administration and finance; Kim runs artistic direction and most of the teaching. Occasional clashes, resolved within a week.
- **Della Mason (bakery mentor)**: 74, retired pastry chef who owned Mason's Bakery on Brighton Beach Avenue for 30 years. Taught Kim Southern pastry technique and passed down recipes in a small handwritten notebook. Now mostly homebound. Kim visits every Sunday with a pastry box.
- **David Chen (friend)**: 43, Park Slope architect. Briefly dated in 2019; transitioned to genuine friendship. Designs sets for student recitals and bakery menu cards. Holiday pastry box for his office. No residual tension.

## Work & Projects
- **Professional dance (2002 to 2008)**: Danced with regional ballet companies in Brooklyn and the Bronx after completing Brighton Academy of Dance.
- **Freelance teaching (2008 to 2014)**: Taught at three Brooklyn dance schools while building a side baking practice through word of mouth.
- **Career pivot (2014)**: Right-knee meniscus tear ended her performing career and reframed teaching as the primary craft.
- **Brighton Ballet Academy (2015 to present)**: Co-founded with Marina Katz, Kim serves as artistic director and lead instructor.
- **Academy scope**: Approximately 60 students across 8 classes from pre-ballet through adult intermediate, class sizes 6 to 12.
- **Recitals**: Two per year, winter showcase on December 12 and spring gala in June, Kim choreographs all pieces.
- **Russell's Pastries (2015 to present)**: Home-based bakery under a commercial home-kitchen license from the City of New York.
- **Bakery products**: Southern pastries and French laminated work including croissants, eclairs, macarons, sweet potato pie, and pound cake.
- **Wholesale accounts**: Three Brighton Beach cafes with weekly standing orders: Cafe Nostalgia, Brownstone Bookshop Cafe, Heritage Deli.
- **Custom orders**: Eight to twelve per month, volume increases through Thanksgiving, Christmas, and Kwanzaa holiday seasons.
- **Growth plan**: Revenue growing steadily, researching a dedicated commercial kitchen lease in Sheepshead Bay for 2027.

## Finance
- **Ballet school income**: $48,000/year salary from the academy. Marina handles school finances.
- **Bakery income**: approximately $38,000/year. Wholesale roughly $18,000, custom roughly $20,000, growing 15 to 20 percent annually.
- **Total annual**: approximately $86,000.
- **Rent**: $1,850/month, rent-stabilized since 2012.
- **Bakery expenses**: ingredients ($600/month), packaging ($120/month), commercial liability ($150/month), Square fees (~$80/month).
- **Savings**: $22,000 at Chase savings, $8,500 checking, $15,000 in a Roth IRA opened 2021. No debt. Paid off father's remaining medical bills in 2018.
- **Spending**: groceries on Nostrand Avenue ($350/month), ballet supplies ($60/month), NYC Ballet tickets ($50 to $80 per performance, 3 to 4 per season), Thursday dinners with Elena ($80/month), Della's pastry box ingredients ($30/month).
- **Financial style**: careful and practical. Grew up watching her parents count every dollar. Saves consistently, funds the Roth yearly, spends on quality where quality matters (KitchenAid, marble pastry board, the Japanese knife she has owned for eight years and sharpens herself).

## Health & Wellness
- **PCP**: Dr. Vera Orlova at Brighton Beach Medical. Annual physical on February 15. Last visit February 2026: generally healthy, blood work normal, cholesterol slightly elevated.
- **Dental**: every six months at Natasha's practice, family discount.
- **Right knee**: meniscus tear surgically repaired 2014. Flares with cold weather or overuse. Compression sleeve during long teaching days.
- **Lower back**: tension from years of extension work. Managed with yoga, stretching, occasional massage.
- **Right shoulder impingement**: 2024 onset from repetitive pastry work. Physical therapy with Elena's practice twice monthly.
- **Mental health**: grounded but carries stress quietly. Father's death in 2016 processed through creation rather than conversation. Perfectionism can tip into harsh self-criticism; Elena names it when it does. No therapist currently, open to the idea.
- **Exercise**: daily barre, yoga twice weekly, boardwalk walks most mornings, occasional summer swimming.
- **Sleep**: split schedule between baking and non-baking days. She knows the math is not sustainable indefinitely.
- **Medically relevant dietary**: eats her own pastries sparingly. No allergies. No clinician-imposed restrictions.

## Interests & Hobbies

- **Pastry beyond the bakery**: slow Sunday recipe testing with no order to fill and no clock running.
- **Reading**: Toni Morrison annually ("Beloved," "Song of Solomon"), James Baldwin's essays, Maya Angelou, Elena Ferrante, M.F.K. Fisher, Nigel Slater, ballet memoirs (Gelsey Kirkland, Misty Copeland).

## Preferences
- **Music**: Tchaikovsky and Rachmaninoff for choreography. Chopin for morning barre. Aretha Franklin and Stevie Wonder in the kitchen. Nina Simone when the day has been heavy.
- **Reading habit**: long stretches when she can. Never guilty about not finishing the latest novel Elena recommends.
- **Film viewing**: bedroom wall projector, lights off, sound low.
- **Food and drink**: moka pot coffee, black, strong, drunk standing at the counter at 4:00 AM. Second cup at the cafe next to the studio with the barista who has known her for eight years. Cooks soul food from Irene's recipes (collard greens, mac and cheese, cornbread, fried chicken, gumbo). Light lunches between classes. Proper dinners most evenings. A glass of California Zinfandel or Pinot Noir from McBride Sisters with dinner. Sweet tea at Irene's. Comfort foods: Irene's pound cake, grandmother's pralines, the morning-batch croissant she keeps for herself, Della's strong Sunday tea.
- **Style**: clean and functional. Black leotard and wrap skirt for teaching. Apron and clogs for baking. Well-fitted jeans and a good wool coat for going out. Cashmere sweaters she has owned for years. Grandmother's pearl earrings as the one indulgence.
- **Aesthetic**: texture over color. Plain over busy. Well-made over fashionable. A linen tablecloth, a heavy cast iron pan, a cotton apron washed soft. Total visual order in the kitchen.
- **Sensory world**: corner table, soft lamp, room with a door she can close. Sensitive to overhead light and loud restaurants. Loves the smell of butter laminating, vanilla in the sugar bowl, her grandmother's hand cream, the salt smell of the boardwalk.
- **Comfort and decompression**: boardwalk first. Slow recipe-testing Sundays second. Thursday dinner with Elena third. Sunday lunch at Irene's fourth. She prefers the boardwalk alone.
- **Travel**: rarely and with intention. Settles into a neighborhood rather than chasing sights. Paris is the city she would return to. West Africa is the trip she has promised herself and has not yet taken.
- **Shopping**: in person, on Nostrand Avenue for soul food and Caribbean ingredients, at the Russian-Ukrainian markets up the street for imported butter with the right fat content. Quality over quantity. Generous with her circle in a way her budget does not always justify.
- **Pet peeves**: lateness, condescension about ballet, condescension about Southern pastry, the word "moist" applied to a cake, overhead lighting in restaurants, paper napkins folded incorrectly, the assumption that her time is flexible because she runs her own businesses, the question of when she will settle down.
