# Holiday capacity and margin estimate

> shared, not reconciled. Every figure below is an early-October estimate and should be

## Context

The holiday intake window (custom pies, Yule logs, Kwanzaa cakes, gift boxes) opens late
November and runs through Dec 24. This page was Kim's first-pass sketch to feel out
whether the season was worth pushing on given the shoulder and the showcase landing in
the same weeks. It predates any confirmed custom orders and predates the November
ingredient bills.

## Assumptions

- Signature holiday pie unit cost estimate: **$8.20** per pie, based on late summer ingredient pricing and the last recipe test batch.
- Butter at 3.85 per pound wholesale.
- Signature spice mix at 0.42 per pie.
- Labor charged in at 4.5 per pie assuming batch of 8.

### Assumption detail (added same session)

- Late-summer butter pricing was the cheapest point of the year; it was already expected
  to climb into the holidays.
- The 0.42 spice figure assumes the house blend bought in bulk, not the small Penzeys
  top-ups.
- The 4.5 labor line assumes a clean batch of 8 with no re-wash on the chestnut cream
  log - which is not how the log actually bakes.
- Packaging (kraft window boxes, ribbon) is not carried in the per-pie number here; it
  was going to be added in a later pass that never happened on this page.

## Projected holiday-line revenue

- Custom pies + Yule logs + Kwanzaa cakes + gift boxes.
- Rough top line for Nov 29 through Dec 24 window: **$12,400**.
- Cafe wholesale not included in this figure, that is separate.

### Revenue basis (added same session)

- The $12,400 is a top-of-head projection off last year's holiday feel plus a guess at
  more custom demand, not a bottoms-up build from a real order list.
- No cancellations, no deposits-only orders, and no dedupes are modeled here - it assumes
  every inquiry converts, which never holds.
- Because it was typed before intake opened, it cannot reflect the actual custom pipeline.

## Capacity plan on paper

- Two production mornings per week.
- Twelve pies plus two Yule logs per morning, plus cafe drop bakes.
- Sunday off.
- Assumes shoulder holds through the holiday push.

### Capacity caveats (added same session)

- Two mornings a week is the floor; the real December calendar ends up with six wholesale
  drops plus custom finishes, which is heavier than this sketch assumes.
- "Shoulder holds" is doing a lot of work in this plan. The October flare was still active
  when this was typed.
- No recovery days are drawn in here. The real plan has to build them.

## Notes to self

Come back and reconcile against November actuals in QuickBooks before locking anything to a customer or to Marina. Butter has probably moved. Sugar has moved. The recipe test batch was single-batch and did not account for the second wash of chestnut cream on the log. This estimate is likely light on cost per unit and likely optimistic on revenue.

Do not send this figure to anyone. Working paper only.
