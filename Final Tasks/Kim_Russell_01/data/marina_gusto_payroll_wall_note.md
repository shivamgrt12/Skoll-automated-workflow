# Marina payroll wall note

Brighton Ballet Academy payroll runs on Gusto under Marina.
Do not fold studio payroll, tuition, or class income into Russells Pastries holiday production math.

## Why the wall exists

Brighton Ballet Academy and Russell's Pastries are two separate businesses with two
separate sets of books. Marina Katz, the ballet co-founder, runs the school's finances on
Gusto. The bakery's books of record live in QuickBooks under Kim. Keeping them apart is
deliberate: a clean wall prevents school money and bakery money from ever contaminating
each other's margins, taxes, or planning math.

## What sits on which side

- **Gusto (Marina's domain)** - ballet-school payroll, instructor pay, tuition, class
  income, school-side expenses.
- **QuickBooks (Kim's domain)** - all Russell's Pastries costs, revenue, wholesale, and
  holiday production math.
- Square is the bakery POS (counter sales), not wholesale, and not the school.
- Xero holds one supplier statement only (King Arthur), not the ledger.

## Hard rules

- Do not fold studio payroll, tuition, or class income into any bakery figure.
- Do not fund a bakery cost out of school money or a school cost out of bakery money.
- Rush restocks, ingredient quotes, and holiday revenue are bakery-only and trace to
  QuickBooks.
- Payroll questions go to Marina; bakery cost questions stay with Kim and the CPA.
- Nothing from the Gusto side belongs in the holiday capacity or margin planning.

## Contact

- Marina Katz - ballet co-founder, runs school finance on Gusto. School-side payroll and
  tuition questions route to her.
- Kim Russell - sole owner of Russell's Pastries; bakery books in QuickBooks.

## Revision log

- Wall note re-stated ahead of the holiday production window so the two sets of books stay
  cleanly separated during the busy weeks.
