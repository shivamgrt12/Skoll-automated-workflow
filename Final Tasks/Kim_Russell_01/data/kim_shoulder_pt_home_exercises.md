# Shoulder + knee home exercises (Elena's handout, my copy)

Home program Elena gave me for the days between PT. This is the reference sheet I keep on the fridge - the actual appointment times live in my schedule note, not here.

> Reference sheet only. Appointment times are NOT on this page - they live in the
> personal schedule constraints note. This is just the home program between sessions.

## Right shoulder (impingement)
Do daily, gentle, stop before the pinch - never push into the arc that catches.

1. **Pendulum swings** - lean on the counter, let the arm hang, small circles. 1 min each direction.
2. **Scapular retractions** - squeeze shoulder blades, hold 5s, 10 reps. Twice a day.
3. **Wall slides** - forearms on wall, slide up only to where it's comfortable. 10 reps.
4. **External rotation with the light band** - elbow tucked, 2 sets of 12. Green band only, not the blue.
5. **Doorway pec stretch** - 30s hold, twice. Opens up the front after a lamination morning.

### Shoulder progression notes (Elena)
- Green band stays until Elena clears the step up; the blue is too much load on the
  impingement right now.
- On a flare day, drop the wall slides and external rotations - pendulums and gentle
  retractions only.
- Warm the shoulder before, ice after. Never ice before.

## Right knee (2014 meniscus)
For the noisy days, especially after two production mornings back to back.

1. **Straight-leg raises** - lying down, 3 sets of 10.
2. **Wall sits** - 20-30s, build up slowly, no deeper than a chair height.
3. **Heel slides** - 2 sets of 15, keep it smooth.
4. **Calf + hamstring stretch** - after standing days, 30s each.

### Knee progression notes (Elena)
- Wall sits build by seconds, not depth - never past chair height.
- If there's swelling, skip the wall sits entirely and elevate; that's a recovery-day
  signal, not a push-through signal.
- Standing partnering rehearsal counts against the knee the same as a production morning.

## Rules Elena drilled into me
- Ice the shoulder after any back-to-back lamination day. 15 min, not more.
- No overhead loading on a flare day. The bakery reach-ins get the step stool, not the arm.
- If the knee swells, it's a recovery day - floor teaching off, one demo max.
- Bands live in the studio bag so I actually do them.

## When to actually do these
- Home program is for the days BETWEEN PT sessions. On a PT day, Elena runs her own
  progression at the clinic; don't double up.
- Morning is best for the shoulder set - before the reach-ins and the shaping.
- The knee set goes after standing days, in the evening, before lights-out.

## Flags to raise with Elena at the next session
- Any new catch or pinch outside the usual arc.
- Nights the shoulder wakes me (the magnesium/Epsom routine is helping, note whether it
  held).
- Any day the knee swells after back-to-back mornings, so she can factor it into the
  recovery-day math.

## Equipment / reference
- Green resistance band (light) - active band for external rotations.
- Blue band (medium) - NOT in use yet, waiting on Elena's clearance.
- Epsom salts and magnesium are on the home grocery list for the soak-and-sleep routine.
