# Recipe development notes (personal R&D)

My own test-kitchen notebook for things I'm playing with off-cycle. Not on any wholesale sheet yet - these are experiments, ratios and impressions, nothing promised to a cafe.

> R&D notebook only. Nothing here is priced, promised, or on a wholesale sheet. If a cafe
> asks about any of these, the answer is "still testing." These are not menu items.

## Cardamom-orange morning bun (test v3)
- Base: standard croissant lamination, but add 1 tsp ground cardamom to the détrempe.
- Filling: brown sugar + orange zest + a whisper of cardamom.
- v2 was too wet in the center - pulled the bake 2 min early. v3: proof a touch less, egg wash lighter.
- Verdict: close. Della loved it. Try a candied-orange top next.
- v3 follow-up ideas: test a cold overnight proof to deepen flavor; the cardamom fades if
  ground too far ahead, so grind day-of.
- Shoulder note: this is a laminated item, so it only ever tests on a light morning.

## Rye-honey sandwich loaf
- 30% whole rye, 70% bread flour, honey for the crumb softness.
- Overnight cold ferment gives the best flavor but the crumb tightens - need to open it up.
- Idea: bump hydration to 78%, longer autolyse.
- Uses Doughlores (the starter) well - the rye blend feeds straight into this loaf.
- Next test: split-batch, one at 76% and one at 78%, same day, to isolate the crumb
  difference.

## Gluten-free almond financier (customer keeps asking)
- Brown butter is the whole game. Don't skip the resting-the-batter step.
- Tested with a 1:1 GF flour + almond meal - held together better than expected.
- Note: keep this line totally separate from wheat prep. Different day, deep clean first.
- Allergen discipline: separate bowls, separate parchment, and it never shares a shaping
  morning with laminated dough.
- If this ever goes on a sheet, it needs its own clearly labeled batch and a note to the
  cafe about shared-kitchen risk.

## Laminated dough scheduling reality-check
- Any new laminated item costs a full shaping morning, and my shoulder can't take two lamination days back to back.
- Rule for myself: never launch a new laminated product in a month that already has a heavy custom-order load. It waits.
- Corollary: December is off-limits for launching anything new. The custom pipeline plus
  six wholesale drops plus the showcase already own the calendar.
- Test days for laminated R&D go on light mornings only, never the day before or after a
  production morning.

## Test-batch discipline (added)
- Single-batch tests do not predict per-unit cost. The signature log especially - the
  second wash of chestnut cream never shows up in a one-off test.
- Write down actual gram weights, not "a handful." Future me needs the numbers.
- A recipe isn't ready for a sheet until it's held across three separate bakes on
  different days.
