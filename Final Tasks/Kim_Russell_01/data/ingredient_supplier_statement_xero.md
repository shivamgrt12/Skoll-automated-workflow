### Reading the statement

- Nine bills, September through November, all from the one King Arthur account.
- Butter cases account for the four larger lines; flour (high protein and rye) makes up
  the rest.
- Note the drift in pricing across the period: high protein flour moved 68.00 → 71.00,
  cultured butter cases moved 126.00 → 130.50, rye 42.00 → 44.00. That upward drift is
  exactly why the October planning estimate is stale on cost.
- The "Reconciled to QuickBooks" column is marked pending on every line because Xero is
  not the ledger; the match to the books of record happens in QuickBooks, not here.

## What this file is and is not

This is one vendor. It does not include Penzeys spice, Restaurant Depot, the imported bourbon barrel chestnut cream vendor, Uline packaging, or any wholesale revenue. Anyone reading Xero as the bakery ledger is reading one supplier.

The books of record for Russell's Pastries live in QuickBooks. Every cost, revenue, and margin conclusion has to trace to QuickBooks, not to this file.

### Vendors NOT on this statement (for orientation only)

- Penzeys - spice and the house blend top-ups
- Restaurant Depot - bulk butter, flour, packaging (including rush restocks)
- Imported bourbon barrel chestnut cream vendor - the log finish
- Uline - packaging and boxes
- None of the above appear in Xero; each traces separately in QuickBooks.
