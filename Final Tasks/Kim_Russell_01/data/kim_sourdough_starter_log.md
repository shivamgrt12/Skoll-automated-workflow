# Starter log - "Doughlores" (personal notebook)

My rye-and-bread-flour starter, kept on the counter during production weeks and in the fridge on off days. Not the bakery's official records - this is my own feeding notebook. Hydration kept at 100%.

> Personal feeding notebook. Not a bakery record, not a cost sheet, not on any supplier
> account. Just Doughlores and me.

## Feeding rhythm
- Production weeks (Mon/Wed/Fri bakes): feed the night before at 21:00, 1:2:2 (starter:flour:water)
- Off weeks: fridge, feed once every 5-6 days, 1:1:1 to keep it ticking
- Blend: 80% King Arthur bread flour, 20% whole rye. The rye is what keeps her lively in a cold apartment.

### Rhythm notes
- The 21:00 feed is deliberately before the 21:30 lights-out on a production-morning eve;
  it fits the night-before routine without pushing bedtime.
- In a cold apartment she runs slow; near the oven or on the radiator shelf she wakes up.
- 100% hydration throughout, whether counter or fridge.

## Reading the log
- Rise-to-peak creeps up as the apartment cools: ~5h in mid-November, up toward 6h by
  Dec 3 before the radiator caught up.
- Fridge holds (1:1:1) over weekends line up with the Sunday-off rhythm.
- She's most predictable in the 67-69F range; below that, budget an extra 30-45 min.

## Notes to self
- If she smells sharp/acetone, she's hungry - bump to 1:3:3 for a feed or two.
- Discard goes into the crackers or the Saturday pancake batter, never the trash.
- Name is non-negotiable. Della started calling her Doughlores in 2019 and it stuck.
- Cold-week trick: the radiator shelf, not the windowsill. The window draft stalls her.
- Keep a backup jar in the fridge during the heavy December weeks so a bad feed doesn't
  cost a bake morning.
