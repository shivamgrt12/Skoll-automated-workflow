I need our holiday wholesale pricing settled and the whole gift-box season ready to commit, and before I put my name to any of it I need the numbers underneath to actually hold. Here is the worry. The prices I have been carrying for the four wholesale accounts were set in earlier rounds against costs that have moved, and some of the figures I have been working from are stale enough that I have lost confidence they still line up with everything we have actually paid and billed since, so I need every one of them run down and made defensible before the December bread pricing locks with all four, because once the partners have it there is no walking it back. Start from what the suppliers have really been charging, not what I think they charge, because a couple of the core ingredients have crept and the per-loaf and per-box cost I have been quoting off is almost certainly behind reality. I want the true landed cost on each thing we sell wholesale and in the gift boxes rebuilt from what we actually paid, and where two records of the same cost disagree I want to know which figure you set aside and why. Keep in mind the gluten-free line carries its own separate ingredient stream that runs pricier and cannot be cross-counted against the wheat side, so hold those landed costs clean and apart or the whole margin picture goes soft. For the four wholesale relationships, each has its own agreed rate, its own weekly spread, its own signed terms, and its own history of what has really been invoiced, and those sources do not all agree with each other on at least a couple of the accounts, so I want the current real price per account confirmed against its terms and the December rate proposed off the rebuilt cost so I am holding margin and not quietly giving it away. One of the four is the newest and still finding its footing, and that is exactly the one I am most worried about underpricing into a hole we cannot climb out of, so give it a hard look. Keep it all in draft for me to review first. On the retail side, last season's gift-box orders ran across the main pre-order storefront and the backup that catches the overflow when the main one lags, and I am not sure every order was ever squared between the two, so I need the real season order count and dollar value reconciled across both, not a number that double counts or quietly drops the strays. Use that reconciled prior-season volume as the floor for what we plan to produce for the pickup and shipping window, so the projection has something solid under it rather than a hopeful guess. Once the costs are rebuilt and the prices are set and the box demand is honest, I want a clear picture of where the season leaves us in cash, the projected margin at the new wholesale rates and the box pricing, the usual holiday uplift folded in, and the fixed monthly load taken out, so I can see whether the season actually cushions the emergency fund or just looks like it does on paper. And I need the staffing answer in the same breath, because all of this only holds if we can physically make it. Look hard at what the current two-person coverage can carry through the December surge against the batch schedule the boxes and the heavier wholesale loads will demand, weigh that against what a third pair of hands would cost and whether the applicants already in the hiring pipeline are worth bringing on for the season, and tell me straight whether we staff up or grind it out. Where the evidence is thin, say so and hold the call open rather than forcing a verdict I will only have to unwind. Nothing here goes out or changes anything without my sign-off, so bring me a clear recommendation to approve rather than acting on it. What I want to come out the other side is a readiness picture I can act on, priority ranked, that tells me the December wholesale prices to hold per account and why, where the box season stands and what it still needs, and the staffing call laid plain, and underneath it a reconciliation I can defend line by line with the stale figures named and the trusted ones sourced, and a cash and margin projection set out clearly enough that I would stake the emergency fund on it. And where the evidence is thin, say so plainly and leave the call open rather than dressing a guess up as a decision I can bank on. Keep it tight and keep it honest. If something does not add up, I would far rather hear that than a clean number you had to squint to believe.
