# Memory: Hari Mooney

## Personal Profile

- **Full name**: Hari Mooney
- **Pronouns**: he/him
- **Born**: Newark, New Jersey
- **Occupation**: High school math teacher (Algebra II and AP Calculus) and chess team coach at Eastwick Preparatory Academy, Newark; nine years teaching experience.
- **Education**: B.A. in Mathematics, Briarwood University (2015). M.Ed. in Secondary Education, Halford State University (2018, completed part-time while teaching). New Jersey teaching certification in Mathematics, grades 7 to 12. US Chess Federation certified coach.
- **Languages**: English (native) and Marathi (fluent, used with parents and Pune family).
- **Cultural identity**: Indian-American. Parents Raj and Meera Mooney immigrated from Pune, Maharashtra, in 1988. Hari was born and raised in Newark, attended Newark public schools, and returned to teach in the city deliberately. Maintains strong ties to the Indian-American community in the Newark-to-Iselin corridor and to extended family in Pune.
- **Philosophy**: Strategic, patient, methodical. Believes any student can learn algebra if you find the right angle of approach. Carries his heritage quietly, keeps work and personal firmly separated, reads the room before speaking.

## Work & Projects

- **Role**: Math teacher (Algebra II and AP Calculus) and chess team coach at Eastwick Preparatory Academy, a Title I public high school in Newark with about 800 students, predominantly Black and Hispanic.
- **Teaching load**: 5 periods, three sections of Algebra II and two sections of AP Calculus, around 130 students total.
- **Chess team**: Eastwick Knights, 14 active members. Practices Tuesday and Thursday after school, 3:30 to 5:00 PM ET. Competes in the NJ State Scholastic Chess League and USCF-rated tournaments. Produced three state-ranked players in five years. Captaincy transitions from Marcus Thompson to rising senior Tyree Coleman for the 2026 to 2027 season after Marcus graduates at the close of the 2025 to 2026 school year.
- **Schedule**: School day 7:30 AM to 3:00 PM ET, Monday through Friday. Chess practice Tuesday and Thursday until 5:00 PM. Grading and lesson planning evenings and weekends.
- **Summer**: Tutors math privately through Brightpath Tutoring Network ($60 per hour, 10 to 15 hours per week, June through August); coordinator on file is Lena Pham. Runs a 2-week chess camp at Eastwick in July.
- **Side project**: Maintains a small personal AP Calc resource blog at `harimooney.org` (Webflow front end on Contentful, Cloudflare DNS, Algolia search, Google Analytics). Posts a worked problem most Sunday evenings after Iselin dinner. Audience is modest but growing.
- **Professional goal**: Wants to eventually become a school administrator (assistant principal, then principal) to influence education policy from within. Considering an Ed.D. program; the cost and time are daunting.
- **Side goal**: Grow the chess program enough to send a team to nationals. Budget is the recurring barrier.
