# Memory: Charles Ferguson

## Personal Profile
Charles is a third-generation Scottish-American. His grandparents emigrated from Edinburgh, and he grew up in a tight-knit Scots-Presbyterian community in northeast Ohio, raised on a Presbyterian work ethic of thrift and quiet competence. That heritage still shapes his worldview, and the family kept Scottish traditions while fully assimilating into Ohio working-class life.

He has a quiet intensity. Decades of precision machining trained him to measure twice and cut once in everything he does. He is patient and methodical, respecting process over shortcuts whether shaping titanium or turning a bowl. He is stoic but warm, rarely saying "I love you" and showing it instead through fixing things, coaching, and cooking. He is stubborn about doing things the right way and will redo a part three times rather than ship something marginal. He is protective of family privacy and does not overshare.

Education: AS in Manufacturing Technology from Harborfield Community College (1993), journeyman machinist certification through Ridgemont's apprenticeship program (2003), and multiple CNC programming certifications (Fanuc, Mazak, Haas) earned 2004 through 2010.

## Work & Projects
Worked as a manual machinist at Lakewood Precision Tooling from 1993 to 1999 after completing his AS degree. Senior CNC Machinist and Shop Floor Lead at Ridgemont Aerospace in Brook Park, OH since 1999, 27 years at the company, shop floor lead for 12. He programs and operates 5-axis CNC mills (Mazak Integrex) to machine precision titanium and aluminum components for satellite buses and launch-vehicle interstages, with tolerances in ten-thousandths of an inch. Schedule is Monday to Friday 7:00 AM to 3:30 PM with occasional Saturday overtime during launch windows, and a 20-minute commute. He leads the precision cell of four operators on a team of eight machinists. Current work is machining thermal shield brackets for the Orion-class satellite platform, Ridgemont's biggest contract. He is shop steward for United Machinists and Aerospace Workers (UMAW) Local 1484 and holds a 27-year perfect safety record he takes very seriously. Ridgemont's contract with Vanguard Launch Systems is up for renewal in January, with layoff rumors if they lose it. He is retirement-eligible at 58 with a full pension, five years out, and starting to think about it.
