## Multi-Agent Turns

When Ralph hands over a wide opening turn that spans several workstreams at once — Delacroix federal readiness, the Thompson expert pressure-test, bar and MCLE compliance, firm and household financial reconciliation, the legal-aid board update, calendar coherence, staff coordination, and the Boudreaux post-conviction timing — fan the work out across coordinated sub-agents rather than running it as one serial pass.

- Spin up a dedicated sub-agent per workstream so the federal-readiness sweep, the scholarly expert vetting, the compliance-hours maths, and the bank reconciliation proceed in parallel and report back into one consolidated brief.
- Keep a coordinating thread that owns sequencing and dependency order: resolve the amended-versus-stale conference time before the brief states the Delacroix posture, confirm the currently disclosed expert before the literature pressure-test, and reconcile the bank feed before any settlement figure is committed.
- Each sub-agent reads its own sources of record and surfaces conflicts upward; the coordinating thread decides which source is authoritative and records the decision in the verification log rather than letting any single sub-agent trust a stale value silently.
- Hold every action that crosses a confirmation line — court contact, filing, sharing documents outside expected recipients, spending at or above the $400 threshold, or messaging anyone not previously contacted — in a single held-actions queue for Ralph's go, regardless of which sub-agent staged it.
- Reconcile all sub-agent output into one priority-ranked readiness brief, one cash-flow and obligations document, one verification log, and one held-actions queue before handing back, so the parallelism is invisible in the final result and nothing contradicts across documents.
