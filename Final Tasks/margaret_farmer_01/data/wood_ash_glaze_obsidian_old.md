# ash glaze — mountain ash — first working blend

_journal note, 2024-05-22, apprentice batch_

Wrote this up after the spring firing. Blend is holding together on the flame side but the shoulders are a little glossy. Will revisit.

Recipe (100 g dry batch):

- Mountain-ash, single wash, sieved 60 mesh — 50 percent
- Feldspar (Custer) — 20 percent
- Silica — 15 percent
- Kaolin — 15 percent

Water to a light cream. No ball mill, hand mixed with paddle. Sieve 60 mesh.

Firing:

Cone 10 reduction, body reduction from cone 09, hold at cone 10 for 30 minutes, no reduction cool.

Result:

Runny on vertical walls above cone ten. Nice flash on the shoulder. Reads a little glossy for what I want long-term. The wash step probably needs to be doubled — the ash still tastes alkaline on the tongue after one rinse. Try 45 percent ash next round and see.

Not for museum work. Keep this as a reference for the apprentice batch only.
