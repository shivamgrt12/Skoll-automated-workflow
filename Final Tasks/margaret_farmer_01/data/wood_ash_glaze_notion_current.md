# Wood-Ash Glaze — Mountain-Ash Blend (current working recipe)

_Last edited 2026-08-14 by Margaret. Loaded into KL-0131 and KL-0132 for the museum reference tiles. This supersedes the older Obsidian note._

## Batch label

`ash_glaze_base / mountain_ash / cone 10 reduction`

## Recipe (100 g dry batch)

| Material              | Percent |
|-----------------------|---------|
| Mountain-ash (sieved through 80 mesh, twice-washed) | 45 |
| Custer feldspar       | 22 |
| Silica (325 mesh)     | 20 |
| Grolleg kaolin        | 13 |

Water to hydrometer reading 1.44. Ball mill 20 minutes. Sieve through 80 mesh again before application.

## Ash preparation

Mountain-ash comes from Mika's oak_kama_taki stack. Burn to fine grey, sieve while dry, wash twice with soft water, decant, dry on newspaper. Do not skip the second wash — the earlier Obsidian note only called for one wash and the surface ran too glossy under reduction.

## Firing

Cone 10 reduction. Body reduction from cone 08. Hold light reduction through cone 9. Down to cone 10, drop damper for reduction cool for 45 minutes.

## Result on the reference tiles

Semi-matte olive with warm ash flow on the flame side. This is the surface I am building the three museum vessels around. Reference tiles: GT-0116, GT-0119, GT-0124, GT-0125.

## Notes

- The older Obsidian entry from spring twenty twenty four has 50 percent mountain-ash and 15 percent kaolin. Do not use that ratio, it crazes on the shoulder of anything thicker than 8 mm.
- The corrected 45/22/20/13 ratio is what actually loaded into KL-0131 and behaved.
- If mountain-ash stock runs low before autumn, blend with chestnut_ash at up to 20 percent of the ash fraction. Do not substitute pine-ash, it flashes yellow under reduction.
