# Autumn open studio, twenty twenty six

_Draft newsletter, not sent. For my review before it goes out through Mailchimp. Segment variants for Kyoto and Kansai, Tokyo travel-in, Paris and European, and warm-but-quiet are all set up in Klaviyo._

Dear friends of the studio,

The autumn open studio at the Higashiyama Craft Collective falls on Saturday, November seventh, twenty twenty six. The doors will be open from mid-morning through late afternoon. There will be tea. There will be a small stack of new wood-fired work coming off the autumn anagama, and a modest selection of earlier pieces I have been holding for people who wanted to see them in the hand before they decided.

This year the work sits close to two threads. The Seasonal Table run for the Shibui exhibition finishes just before the open studio, and a small handful of those pieces will be on the shelves in the studio for anyone who prefers to hold something before it goes to a gallery. Alongside them will be the surface tests from the year's mountain-ash blend, the same blend the museum vessels are built around, though the museum vessels themselves stay in the studio. Those are not for sale and are not on view.

For collectors coming in from Tokyo, the Shinkansen puts you in Kyoto Station by late morning. The collective is a fifteen-minute walk from Higashiyama-Sanjo Station, or a short taxi from the station. For collectors coming from Paris and Europe, if you would like a longer studio conversation outside the open studio hours, please write and I will find you a quieter slot around the opening at Galerie Terre.

For those who have not been in the studio for a while, come in anyway. It is a slow afternoon, no pressure to buy, and I would like to see you.

Warmly,
Margaret

_Postscript. The newsletter goes out to the four segments in the following order. Kyoto and Kansai receive the message first, with priority for early browsing slots. Tokyo travel-in receives a version with train and taxi notes. Paris and European receive a version with an offer of a private studio hour timed to the Galerie Terre opening. Warm-but-quiet receive a shorter version that opens with a note that they have been on the list for a long time and are welcome back with no expectation._
