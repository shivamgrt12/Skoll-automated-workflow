# Mother's beef stew recipe

_From Carol Farmer, transcribed by Margaret sometime around the last Portland visit. Nothing to do with the studio, keep it in the personal folder._

Serves four. Cook slowly.

Ingredients:

- Beef chuck, 900 g, cut into large cubes
- Yellow onions, two large, rough chop
- Carrots, four, thick coin
- Celery, two ribs, thick slice
- Garlic, six cloves, crushed
- Bay leaf, two
- Dried thyme, one teaspoon
- Tomato paste, two tablespoons
- Red wine, one cup, whatever is open
- Beef stock, three cups
- Flour, three tablespoons
- Butter, two tablespoons
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Small waxy potatoes, twelve, halved
- Frozen peas, one cup, added at the end

Steps:

1. Season the beef and dust it with flour. Brown in butter in a heavy pot in batches, do not crowd.
2. Set the beef aside. Sweat the onion, carrot, celery in the same pot until soft.
3. Add the garlic and tomato paste, stir for a minute until it darkens.
4. Deglaze with the wine. Scrape the bottom.
5. Return the beef, add stock, bay, thyme. Bring to a low simmer, cover, hold at a bare bubble for two hours.
6. Add potatoes. Cook another forty minutes.
7. Add peas in the last five minutes. Salt and pepper to taste.

Notes from Mom: use whatever wine is open. Do not cook it hard. If it is boiling you have gone too far.
