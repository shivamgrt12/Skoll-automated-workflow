# Reading notes, Soetsu Yanagi

_Obsidian commonplace, personal. Not for public sharing._

Reading _The Unknown Craftsman_ again this year. The parts I keep circling back to.

- The idea that the maker's ego is a distraction from the work. Yanagi puts it more sharply than I would, but the intent lines up with how I want to feel at the wheel.
- His treatment of "irregularity" is easy to misread. He is not asking for uneven work as a style. He is asking us to leave room for what the material does. That is a different thing.
- I stay careful with the way "wabi-sabi" gets attached to his writing. He does not use it in the way it circulates now. When it appears in English commentary around him it usually means less than the writer thinks it does.
- The chapter on the tea bowl is the one I keep re-reading. It reads differently after a wood firing than it does after a gas firing. The scale of intention is smaller after a wood firing.

Note for self, keep this out of any statement I write for the museum. If Tomomi asks about influences directly, I can talk about Yanagi in conversation, but not in the wall label. The wall label needs my own voice, not a citation.
