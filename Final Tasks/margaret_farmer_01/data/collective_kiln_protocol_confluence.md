# HCC Kiln Protocol

_Confluence page, owner Mika Tanabe, last edited 2026-07-18. This document replaces every earlier protocol page and every older Trello card describing kiln rotation._

## Kilns

The Higashiyama Craft Collective operates two kilns.

- Gas reduction kiln, 0.9 cubic meter chamber, capable of cone 10 reduction. Runs approximately every second week during the working year.
- Anagama, wood-fire, single chamber with side stoke, capable of cone 12 to 13 with natural ash. Fires twice yearly, spring and autumn.

## Cost sharing

- Gas reduction: eight thousand yen per firing, split evenly between the members whose work is loaded. Booking is one member, one firing.
- Anagama: forty thousand yen per member per firing, covers wood, refractories, food during the loading and firing week.

## Booking rules

- Gas reduction slots open six weeks ahead on the Confluence page. First-come.
- Anagama loading crew is drawn from members who have committed to at least one full stoking shift and one unloading shift.
- Cancellation less than seven days out forfeits the slot.
- All wood must be split, stacked, and covered by the Monday before loading begins.

## Loading and unloading

- Anagama loading is a five-person shared job across three days.
- No solo unloading. The kiln stays sealed for a minimum of ninety-six hours after the last stoke.

## Firing responsibilities

- Every member on the loading crew is assigned a stoke shift. A shift is six hours.
- The lead coordinator holds the firing log and the pyrometer.
- Reduction cool is optional and must be agreed at the loading meeting.

## Refire and second-fire policy

- Refires are booked as normal gas reduction slots.
- The collective does not underwrite refires. Any second glaze fire is on the member.

## Museum and gallery pieces

- Members with commissioned work for permanent collections may reserve up to two consecutive gas reduction slots per month. Reservations must be made through the coordinator, not by direct calendar edit.
- Wood-fired commissioned work follows the normal anagama rotation. There is no separate priority.

## Wood sourcing

- Wood for the anagama is sourced by the coordinator. Members contribute a share proportional to their loaded work.
- Mountain-ash from Mika's oak_kama_taki stack is reserved for members who have signed onto the anagama crew.

## Contact

Mika Tanabe is the primary coordinator. Escalations to the HCC steering committee via the Confluence steering space.
