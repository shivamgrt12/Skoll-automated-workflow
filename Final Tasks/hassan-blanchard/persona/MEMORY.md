# Memory: Hassan Blanchard

## Personal Profile

Hassan was born and raised in southwest Atlanta by his mother Denise and maternal grandmother Mabel, with deep roots in the Black church community that raised him. He graduated from Westlake High School in 2025 and is the first person in his immediate family to attend college. His faith is intact but his church attendance has gone sporadic since starting school; Pastor Owens's check-ins are the thread he has not let go of.

He code-switches between three registers: his grandmother's church voice, the ATL vernacular of his neighborhood crew, and the professional diction he is learning at college. He can feel guilt about all three at different times, like he is performing no matter which one he picks. The Atlanta identity is specific to him: the neighborhood, the music, the cadence he has spoken since elementary school, and he reads the difference between Atlanta culture and generic labels fluently.

He overthinks social situations, then compensates with humor that lands most of the time; the misses get replayed at 2 AM. He laughs loudly and easily, which disarms people expecting a more serious version of him. Both versions are real. He shows up when it matters and disappears to recover, is at his best in small groups of three or four, and carries a watchful generosity: he notices who has gone quiet even when he is at capacity.

He has been the family's emotional bookkeeper since he was twelve, translating between his mother's worry and his grandmother's quiet endurance. He is good at it and tired of it, and he does not blame anyone for the weight. His core philosophy: reliability is the love, drama is optional. He treats his father's absence as a fact rather than a wound to keep prodding, but he is quietly afraid of becoming his father, who talked big about providing and disappeared; the fear shows up as over-reliability, saying yes to everything and telling no one.

He believes Mabel's life has a dignity the medical system does not always reflect, and that conviction half-consciously powers the nursing track. He carries an unvoiced question about whether nursing is his own path or just the one her diagnosis made emotionally legible; he has told no one, not Denise, not Marcus, not Aisha, not Pastor Owens. He resents the "you're so inspiring" framing from people who do not know his life, and he has stopped engaging with peers who treat financial aid status as social commentary. Questions about his father from people outside the situation get a short answer.

## Work & Projects

- Pre-nursing student on the B.S. Nursing track at Cedarbrook University, heading into his sophomore year in fall 2026. First semester (fall 2025) closed at a 2.8 GPA; the nursing program's clinical phase requires a 3.0 prerequisite GPA and financial aid renewal requires a 2.5 minimum, so GPA recovery is the central project of the year.
- Fall 2026 course load includes Pharmacology with Professor Okafor and Health Assessment.
- Part-time student worker with Cedarbrook University Dining Services: serving food, restocking stations, and cleanup after meal periods. Tuesday and Thursday evening shifts plus Saturday mornings, 12 to 15 hours a week at $11.50 an hour. He picks up extra shifts when offered, including over summer 2026 while staying near campus.
- One free meal per shift saves roughly $40 to $50 a week, which makes the job too valuable to drop even though evening shifts collide with study groups and the Saturday shift eats his sleep-in morning.
- Community service: 40 hours per semester required through the Cedarbrook Health Equity Program as a financial aid condition. Currently at 12 hours, volunteering at neighborhood health screening clinics.

## Finance

- Income runs roughly $600 to $700 a month from the dining hall job, deposited at Peach State Credit Union (checking and savings) via mobile deposit.
- Financial aid covers tuition, housing, and the meal plan: a need-based grant plus a small academic scholarship, renewable only with the GPA minimum and the service hours.
- Monthly expenses: about $80 phone bill (his own line on his mom's family plan), $50 personal supplies and toiletries, $40 to $60 groceries beyond the meal plan, $30 MARTA card, and $20 to $40 discretionary.
- Savings sit around $380 at the credit union; no real emergency fund.
- No debts yet. He dreads student loans if the GPA slips and the scholarship goes.
- The monthly $50 to $75 he sends Aunt Keisha for Mabel's expenses is tight and sometimes means skipping personal spending; the version of him that lets it lapse is a version he refuses to become.
- No credit card; he has been advised to get one to build credit and is scared of debt.
- Stress points: textbook costs of $200 to $400 a semester even with rentals, the standing fear of losing aid, and the guilt of not contributing more to his grandmother's care.

## Health & Wellness

- Generally healthy with no chronic conditions. No alcohol, smoking, vaping, or substance history.
- Intermittent tension headaches around midterms and finals, managed with ibuprofen and sleep.
- Mild seasonal allergies in spring; takes over-the-counter loratadine when it flares.
- Sleep runs 5 to 6 hours on weeknights with weekend catch-up. He knows it is not sustainable and has not fixed it.
- Stress is chronically elevated but he frames it as "being busy" rather than a mental health issue. He has not used Cedarbrook's free counseling sessions.
- Has been grinding his teeth at night lately; morning jaw soreness, not yet addressed.
- Exercise is intramural basketball and daily campus walking, nothing structured.
- Family health context: Mabel has Type 2 diabetes (managed with metformin and diet), early-stage congestive heart failure diagnosed in 2025, and occasional mobility issues. Denise has high blood pressure, medicated but aggravated by stress and long hours. Hypertension and diabetes run on both sides of the family, which partly motivates his healthier cooking.

## Interests & Hobbies

- **Cooking**: Learned from Mabel; Southern comfort food, oxtail stew, collard greens, cornbread from scratch, with healthier budget versions of his own. He treats it like a secret hobby, cooking in the dorm's communal kitchen near midnight; his high school crew would clown him, so the cooking stays for him. He has been chasing Mabel's oxtail stew for two semesters and has not quite gotten it right.
- **Basketball**: Intramural on Thursday nights, more stress relief than competition, though he plays harder than the league requires. It gave him a campus community he did not have to engineer.
- **Music production**: Lo-fi beats in GarageBand on the MacBook. The beats are not good yet, he has played them for no one, and the privacy is part of the pleasure.
- **Voice memo journaling**: 2 to 3 minute reflections a few times a week. He rarely listens back; the recording is the practice.
- **Sneaker culture**: Follows drops and resale markets, owns three pairs he rotates and cleans obsessively. Mostly aspirational browsing rather than buying.
- **Documentaries**: Health and social justice documentaries on streaming and YouTube.
- **Late-night library studying**: Genuine peace, not loneliness; the only time his brain stops running everyone else's problems.

- **Shopping**: Amazon for basics, Aldi and Walmart for groceries. Brand-loyal only where he has researched: Nike sneakers, CeraVe, the refurbished-store MacBook. Everything else gets bought on "good enough and cheap." Tracks every dollar.

- **Sensory world**: He notices sound first in any space. He loves the smell of garlic and bay leaf simmering at midnight and the specific smell of Mabel's house; he cannot stand fluorescent buzz in hallways, synthetic cleaner smell in the dining hall on Saturday mornings, or the dorm's cheap polyester sheets. The after-midnight quiet is the sensory hour he most wants to keep.
- **Travel**: Barely traveled; one flight, the high school orchestra trip to Washington D.C., and he remembers everything about it. Wants to see New Orleans and drive across the South with Jamal someday. Flies cheap and rarely, with lingering check-in anxiety. Fully fluent in MARTA-and-bus Atlanta.
- **Pet peeves**: Group projects where his careful effort gets treated as a free ride, 7 AM fluorescent hallways, and the Saturday morning dining hall plating.
