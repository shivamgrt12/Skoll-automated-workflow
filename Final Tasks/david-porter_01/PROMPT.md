--- TURN 1 ---
Before I sit down with Hartford for the insurance renewal and before I give Karen Wells an answer on the Providence Manor wholesale renewal, I need my whole money-and-commitments picture trued up, because right now I am running on a pile of numbers I stopped trusting a while ago and I refuse to walk into either conversation guessing. Start with the wedding cakes, that is where the real money and the real exposure live. I want every booking on the calendar from now through the end of next summer's wedding season pulled together into one honest picture, the couple, the date, the tier count and flavor, the price, what deposit actually landed, what balance is still owed, and where the flowers stand with Karen, because those bookings are scattered across the tracker, the CRM, the booking tastings, and the books, and every one of those sources was last touched by a different hand on a different day. I have a bad feeling that some cakes are sitting in one place and missing from another, that a couple of deposits got recorded wrong, and that at least one price on paper is not the price we actually agreed, so I want each booking checked against more than one source before you tell me it is clean, and anything that only shows up in a single system flagged loudly rather than quietly folded in as fact. Where a deposit or a balance disagrees between what the books say and what the sheet assumes, do not just pick the friendlier number, run it down and tell me which one is real and why, and if the evidence is thin then leave it open and say so instead of forcing a verdict. Then the wholesale side, because that is the other thing Hartford will ask about and the thing Karen Wells is waiting on. I have seven standing accounts and a monthly total I have been quoting for months, and I no longer trust that the per account figures, the past due items, and that headline total still agree with what we have actually been invoicing, especially since the flour went up fifteen percent and I never passed a cent of that through to the wholesale pricing. Providence Manor is the knot in the middle of it, they want roughly forty percent more banquet dessert volume, the renewal terms are not signed, and there are at least two different dollar figures floating around for what that account is even worth right now, so I need the current billed reality separated cleanly from anybody's proposal before I decide anything. Which brings me to the number Hartford actually cares about, last month's closed profit and loss and the food cost, because my food cost has been running over target on the flour and I want the real overshoot in dollars, not a percentage someone eyeballed, worked out against what we should be spending, so I can defend the figure across the table and know exactly how much of the pain is flour I could source cheaper somewhere else. Verify that month's picture against itself, the sales lines, the cost of goods, the operating side, and if any working file I have been leaning on looks like it wandered in from some other business entirely and does not match the bakery, throw it out and say why rather than letting a stray number poison the whole read. Once the ledger is clean and the P and L is defensible, I want the wholesale renewal packaged so I can decide on the spot, the current worth of Providence Manor laid next to their volume ask and next to the pricing I would need after the flour pass through, the options spelled out plainly with the tradeoffs, but nothing sent, nothing countered, nothing committed to Karen Wells until I have read it and said go, because that account is mid negotiation and I am not putting a number in front of her by accident. Same restraint on anything that costs real money, the flour switch, the cafe counter, none of that moves without my sign off. Fold in the tax picture too while the numbers are fresh, the next estimated quarterly that comes due against a realistic read of where the year lands, so the renewal decision and the cash it frees or costs are sitting in the same frame and I am not solving one and breaking the other. And if anyone has been poking around asking for the buttercream formula or the cake technique, and I think at least one has, the answer is a warm no, that recipe does not leave this kitchen. What I want at the end of all this is three things I can actually hold, a reconciliation of the full cake and wholesale ledger with every discrepancy and every orphaned booking called out and my best defensible number on each, a verified money and food cost read for the latest closed month I can carry straight into the Hartford renewal without flinching, and a Providence Manor renewal memo that lays out the decision cleanly and waits for my word before a single figure goes anywhere near their office.
