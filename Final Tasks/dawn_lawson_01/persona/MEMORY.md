# Memory: Dawn Lawson

## Personal Profile

Dawn Lawson is an International Master in chess who has lived in the Berkeley Hills with her husband Nathan since 1999. She grew up in the Midwest, earned an MS in mathematics at the University of Chicago in the 1980s, and built her career as a private coach, monthly columnist, and senior tournament player rather than as an academic. She walks Grizzly Peak every morning, plays Chopin and Rachmaninoff on a Steinway baby grand in the front room, and reads a paper newspaper on Saturdays with walnut levain from Golden Gate Bakehouse.

She is precise, intellectually exacting, and dryly funny. Her warmth is structural rather than effusive: she remembers what people said three months ago, defends them when they are not in the room, and creates space for them to do their best thinking.
