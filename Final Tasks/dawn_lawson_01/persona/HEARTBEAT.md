# Heartbeat: Dawn Lawson

## Recurring Events

### Daily

- 6:30 AM rise, French press strong, three cups before noon, the first one standing at the kitchen window.
- 7:00 AM Grizzly Peak walk, alone, no music or podcast, the route where problems sort themselves out.
- 9:00 AM check email at the desk, twice daily window, the second pass at 7:00 PM.
- 8:00 to 8:30 PM piano practice, Chopin or Rachmaninoff depending on the week.
- 7:00 PM dinner with Nathan, sharp, the non-negotiable hinge of the day.

### Weekly

- **Monday**: 8:00 AM lesson-plan review for the week, 2:00 to 6:00 PM private lessons (two students), Nathan at Bayside campus.
- **Tuesday**: chess study and writing morning, no lessons, occasional first-of-month classic literature reading group at the house hosted by Nathan.
- **Wednesday**: 2:00 to 6:00 PM private lessons (two students), Nathan at Bayside, evening at home with piano and reading.
- **Thursday**: 5:00 to 7:00 PM Bayside University chess club, Morrison Hall Room 206, demonstration game prepared in advance.
- **Friday**: 2:00 to 6:00 PM private lessons (two students), Grandmaster Review article work, Nathan writing at home.
- **Saturday**: 8:30 AM Golden Gate Bakehouse for walnut levain and coffee, 10:00 AM to noon Elena Volkov coaching, afternoon piano or chess study.
- **Sunday**: 6:00 PM Pacific video call with Ethan and Lily in Brooklyn, evening tea and conversation with Nathan.

### Monthly

- 1st of the month: Grandmaster Review article progress check, deadline always sits on the 1st in her head even when the calendar says the 16th.
- First Tuesday: classic literature reading group at the house, Nathan's invitation list.
- One Saturday a month: Salon Evening for six to eight academic friends, wine, cheese, a topic introduced without slides.
- Last Sunday of each quarter: Berkeley Chess Foundation small donation review with Nathan.

### Quarterly

- Steinway tuning by the technician, on a weekday morning before lessons begin.
- Tournament-calendar review with Elena and her parents, including which open events she should enter next.
- Joint household financial review with Nathan, including a quick read of the Vanguard money market and IRA positions.
- Catch-up with CPA Linda Nakamura around quarterly tax planning.

### Seasonal / Variable

- Autumn: Berkeley Chess Festival organisation, simul at Eastshore Community Library, intercollegiate match calendar at Bayside, US Senior Open consideration in October.
- Winter: holiday salon evening, Brooklyn visit to Ethan and Lily, family Christmas at home.
- Spring: garden planning (tomato varieties, trellis configuration), tournament travel as a player, university chess club end-of-year review.
- Summer: garden harvest such as it is, Lily's birthday in Brooklyn, lighter teaching weeks, more piano.

### Annual

- January: annual physical with Dr. Robert Yamamoto at Berkeley Internal Medicine for both Dawn and Nathan.
- February: Ethan's birthday on the 18th, card and a thoughtful book in the mail to Brooklyn.
- March: spring tournament season begins for her senior events.
- October: Nathan's birthday on the 11th (dinner at Maison Terre most years), Grandmaster Review Candidates Tournament analysis column, intercollegiate match against Bayside Institute of Technology, festival prep.
- November: Berkeley Chess Festival at the Bayside student union, Ethan and Lily visit week-long.
- December: holiday salon evening, family Christmas at home, Daniel's birthday on the 12th with a chess book in the mail.
