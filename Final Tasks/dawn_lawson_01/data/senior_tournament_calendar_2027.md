# Candidate 2027 Senior Tournaments

Rolling notes on senior events I would consider entering as a player through 2027. Not all of them are viable. This list is the frame Endgame and I whittle down before entry deposits go in. Deposit windows vary by event and each entry note flags the practical constraint.

1. **Western States Senior Open**. Reno, Nevada, February 12 through 15, 2027. Entry fee 165 dollars for the Under 65 section and 195 dollars for the Open Senior. Five-round Swiss, Senior 50-plus and Open Senior sections. Quick drive plus one flight leg from Oakland, fits the winter senior swing, low travel drag.

2. **Golden Gate Senior Invitational**. San Francisco, March 20 through 22, 2027. Entry fee 140 dollars. Four-round Swiss, Senior 60-plus, two-day format. BART commute, zero lodging cost, anchors the spring column. Highest expected value for the effort of any event on the list.

3. **National Senior Chess Championship**. Saint Louis, Missouri, April 14 through 20, 2027. Entry fee 285 dollars. Nine-round Swiss, Senior 65-plus qualifier. Three nights minimum plus travel, highest FIDE rating value on the calendar. Requires the tax refund window to have cleared before deposits go in.

4. **Pacific Coast Senior Open**. Portland, Oregon, May 8 through 10, 2027. Entry fee 160 dollars. Six-round Swiss, Open Senior. Pair with a visit to Daniel, he can host and the venue sits under thirty minutes from his neighborhood, so this is one of the cheaper events on the year for me.

5. **New England Senior Classic**. Boston, June 5 through 7, 2027. Entry fee 175 dollars. Five-round Swiss, Senior 60-plus. Pair with the Brooklyn window before Lily's July 18 birthday, a cross-country leg is only worth it if two events cluster.

6. **Empire State Senior Weekend**. Albany, New York, July 10 through 12, 2027. Entry fee 155 dollars. Four-round Swiss, Senior 60-plus. Sits eight days before Lily's fifth birthday, Airbnb in Brooklyn absorbs both ends. This is the tightest fit against the family visit and needs a hard call rather than a soft one.

7. **Chicago Autumn Senior Congress**. Chicago, September 24 through 26, 2027. Entry fee 195 dollars. Six-round Swiss, Open Senior. Strong field but overlap risk with the Berkeley Chess Festival prep window, and MS Univ Chicago alumni angle is not a factor for entry. Probable pass on schedule grounds.

8. **US Senior Open**. Kansas City, Missouri, October 8 through 12, 2027. Entry fee 320 dollars. Seven-round Swiss over five days, Open Senior. This is the big rating event of the year and the annual call I keep making late. Overlaps hard with the November Chess Festival prep window and the October Grandmaster Review deadline on the sixteenth. Requires an early decision by no later than August 15, 2027.

9. **Redwood Senior Rapid**. Sacramento, November 20 through 21, 2027. Entry fee 90 dollars. Four-round rapid, Senior 60-plus. Rapid format, low FIDE rating impact, minimal travel. Skip if the Festival wrap-up has not fully cleared by that point in the calendar.

Two Brooklyn windows the calendar should protect. One earlier in the year around March or April for Ethan and Lily. One tighter to July 18, 2027 for Lily's fifth birthday. Any tournament that lets one of those windows breathe is a plus, and any that squeezes them is a minus regardless of rating value. The US Senior Open call in item eight is the annual open question I keep circling and never finish, and it belongs in the six-week close, not in a January scramble.
