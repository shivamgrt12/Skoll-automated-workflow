# Gift Shortlist - Rough Thoughts 2026

Kept in the Obsidian personal notes as a running scratch, tidied up in early autumn and not since. Nothing here is a decision. All of it is starting shape.

## Daniel (December 12 birthday)

- Chess book direction as usual, hardcover if I can find one
- Something on the Soviet endgame school if his shelf does not already carry it
- A Botvinnik or Smyslov memoir in a clean reprint might land, same caveat
- A recent match-book on a modern World Championship cycle is a fallback
- I do not have a full picture of his current shelf, so any pick needs a cross-check against the last three or four titles I sent him
- Keep landed cost under one hundred fifty dollars including expedited shipping

## Nathan (Christmas)

- Garden angle first, a proper set of Japanese pruning shears he has admired at the nursery
- Study angle second, a modest slipcased edition of an American short story collection he does not have yet
- Combine both if the pruning shears run light on their own
- Cap around one hundred eighty dollars total across whatever combination lands

## Ethan (Christmas)

- Something for the Brooklyn apartment rather than the office
- A small kitchen tool he has mentioned but never bought for himself
- A hardback on the history of the fintech domain, non-technical enough to actually read
- Nothing that looks like professional advice or a career suggestion
- Cap around one hundred twenty dollars

## Lily (Christmas plus early 2027 window)

- She turns five on July 18, 2027, so the Christmas gift can sit at the four-and-a-half mark
- A story hardback that reads out loud well over a long visit
- A small wooden puzzle set with pieces large enough for small hands
- Nothing chess, since the little set already lives by her bedside in the guest room
- Cap around eighty dollars

Consolidate into one shipping run where the Brooklyn parcels and the Portland book can share carrier windows. Nothing lands after December 22.
