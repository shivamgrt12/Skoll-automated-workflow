# Memory: Jesse Perez

## Personal Profile

- **DOB**: November 14, 1986
- **Heritage**: Son of Oaxacan immigrants who arrived in Queens in 1982, raised in Spanish and English braided together.
- **Citizenship**: U.S. citizen, born in Queens, New York.
- **Languages**: English (native), Spanish (fluent), Portuguese (conversational), basic French.
- **Bilingual thinking**: Numbers and grief arrive in Spanish, professional vocabulary in English.
- **Cultural identity**: Carries awareness of being Mexican-American in a predominantly white institutional art world.
- **Education**: B.A. Art History, Williams College, 2009. M.A. Art Conservation (textile), Winterthur/University of Delaware, 2012.
- **Fellowship**: Post-grad at the Textile Museum, Washington D.C., 2012 to 2014.
- **Museum career**: Associate Conservator, Textile Conservation, The Metropolitan Museum of Art, July 2014 to February 2020.
- **Career transition**: Left The Met after his father's death in October 2019 to build an independent practice in Boston.
- **Temperament**: Patient and observant, with quiet stubbornness on matters of principle over preference.
- **Inner critic**: Gets louder when tired, perfectionism he has spent years negotiating down to something livable.
- **Conservation ethic**: Believes intervention should be minimal and reversible, an ethic that became a way of living.
- **Documentation philosophy**: Believes documentation outlasts the documenter and the work matters even when unseen.
- **Family anchor**: Family is the axis his decisions orbit, and the loss of his father remains inseparable from the leap.

## Work & Projects

- **Business**: Perez Textile Conservation, sole proprietorship, ground-floor studio in Jamaica Plain with internal staircase to apartment.
- **Equipment**: Humidification chamber, fume hood, fiber analysis microscope, UV imaging setup, archival document scanner, and archival storage.
- **Client base**: Museums, historical societies, and private collectors across New England.
- **Specializations**: Latin American textiles, Ottoman embroideries, and American colonial-era samplers and coverlets.
- **Services**: Condition assessment, stabilization, cleaning, mounting, and long-term storage consultation.
- **MFA Ottoman Embroidery Suite**: Six 17th- and 18th-century panels for the Museum of Fine Arts, stabilization and mounting phase, exhibition target December 2026.
- **Peabody Essex Sampler Collection**: Condition survey and treatment plan for 22 American samplers (1750 to 1850), report due January 2027.
- **Private Oaxacan Textile Commission**: 19th-century woven textile for a private collector via Dr. Amina Rashid, stitching consolidation in progress, delivery November 2026.
- **Studio hours**: Monday through Friday, 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM, with evening and Saturday admin work.
- **Conference**: Presenting at the AIC annual meeting in Salt Lake City, November 11, 2026, on reversible mounting techniques.
