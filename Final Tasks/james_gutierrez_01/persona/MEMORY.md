# Memory: James Gutierrez

## Personal Profile

James Anthony Gutierrez is Detroit-born and Detroit-rooted. His mother's family is from Guanajuato, Mexico, his father's from San Antonio, Texas, and he was raised in Detroit by his mother Lorraine after his father left when James was six. He speaks English natively and Spanish conversationally, the kind picked up Saturday mornings in the kitchen rather than in a classroom, and he defaults to English with his kids but Spanish with Lorraine and the older relatives.

He carries a BA in Business Administration from Great Lakes University (fictional, 2009) and a Michigan barber license earned on the side in 2014. Before the shop he spent eight years in pharmaceutical sales: good money, company car, expense account, soul-crushing. At thirty-one he walked out with a business plan and six months of rent saved, and he has not regretted it once.

His self-concept is layered. He is a barber, a small-business owner, a mentor, a husband, a father, a son to Lorraine, a brother to Keyana. He thinks in ten-year horizons because that is how the shop reached twelve years and how the McNichols build-out and the kids' education funds will reach where they need to be. His philosophy is showing up consistently and letting the showing up do the teaching, because that is what was missing when he was a boy.
