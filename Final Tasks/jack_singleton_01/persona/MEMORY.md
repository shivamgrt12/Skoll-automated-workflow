# Memory: Jack Singleton

## Personal Profile

- **Full name**: John "Jack" William Singleton
- **Pronouns**: he/him
- **Born**: Annapolis, Maryland
- **Occupation**: Owner-operator of the F/V Bay Runner, commercial blue crab fisherman April through November and charter captain June through August, third-generation Chesapeake waterman.
- **Education**: Annapolis Senior High School, 1998. USCG 100-ton Master Captain's license, current through 2028. Maryland DNR commercial crabbing license, annual.
- **Cultural identity**: Annapolis townie, working-class Catholic, deep Irish-American roots. Grandfather started the family on the water in the 1940s; father Brian ran a Bay Runner before this one. Jack is the youngest of three; his older sisters left for the suburbs years ago, leaving Jack as Kathleen's closest child.
- **Philosophy**: Show up at 4 AM, work the water clean, family first, finish what you start. Believes the Bay deserves the respect his grandfather paid it, crew loyalty buys decades, and complaining about the weather never moved a pot.

## Key Relationships

- **Bridget Singleton (wife, 43, born November 18, 1982)**: School nurse at Annapolis Christian Academy, K through 8. Met Jack at a Naval Academy graduation party in 2002, married 2005. Household financial anchor and co-decision-maker. Steady, organized. Jack's designated ICE contact.
- **Patrick Singleton (oldest, 16, born January 22, 2010)**: Junior at Annapolis High. Varsity lacrosse defenseman, summer deckhand on the Bay Runner. Has his learner's permit and his sights on driving the boat solo when Jack signs off. Considering the Coast Guard Academy.
- **Siobhan Singleton (middle, 13, born February 12, 2013)**: Eighth grader at Bay Ridge Middle School. Sailing crowd, races Optis with the Severn Sailing Association, also swims competitively. The household's quiet observer.
- **Connor Singleton (youngest, 9, born December 4, 2016)**: Fourth grader at Hillsmere Elementary. U-10 lacrosse, Jack assistant-coaches. The cuddly one.
- **Kathleen Singleton (mother, 72, born March 8, 1954)**: Widowed since 2018, lives alone in the family house in Severna Park. Mild hypertension and early arthritis, sharp otherwise. Jack visits every Sunday morning to check the pill organizer, fridge, and house repairs.
- **Uncle Frank Singleton (uncle, 71)**: Brian's younger brother, retired Annapolis fire captain. Hunting partner Eastern Shore November through January. Sunday dinner regular.
- **Tommy Novak (first mate, 38)**: Eleven years on the Bay Runner. Lives in Eastport with his wife and two kids. The one Jack would leave in charge without thinking twice.
- **D.J. Johnson (deckhand, 26)**: Third season. Strong worker, Jack is grooming him toward a possible mate role.
- **Mike Rawlings (deckhand, 22)**: Second season. Reliable, quiet, good with customers on charter days.
- **Pete Andersen (charter agent, 52)**: Owner of Chesapeake Charter Connection. Books most of Jack's summer charter slate, 15% cut, steady clients since 2014.
- **Kevin Doyle (best friend, 46)**: Annapolis townie, contractor, leads Tuesday open mic at Dockside Pub. Jack's oldest non-family friend; they have been playing guitar together since high school.
- **Dr. Robert Kessler (pain management)**: At Cloverdale Health. Manages the herniated L4-L5 disc protocol and Gabapentin.
- **Dr. Margaret Walsh (primary care)**: At Annapolis Internal Medicine. Annual physical, blood pressure, Losartan.
- **Dr. Linda Chen (dermatologist)**: At Annapolis Dermatology. Annual skin check after 2022 basal cell removal off Jack's neck.
- **Dr. Sarah Kim (pediatrician)**: At Annapolis Pediatrics. The kids' primary care.
- **Frank DiMaggio (CPA, 58)**: Solo practitioner, twelve years on Jack's taxes. Quarterly check-ins. Knows the Bay Runner books cold.

## Work & Projects

- **Vessel**: F/V Bay Runner, 42-foot fiberglass deadrise, twin Cummins QSB 6.7 diesels, hydraulic pot puller, Garmin chartplotter, Furuno radar. Slip 47 at Annapolis City Marina. Jack runs the second family hull, bought 2014.
- **Commercial crabbing**: April 1 through November 30. Three to four trips per week, 300 pots per trip, 900 pots total registered with Maryland DNR. Sells dockside to walk-ups and to Wye River Seafood and Annapolis Crab Pickers. Crew of three.
- **Charter business**: June 1 through August 31. Three to four charters per week through Pete Andersen at Chesapeake Charter Connection. Rockfish, bluefish, and flounder. Half-day and full-day rates. Bridget runs the Instagram and the `bayrunnercharters.com` page, and Patrick built the site's booking widget for a school project that Jack tests and feeds back on.
- **Off-season**: December through March. Bay Runner haul-out at Marshwood Partners mid-December. Pot rebuilding in the marina shed and the garage. Cold-weather contract work when Pete surfaces it.
- **Coaching and community**: Assistant coach for Connor's U-10 Annapolis Recreation lacrosse team, March through May. Maryland Watermen's Association annual meeting and Annapolis Boat Show each winter.
- **Open projects**: 2027 charter slate Pete is filling now. Annual DNR pot inspection by April 15, 2027. Patrick's solo sign-off conversation, pending his seventeenth birthday in January.

## Finance

- **Income**: Bay Runner grosses about $164,000 to Jack on a 3-year average, commercial blue crab around $116,000 and summer charter around $48,000 after Pete's 15% cut. Net to Jack about $92,000 after costs. Bridget's school-nurse salary $54,000. Household gross around $218,000; net roughly $138,000.
- **Take-home pattern**: Cash flow heavy April through November, very tight December through March. The off-season is when the back tightens and the budget tightens with it.
- **Monthly fixed**: mortgage $2,150, engine loan $640 (two years left), slip $480, boat and charter liability $720, home insurance $185, utilities and groceries roughly $1,700, kids' activities $400, phone and internet $230.
- **Savings**: about $42,000 at Annapolis Maritime Bank (off-season buffer). SEP-IRA about $186,000 through Frank DiMaggio. 529s: Patrick $34,000, Siobhan $18,000, Connor $9,500.
- **Debts**: mortgage about $214,000 with 16 years to go. Engine loan $14,800 with two years left. No credit card debt.
- **Financial goals**: monthly 529 contributions when cash allows, knock out the engine loan early in 2028, no new boat debt until Patrick is settled.
- **Banking**: checking, savings, engine loan at Annapolis Maritime Bank, in branch only. Bay Runner business checking same bank, linked to QuickBooks via Plaid.

## Health & Wellness

- **General**: A waterman on the water since age twelve. Strong, weathered, tired more often than he says.
- **Lower back**: Herniated L4-L5 disc diagnosed 2022 after a winter rebuild season. Gabapentin 300mg three times daily, quarterly Dr. Kessler appointments at Cloverdale Health, physical therapy plan he skips more than he should. One or two episodes a year that knock him out for two to three days.
- **Hypertension**: Diagnosed 2020. Losartan 50mg every morning. Last reading 132/84.
- **Dermatology**: Basal cell carcinoma removed off his neck in 2022. Annual full-body skin check with Dr. Chen every March.
- **Knee**: Right knee aches in the cold, old football and years of jumping off the boat. No surgery yet.
- **Sleep**: 6 hours April through November, 7 hours December through March if honest. Up at 4 AM every working day.
- **Stress**: High during the season. Managed with the routine, open mic Tuesdays, and homebrew batches.
- **Diet (medically relevant)**: No known allergies. Sodium per Dr. Walsh; mixed compliance. Folgers black, two cups before dawn. Two beers most nights.

## Interests & Hobbies

- **Home brewing**: 5-gallon batches in the garage. Two house recipes: "Bay Red Ale" (Irish red) and a Vienna-style amber lager. Enters the Maryland Homebrew Competition each February with the winter batches. Two regional ribbons in five years.
- **Open mic and guitar**: Tuesday nights at Dockside Pub with Kevin Doyle. Classic country, southern rock, the occasional Tom Petty. Plays a 1998 Martin DR-15 from his father.
- **Waterfowl hunting**: Layout boat on the Eastern Shore with Uncle Frank November through January, duck and goose. Less about the bag than the morning.
- **Coaching**: Connor's U-10 lacrosse team. Knows the game from Patrick's varsity years.
- **Watermen community**: Maryland Watermen's Association meetings, the annual Singleton-house crabcake cookoff each fall when the crabs are fattest, the boat show in January.
- **Reading**: William Warner's "Beautiful Swimmers" every other winter, Chesapeake Bay history, the occasional Pat Conroy novel.
