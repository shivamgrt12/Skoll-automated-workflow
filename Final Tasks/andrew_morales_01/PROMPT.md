--- TURN 1 ---

I need this whole record trued up before I walk into a room with A&R and get asked something I cannot answer straight. Five songs, and depending on which board or list or sheet I open I get a different story about every one of them. Three are supposed to be tracked and two are not, but which three depends on who was updating what and when, and I have quit believing any single one of those places. That is the kind of sloppy that gets a writer eaten alive in this town. So what I want first is one honest picture, song by song, of where each of these actually stands. Written, demoed, tracked, comped, mixed, mastered, and what is genuinely still owed on it, whether that is a vocal comp or a fiddle part or a second verse that does not embarrass me. Not a summary. Song by song, with the reasoning showing, because I will be asked to defend it and I will not have you in my pocket when I am. A lot of what is sitting in those boards went stale back when this thing moved slower than the paperwork did, and I have lost confidence that any of it still lines up with what DT and Terrell and I have actually put down since. Where two places disagree, I want to know which one you leaned on and which one you set aside and why you called it that way. If the evidence is thin, say it is thin and leave it open. I would rather carry an honest question into that room than a clean number you invented to make the page look finished. Then the money, because this record eats and I only have so much left to feed it. I put a hard ceiling on it out of my own pocket, and I have been paying for pieces of it out of three or four different places depending on what was in my hand when the bill came, so the running total I keep telling myself is a number I wrote down once and have not earned since. I want a real cash picture for finishing this thing, one I can trust, and I want the two remaining tracking days plus the mix and the master costed against what is actually left rather than against what I wish were left. If that comes up short, show me the size of the hole and where it came from, no soft landing. The session book is part of this too. That is the money quietly paying for the record, and it runs through more than one set of books because my bookkeeper keeps hers and I keep mine, and I already know they do not agree because they never do. Reconcile them and tell me which one is carrying the truth. A chunk of what has come in is not mine to spend either, because the tax on it is coming whether I planned for it or not, so put that set aside honestly in the same picture instead of letting me pretend it is spending money. The splits are the part that keeps me up. I have been burned twice on authorship, so I keep everything, the drafts, the memos, the timestamps, all of it, and I still have co-writes on this record where what the paperwork says and what the catalog says and what I remember shaking on in the room are three different stories. Walk it down and give me a position I can defend with the evidence hanging off it, before anybody puts a pen anywhere near it. R.V. has been asking me to get it papered and I am not putting my name to a number I cannot back. I also want to know where my catalog actually sits out there. Vanessa has been pitching me into rooms I do not sit in, and between what her side tracks and what I track I have songs I think are on hold that may have already been passed on, and maybe one that got a yes I never properly registered. Run that down, all of it, not just the five. What is out, who has it, what came back, what has gone quiet long enough that quiet is its own answer. I want to walk in knowing which of my songs are already spoken for so I am not standing there pitching something that is not mine to pitch. Two of these songs still are not finished, and that is on me. I have got four hundred and some fragments sitting in that library from back when I was disciplined about filing them, and somewhere in there are the pieces those two are missing. Go through them properly against what each of those songs actually needs and bring me real candidates with a reason attached, not a shuffle. DT has been asking what he is tracking on those last two days and I have not had an answer for him. And the running order. I want a sequence I can sign off on and the case for why it runs that way, the keys, the tempos, where the record breathes and where it hits, because a five song demo lives or dies on the first thirty seconds and the last one. The last two days are already pencilled at Copperline and DT is holding the room for me. Bring me the readiness picture, the cash picture, the splits position, and the pitch picture, and make every number in all four one I could defend cold in a room full of people who do this for a living.
