# Memory: Andrew Morales

## Personal Profile
Andrew was born with melody in his blood. His grandmother sang in a San Antonio mariachi ensemble, his mother played piano in Tejano dance halls, and he learned to harmonize before he could read, carrying three generations of Latino Southern music in his throat. He is Hispanic and Latino: his mother's family is Mexican-American from San Antonio and his father's family is from El Paso, and he was born and raised in San Antonio. His father passed when Andrew was 15, and music was how he survived the grief. He earned an A.A. in Music Performance from Alamo City Community College in 2012 and attended Ridgeline School of Music songwriting workshops in 2014 and 2015 as a non-degree student. He lives in the tension between two Nashvilles, the country music machine that wants his voice but not always his face, and the Latino creative community that raised him up and keeps him grounded. He is deeply Tejano in ways Nashville does not always understand: he cooks mole from scratch, speaks Spanish with his Abuelita, treats Dia de los Muertos like a holy day, and carries hot sauce in his pocket without irony. Quietly ambitious beneath an easy charm, he wants a solo record deal, not just session work, and will not compromise his sound for a trend or turn in a song that feels dishonest even when the money is good. He is generous with his talent to a fault, and he carries the specific loneliness of being excellent at something the industry treats as secondary.

## Work & Projects
- **Session singing**: Sings backup and harmony at studios across Nashville, primarily Copperline Studios, also Magnolia Sound and Bluebird Track Studio. Booked through word of mouth, typically three to five sessions per week at $250 to $400 per session (three to four hours), sometimes union scale for larger productions. Known for blending into any vocal style, from mariachi belt to breathy country whisper in one take. Regular clients include producers DT Thompson, Hank Beaumont, and Lisa Nakamura.
- **Songwriting**: Publishing deal with Silverbell Music Group since 2022; they pitch his songs to country artists. Monthly advance of $2,000 against future royalties. Three songs recorded by other artists, the biggest a track on Carly Thornton's album that reached number 42 on country radio. Co-writes two to three times a week with rotating Nashville writers, scheduled through Vanessa. Keeps all original drafts, voice memos, and timestamped lyric sheets in Drive after learning the hard way about proving authorship.
- **Solo demo EP**: Recording a five-song demo EP at Copperline Studios with DT Thompson producing. Self-funded, budget around $8,000 total, roughly $5,200 spent. Three of five songs tracked, two remaining, targeting completion ahead of formal pitching to labels and A&R contacts beginning October 2026. Goal is to use the EP to pitch for a solo artist development deal with a label.

- **Vocal health**: Treats his voice as a professional instrument. Vocal-cord nodule scare in 2023, resolved with rest and vocal therapy; now does daily vocal exercises to prevent recurrence. ENT checkup every six months with Dr. Brenda Gilliam at Harmony Point Medical.
- **Physical**: Good overall. Mild acid reflux triggered by spicy food and late-night eating, managed with omeprazole as needed. Last physical November 2025 at Harmony Point Medical, next due November 2026. Dental cleaning every six months at Cumberland Smile Dental. No food allergies.
- **Mental health**: Manages anxiety, especially around career uncertainty. Sees therapist Dr. Lorraine Hicks every other week, started after the 2023 vocal scare. Seasonal low mood in January and February through the gray Nashville winter.
- **Fitness and sleep**: Yoga Monday, Wednesday, and Friday mornings (7:30 AM class at Sunrise Flow) for vocal-cord health and anxiety. Neighborhood walks for songwriting inspiration, occasional hiking at Radnor Lake. Sleeps seven to eight hours, but late studio sessions can push bedtime to 1 or 2 AM, then he sleeps until 9.
- **Dietary (medical)**: No strict restrictions. Trying to eat more vegetables per his doctor. Avoids spicy and late-night food when reflux is acting up.
