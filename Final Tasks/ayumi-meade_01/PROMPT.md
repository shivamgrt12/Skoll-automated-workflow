Start with the shortfall itself. I have a working target of four point eight million to close and three broad levers on the table, administrative consolidation, a facilities bond, and reductions to instructional staffing, and I have been telling the board for a long stretch that the last of those is my last resort and not my first. Model those three levers honestly. Lay the arithmetic out so I can defend it under cross examination and spell out the stakeholder impact for each so no board member gets to pretend that a spreadsheet line does not touch a human. Whatever you present as the defensible option must name the assumption you leaned on and the assumption you set aside, and if the math argues against my preferred posture I want to know that plainly before the president does. On the operating picture I want the whole district read, not a west side snapshot padded with two east side anecdotes, so run all thirty one schools as one population and give me adoption, engagement, and vacancy patterns broken out cleanly enough that when the president asks me why one high school looks different from a middle school across the highway I can answer without fumbling under the table. Some of the counts I have been quoting in cabinet came out of an older summary sitting in the policy space that has lost its freshness, and I have lost trust that those figures still line up with what the staffing side is reporting from its own records, so the numbers you put in front of me have to be defended source by source and the ones that do not line up need to be flagged and reconciled, not smoothed. Do the same discipline on the donor and partner side, because the pledge figures our best friend on the principal bench and I have been trading for the foundation drive and the numbers the alliance treasurer runs against her own books do not agree in the direction I would prefer, and I want the truthful picture rather than the flattering one. On the bell schedule feedback take the survey responses, cluster them honestly, and give me the split between families and staff without collapsing one into the other, because the union president will make me pay for any shortcut he can spot from the back row and I would rather he not find one. The east side portal question is a piece of this too. The audit called us out on access and the numbers I quote need to reflect what the classroom platform and the family portal are actually seeing across our east side buildings, not what a marketing dashboard tells me at a glance. Everything I have named wants reconciling against a second surface before it lands in the brief, and where the two do not agree tell me plainly which figure you trusted, which one you set aside, and why. I would rather you hold a line as unresolved and say so than pretend to an answer we cannot defend. The readiness brief is the one I walk in with, so shape it as something I can hand a board member cold and have them come out knowing where the money picture stands, where the equity work stands, where our east side proof of impact is helping our case, and what the temperature is around the schedule question. The reconciliation belongs beside it as the receipts trail so any figure in the brief traces back cleanly. The parent update is a shorter piece, respectful and specific, not a marketing swing, and it stays as a draft until I read it, because anything that goes out under the district name is mine to clear and mine alone. The donor note is internal, meant for the deputy and me, and it names the partners I should reach out to personally and the ones we let the foundation team carry, and nothing on that front lands with a new outside contact before I have looked at the roster. Do not send anything to families, the board, a partner, or our public channels until every draft has been across my desk, and do not commit anything to the cabinet policy space that reads like a decision when it is still a draft option. On money, nothing goes out under my sign off without a conversation first, and hold the line hard on anything that touches personnel identifiers, disciplinary detail, or figures that belong to the pre decisional budget conversation, because those are not ours to push into the open. If a source is thin or contradicts itself, tell me and hold. I would rather walk into that room short one bullet than confident on a wrong number.
