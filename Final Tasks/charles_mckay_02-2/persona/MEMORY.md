# Memory: Charles McKay

## Personal Profile
Charles McKay is Scottish-American, built from Wyoming granite by decades of high-plains winters, calving seasons, drought years, and fence-mending. His grandparents emigrated from Edinburgh in 1923 and homesteaded in northern Wyoming, and the family has ranched the same land for over a hundred years. He was raised Presbyterian, attends church occasionally, and is not devout: "the land is my church." He holds a BS in Agricultural Science from the University of Wyoming in Laramie (1981), took extension courses through the Park County Agricultural Extension, and is self-taught in meteorology through decades of observation, reading, and equipment.

He is fiercely independent but not anti-social. He will help a neighbor calve at 2:00 AM unasked, lend equipment without a contract, and sit with a grieving family, yet he needs his solitude on the fence lines, at the weather console, and on the porch. He is steady and unshakeable, having seen worse than whatever the moment brings. His humor is bone-dry and delivered straight-faced. Beneath the stoicism he is sentimental: he keeps his father's ranch journals back to 1962, named sections of fence after his kids, and grieved selling the herd at 62 though he will deny it. He embraces useful technology like weather stations and satellite data but resists change for its own sake, which makes him both the most pragmatic and the most stubborn voice on the school board.

## Work & Projects
- **Ranch property management**: 2,800 acres of mixed grassland, creek bottom, and timbered hills. The 280-head Black Angus herd sold in 2021; the land was retained. Grazing lease to Roy Patterson runs $18 per acre per year on 1,200 acres ($21,600). A hay lease on 400 acres brings $8,000. Ongoing maintenance covers fencing, the well-and-stock-tank water system, road grading, and wildfire mitigation. He is weighing a conservation easement through the Wyoming Land Trust that would protect the land permanently for a sizable tax benefit, and is emotionally conflicted about limiting the kids' future options. Recent work included gathering three well-pump replacement quotes for the 320-foot well.
- **Park County School Board**: Elected 2022, term through 2026, considering re-election. Represents rural District 3 (three elementary schools, one middle school into the county high school) and sits on the Transportation and Facilities subcommittee. The board meets the second and fourth Tuesdays. The defining fight is Superintendent Morrison's proposal to consolidate Sage Creek Elementary and Pronghorn Valley Elementary, which Charles opposes because it would add 45-minute bus rides for young children. He has compiled consolidation case studies from four Wyoming counties, drafted a Cody Enterprise letter to the editor against the proposal, and organized a constituent meeting at Sage Creek that reached 85 families.

## Finance
- Income: grazing lease $21,600 per year ($1,800 per month), hay lease $8,000 per year ($667 per month), Social Security $2,850 per month, and self-funded SEP-IRA withdrawals $1,500 per month, totaling roughly $6,817 per month.
- Concerns: rising property taxes (about $6,800 per year), maintenance costs with no cattle income to offset them, the conservation easement tradeoff (a potential six-figure tax benefit against lost flexibility), and a strong wish to pass the land to the kids intact.

## Health & Wellness
- **Hypertension**: Diagnosed 2016, Lisinopril 20mg daily, BP usually 134 to 140 over 82 to 88; the doctor wants it lower.
- **Type 2 diabetes**: Diagnosed 2023, Metformin 500mg twice daily, A1C 7.1, needs better diet compliance.
- **Bilateral knee osteoarthritis**: From decades of ranch work, right knee worse, Meloxicam 15mg daily. Stiff in the morning, loosens up. Has considered knee replacement but does not want the downtime.
- **Mild hearing loss**: Left ear, likely noise-related, refuses a hearing aid.
- **Weight**: 210 lbs at 6 feet; the doctor wants 20 lbs off.
- **Mental health**: Grief from Eleanor's death managed through routine, work, and stoicism. He declined therapy and processes through the land, the dog, and talks with Rev. Crawford.
- **Sleep**: 10:00 PM to 5:30 AM, sleeps well, wakes early by habit.
- Providers: Dr. Michael Ashford (primary care) at West Park Medical Center, annual checkup each March; Dr. Linda Prescott (dental) at Bighorn Dental every six months; annual vision exam at High Plains Eye Center, distance glasses for driving and reading glasses for the computer.
- Diet note: doctor advises cutting red meat and salt; Charles nods and changes nothing. No formal dietary restrictions.

## Interests & Hobbies
- **Amateur meteorology**: Maintains a Davis Vantage Pro2 station, logs daily observations, contributes to Weather Underground, and tracks long-term precipitation and temperature trends. Member of the Wind River Meteorological Society and invited to give talks. Identifies cloud formations and predicts storms by smell and wind shift.
- **Fly fishing**: The Shoshone River and its tributaries in fishing season. Ties his own flies and takes the grandkids when they visit.
- **Woodworking**: A small shop in the barn for furniture and ranch repairs. Built a cradle for each grandchild.
- **Reading**: Weather science (subscribes to the Highplains Meteorological Review), agricultural, Wyoming, and Scottish history, and mystery novels by Tony Hillerman and Craig Johnson.
- **Whisky**: Single malt Scotch neat, a Glenfiddich 18 for most evenings and a Laphroaig for cold nights or when he is feeling fancy, one pour in the evening and occasionally two.

## Home & Living
- Ranch house: 4-bedroom, 2-bath, built 1958 and extensively renovated 1995, about 2,400 sq ft, stone foundation and log-frame upper, heated by wood stove plus propane furnace. Charles uses the master bedroom and the ranch office; the other rooms stay ready for kids and grandkids.
- Kitchen: functional, cast iron skillets, a table that seats eight, Eleanor's recipe box on the counter. Living room has the wood stove, Eleanor's bookshelves, a worn leather recliner, and a west window facing the Absaroka Mountains.
- Outbuildings: barn with woodworking shop and hay and equipment storage, the preserved 1923 homestead cabin, an equipment shed, empty corrals, and a stock pond. The property runs to rolling grassland, Sage Creek through the south section, timbered hills west, and road access via County Road 6WX.
- Vehicles: 2020 Ford F-250 Super Duty (diesel ranch truck) and 2017 Subaru Outback (road car).
- Open needs: barn roof repair (about $8,000), a well pump that may need replacing (monitoring), and 400 yards of new south-fence wire (about $3,500 in materials plus labor).

- **Music**: Old country (Johnny Cash, Merle Haggard, Willie Nelson), Scottish folk (The Corries, Dougie MacLean), and classical while working in the shop.
- **TV**: The Weather Channel constantly, Yellowstone (with opinions on its accuracy), Jeopardy nightly, and nature documentaries on The Prairie Channel.
