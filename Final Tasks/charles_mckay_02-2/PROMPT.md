--- TURN 1 ---

I want this consolidation fight buttoned up airtight before the special session, no loose ends the superintendent can pull on when he stands up to argue his case, so pull everything I have been gathering into one board-ready decision brief I can set on the table and work from without fumbling for a figure. Build the argument on the three planks I keep coming back to, the bus time these little kids would eat every single day, the money the district swears it saves, and the community that quietly comes apart when a country school gets shuttered, and rank the whole thing by what actually moves a vote so the strongest point sits up top and the soft ones ride in back. The heart of it is the outside record, so go back through the case files I put together on the four counties that already walked down this road and true up what the folks pushing the merger promised against what the county ledgers actually showed once the buses were rolling and the fuel and the overtime and the extra driver hours got tallied, because my recollection is the savings evaporated and I need the real net in black and white, not a slogan. While you are in the case material, take my window count on how long a first grader sits on that bus and run it back against the district's own posted materials on the routes, and where my working notes and the loose talk in my planks disagree on the minutes, lean on the sturdiest source and carry that number and only that number into every place the figure appears, because if one page says one thing and the next page says another the superintendent will drive a truck through the gap. I have got a nagging worry that some of what I am carrying is stale, and the vote map is the worst of it, so reconcile where every seat on the board actually stands by reading the real back and forth in both my mail stacks alongside the quiet chatter in the coalition room, and pay close mind to the man proposing this thing, because I have him written down one way in my old tally and my gut says the record now reads the other way entirely, and I need the seat that swings this settled with confidence before you write a single line of the whip section. Same worry rides on the date itself, since the room has been buzzing about the thing getting pushed to the dead of winter, but the calendar is the calendar, so trust the hard scheduling entry over the hallway rumor and anchor the brief to the session that is actually coming. Then take the people, because the number I have been repeating about how many families we reached is a round figure I said out loud once and never checked, and I need it honest, so sweep every list I keep the supporters on, the sign in sheets from the meeting, the survey folks, the newsletter rolls, the whole scattered lot, and fold them into one clean roster of distinct households with no soul counted twice and no one who asked me to quit writing them left on it, and tell me the true count that survives that scrub so I stop quoting a number I cannot stand behind. Read the bus route survey properly while you are at it, not just the headline, and pull what the parents actually said into the busing plank so that argument stands on their words and not just mine. Give me a plain look at who is even reading what we put out on the district page, since I am tired of guessing whether the message lands. Sort my follow up list too and tell me what is rotting at the bottom that should have been handled a section of time ago. Two things I want written but held on my say so and not one inch further, the letter to the paper, tightened and ready with the trued up figures folded in, sitting as a draft only until I read it with my own eyes, and a plain notice to the households on that clean roster telling them the session is coming and their voices matter, also draft only, not a word of it sent, and nobody on it who has asked to be left be. When you touch the superintendent at all keep it strictly to the official and measured register, agenda and correspondence only, not a whisper of our strategy, and keep the family and the money and anything about my health clear out of the coalition room where the volunteers can see it. Do not commit me to any new meeting and do not go leaning on the undecided seat on my behalf without my word first. Fan your people out and work these threads at the same time rather than plodding one after another, the ledgers, the vote map, the roster scrub, the survey, the readership, the follow ups, all of it moving together, and pull it back into one coherent picture. Where the evidence comes up thin or two records fight and you cannot settle which is straight, say so plainly and leave the conclusion open rather than papering over it, because I would rather walk into that room knowing exactly where the soft ground is than get caught flat footed in front of a full house.
