## 1. Scope and eligibility

This protocol governs the use of the delivery-ward birthing pools for labour immersion and water birth for women presenting to the delivery ward at Västra Götaland University Hospital. Eligibility is unchanged from R1 and requires singleton pregnancy at 37+0 to 42+0 weeks gestation, cephalic presentation, no known contraindications listed in Section 5, and informed consent documented in the maternity record. Multiparity is not an eligibility criterion. Primiparous women are eligible under the same clinical requirements with the second-stage clarification in Section 4 applied.

## 2. Water temperature range **[CHANGED]**

Water temperature is maintained between 36.8 and 37.5 degrees Celsius during the second stage of labour. Temperature is measured at pool entry, at 30-minute intervals thereafter, and immediately before delivery. Temperature outside this range is corrected before the second stage progresses. This range replaces the R1 range of 36.5 to 38.0 degrees Celsius.

## 3. Duration limits

Immersion in the first stage is not time-limited when maternal and fetal parameters remain within normal ranges. Second-stage duration limits are set in Section 4.

## 4. Fetal monitoring cadence and second-stage escalation **[CHANGED]**

Intermittent auscultation is performed every 15 minutes during the first stage and every 5 minutes during the second stage, following the standard hospital cadence. For primiparous women, escalation to the responsible obstetrician is triggered if the second stage exceeds 90 minutes with the woman remaining in the pool. For parous women, the R1 escalation threshold of 60 minutes is retained. This clarifies R1 language, which was silent on the primiparous cohort.

## 5. Exclusion criteria

Unchanged from R1. Exclusion applies where any of the following are present: suspected chorioamnionitis, rupture of membranes beyond 24 hours without spontaneous labour, meconium-stained liquor of any degree, maternal fever above 37.8 degrees Celsius, known bloodborne infection requiring precautions, non-cephalic presentation, multiple pregnancy, gestation below 37+0 weeks or above 42+0 weeks, and any obstetric concern raised by the attending midwife or obstetrician.

## 6. Escalation pathway to obstetrics

Unchanged from R1. Escalation is to the responsible obstetrician on duty via the ward escalation channel. Immediate exit from the pool is required in cases of maternal collapse, fetal heart rate concern not resolving within one auscultation cycle, meconium liquor appearing after pool entry, and any indication for operative delivery.

## 7. Postpartum handover

Unchanged from R1. Standard postpartum monitoring applies. Neonatal handover to the paediatric team follows the hospital neonatal handover protocol and is not modified by this document.

## 8. Documentation

All immersion events, temperature readings, and auscultation cadences are documented in the maternity record at the intervals specified above. No change from R1.
