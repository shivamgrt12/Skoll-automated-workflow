## Subject

Submission of VGR-DEL-WB-2026-R2 for departmental approval, with staged staff rollout January 12, 2027 through March 30, 2027 and formal go-live on April 6, 2027.

## Body

On behalf of the water birth protocol working group, I am submitting the revised water birth protocol (VGR-DEL-WB-2026-R2) for your approval at our meeting on November 27, 2026. The revision changes two clinical parameters against the current protocol (VGR-DEL-WB-2023-R1). Everything else is unchanged.

The two changes are a narrowing of the second-stage water temperature range from 36.5 to 38.0 degrees Celsius down to 36.8 to 37.5 degrees Celsius, and a clarification of the primiparous second-stage escalation threshold at 90 minutes. Both are supported by moderate to high strength evidence from studies indexed in the working group evidence log. A documentation reference for pool hygiene cadence is also updated to the 2025 European infection control consensus. This is not a clinical change.

The submission dossier covers the evidence base, the R2 protocol draft with the parameter change log, the training rollout and its cost envelope, the reconciled equipment picture, the risk register including the outcomes held open on insufficient evidence, and the reconciliation log naming every source we trusted and every source we set aside.

The working group has been deliberately conservative on the long-horizon neonatal infection domain beyond 28 days. The evidence is thin and the revision does not claim a benefit or an absence of risk. That gap is carried in the risk register as a monitored item for the next review cycle rather than as a claim in the protocol text.

The equipment picture has one conditional dependency. Pool B seal replacement is complete and awaiting biomed sign-off scheduled for November 24, 2026. If that sign-off slips, the training rollout contingency documented in the training rollout plan applies for the first cohort.

The working group requests your approval to proceed with the staged rollout on the timeline set out above. Dr. Henrik Olsson and the senior midwives group have received a pre-meeting briefing and are prepared to raise any questions at the meeting rather than in advance.
