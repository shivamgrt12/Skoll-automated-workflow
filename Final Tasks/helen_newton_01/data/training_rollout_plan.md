# Training Rollout Plan — VGR-DEL-WB-2026-R2

**Rollout window:** January 12, 2027 through March 30, 2027
**Go-live:** April 6, 2027
**Co-owners:** Helen Newton (working group lead), Malin Eriksson (team lead, delivery ward)
**Related artifacts:** `training_costing.csv`, `equipment_readiness.md`
**Distribution:** Department head, obstetrics leadership, senior midwives, working group

## Purpose

Bring the delivery-ward midwife establishment to full R2 competency in advance of the April 6, 2027 go-live, without eroding shift coverage, without pulling recovery days from the night-shift rotation, and without pushing any midwife into an out-of-hours training slot they did not opt into. The plan is deliberately staggered so no single week loses more than four midwives to training at any one time.

## Rollout window

Eleven calendar weeks, January 12, 2027 through March 30, 2027. Three cohorts, six modules per cohort, each module a single 3.5-hour block. Cohorts A and B complete all six modules inside a four-week window. Cohort C completes the same six modules inside a three-week window with denser scheduling.

## Cohort structure

| Cohort | Window | Size | Composition |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cohort A | January 12, 2027 – February 8, 2027 | 12 midwives | Senior midwives and shift leads, sequenced first so they can co-sign later cohorts |
| Cohort B | February 9, 2027 – March 8, 2027 | 12 midwives | Established midwives with more than three years on the delivery ward |
| Cohort C | March 9, 2027 – March 30, 2027 | 10 midwives | Newly qualified and recently rotated-in midwives, including the mentorship pilot cohort |

Total establishment covered: 34 midwives. Cohort C is intentionally smaller so newly qualified and recently rotated-in midwives can be paired closely with Cohort A assessors during sign-off. Bank and pool staff are covered under a separate abbreviated pathway that is not part of this rollout and is tracked separately in `working_group_action_status.md`.

## Module sequence

1. **Evidence and parameter changes.** What changed against R1, what did not, and why. Covers Section 9 of the protocol draft.
2. **Water temperature management.** Practical drill on the R2 range (36.8 to 37.5 degrees Celsius), correction procedure, and documentation cadence.
3. **Fetal monitoring in the pool.** Auscultation cadence refresh with the second-stage clarification for the primiparous cohort at 90 minutes.
4. **Escalation to obstetrics.** Scenario-based drill including immediate-exit triggers. Co-facilitated by an obstetrician from the working group.
5. **Pool hygiene and biomed handover.** Cadence, documentation, and the interface with the biomed team.
6. **Consolidation and competency sign-off.** Case-based discussion, protocol Q&A, and formal sign-off by a senior midwife (Cohort A) or Cohort A member (Cohorts B and C).

## Competency sign-off

Each midwife signs off on completing all six modules through a short case-based conversation with a designated assessor. Malin Eriksson holds the register. Sign-off is a documented conversation, not a written examination. Midwives who cannot attend a scheduled module within their cohort window are offered a make-up slot inside the same window before being deferred to the next cohort.

## Coverage strategy during training blocks

Modules are scheduled between the day-shift handover at 15:30 and the evening-shift start at 14:30 the next day, so no midwife is pulled off an active labour on the pool during a training slot. Backfill is provided by the mentor from the prior cohort where possible, reducing agency use. Where backfill is required, it is drawn from the internal float pool. No training slot may fall on a midwife's protected recovery day after a night-shift block. This constraint holds without exception.

## Dependencies on equipment readiness

The training rollout depends on both birthing pools being operational from January 12, 2027. Pool A is confirmed ready. Pool B is contingent on biomed sign-off scheduled for November 24, 2026. If Pool B sign-off slips, Cohort A can still complete Modules 1 through 4 using Pool A alone, but Modules 5 and 6 need both pools. Contingency reflected in the risk register as `RR-EQ-001`.

## Assumptions carried into costing

- Backfill cost is calculated at the internal rate, not the agency rate.
- Mentor overlap between Cohorts A and B is credited at 20 percent. If the overlap does not hold in practice, the training-costing sheet recomputes to a ceiling of 1,224 hours.
- No midwife on parental leave or long-term absence during the rollout window is included in the establishment figure of 34.
- Bank and pool staff pathway is out of scope for this costing and tracked separately.
- Every assumption above can be corrected. If any of them is wrong, `training_costing.csv` recomputes cleanly by adjusting the affected cells.
