# Midwifery Mentorship Pilot — Mid-Cycle Readout

**Reference date:** December 18, 2026.
**Cohort launch:** October 10, 2026.
**Co-leads:** Helen Newton, Malin Eriksson.
**Distribution:** Midwifery team only. Not for obstetrics distribution.

The pilot is at its first honest mid-cycle read. This document is for Helen and Malin to walk through together. Nothing here goes further without agreement between the two of us.

## Cohort snapshot as of December 18, 2026

- Twelve mentor-mentee pairings live at launch on October 10, 2026.
- Eleven pairings remain live at the mid-cycle read (ten active, one paused). One pairing (P-07) dissolved on November 22, 2026 by mutual agreement when the mentee moved to the antenatal ward and the pairing lost its shared context. No adverse process reason.
- Median contact frequency across the eleven active pairings over the last 30 days is one substantive conversation per fortnight, which matches the design target of one to two per fortnight.
- Mean mentee rating on the anonymous feedback survey is 4.1 out of 5, with a distribution weighted toward the 4 and 5 bands. Two ratings sit at 3 and both correspond to pairings flagged for review below.

## What is genuinely working

- **The paired reflective-practice sessions**, particularly the ones held during the day-shift changeover window. Mentees consistently name this as the most useful part of the pilot in the free-text feedback.
- **The de-identified case discussion format**, adopted at Helen's suggestion, is holding the confidentiality line without dulling the learning. Malin's observation notes confirm this.
- **The mentor training briefing** held on October 8, 2026 is paying off. Mentors are asking better questions and are notably less prone to slipping into a supervisory posture than they were in the informal pairings we ran in 2024.
- **The Box shared workspace** where Malin has been dropping her observations is working as a genuine working document rather than an archive.

## Where the pairings have gone thin

- **P-04 and P-09.** Both mentees report contact frequency below the design target and cite mentor availability. In both cases the mentor is on a heavy night-shift rotation that runs through the mentorship contact windows. Neither pairing is dysfunctional, both are under-served.
- **P-11.** Mentee rating of 3. Free-text feedback describes the conversations as too focused on protocol recall and not enough on the reflective dimension. The mentor may benefit from a short refresh on the reflective-practice framing.
- **P-02.** Mentee has been on short-term absence since November 14, 2026. Pairing paused, not dissolved. Restart planned January 5, 2027.

## Two or three adjustments before momentum is lost

1. **Rebalance P-04 and P-09** by pairing the mentees with a secondary mentor for the January and February contact windows, without dissolving the primary pairing. Coverage without disruption. Malin to propose the secondary mentors by December 20, 2026.
2. **Refresh the reflective-practice framing** for mentors flagged in P-11 and any pair where the last two feedback ratings landed at 3. Short 30-minute drop-in, offered twice in January 2027. Helen to draft the framing note by December 22, 2026.
3. **Formalise the changeover-window sessions** as a recognised part of the pilot rather than an emergent practice. Update the pilot handbook and log it in Box before the end of December 2026.
