# Evidence Synthesis — Water Birth Protocol Revision R2

**Distribution:** Department head, obstetrics leadership, senior midwives, working group.
**Related artifacts:** `evidence_reconciliation_log.csv`, `evidence_log_deidentified_sample.csv`.
**Status:** Final for approval submission.

## Scope

This synthesis covers the evidence base underpinning the two proposed changes in VGR-DEL-WB-2026-R2 and the outcome domains most commonly raised by obstetrics colleagues when a water birth protocol is revised. It resolves the neonatal-outcomes evidence gap that has been open in the working group log since summer 2026, and it names the source trusted and the source set aside wherever two disagreed. The synthesis is deliberately conservative. Where the evidence is thin, the conclusion is held open rather than forced.

## Method

- Each row in the working group evidence log (`EL-####`) was assessed for methodological weight (A, B, C), with A reserved for randomised controlled trials, systematic reviews, and large prospective cohorts with pre-registered protocols.
- Each study was cross-checked against the two hospital guideline documents senior obstetrics cites (GL-OB-2019-08, GL-OB-2021-14). Where a citation in a guideline no longer matches a stronger newer study, the newer study wins under the working group's stated rule and the disagreement is recorded in `evidence_reconciliation_log.csv` with the value trusted and the value set aside.
- Every finding below is tied back to at least one evidence-log row. Confidence ratings are `high`, `moderate`, `low`, or `insufficient` and follow the definitions the working group agreed on May 4, 2026.
- The evidence log is maintained de-identified from inception; no row can be traced to a woman cared for at the hospital.

## Findings by outcome domain

### Neonatal thermoregulation

The R2 water temperature range (36.8 to 37.5 degrees Celsius) is supported by moderate-to-high strength evidence. Two 2024 prospective cohorts (`EL-0117`, `EL-0121`) and one 2025 systematic review (`EL-0134`) converge on a narrower operational range than GL-OB-2019-08 currently cites. Neonatal thermal-instability rates in the pooled cohort at the R2 range are numerically lower than at the wider R1 range, though the confidence interval overlaps and the working group does not claim a causal reduction. **Confidence: moderate-to-high.** GL-OB-2019-08 (source set aside for this parameter) reflects 2017 consensus and predates the two 2024 cohorts.

### Neonatal infection, first 72 hours

R2 retains the existing exclusion criteria for suspected chorioamnionitis and prolonged rupture of membranes above 24 hours. Evidence from `EL-0089`, `EL-0102`, and `EL-0128` (2022 through 2025) supports the continued exclusion. Early-onset neonatal infection rates in water birth cohorts meeting the exclusion criteria are not statistically different from land birth in the same populations. **Confidence: moderate.**

### Neonatal infection, long-horizon follow-up beyond 28 days

Evidence is thin. Only two studies (`EL-0091`, `EL-0141`) follow neonatal outcomes beyond 28 days, both with sample sizes below 400 and neither with pre-registered protocols. The working group holds an open conclusion here. R2 does not claim a long-horizon infection benefit or an absence of risk. This gap is carried into the risk register as `RR-EV-002 — monitored gap` and is the correct answer to any question at the meeting on this domain. **Confidence: insufficient.**

### Maternal outcomes — perineal trauma

R2 maintains current guidance. Evidence from six studies indexed at methodological weight A or B (`EL-0072`, `EL-0088`, `EL-0110`, `EL-0119`, `EL-0126`, `EL-0139`) shows a consistent reduction in severe perineal trauma (third and fourth degree) in water birth cohorts compared to land birth in comparable populations. GL-OB-2021-14 cites a 2018 review that is now superseded by the 2023 and 2024 studies. **Confidence: high.** The 2018 citation is the source set aside.

### Maternal outcomes — postpartum haemorrhage

The R2 second-stage duration limit (retention of R1 language with a clarified escalation trigger at 90 minutes for the primiparous cohort) is supported by moderate evidence (`EL-0104`, `EL-0122`, `EL-0135`). No change to the PPH management pathway is proposed. **Confidence: moderate.**

### Water quality and pool hygiene

R2 incorporates the biomed-recommended pool cleaning cadence from the 2025 European infection control consensus (`EL-0130`). This is a documentation update rather than a clinical change. **Confidence: high.**

## Open questions

1. Long-horizon neonatal infection follow-up beyond 28 days remains open. R2 will not claim a benefit or absence of risk in this domain.
2. Two hospital guideline citations (GL-OB-2019-08 clause 4.2 and GL-OB-2021-14 clause 6.1) will need to be updated to reflect the sources R2 relies on. This is a downstream action for the guideline maintainers and is flagged in Section 10.
3. Whether the primiparous 90-minute second-stage clarification should be piloted with an audit cycle before formal adoption. Working-group recommendation: yes, from April 6, 2027 through October 6, 2027.

## Sources trusted vs sources set aside

Recorded in full in `evidence_reconciliation_log.csv`. Seven disagreements surfaced, six resolved under the working group's stated rule that the newest and most authoritative source wins. One conflict was held open on insufficient evidence rather than resolved.
