# Memory: Helen Newton

## Personal Profile

Helen Newton is a Bristol-born British woman who has lived in Gothenburg for most of her adult life, and her identity now sits in the long overlap between the two places. She is a registered midwife (barnmorska) with ten years of clinical experience, steady and precise by temperament, with a tenderness she protects through evidence and careful language rather than armour. Off duty she is dry and quietly funny, wittier with close friends than with patients.

- **Education**: Bachelor's in Nursing (sjuksköterskeexamen), Gothenburg University of Health Sciences, 2013; Master's in Midwifery (barnmorskeexamen), 2016; advanced certification in water birth and physiological birth management, 2022.
- **Languages**: English (native), Swedish (fluent, essential for work and daily life), Norwegian (conversational), basic Spanish from a semester in Barcelona.
- **Citizenship and roots**: British citizen, born in Bristol. Moved to Gothenburg for university and stayed. Follows the Guardian and certain BBC shows, returns to Bristol two or three times a year. Holds her British identity with affection and her Swedish identity as the one that shapes daily life.
- **Cultural rhythm**: At home in the Swedish public-healthcare model, the consensus work culture, the Friday fika ritual, and the seasonal markers of Allhelgonadagen, Lucia, and Jul. Conscious of being an immigrant and takes it seriously.
- **Philosophy**: Believes birth is one of the most consequential, intimate moments in a life and that those supporting it owe the birthing person full attention, honest evidence, and space. Believes in physiological birth, informed choice, and public healthcare as a moral good. Not formally religious; her sense of meaning runs through work, close relationships, and the natural year. Her counsellor calls this her practical theology.

## Work & Projects

- **Role**: Registered midwife at Västra Götaland University Hospital, delivery ward (förlossningen). Manages labour, delivery, and immediate postpartum care; handles 8 to 12 deliveries per week depending on shift pattern. Specializes in physiological birth and water birth management.
- **Shift pattern**: Rotating. Day shifts 07:00 to 15:30, evening shifts 14:30 to 22:00, night shifts 21:30 to 07:30. Approximately 38.25 hours per week. Schedule published monthly via the hospital Heroma system.
- **Water Birth Protocol Development**: Leading a working group to update the hospital's water birth guidelines (evidence review, staff training plan, equipment assessment). Target: approved protocol by December 2026. Incorporating feedback from Dr. Henrik Olsson and senior midwives, with an outstanding evidence gap on neonatal outcomes.
- **Midwifery Mentorship Program**: Co-designing a structured mentorship program for newly qualified midwives with Malin Eriksson. Pilot launch October 2026.
- **IBCLC certification**: Studying for the International Board Certified Lactation Consultant exam through self-study and online modules, with three intensive study weekends mapped against shifts.
- **Professional development**: Considering a PhD in midwifery science at Gothenburg University of Health Sciences, exploring options for 2027 to 2028.
