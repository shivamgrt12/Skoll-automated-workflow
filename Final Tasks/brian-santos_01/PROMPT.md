--- TURN 1 ---

We are running out of runway on the tea shop before the December rush swallows me whole, and I want a clear-eyed picture of where Mountain Morning Teas actually stands before I start promising delivery windows to holiday customers I cannot keep. Every year I tell myself I am going to stay on top of the books through the fall market weekends, and every year I come up for air in November and realize I have not reconciled anything since Labor Day, and there is a pile of orders sitting across the storefront, the Saturday market till, and whatever the online side has been doing, and I have no confidence they line up with what the ledger says. I want you to pull every order that came in since the last time the books were clean and reconcile the shop's takings against what actually landed in the shop account, and tell me where money is missing or where it doubled up or where an order got recorded in one place and forgotten in another. The Saturday market days blur together after a while and I know the tap-to-pay side and the online carts do not always agree with what I hand-tally at the end of the day, and I want the discrepancies laid out with real numbers so I can decide what to chase before the holiday orders start crowding out any hope of catching a mistake. Somewhere in there are probably a hundred individual jar orders since September, plus the standing reorders from the co-op and the two cafes, plus that little wave of gift orders that always starts around Thanksgiving, and I need to know how many are actually paid, which are still sitting open, and whether any got stuck because a card failed or a shipping label never printed or a customer clicked something weird at checkout. If there are people sitting on unpaid balances or orders that shipped and never got charged, surface them now while everyone is still answering their phones, and put a message together for each one so I can look them over on Sunday and get out whatever holds up. I also want to know what my actual margin looks like this season, because the jar and label costs went up in the spring and I am not sure I priced the fall reorders to reflect that. Show me what supplies I have paid for since summer and what my wholesale unit cost is running now, and tell me whether my current blend prices are still leaving me anything after packaging costs come off, after the shipping goes out, and after the card side takes its cut, or whether I have been quietly losing money on the popular blends and making it up on the seasonal ones. I need enough detail to argue with, not a headline, and I want you to say clearly what you trusted when the storefront side and the accounting side disagreed, because I know one of them is stale on some of these and I would rather know than pretend they agree. On the inventory side, I do not want to walk into the holiday sale blind. Tell me what blends are running low, whether that is on the jars themselves, the labels, or the actual herb stock, and what the reorder lead time looks like given what my suppliers have been doing about shipping this fall. If there is a blend I am going to run out of before the last market weekend, I want to see that now, not the morning of, and I want a plain read on which blends earn their shelf space through the rush and which ones I should quietly let go until spring. Somewhere in that pile is the wholesale side too, and I want to know if either of the cafes or the co-op is due for a reorder I might have missed while I was heads-down on clinic weeks, because I would rather reach out first than have Emma from the co-op text me on a Friday afternoon asking where her chamomile is. Pull the customer-facing side into shape while you are in there. Anyone who has ordered from me twice or more this year deserves a proper thank-you before I ask them to buy again, and there is a slow-drip welcome sequence for tea-club sign-ups that I set up in the spring and have not looked at since, and I want to know it is still doing what I asked it to before I push the seasonal newsletter out. Put the newsletter draft together, and the follow-ups, and whatever you would send the cafe if a reorder is running late, and land the whole stack in front of me on Sunday afternoon when Neil has the kids at his mom's, and I will sit with them then. I want to come out of this knowing where the shop's money actually sits and what needs restocking or repricing before the rush, and stop opening the storefront tab late at night trying to remember whether a customer named Marcy ever paid.
