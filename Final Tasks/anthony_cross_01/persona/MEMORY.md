# Memory: Anthony Cross

## Personal Profile

Anthony is a former commercial shrimp-boat fisherman who runs Bayshore Sustainable Aquaculture on Clear Lake Shores, Texas, with his business partner Derek Patterson. He lives alone in a small clapboard cottage on the leased waterfront property near NASA Road 1, with a screened porch overlooking the channel and a workshop out back where he restores a 1978 Carolina Skiff in slow hours.

He is Anglo-American with four generations of Galveston Bay and League City roots, the family originally from Yorkshire, England. He holds a high school diploma from Clearwater High School in League City (2011), an Aquaculture Business Certificate from Gulf States Institute Online (2022), and ongoing Marine Biology continuing-education coursework at Brazosport University extension. He speaks English only.

He spent ten years on commercial shrimp boats, including his father's boat the Margaret Anne, before transitioning to aquaculture in 2023. The shift was driven by watching estuary collapse and habitat destruction firsthand, not by ideology. The transition strained his relationship with his father, who views the move as a betrayal of the family trade.

He is quietly superstitious in ways he would deny if pressed (no Friday tank-cycle starts, a specific dock knot, his grandfather's pocket knife in the truck). He processes emotion through labor: built three grow-out tanks the week the divorce papers arrived, repaired the entire dock when his dog died. He reads marine biology journals and Wendell Berry essays alongside Patrick O'Brian novels, mostly keeps it to himself because the marina crowd does not run book clubs. He has a complicated relationship with the gentrification of Galveston, where the channels he fished as a kid are now condo developments.

## Work & Projects

- **Role**: Co-owner and Operations Manager, Bayshore Sustainable Aquaculture, LLC.
- **Business partner**: Derek Patterson (50/50 ownership).
- **Tenure**: 2 years (started January 2024).
- **Operation**: Pacific white shrimp (Litopenaeus vannamei) in recirculating aquaculture systems plus Eastern oysters (Crassostrea virginica) in rack-and-bag systems in permitted tidal lease areas.
- **Site**: 2-acre leased waterfront on Clear Lake Shores with RAS building, nursery, grow-out tanks, oyster processing shed, and dock access.
- **Production**: ~8,000 lb shrimp per year (4 cycles), ~50,000 oysters per year.
- **Clients**: The Boathouse (Chef Marcus Rivera, primary, 80% of oyster harvest), 4 other Galveston restaurants, Saturday farmers' market at the Galveston Strand.
- **Staff**: Jake Dawson (full-time farm hand), one part-time delivery driver shared with another farm.
- **Year 2 of operations**, not yet profitable. Running on the Lonestar Foundation Small Business Innovation Grant ($75,000 over 2 years, year 2) plus restaurant revenue.
- **Current challenge**: Scaling production to meet restaurant demand without overextending cash flow; TPWD permit renewal for oyster lease expansion in progress.
- **Career timeline**:
  - June 2011 to August 2013: Deckhand, Margaret Anne (Robert Cross's shrimp trawler), Kemah waterfront.
  - September 2013 to December 2018: Deckhand and second captain, various commercial shrimp boats out of Galveston and Freeport.
  - January 2019 to December 2021: Captain, F/V Bay Runner (shrimp trawler), Galveston.
  - January 2022 to December 2023: Transition year. Aquaculture Business Certificate (Gulf States Institute Online, 2022); planning and capital assembly with Derek Patterson.
  - January 2024 to present: Co-owner and Operations Manager, Bayshore Sustainable Aquaculture.
