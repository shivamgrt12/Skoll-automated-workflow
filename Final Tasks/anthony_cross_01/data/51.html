Bayshore Sustainable Aquaculture

Recirculating shrimp and rack-and-bag oysters, raised on Galveston Bay

About the Farm

Bayshore Sustainable Aquaculture is a small two-acre operation run by Anthony Cross and his partner Derek Patterson on a leased waterfront near NASA Road 1. Anthony spent ten years on commercial shrimp boats before the move to land-based aquaculture in 2024. The work is biology first: tide windows, water chemistry, and harvest cycles set the pace, and the calendar comes second.

Shrimp

Pacific white shrimp grown in recirculating systems across four cycles a year, sized for restaurant lines.

Oysters

Eastern oysters in rack-and-bag gear on a permitted tidal lease, graded select and standard.

Practice

Clean water is the whole job. Readings logged at five every morning, biosecurity routine held seven days a week.

How It Runs

  • Recirculating grow-out hall with nursery raceway and moving-bed biofilter
  • Rack-and-bag oyster gear maintained from a 16 foot aluminum skiff
  • Grading and bagging in the processing shed, cold hold for delivery
  • Saturday delivery to restaurant accounts before ten, plus the Galveston Strand market
  • One full-time farm hand, Jake Dawson, and a shared part-time delivery driver

Primary Account

The Boathouse takes about 80 percent of the oyster harvest. Chef Marcus Rivera confirms counts Friday afternoon and the order moves with the weekend menu.

Standing Book

Four more Galveston-area kitchens fill weekly volume and spread single-account risk.
