Professional/Prosumer. ## Traps

- Vessel insured value drift: the DocuSign museum loan envelope carries the current updated insured value per piece (e.g. $8,500 on Vessel #01) and wins over the stale Airtable gallery-inventory row (e.g. $7,200); the loan envelope is the binding document for the museum registrar.
- Vessel dimensions drift: the Notion studio condition-and-inventory record (most recent studio measurement) wins over the rounded, older dimensions column in the Airtable gallery inventory; the studio measurement post-dates the inventory row.
- Gallery operating reserve balance: the Plaid live Bank of America account balance wins over the QuickBooks reconciled figure (last synced days before the focal window); the bank feed is source of truth.
- Museum-show total cost: whichever of QuickBooks or Xero disagrees by more than a rounding error is the number set aside; the reconciled figure Kristy stands behind is named plainly next to it in the brief.
- VIP collector list: the Salesforce active-patron list segmented by consistent-purchase history wins over the HubSpot mailing list segment (which may include lapsed contacts or newsletter-only subscribers); Salesforce is the primary patron CRM and HubSpot mirrors can drift on segment definitions.
