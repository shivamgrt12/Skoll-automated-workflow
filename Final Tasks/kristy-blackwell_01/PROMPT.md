--- TURN 1 ---

The Southwest Arts Museum opening lands in a two-week window with the crate leaving the gallery for Santa Fe on the last working morning before the reception, and I need the whole run-up organized so nothing on the museum side or the gallery side collapses while I am split between the two rooms. This is the show that puts everything on the record. Every load-bearing figure in the museum documentation has to survive a museum registrar and an insurance adjuster looking at it hard, and every collector and press contact I want in that room on opening night has to have received the right thing at the right moment without me spending the final stretch chasing my own outbox. I have been carrying pieces of this across several places for months and the version numbers no longer agree. The insured values on the museum loan documentation should line up with the condition-and-inventory record I keep for the vessels in the show and the pricing sheet the gallery works from, and I have lost confidence they still match on every piece. I need the discrepancies run down and every insured figure defensible before anything leaves the studio in a crate, and where two records disagree I want the source I stood behind called out next to the source I set aside so I know what story I am carrying into the museum registrar's office. Pull the show's works one at a time, cross the vessel dimensions and current insured value on the loan documentation against the condition-and-inventory record the studio keeps and the current pricing sheet, and give me back a locked manifest that matches on every piece and has the condition report language ready for the museum's file. Confirm the certificate of insurance from the carrier reads through to the museum's requirements on named-insured and endorsement, and if the endorsement is not in hand, tell me the last point at which we should chase it before the crate leaves. On the press and collector side, the same story has to hold together across the announcement the museum controls, the mailing list I own, and the gallery's public channels. I want a full press-and-collector outreach package drafted and staged for me to look at before anything goes out, with the announcement copy in one place, RSVP tracking against a real invitation list, and private-preview offers to my top patrons ranked so the ones who have collected consistently see the offer before the general list does. Nothing goes to a press contact I have not written to before, and nothing goes to a new collector, without me looking at it and saying send. Draft, hold, surface for me. On the gallery side, the current show still has to keep running, the front-of-house cannot go dark on the opening weekend when I am in Santa Fe, and the next exhibition has to stay on install schedule. Put together a continuity brief for the gallery team covering the coverage rota for the two days I am at the museum, the state of the current show that Lena needs for walk-ins, the install milestones for the next opening, and any inventory or supply order that has to be placed before I go so the studio assistant is not blocked on the museum-piece firing schedule. Anything the brief needs to reach beyond the gallery team gets held for me to read first. On the money side, the museum show has real costs sitting in a handful of invoices that need to reconcile against the gallery operating reserve and my own tracker, and I want the actual balance in the operating account rather than the round figure I keep quoting to myself, then the reconciled museum-show cost against the operating reserve so I know where I am before the last few invoices land. Cross the reconciled figures against the second bookkeeping tool I have running as a sanity check, and if those two disagree by more than a rounding error, call out the one I can defend next to the one I set aside so I know which number is going in the show file. Any single commitment at or above four hundred dollars stops and waits for me. What I want out of this is three pieces I can actually walk into the museum weekend with, a museum install and logistics dossier that reads as one document a registrar would sign off on, a press-and-collector outreach package staged for a final review pass before anything leaves my outbox, and a gallery-and-finance brief for the team plus my own numbers so I know the room is still running and the operating account can carry the show. Anything with health or family detail on it stays inside the authorized circle, and anything with the gallery's financial state or my personal income on it goes only to people I have already told you can see it. Do not commit or send under my name to anyone I have not written to before, do not schedule anything over the full studio day block or my early-morning kiln hours without checking, and where the evidence is thin on any figure, hold an open conclusion and name what would close it rather than papering over. I want the whole package sitting somewhere I can open in the studio before the crate leaves.
