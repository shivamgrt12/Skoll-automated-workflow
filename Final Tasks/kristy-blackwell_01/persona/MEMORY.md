# Memory: Kristy Blackwell

## Personal Profile

- **Full name**: Kristy Blackwell, she/her, American, raised in Albuquerque with deep roots in the Southwest region.
- **Education**: BFA in Ceramics from Southwest Arts Institute in Santa Fe (2008) and MFA from Desert Mesa University (2012).
- **Creative philosophy**: She processes ideas through making before speaking and treats art as cultural conversation rather than decoration.
- **Core values**: Precision in language, seasonal rhythm, and intentional representation guide every show and piece she creates.

## Work & Projects

- **Post-MFA teaching**: Adjunct ceramics instructor at Desert Mesa University from 2012 to 2014 while building an independent studio practice.
- **Independent studio years**: Full-time studio artist from 2014 to 2017, selling through Desert Rain and Turquoise Mesa galleries in Santa Fe.
- **Gallery operations training**: Exhibition coordinator at a contemporary gallery in Albuquerque from 2017 to 2019, learning artist representation and collector relations.
- **Gallery name**: Modern Edge Gallery, founded 2019 on Central Avenue in Nob Hill, 1,800 sq ft exhibition space.
- **Gallery mission**: Represent emerging contemporary artists with strong curatorial integrity, rejecting cliche art and tourist-oriented pieces entirely.
- **Represented artists**: Currently 18 artists working across painting, sculpture, ceramics, textiles, photography, and mixed media disciplines.
- **Exhibition cycle**: New show opens every six to eight weeks, producing seven to eight exhibitions per calendar year.
- **Gallery manager**: Lena Taylor handles full-time daily operations, floor coverage, and visitor relations at $42,000 per year.
- **Annual revenue**: Approximately $280,000 per year in total gallery sales at 50% commission split with represented artists.
- **Monthly rent**: $2,800 per month for the Central Avenue gallery location in Nob Hill, Albuquerque.
- **Notable collectors**: Helen Whitfield in Santa Fe, the Garza Foundation in Denver, and several museum acquisition committees.
- **Current exhibition**: "Water Memory" by Sarah Nelson, a photography and installation exhibition, opened March 20, 2026.
- **Contract template**: Artist representation contract template has been drafted and finalized for onboarding new gallery artists.
- **Studio setup**: Detached casita equipped with electric kiln, gas reduction kiln, wheel, slab roller, and glaze chemistry station.
- **Current series**: "Vessel as Landscape," large coil-built vessels 24 to 36 inches using specialty clay and natural pigments.
- **Pricing range**: Small works $800 to $2,000, large vessels $3,500 to $8,000, and installations $12,000 to $25,000.
- **Solo show**: Southwest Arts Museum in Santa Fe opening October 2026, the most significant career milestone to date.
- **Residency decision**: Declined Prairie Arts Residency for 2026 to dedicate full focus to museum show preparation instead.
- **Sales channels**: Sells primarily through Modern Edge Gallery, also via Desert Rain and Turquoise Mesa galleries in Santa Fe.
- **Kiln upgrade**: Considering the L&L e28T kiln as a replacement for the current studio electric kiln unit.
