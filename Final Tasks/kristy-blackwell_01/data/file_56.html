Before You Reach Out

A few things worth knowing about how the gallery works with artists and collectors.

Consignment and Split

Represented work is taken on consignment at a 50 percent commission split, with the same terms for every artist on the roster. Pricing is set together.

The Exhibition Cycle

The gallery runs seven to eight shows a year on a six to eight week cycle. Most new artists open six to twelve months after a signing, which leaves time to build a coherent body of work.

What We Look For

We sign emerging contemporary artists with a real practice and somewhere to travel, not a single strong piece. Every serious proposal gets read by the director.

What the Roster Covers

Painting
Ceramics
Photography
Mixed Media

Painting anchors the roster and the year. We balance the calendar so a painting heavy run is offset later by other materials, keeping the program legible to collectors who follow one medium.

Ceramics has a strong place at the gallery, from functional bodies of work to large sculptural form. The director is a coil built vessel artist, so the material gets close attention.

Photography rounds out most seasons, often paired with another medium in a two person slot. We look for a sustained project rather than a loose set of images.

Mixed media work is welcome when it reads as a coherent practice. We want to understand the material logic before it goes on the wall.
