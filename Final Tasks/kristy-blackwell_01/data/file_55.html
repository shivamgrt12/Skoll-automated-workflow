Coil Built Vessel Series

Vessels That Read as Terrain

A series of large coil built vessels drawn from high desert ground, heading to a Santa Fe museum opening in October 2026.

On the Series

Each vessel begins as a single coil and rises by hand over weeks, until the form stands closer to terrain than to anything you would set on a table. I build the surface in layered slips that hold the color of a particular stretch of mesa, its sediment, its weather, its slow erosion. The title, vessel as landscape, is not a metaphor I added later. It is how the work behaves in a room, asking to be walked around rather than looked at. The museum show gathers a small number of major pieces so that each one holds a full wall of floor space, and restraint carries as much meaning as the making.

Selected Vessels

Major works from the series traveling to the October 2026 opening.

Mesa Seam

A tall coil built vessel whose vertical seam reads like a fault line, surfaced in layered ochre and ash slips drawn from a single ridge.

Sediment Standing

A wide bodied form built to chest height, its banded surface laid down in sediment layers that record weeks of slow construction.

Erosion Vessel

A piece whose softened lip and worn glaze suggest weather acting on stone, the most overtly landscape reading of the group.

Dry Wash

A narrow vessel with a pale, cracked slip that holds the look of a riverbed in late summer drought.

High Ground

The largest work in the show, raised over several weeks, with a horizon line of darker slip ringing the upper third.

Night Mesa

A deep toned vessel finished in iron rich slips, built as the quiet anchor at the far end of the room.

Making and Firing the Series

Jan 2025
First Coils Raised
Studio work began on the largest vessels, raising the first standing forms one coil at a time.
Apr 2025
Slip Tests
A full season of slip and color tests against high desert reference, settling the palette for each vessel.
Aug 2025
Forms Completed
The core group of major vessels reached full height and dried slowly under cover for even shrinkage.
Nov 2025
Bisque and Glaze Firings
Vessels moved through bisque and glaze firings in sequence, with surfaces refined between each.
Feb 2026
Final Surface Work
Last surface passes and condition documentation completed in the studio ahead of crating.
