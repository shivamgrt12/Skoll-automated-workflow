# Memory: John Walker

## Personal Profile

- **DOB**: January 17, 1978

John Francis Walker grew up in South Boston on Dorchester Street, son of Boston FD firefighter Frank Walker Sr. and Mary Walker, parish school secretary at Gate of Heaven. He is third-generation Irish-American; his grandparents emigrated from County Cork in the 1920s and never went back. Boston Latin Academy, South Boston High, Associate's in Fire Science from Quincy College in 2000, then early-2000s part-time security and per-diem EMT shifts on the South Shore, Massachusetts Firefighting Academy class of 2004, joined QFD that summer. The family moved to Wollaston when Patrick was born in 2006.

He is Catholic in framework even where the practice has fallen off since Mary died in March 2021. He still sketches the sign of the cross at serious accidents on Route 3, and a small photo of his mother on the basement desk gets dusted more often than anything in the room. His temper runs hot and fast and then cools, inherited from his father and held back from his sons. His humor is dark and firehouse-fluent; his preferred form of love is showing up. Colleen calls the wall around what he carries "the Walker wall."

He believes a life is what a person does for the people they are responsible for. The job funds the points; the marriage and the children and the men he goes into bad places with are the points. He has buried more brothers than he wanted to, and he expects to bury more.

## Work & Projects

John has 22 years with the Quincy Fire Department, joined summer 2004 after the Massachusetts Firefighting Academy, promoted to Captain at the 12-year mark in 2016. Assigned to Station 2, Quincy Center, A-shift, running Engine 2 (2019 Pierce Enforcer) with Ladder 2 (2016 Pierce Ascendant) cross-staffing. Schedule is 24 on / 48 off, plus 2 to 3 OT 24s per month covering B-shift or C-shift vacancies. Base salary $88K; OT pushes QFD compensation to about $105K. Credentials: Fire Officer I-IV, EMT-Paramedic, Hazmat Technician, CISM, standard NFPA. He is IAFF Local 792 shop steward, handling grievances, contract negotiation, and disciplinary hearings.

The Battalion Chief promotional exam is November 10, 2026. Promotion raises pay by about $15K and shifts him to a 40-hour command role with no engine time. He is conflicted: the money would help with Maeve's dance, Patrick's tuition, and the pension bump, but the firehouse floor is who he is. Colleen wants him to take it. Study prep covers NFPA 1021, ICS-400, Blue Card IC, and department SOPs. The books are bought; the cracking of them is the issue.

He is also adjunct EMT-Basic instructor at Quincy College Health Sciences since 2023. Two sections per semester, Tuesday and Thursday 6:00 to 9:00 PM, off-duty days only. Adjunct pay $8K/year, no benefits. Popular with the mostly career-changer and pre-nursing students; he enjoys teaching because it does not hurt his body.

## Health & Wellness

- **Primary care**: Dr. Kevin Walsh, Redbud Healthcare, Quincy. Next appointment November 6, 2026.
- **Cholesterol**: High LDL. Rosuvastatin 10 mg nightly with dinner. Last panel: total 238, LDL 152, HDL 44 (low), triglycerides 210 (high). Compliance poor; firehouse food culture works against him.
- **Right rotator cuff**: Chronic, 3+ years. Repetitive strain from hose work, ladders, forcible entry. Never imaged. Manages with 600 mg ibuprofen 2-3x daily and ice. Worse in cold and after 24s. Overhead reach is the failure point.
- **Tinnitus**: Bilateral, moderate. Years of siren and SCBA alarm exposure. Constant high-pitched ringing, worse in quiet rooms. White noise machine at the bedside; no workers' comp claim filed.
- **Sleep**: Difficult. Hancock Street nightmares 2-3x/week, wake Colleen. White noise helps the tinnitus, not the dreams.
- **PTSD-adjacent (undiagnosed, untreated)**: November 2019 3-alarm fire at 47 Hancock Street; John's company entered and found 8-year-old Lily Tran in a second-floor bedroom, smoke inhalation, four minutes too late. He carries the unit citation in a drawer and does not look at it. Symptoms: the nightmares, hypervigilance on structure-fire calls, route avoidance past Hancock Street, and a six-month post-incident drinking period he pulled back from after Colleen confronted him. Refuses EAP, peer support, and therapy.
- **Diet (medically driven)**: Colleen pushes lean protein, more fish, less red meat since the rosuvastatin started. He complies at home and cheats at the station and the grill.
- **QFD annual physical**: Annual physical and fit test in November, required for active duty status.
- **General**: Physically strong, wearing down. Knees creak on stairs, back stiffens after long shifts, hands calloused, fifteen pounds heavier than at 40.
