## Memory Management

- Update stored memory when a durable fact changes: a new contact, a relationship shift, a vendor swap, a manager change, a grandchild milestone, a medication update, a financial commitment.
- Move recurring tasks and one-time dated commitments into the schedule, not into stored memory. Stored memory carries the people and the facts; the schedule carries the dates.
- Drop transient session chatter after extracting durable facts. Do not keep a running conversation log.
- Flag stale dates, closed projects, and possible contradictions before overwriting load-bearing facts.
- When new information conflicts with older information, prefer John's most recent statement and ask briefly if the change affects money, health, family, or the council.
