# Memory: John Ramirez

## Personal Profile

- **DOB**: November 22, 1962

John Alejandro Ramirez is a self-made man who left home at nineteen with nothing and worked dish rooms, construction sites, and gas stations before finding auto body work. He started Ramirez Auto Body in 1992 in one rented bay on Market Street and grew it to three locations and eighteen employees. He won a Ward 5 council seat in 2023 on shoe leather and neighborhood trust, not money or connections.

His cultural self is layered. Spanish at home with his mother growing up, English everywhere else, with Peru in the food, the music, and his brother's voice on the Sunday call, and Paterson in the corner-store geography of his whole adult life. He came up Catholic and still goes to mass at St. Michael's, where he sits on the parish council. Technical diploma in auto body repair from Passaic County Technical Institute, 1986. He believes work has dignity in proportion to the care it is done with, that loyalty is not a transaction, and that providing is one way to love someone but not the only one.

## Work & Projects

Ramirez Auto Body, founded 1992 in one rented Market Street bay, now three locations with eighteen employees and roughly $2.2M annual revenue. Services: collision, paint refinishing, frame straightening, dent repair, insurance claim processing. Market Street (main, six bays, insurance-focused, Hector manages, John's office), Broadway (four bays, insurance and walk-in mix, Tony manages, bought from Tony's old shop in 2012), Totowa (three bays, suburban clientele, Luis manages, opened 2022). DRP certified with State Farm, Allstate, Geico, Progressive, NJ Manufacturers.

- **Customer appreciation day**: Annual event on October 5 at the Market Street location with free estimates, food, and giveaways for the community.

Active concerns: labor shortage in the trade, losing techs to dealerships that pay more. Insurance reimbursement rates lagging material and labor cost increases, constant fight with adjusters. Totowa burning roughly $3K a month, needs another eighteen months to profitability. $85K ADAS calibration equipment decision pending. Market Street lease up December 31, 2026 with the landlord hinting at a 12% increase. PPG and Axalta paint and materials up 22% in two years, rising quarterly.

Council role: Ward 5 on the Paterson City Council since 2023. Council meets at City Hall the 1st and 3rd Tuesday at 7:00 PM. John sits on the budget committee; FY2028 hearings start November 3, 2026. Still learning parliamentary procedure, never unsure about what his constituents need.

## Health & Wellness

5'10", 205 lbs (Carmen would prefer 190), solid build, weight in the midsection. Hands scarred and thick at the knuckles from decades of body work, and he is quietly fond of them.

Type 2 diabetes, diagnosed eight years ago. Metformin 1000mg twice daily (morning and evening with meals), Jardiance 25mg every morning. Last A1C 7.1, down from 7.6, goal under 7.0. Fasting glucose runs 130 to 150, should be under 130. Diet compliance is good at home (Carmen runs dinner) and poor between shops, where fast food, bakery stops, and skipped meals are the pattern. Checks blood sugar 4 to 5 times a week; Dr. Reyes wants daily.

Hypertension, diagnosed twelve years ago. Lisinopril 20mg and Amlodipine 5mg every morning. Last reading 138/86, goal under 130/80. Worse during council budget season and insurance disputes.

Gout, diagnosed six years ago. Allopurinol 300mg every morning, last uric acid 5.8 (well controlled). Flares two to three times a year, usually right big toe or left ankle. Triggers: shellfish, red meat, beer, dehydration, stress. Colchicine 0.6mg as needed during flares.

Right knee replacement (total knee arthroplasty), September 2024, Dr. Antonelli at St. Joseph's Health. Recovery went well; twelve weeks PT; walks without limp but stiffens after long standing. Uses the knee as his barometer for weather changes. Cleared to dance salsa; Carmen tested it within four months.

Dietary picture is diabetes-driven and gout-cautious: lower carbs at home, watch shellfish and red meat and beer. He complies with Carmen's house menu and cheats between shops. Coffee is strong with a little sugar from a thermos. Alcohol is moderate (Pisco Sour on occasion, Modelo Especial with the guys, a glass of wine with Carmen on date nights).
