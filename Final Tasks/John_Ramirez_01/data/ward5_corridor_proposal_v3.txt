WARD 5 SMALL-BUSINESS CORRIDOR PROPOSAL
=========================================

(Second reading draft, version 3)
Sponsored by: Councilman John Ramirez (Ward 5)
First reading: October 6, 2026
Second reading: scheduled

PURPOSE

To establish a designated small-business support corridor along Market Street between East 18th Street and Park Avenue, providing targeted support for the 87 active small-business taxpayers in that corridor.

BACKGROUND

Ward 5 contains 134 small-business taxpayers across the council district. Of those, 87 sit within the proposed Market Street corridor. Together they employ approximately 410 residents and generate roughly $14M in annual receipts. The corridor has lost 11 storefronts to vacancy in the past 18 months, primarily due to:

- Lease cost increases averaging 18% at renewal (citation: corridor lease comp study, Ward 5 Office)
- Parking limitations during the East 18th repaving project
- Loss of foot traffic during the 2024-2025 sewer reconstruction

KEY ASKS FOR FY2028

1. **Small-Business Grant Program scaling**
   - Current: $250K total pool / 8-12 grants of $20-30K each
   - Proposed: $400K total pool / 15-20 grants of $20-30K each
   - Eligibility: storefront businesses with <20 employees, 3+ years in Paterson
   - Match: 1:1 dollar match required

2. **Parking Moratorium Relief**
   - Restoration of 14 on-street parking spaces along Market Street that were removed in 2024 for the bike-lane buffer
   - Conversion of one alley to small-vehicle parking (capacity 22)
   - Estimated cost: $180K one-time

3. **Street-Level Facade Matching Fund**
   - 50% matching fund for facade improvements, capped at $8K per business
   - Total fund: $80K (covers approximately 20 businesses)

ANTICIPATED OPPOSITION

- Budget committee may push back on the scaled grant program given competing demands from Parks, Public Safety, and Education
- Some Ward 3 and Ward 6 council members may argue for cross-ward allocation rather than corridor-specific spend

GROUND-TRUTH ANECDOTES (from constituent emails Oct 2026)

A. Sabor Criollo (Maria Pedraza, owner, 12 years in Paterson)
"We weathered the sewer construction with savings. The lease increase this October hit us at the same moment our two-block business district lost two more storefronts. We need either rent relief or a sign that the city is in this with us."

B. Ramirez Auto Body (John Ramirez, owner, 34 years in Paterson)
"My Market Street bay has been on the same lease since 2022. My renewal is asking +12%. If I can't anchor at corridor pricing, I have to seriously consider walking, and that takes 8 jobs and a decade of community presence with me."

C. Mendez Hardware (Carlos Mendez, owner, 22 years in Paterson)
"We applied for the small-business grant program last year and the pool was depleted by Q2. We made it work without the grant but we needed it. Twenty grants instead of ten would have saved one storefront I know of personally."
