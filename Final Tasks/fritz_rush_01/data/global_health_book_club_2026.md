# Global Health Book Club 2026 - Reading List & Notes

Monthly book club with Glenwood College Division of Pediatric Infectious Disease
faculty and fellows. Fritz organizes. Meets first Thursday of each month at 7:30 PM,
either Fritz's dining table or Kevin Osborne's back porch depending on weather.

Focus: narrative nonfiction and long-form journalism on global health, infectious
disease history, implementation science, and the political economy of medicine. No
textbooks. No unpublished manuscripts. No R21 preview material.

## Standing rules (Fritz's list)

- No promotional material for anyone's own research
- No R21 or grant-cycle content
- No conference recaps as discussion topic
- The book is the book. If someone did not finish, they still come and listen
- No book longer than 400 pages
- One month per year we read a book none of us would pick, chosen by lot

## Notes from Feb 5, 2026 (Mountains Beyond Mountains, Fritz hosted)

Group discussion focused on Paul Farmer's approach to implementation as a moral
question. Hassan raised whether the biography over-romanticizes the individual
physician-founder model, and Fritz agreed the book has that flaw. Preeti asked what
implementation science literature had absorbed and what it had rejected from Farmer's
approach. Good discussion. Yolanda took notes for the group.

## Notes from May 7, 2026 (And the Band Played On, Janet hosted)

The room split on whether Shilts' journalistic method aged well. Kevin argued the
book remains the single most important document on the political failure of the
early AIDS response. Fritz agreed but noted the book's silence on affected
communities' own responses. Long conversation. Meeting ran to 9:45 PM which for us
is a record.

## Notes from Jul 2, 2026 (Spillover, Hassan hosted)

Timely given post-COVID context. Discussion focused on Quammen's framing of
zoonotic spillover as inevitable rather than preventable. Group generally agreed
the framing is useful but incomplete. Marcus raised the implementation-science
question about surveillance vs response, which is where the book lands well.

## Notes from Sep 3, 2026 (The Wisdom of Whores, Fritz hosted)

Pisani's book on HIV epidemiology and the politics of what gets counted. Excellent
discussion. Group agreed this was one of the best reads of the year. Fritz brought
the cheese plate as usual. Meeting ended on time at 9:15 PM.
