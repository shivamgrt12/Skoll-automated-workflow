# Memory: Jessica Murray

## Personal Profile

- **DOB**: February 17, 1976

Jessica Marie Murray is a master plumber and licensed general contractor in Providence, Rhode Island, who has spent three decades in the trades. She worked general construction labor around Providence from 1994 to 1996 before securing an apprenticeship spot at the UA Local 51 apprenticeship program (1996 to 2000), got her master plumber's license in 2000 (RI Master Plumber License #MP-20-0847, issued by the Rhode Island Contractors' Registration and Licensing Board), her general contractor's license in 2004 (RI General Contractor License #GC-04-2391), and opened Murray Plumbing & General Contracting, LLC in 2006.

She is a high school graduate (Cranston High School West, 1994) with no further formal education and no apology for it. Her family has been in New England for generations. The Rhode Island accent surfaces under stress and around her mother. English only.

She reads people the way she reads a wall: looking for what holds and what gives. Direct without being unkind, except when she has decided someone has earned it. Long fuse for genuine effort, short fuse for excuses dressed up as explanations. Humor is the load-bearing wall: dry, quick, constant. She is more extroverted than she presents on first meeting, generous with the people she has earned and uninterested in expanding the circle.

Her working-class identity is the architecture of how she sees the world, carried without performance and without apology. Her philosophy is short: work well done is its own argument, loyalty matters more than agreement, money is a tool, family is the gravity. Sunday brunch at her mother's is the closest thing to a religious observance in her life now, and she rearranges the week around it.
