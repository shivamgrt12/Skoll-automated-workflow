STOLLEN (Christstollen)
Original recipe from Solingen, adapted by Hannelore Sauer circa 1960

Fruit soak (begin 2-3 weeks before baking):
  250g raisins (golden preferred)
  100g currants  
  75g candied orange peel, finely diced
  75g candied lemon peel, finely diced
  50ml rum (dark, not spiced)
  Store in sealed jar, shake daily

Dough:
  500g bread flour (NOT all-purpose, the protein matters)
  200g unsalted butter, room temperature
  100g granulated sugar
  7g instant yeast (or 20g fresh)
  150ml whole milk, lukewarm
  2 large eggs
  1/2 tsp salt
  1 tsp vanilla extract
  Zest of 1 lemon
  1/2 tsp ground cardamom
  1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  1/4 tsp ground mace (Hannelore's secret - most recipes omit this)

Filling:
  200g marzipan (homemade if possible - 1:1 almond flour to powdered sugar, bound with egg white)
  100g blanched almonds, roughly chopped

Method:
  1. Warm milk to 110F, bloom yeast 5 minutes
  2. Mix flour, sugar, salt, spices in large bowl
  3. Add butter in pieces, eggs, vanilla, lemon zest, yeast mixture
  4. Knead 10 minutes until smooth and elastic (Hannelore: "it should feel like a baby's skin")
  5. Drain fruit, reserve rum. Fold fruit and almonds into dough gently
  6. First rise: 90 minutes, covered, warm place
  7. Shape into oval, roll marzipan log, fold dough over it
  8. Second rise: 45 minutes
  9. Bake 350F for 45-50 minutes until deep golden
  10. Brush immediately with melted butter (generous)
  11. Dust heavily with powdered sugar when cooled
  12. Wrap in foil, store 1-2 weeks before slicing (it gets better with time)

Hannelore's notes in the margin: "Do not rush step 11. The butter must be hot and the sugar must be thick. This is where Americans always cut corners and wonder why it tastes like cake instead of bread."

Amy's note (2024): Tried this December 2024. Used store marzipan (mistake). Next time make homemade. The mace really does matter. Took photos of the cross-section - beautiful layers.
