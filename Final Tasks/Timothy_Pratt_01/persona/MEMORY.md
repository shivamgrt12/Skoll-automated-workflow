# Memory: Timothy Pratt

## Personal Profile

- Full name: Timothy James Pratt.
- Pronouns: he/him.
- Identity: Methodist on the years he goes; Vietnam-era U.S. Army veteran (1971 to 1974, non-combat support); does not perform grief and does not perform pride.
- Ethnicity and background: White, non-Native; fifth-generation Montana rancher on the high prairie south of Browning, family on the same stretch since his grandfather's grandfather, lifetime of working relationships across the line to the Blackfeet Reservation.
- Education: Browning High School (1971); U.S. Army training during service (1971 to 1974). No college degree.
- Occupation: Semi-retired rancher and sole operator of the home section; advisory member on the Browning Town Council; mentor through the County Agricultural Extension program, forty years running cattle and four years semi-retired since selling the main herd in 2022.
- Specialization: Cattle and horse husbandry, fence and pasture management, grazing-lease administration, weather and animal-behavior reading on the Rocky Mountain Front, agricultural policy and water rights at the practitioner level.
- Marital status: Widowed February 2022. Married Mary Pratt forty years; she died of complications of systemic lupus erythematosus at 63. No subsequent relationships and not in the market.

## Work & Projects

Timothy is a semi-retired rancher with a continuous career timeline. After his Army discharge in 1974, he returned to the family ranch and worked alongside his father from 1974 through 1982, taking on increasing responsibility for cattle, fences, equipment, and the grazing rotation. He formally assumed the operation in 1982 when he married Mary and his father stepped back to a part-time role on the place. He sold the main herd (over 200 head) in 2022 after Mary's death and his diabetes worsened, and he now keeps thirty head of Angus cross on the home section. He retains 1,200 acres and leases grazing rights to Calvin Hunter and two other neighboring ranchers. He still does daily ranch work (fence checking, feeding, horse care) at a reduced pace since the knee surgery, and Tyler helps on weekends and school breaks. He sits on the Browning Town Council as an advisory member, non-voting, attending monthly meetings on the first Tuesday at the Browning Town Hall and weighing in on land use, agricultural policy, and water rights from the practitioner side. He mentors young ranchers through Dr. Margaret Spencer's County Agricultural Extension program, hosting two to three mentees per season on his property for hands-on training, and he participates in the County Cattlemen's Workshop every March.

## Health & Wellness

- **Type 2 diabetes**: diagnosed 2016. Metformin 1000 mg twice daily with meals. A1C 7.4 at the February 2026 check; Dr. Walker wants it below 7.0. Fasting glucose checked three times per week with the OneTouch Verio, typically 118 to 155 mg/dL, target under 130. Diabetic retinopathy screening annually; last screening clear, January 2026. Mild bilateral foot neuropathy; careful with boots and cold exposure.
- **Chronic kidney disease, Stage 2**: diagnosed 2024. eGFR 68 at February 2026; creatinine 1.4 mg/dL. Low-protein diet recommended; he finds it difficult on a rancher's plate. Extra hydration emphasized; carries a water bottle now at Elaine's insistence. Six- to nine-month nephrology rhythm with Dr. Crawford while eGFR holds.
- **Left knee replacement**: surgery November 2025 at Northern Rockies Medical Center. Physical therapy completed February 2026. Stiffness and occasional swelling after long days. Uses a cane on uneven terrain only when Elaine is watching. Right knee shows early arthritis.
- **Hypertension**: Losartan 50 mg daily, also protective of the kidneys. Typically 145/88, target under 135/85. Weekly cuff readings on an Omron monitor that Elaine logs.
- **Hearing loss**: moderate, bilateral, worse in the left ear, likely noise-related from ranch equipment and firearms. Phonak behind-the-ear hearing aids since 2024. Still misses things in group conversations and wind.
- **Sleep**: bed at 9:00 PM, wake at 4:45 AM, six to six and a half hours, nocturia once per night.
- **Mental health**: stoic. Misses Mary deeply, visits her grave monthly. Finds purpose in the ranch, Tyler, and community responsibilities. Winter loneliness is the hardest stretch.
- **VA care**: Montana Valley VA Clinic handles hearing aids and the annual VA physical. Vietnam veteran, Army, 1971 to 1974, non-combat support.
- **Medication list**: Metformin 1000 mg twice daily; Losartan 50 mg morning; Tylenol as needed (avoids NSAIDs due to kidney); daily multivitamin; vitamin D 1000 IU.
- **Vaccines**: flu and COVID current; both each fall at the Browning Community Health Center.

## Interests & Hobbies

- Horses are the center. He keeps six and rides daily when the knee allows. Sage is the mare he reaches for without thinking.
- The land itself: walks the fences, watches the grass month over month, tracks elk migrations as old neighbors, sits at the lower cottonwood flat each September when the leaves turn gold.
- Fly tying in winter at the south-window workbench; flies for Birch Creek and the upper Two Medicine.
- Woodworking in the shop, mostly utilitarian: gates, fence work, the occasional small piece for the grandkids.
- Hunting season: elk first, then deer, with Tyler when Tyler can get away.
- Reading: Louis L'Amour and Western histories, kept on the shelf he built when Mary was pregnant with Elaine.

- Views of the Rocky Mountain Front from every window; Chief Mountain visible to the northwest.
- Kitchen: functional and unchanged in twenty years. Large table where Mary used to spread her lesson plans.
- Living room: Mary's quilts in real use, a glass case of her knitted blankets, family photographs along one hallway.

- 2018 Ford F-250 diesel, 140,000 miles. Pulls the horse trailer.
- Heat: propane furnace plus a wood stove in the living room. Timothy splits his own wood with help from Tyler now.
- Rural security: locked doors, ranch dogs (Blue, a 9-year-old Australian Shepherd), motion-sensor yard light. Nearest neighbor is Calvin, two miles east.
- Internet: HughesNet satellite. Adequate for email and browsing, slow for anything else.
