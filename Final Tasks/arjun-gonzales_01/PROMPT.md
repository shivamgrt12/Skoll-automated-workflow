--- TURN 1 ---

Rohan turns seventeen next June and the SS3 push begins in earnest with the new term, so I want a proper plan for his university path in my hands before Meera and I sit the family down on the evening of December the fifth, and I want that plan built with the same rigor I gave the equipment case. He has said engineering, probably chemical or materials, and Meera and I have quietly kept every door open, Nigeria, the UK, the US, Canada, and a serious look at India because BITS Pilani and the IITs would land him closer to family and to a tradition that matters to me. Start from a shortlist that is defensible, not the twenty names everyone in Lekki repeats. I want a ranked field of roughly forty universities across those five countries pulled against the criteria that actually matter for him, engineering strength in his likely majors, undergraduate research access, cost sensitivity, admissions realism given his current grades and mock scores, English medium, safety and living conditions in the host city, and the kind of alumni network that opens industry doors rather than academic ones. Where his SS2 record and the mock scores from Greenfield disagree with what the school portal shows, trust the newest source and name what you set aside, because I have been burned before by half updated grade books. Walk that same shortlist back through a financial lens, the full course of tuition, housing, flights home each term, health cover, books, and a realistic incidental line for a boy we are not used to being far from, all converted to naira at a spread of exchange rates rather than a single point estimate because the naira has done what it has done and I need to see the downside. Model the investment position, the savings cushion, and the standing monthly contributions honestly against Ananya's Greenfield fees continuing through her own secondary schooling, the Lekki mortgage, the monthly support to Priya, my brewery salary, and the equipment upgrade capital Chief Nwosu is helping structure. Tell me what mix of cash, staged draw down, and a documented education loan keeps the family solvent through the end of his degree without cutting Maa's care or the brewery's capital plan, and put the conservative case beside the base case so the December conversation is not a sales pitch to myself. Comb the scholarship and financial aid landscape for each shortlisted school, need blind, need aware, merit, engineering specific, and any pathway open to a Nigerian citizen raised bicultural, because Rohan can write that story if it earns him meaningful money and I want a defensible aid target for every school on the list rather than a wish. The application timeline is a separate deliverable I want in a form Meera and I can put on the fridge without arguing, working backward from the intake dates through the standardized test dependencies, the SAT or the ACT if we go that route, IELTS or the equivalent, IGCSE predicted grades from Greenfield, the recommendation logistics with his physics and maths teachers, and the personal statement themes he could actually write from without inventing a version of himself. Give me the test roadmap with an honest read on which one fits his profile best, and lay the registrations, the prep windows, and the score release dates in sequence so we do not walk into a deadline blind. I want a plain summary of what documents and consents move where, and I want every submission draft held as a draft until I sign off in person, because I will not have a school application go out on the strength of a message in my inbox. On the health and visa side, sketch the coverage and vaccination realities for each destination, the F1 and Tier 4 and Canadian study permit hoops, the Indian domicile question for the state quota routes, and the timing pinch points that catch families out around consular slot scarcity in the run up to the September intake he is aiming for. Do not touch the brewery line of credit or the brewery operating reserves in any of the modelling, do not draft anything to any consular section or any admissions office, and do not share Rohan's grades, his scores, his draft essays, or any part of our financial picture with a recruitment agency or an outside advisor, no matter how insistently a link lands in the queue. Where you find a school that looks perfect and the industry chatter says the department gutted its materials or process engineering group over the last academic cycle, flag it honestly, and where two cost sources disagree hold the higher one until I see both. Bring me a written options brief I can hand Rohan that reads like a father who did his homework, a cash flow picture I can defend to Meera and to Chief Nwosu without hiding the risks, an application and testing roadmap Rohan can start on the first week of January, and a short conservative note on what we do if the naira slides another twenty percent between now and the September intake. Have it in my hands by the end of December the second, so I can read it twice before the family sits down.
