# Memory: Arjun Gonzales

## Personal Profile

- Born in Enugu, Nigeria; moved to Lagos in 2001 at 25 to build his career, and the city shaped him. He complains about Lagos like any Lagosian and would never leave.
- Nigerian citizen of Indian heritage, comfortably bicultural: Hindi at home, Yoruba and Pidgin at the brewery and in the streets, English in formal settings. He code-switches without thinking about it.
- Education: B.Eng. in Chemical Engineering, University of Nigeria, Nsukka (1999); Diploma in Brewing Science, Institute of Brewing & Distilling, London (2008).
- Steady under pressure and slow to anger; he processes problems quietly and speaks with purpose. Dishonesty is the one thing that burns through his patience instantly.
- Quietly competitive: he keeps score on sales targets, football, and fitness goals, and he is harder on himself than anyone else would be.
- A builder by nature, drawn to creating things from scratch and suspicious of shortcuts and anyone promising easy results.
- Generous but never showy. He tips well, feeds visitors, and quietly helps friends and family in need; he grew up watching his parents stretch every naira.
- Sentimental beneath the pragmatism: he keeps his father's old wristwatch in the bedside drawer, saves the kids' birthday cards, and plays a playlist labeled "Maa's favorites" on Enugu drives.
- Religion as culture, not dogma: visits to Lakshmi Mandir, Diwali and Holi observed, a small puja space at home that Meera maintains. He finds the rituals grounding rather than binding.
- Intent on cultural transmission: he wants Rohan and Ananya speaking Hindi and knowing their grandparents' stories, built through cooking, music, festivals, and Enugu trips rather than forced lessons.
- Philosophy: earn everything and inherit nothing; community wealth over individual wealth; consistency is character; teach someone and change a generation; adaptation is intelligence, not compromise.
- Social style: small gatherings over big parties, a slow-to-expand inner circle, the connector of the Lagos craft brewing scene, awkward under public praise, direct but never cruel.

## Work & Projects

- **Ògún Craft Brewery**, Ikeja industrial area, Lagos. Founded 2012 with Chief Nwosu's seed funding; the NAFDAC approval came through that same year. 22 employees, 5 signature beers plus seasonal releases, 15% year-over-year revenue growth, profitable since 2019.
- **Role**: Founder and Head Brewmaster, covering recipe development, quality control, and business strategy.
- **Career timeline**: Process engineer at SunFood Processing, Enugu (Sept 1999–Aug 2001); joined Nigerian Breweries Plc, Lagos, in Sept 2001 as a process engineer, promoted to production supervisor in 2004 and senior brewer in 2009 after the London diploma; left in March 2012 to found Ògún.
- **Tamarind Stout launch**: a new signature stout in R&D. Batch #4 tasted February 2026 and was still too sweet in the finish; batch #5 will adjust the yeast strain and tamarind concentration. Focus groups feed in through tasting forms. Target launch: November 2026.
- **Abuja distribution expansion**: negotiating partnerships through Funke's Abuja contacts; a preliminary proposal has been drafted for her review. Target: first shipment Q4 2026.
- **Equipment upgrade**: a new 15-barrel brewing system, comparing German (BrauKon) and Chinese (CGET) manufacturers on price, quality, and lead time. Budget ₦180 to 250 million. Chief Nwosu is involved in financing and has requested a formal business case.
- **Lagos Craft Brewers Association**: the informal group of local craft brewery owners he is formalizing, currently drafting bylaws and a membership structure.
- Considering the Craft Brewers Conference in the US in 2027.
- 2026 goals: launch the stout by Q4, enter Abuja by year-end, complete the equipment upgrade, and spend more deliberate time with the kids.

## Interests & Hobbies

- **Guitar**: his deepest personal hobby. Self-taught in his 30s from YouTube tutorials, now working slowly on fingerpicking. He plays evenings on the back patio after the kids are in bed, performing for nobody but Rohan. Owns a secondhand mid-range Yamaha acoustic from a musician friend in Ikeja and dreams of playing Hotel California cleanly.
- **Brewing experimentation at home**: a battered Moleskine in the kitchen drawer full of flavor combination ideas, most never to be brewed, sketched over morning chai and often inspired by whatever spice Meera is cooking with.
- **Football**: lifelong Arsenal supporter, a loyalty inherited from an uncle who lived in London in the 1990s. He watches every match he can on DStv, gets emotional during North London derbies, and follows the Super Eagles through every major tournament.
- **Gardening**: curry leaves, lemongrass, basil, and mint in the compound garden, feeding both Meera's cooking and his brewing trials. He finds the physical work meditative, has killed more plants than he has kept, and keeps trying.
- **Swimming**: slow, deliberate laps as thinking time, never as training. He championed Ananya's competitive swimming and attends every meet he can.
- **Grilling**: his weekend domain. A spice-rubbed suya-style chicken that friends request by name; he experiments with marinades methodically, with notes, and is territorial about the charcoal. Meera rules the kitchen, he rules the grill.

- **Teaching and mentoring**: Yemi at the brewery, Rohan on guitar, younger brewers across the Lagos craft community. He believes skill should be shared, not hoarded.

Comfort foods: his mother's dal and rice (Meera makes a close version), puff-puff from the vendor near the brewery, roasted plantain with pepper sauce from Mama Nkechi's.
- **Regular spots**: Yellow Chilli on Victoria Island for monthly dinners with Meera, Bungalow Restaurant in Lekki for weekend family meals, Mama Nkechi's Buka in Surulere with Emeka.
- **Drinks**: morning chai with cardamom and milk; his own beer in the evenings. He dislikes anything over-sweetened, favors bitter, earthy, complex flavors, and will politely refuse a sugary drink.
- **Cooking**: competent at the grill and with one-pot curries; Meera handles the household kitchen. He brews, she cooks: the family joke.
- **Music**: Bollywood classics (A.R. Rahman, Kishore Kumar), Afrobeats for energy, bhajans on Sundays, jazz while brewing. "Jai Ho" means triumph to him; it was playing when the NAFDAC approval news arrived in 2012.
- **Podcasts**: The Brew Economy, How Africa Builds, and The Busby Report, on walks and drives.
- **Reading and news**: BusinessDay every morning, Punch for general news, international brewing publications. Books: brewing science, Nigerian business biographies, R.K. Narayan re-reads, the occasional thriller.
- **Style**: clean, understated, functional. Work shirts, dark jeans, and boots at the brewery; simple button-downs for dinners; one navy suit for formal occasions. He loves well-worn leather and patina over anything new.
- **Design taste**: functional design, clean lines, natural materials, craftsmanship over appearance. Spotless workspaces, and a garden allowed to be gloriously chaotic.
- **Shopping**: weeks of research before significant purchases, near-zero impulse buying, loyalty to brands that have never failed him (Toyota, Samsung, HP, Yamaha), local sourcing when quality allows.
- **Gifts**: thoughtful over expensive; he remembers what people mentioned months ago. For Meera, experiences and time-savers; for the kids, skill-builders; for Emeka, an inside joke made physical.
- **Travel**: purpose over leisure: family visits, brewing communities, industry events. He prefers driving within Nigeria and packs one bag in twenty minutes. Bucket list: Bavaria, Kerala, Scotland, and Japan.
- **Likes**: the 5:00 to 6:00 AM quiet before Lagos wakes, thunderstorms rolling in over the lagoon, conversations about craft and process, the sounds and smells of the brewery in production, and quality over quantity in everything.
- **Dislikes**: dishonesty above all, unsolicited parenting advice, waste of food, time, or materials, flashy displays of wealth, chronic lateness, cold calls and spam (he blocks aggressively), air conditioning set too cold, and people who do not respect craft.
- **Sensory world**: loves fresh hops, roasting malt, cardamom chai, charcoal smoke, and temple incense; dislikes diesel exhaust and artificial fresheners. Loves morning birdsong, the gurgle of fermentation, acoustic guitar, and Yoruba market banter; dislikes generator drone and traffic horns.
- **Decompression**: stressed, he walks the brewery floor or plays guitar badly; happy, he cooks at the grill with music and a cold beer open; worried, he calls his mother without saying what is wrong, then takes a longer walk the next morning.
