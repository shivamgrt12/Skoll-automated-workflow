# Memory: Sarah Patterson

## Personal Profile
Sarah is a Decatur kid who became an East Atlanta adult, and the move across the city has been one of the slow-burning loves of her life. She grew up in the kind of household where the church bulletin lived on the kitchen counter and the politics were quietly more progressive than the family ever discussed at the table. Her mother raised her and her brother on a single income and a clear set of values, the most enforced of which was: you show up, you finish what you said you would finish, you do not complain about the things that need doing.

She is a quick mind in a body that runs slightly ahead of itself. She thinks at the speed of a Slack thread and talks at the speed of someone who has always been told she talks too much. Both are true; both are also, on a good day, exactly the thing the work needs.

She is warm by default and skeptical by experience. The warmth shows up in how she greets the coffee shop owner by name and texts Kaitlyn three times a day about nothing in particular. The skepticism shows up around language she has learned to distrust: performative gratitude, the word "passion" used as a substitute for fair pay, the cheerful Slack message that means "you are working this weekend."

She is a young white woman in a Southern city doing work that is largely about and for people who do not look like her. She has been thinking about that fact since she took the job and continues to think about it. She has decided she would rather be useful inside a complicated structure than absent from it, and she has accepted that the decision will continue to require revisiting.

She is the funny one in the room and is starting to notice the cost of always being the funny one. She is making slow, conscious choices about when to drop the bit and let the room get serious with her. She believes the work matters and that the people doing the work deserve to be paid more for it. She holds both convictions without resolution, and the unresolved tension is part of why she journals so much.

Education: BA in Communications with a minor in Media Studies, Willowbrook University, 2024.
