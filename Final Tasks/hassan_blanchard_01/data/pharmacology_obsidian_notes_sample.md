## Week 1-2 basics

- Pharmacokinetics ADME. Absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion.
- First-pass effect happens in the liver. Oral drugs lose potency before
  systemic circulation. IV avoids first-pass.
- Half-life = time for plasma concentration to drop 50 percent. Steady state
  after roughly 4 to 5 half-lives.
- Therapeutic index = LD50 / ED50. Narrow index drugs need blood monitoring.
  Warfarin, digoxin, lithium, phenytoin, theophylline.

## Autonomic nervous system

- Sympathetic = adrenergic. Alpha 1 vasoconstrict. Beta 1 heart rate up.
  Beta 2 bronchodilate.
- Parasympathetic = cholinergic. Muscarinic receptors GI motility salivation
  bradycardia miosis.
- SLUDGE mnemonic for cholinergic crisis. Salivation Lacrimation Urination
  Defecation GI upset Emesis.

## Cardiovascular class

- ACE inhibitors end in -pril. Block angiotensin II. Watch potassium and dry
  cough. Contraindicated in pregnancy.
- ARBs end in -sartan. Same idea, less cough.
- Beta blockers end in -olol. Mask hypoglycemia symptoms. Cautious in diabetics
  and asthma.
- Calcium channel blockers end in -dipine (dihydro) or verapamil/diltiazem
  (non-dihydro). Non-dihydro slows AV node.
- Statins end in -statin. Watch liver enzymes and rhabdo.
- Digoxin toxicity signs. Yellow-green halo vision, GI upset, arrhythmias.
  Antidote digoxin immune fab.

## Respiratory class

- Short acting beta 2 agonist albuterol rescue.
- Long acting beta 2 agonist salmeterol control (never alone in asthma).
- Inhaled corticosteroids fluticasone. Rinse mouth after.
- Leukotriene modifier montelukast. Watch for mood changes.

## Antimicrobials

- Penicillins end in -cillin. Cross-reactivity risk with cephalosporins.
- Vancomycin needs peak and trough. Red man syndrome not a true allergy.
- Fluoroquinolones end in -floxacin. Tendon rupture risk.
- Aminoglycosides end in -mycin (gent tob amika). Nephrotoxic and ototoxic.

## Quiz patterns I noticed

- Okafor loves to sneak in a wrong-sounding drug ending. Read carefully.
- Multi-select on adverse effects. She wants ALL correct answers.
- One question per quiz is a math dose calculation. Do those first before
  brain gets tired.
