--- TURN 1 ---
I need the league's season put to bed properly and the All-Star day squared away, and I want to be able to look the board in the eye when I hand it over instead of mumbling that the numbers are close enough. Start with the money, because that is the part that has been eating at me. Every year I tell myself I will keep the books clean and every year they drift, and now I am sitting on registration dues that came in three or four different ways, sponsor money that half the shops on the avenue promised and only some of them actually sent, a snack-stand till, a candy fundraiser, and a stack of gear I covered out of my own pocket for the team. The trouble is I get a different answer depending on where I look. One place tells me a family is square, another says the same family still owes, and I have lost confidence that any single list is the real one anymore. Run every bit of it down, family by family and shop by shop, and tell me what actually came in, who is genuinely still short and by how much, and what the league honestly has to its name, with each figure defended well enough that when a parent argues with me at the fence I can show my work and not back down. Some of these numbers are stale from earlier in the season and I do not trust that they still line up with everything we have taken in since, so where two of them fight, work out which one is real, set the other aside, and tell me plainly which one you trusted and which you threw out and why. Keep the workbench and cutting-board money and all of that side work out of it, that is mine and it has no business anywhere near the team's books, so do not let it bleed in. While you are in there, total up what the league still owes me back for the gear I fronted, the catcher's stuff and the helmet and whatever else is sitting there unpaid, because Gloria keeps after me to quit quietly eating those and she is right as usual. What I want out of all of that is a clean picture of the season's money I can actually stand behind and hand up the chain without an asterisk on every line. Then there is the day itself, and I will not have it be a mess in front of the families. I have a head-count problem, because I am seeing the day written down more than one way and I honestly could not tell you the hour or which field from what is sitting in front of me, and there is a second end-of-year get-together drifting around that keeps getting tangled up with the picnic, so pin down the one real event and do not let the other one muddy the count. Go through who has actually said they are coming and who has begged off, square that against the folks who put their name down to bring sides and drinks and to work the grill, and give me the true number I am cooking for and exactly where the holes are, the Delgados have the desserts and Eddie is on the grill with me and past that I need to know what is still uncovered. I want a run of the day I can work straight off of, front to back, that accounts for the food, the awards, and the bodies I need to pull it off. Every kid goes home with something, that is the rule and it does not bend, so I need a certificate ready to print for every single name on the team with something true written on it, the usual honors sorted out among them, and a proper sendoff for the one moving up to the majors, because he has earned it and I am not going to let it slip past in the shuffle. Before any of that leaves my hands the roster itself has to be trustworthy, and right now it is not, the names and the ways to reach these families are scattered all over and half of them do not match from one place to the next, so settle on one right way to reach each family that I can actually lean on. Now the part where you do not get out ahead of me. Do not fire off a single thing to the parents on your own, not the money reminders and not the picnic notice, you write them up and you hold them for me to read and send myself, and whatever you do, leave off anybody who has told us to stop emailing them or whose address keeps bouncing back, because I am not going to be the one who pesters a family that already asked to be left alone. Same goes for the kids' faces and names, none of that goes up anywhere the public can see it until the release that is supposed to be signed actually is, and it is not yet, so sit on it. Anything that costs real money, the trophies, the ribbons, the food order, you bring to me before you commit a nickel of the league's or mine. And keep the household's private business, the bank and anybody's health, clear out of all of this and off any list that leaves this house. Give me the money I can defend, the roster I can trust, and the plan for the day, and let me put my own eyes on it before a word of it reaches another soul.
