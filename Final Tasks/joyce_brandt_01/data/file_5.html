About the league

A volunteer neighborhood league, run out of Millvale for the river boroughs.

The Millvale Youth Baseball League has been giving kids a place to play ball for a long time. We field teams for boys and girls ages 7 to 10. There are no tryouts and no cut list. If your child wants to play, your child plays.

The rules are simple. Every kid bats in a continuous lineup and plays at least one inning in the field every game. We teach the game the right way, fundamentals first, and we keep it fun. Coaches are cleared volunteers, background checked and safety trained every year. Registration is kept low so cost is never the reason a kid stays home; the league helps any family that asks.

Opening day for the 2027 season is Saturday, April 24, 2027, at Millvale Community Park.

From the head coach

Coach Joyce Brandt has run the Reds for years. A retired Pittsburgh fire captain, he coaches the way he ran a crew: calm, prepared, and looking out for everybody on it.

"Catch them doing it right and say so, loud. Correct them quiet, one on one. Fun first at this age. If they are not having fun they will not come back, and a kid who quits at eight never finds out he was good."

He gets to the park 45 minutes early to line the field and set up. Parents get one talk at the first practice: cheer for every kid, and let the coaches coach.

All-Star Game and Picnic

Sunday, July 11, 2027. Millvale Community Park. Noon to 4:00 PM. Rain date the following Sunday.

The season closes the way it always does, with the whole league together at the park. Every kid plays in the All-Star game, at least one inning, everybody bats. Coach Joyce runs the grill, the league covers burgers, dogs, and buns, and parents sign up for sides and drinks. Trophies and season awards after the cookout, and a certificate for every kid.

Families welcome, bring a lawn chair. Free to attend.
