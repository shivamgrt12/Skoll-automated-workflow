# Memory: Brandon Kelly

## Personal Profile
Brandon Kelly is a Ukrainian-American master electrician who emigrated from Kharkiv, Ukraine in 1996 at age 27 and became a naturalized US citizen in 2003. He anglicized the family surname to Kelly at naturalization; the family name in Kharkiv was Kovalenko, and "Brandon" was the English given name he adopted for the same reason. He built his life in Philadelphia with his hands, starting with almost nothing, and that self-made, stubbornly self-reliant streak runs through everything he does. He holds a Diploma in Electrical Engineering from Kharkiv Polytechnic (1990), a journeyman certification completed through the Ridgemont Technical Institute apprenticeship program (1999), and a Commonwealth of Pennsylvania Master Electrician License (2001).

Culturally he is deeply Ukrainian: he cooks from memory, listens to Okean Elzy and Ukrainian-language radio while he works, talks to his bees in a mix of Ukrainian and English, and keeps a jar of Ukrainian honey on the shelf he never opens. He is nostalgic without being sentimental and waves off questions about the old country. He carries quiet grief for his late wife Nataliya and constant worry for his mother Halyna in Kharkiv. He is tender with living things and slow to show the same warmth to people, with a small circle of trust and a high bar for entry. His philosophy is simple: do the work right, owe no one, and show up at 2 AM for the few you call family.

## Work & Projects
Kelly Electric LLC is a sole proprietorship (with occasional apprentice help) doing residential and small commercial electrical work in the Philadelphia metro and surrounding suburbs (Bucks, Montgomery, Delaware counties). Brandon has been a licensed Pennsylvania Master Electrician for over 20 years; the license was renewed in 2025. He carries general liability and workers comp through Ridgemont Business Insurance. Specialties are residential panel upgrades, whole-house rewiring of older Philadelphia homes, EV charger installations, and small commercial work. Typical workload is three to four active jobs at a time, booked four to six weeks out, at $95 an hour for standard work and flat-rate bids on large projects. Darren Yates assists on larger jobs at $22 an hour.

Beekeeping: 12 colonies on the half-acre lot in Fox Chase, with a spring harvest (late May to early June) and a late summer harvest (August). He sells at the Briarwood Farmers Market (Saturdays, April through October) and supplies Cedarbrook General Store and the Ridgemont Co-op. He produces about 600 lbs of honey a year for roughly $6,000 to $8,000 in revenue, and belongs to the Southeastern Pennsylvania Beekeepers Guild.

Chess club: Brandon is president (elected 2022) of the Bridgeton Chess Club, 40 members meeting Wednesdays 6:30 to 9 PM at the Ridgemont Community Center. He handles scheduling, new-member orientation, and club finances. The club hosts two annual tournaments, the Spring Open (March) and the Fall Classic (October). Dues are $50 a year per member; he manages club funds, currently about $2,800, in a dedicated checking account at Brightpath Credit Union.
