# Memory: Mark Campbell

## Personal Profile

Mark Edward Campbell is a Philadelphia-based wedding and event planner who built Campbell Events from scratch in 2012 and has run it for 14 years. He grew up Episcopalian in a family where faith structured the calendar even when belief was uncertain; he maintains the traditions (Sunday dinners when the kids are home, Christmas, Easter) as a rhythm rather than a practice, and carries a low-grade guilt about the distance from it without enough discomfort to change. His cultural identity is deeply Philadelphian: the city's mixture of blue-collar pragmatism and old-money pretension mirrors his own experience of straddling worlds, serving wealthy clients while running a middle-class household, doing creative work inside the economics of a small company. He holds a B.A. in Communications from Ridgemont College (completed circa 1995) and a Certificate in Event Management from the Philadelphia Institute of Hospitality (completed circa 1998). He speaks basic French (menus and pleasantries, not fluent). He has been married 24 years to Julia Campbell.

His personality is organized around a central tension: he is extraordinarily competent professionally and privately depleted by it. He gives his best energy to strangers' most important days and returns home with what is left. He knows this; it troubles him more than he admits. He is not good at letting himself be helped, and not good at choosing his own enjoyment over the next client. The question of what "enough" looks like in his business and his life is one he returns to without resolution. He believes that the details matter as a governing principle extending from wedding timelines to how he loads the dishwasher. He believes care shows in small things, that most problems are solvable if broken into components, and that reliability is the most underrated virtue. These beliefs have made him excellent at his job and occasionally difficult to live with.

## Work & Projects

Mark is sole owner and lead planner of Campbell Events, founded 2012 (14 years). Pre-2012 career: event coordinator at Whitman and Reed Hospitality (Philadelphia) 1995 to 2005 (junior 1995 to 1998, senior 1998 to 2005); director of weddings at Old City Events Group (Philadelphia) 2005 to 2011; brief solo consultancy late 2011 transitioning into Campbell Events in 2012. Operates as a one-person shop with Becca Hartwell as a part-time contractor. Annual gross revenue approximately $210,000 (2025 was $198,000). Business is seasonal: April through October is peak wedding season; January through early February is near-dormant. He takes PTO in January; January 2026 he and Julia went to Cancun for a week. He works Monday through Friday, typically 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM ET, from a home office plus occasional co-working space on Germantown Ave. Saturdays are wedding days during season: on-site 8:00 AM through cleanup. Manages 3 to 4 weddings per month during peak season. Currently booked wedding clients (signed contracts, deposits cleared, dated late 2026 through fall 2027): Chen-Morrison, DiStefano-Grant, Holloway-Park, Kaplan-Rivera. ## Interests & Hobbies

Sunday morning biscuit-making is a near-spiritual ritual: buttermilk biscuits from his mother's recipe, made with attention to the physical process (cutting butter into flour by hand, the specific fold-and-turn motion, the oven heat). He takes this seriously and does not rush it. Morning walks with Benny are the only unscheduled, agenda-free part of his day; the route's familiarity is the point. He was a serious amateur photographer in his twenties and still has the compositional eye; the Canon DSLR lives in a closet he rarely opens. Event planning killed photography as pleasure and the not-returning to it has become a small grief. He reads history and biography in bed for about 20 minutes before sleeping, looking for patterns in how other people managed complexity under pressure. Cooking shows have his genuine attention (Chopped, Great British Bake Off): the combination of skill, time pressure, and presentation speaks to the event planner in him. He loves the moment at a wedding when the couple first sees each other and everything he planned becomes irrelevant because what's happening is real.
