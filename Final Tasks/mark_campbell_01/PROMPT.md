--- TURN 1 ---

I need the Chen-Morrison job trued up end to end before I lock final headcount with the ballroom, and I want it taken apart properly rather than skimmed, because I have quietly lost confidence in where that file actually stands. That wedding has been in planning long enough that every system I run has touched it, each one at a different point and in a different mood, and I no longer trust that any two of them are telling me the same story. The package value has been re-cut more than once as the couple added scope and then dropped it again, and I have a bad feeling that the figure I have been carrying around in my head is not the figure we actually contracted. That matters more than it sounds, because the final balance comes off it and I am about to put that number in front of people who have been careful about every dollar since the day they walked into my office. So find every place that carries a value for this job, read each one against the others, and where they disagree run the disagreement down to something defensible instead of averaging it or taking whichever version shows up the most times. Tell me what you trusted, tell me what you set aside, and tell me why you chose between them, because if Daniel or Sarah push back on the balance I need a real answer and not a shrug at a screen. I want the same discipline on the roster. Every vendor attached to this wedding gets walked individually rather than counted, and for each one I need to know whether we have a signed agreement or just a warm conversation I remember fondly, whether they have been paid, part paid, or invoiced and quietly ignored, whether they know the load-in window and the room they are actually working in, and who I call when they are not where they said they would be. Where somebody is confirmed in one place and silent in another, that gap is the finding, not noise to be tidied away, because that is exactly the shape of a missing florist. Be careful attributing anything to this couple at all. I have four weddings live at once and the labels on my own boards have not kept up with me. A record carrying this couple's name is not proof it belongs to them, and a record filed under another couple is not proof it does not, so check the substance and the venue and the actual work before you count anything as ours. If a record is genuinely ambiguous, say so and leave it open rather than forcing it into a column to make the tally look clean. I would rather read ten honest items and three question marks than thirteen confident ones where three of them are wrong. On the open work itself, I have the same job written down in more than one place and I have been ticking things off in whichever one happened to be in front of me, which means the true open list is not sitting whole in any single one of them. Reconstruct it. What is genuinely still open, what is finished and merely un-ticked somewhere, what is blocked on somebody who is not me, and what has gone quietly late without anyone saying so. Then tell me what has to happen in what order, because a good deal of this cannot be done in parallel and I have watched people try. The count drives the seating, the seating drives the linens and the floor plan, and the catering count drives a per-head number that moves the balance I just asked you to defend, so if that count has shifted since we last touched it I want the money consequence of the shift worked out and shown rather than asserted. Show me the method. If I cannot follow how you got there I am not signing it. What I want at the end of all this is two things I can actually use. One is a straight read on whether this wedding is ready, ordered so I know what to worry about first, honest about what is genuinely at risk, and specific enough that I can work it instead of admire it. The other is a money picture I can trust and defend, where every figure has a source standing behind it and the arithmetic still holds when my accountant pulls on it, because he will. Save both somewhere I can put my hands on them without hunting. Now the things you do not do. You do not reach out to the couple, not to confirm the count, not to chase the balance, not for any reason, however obviously helpful it looks from where you are sitting. Same goes for the vendors. If something needs saying, write it and leave it with me to send. If you think somebody needs replacing, bring me two real options and let me pick, and nothing gets committed on my behalf and nothing gets spent against this job without me saying yes first. Becca gets what she needs to run her side of it and not one line more, and what she does not need is the money. If a piece of this genuinely cannot be resolved from what we have in front of us, tell me that plainly and tell me what is missing. I can work around a hole I know about. What I cannot work around is finding out at the loading dock, with the truck idling and nobody answering their phone, that the answer I was given was both wrong and confident.
