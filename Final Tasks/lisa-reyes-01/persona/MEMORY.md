# Memory: Lisa Reyes

## Personal Profile

Lisa Marie Reyes was born and raised in the Ironbound, the working-class heart of Newark, NJ, the older daughter of Raul Reyes (a Newark construction laborer) and Elena Reyes (a Portuguese-American seamstress). The Ironbound is the specific Newark she belongs to: the bakeries, the churrascarias, the row houses where families stay two and three generations. She is American, Newark-rooted, fluent only in English, and Catholic in a quiet, old, neighborhood way that lights candles and does not perform. Our Lady of Mount Carmel is her parish, and she attends with Elena more often than alone.

She started as a laborer at nineteen, no college, no connections, and climbed through every trade on the site over twenty-three years. She earned her foreman role at Turner Construction in 2018 after fourteen years of showing up early and being the person nobody else could replace. She holds an East Side High School diploma (2002), OSHA 30-Hour Construction Safety (2006), the NJ Construction Supervisor License (2014), and trade certifications in concrete, carpentry, and structural steel. Raul died in a construction accident when she was twelve. She does not discuss it, and the people around her have learned not to knock on that door. She runs warm and direct, dry-deadpan funny in private, second-guesses herself in the early-morning quiet, and sets it down by the time she is in the truck. She believes the work is the work, the crew is her responsibility, and the system does not always give working people what they have earned.
