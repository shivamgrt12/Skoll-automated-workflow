Our Work

Why Community Outreach Matters

Research that stays in journals doesn't protect reefs. Our program connects Phase III monitoring data to the people who live, surf, swim, and snorkel alongside Oahu's coastal ecosystems every day.

🔬

Science Communication

We translate Phase III monitoring data — coral cover percentages, bleaching alerts, temperature trends — into language and visuals that any community member can understand and act on.

🏫

Educator Programs

We bring reef curriculum directly to K–12 teachers, complete with hands-on activities, laminated reef ID guides, and Google Classroom materials they can use in any subject area.

🌊

Public Events

From the Waikiki Aquarium to Hanauma Bay, our public events give community members the chance to see real reef footage, ask a marine biologist, and learn what they can do to help.

📡

Reef Health Alerts

When our monitoring data triggers a bleaching alert — as happened at Waikiki Reef Station C in November 2025 — we communicate that information to the public, not just to funders.

Reef Science

What Our Data Shows

Phase III monitoring data, collected by Dr. Peterson and the PRRI team at three Oahu sites since 2023. Numbers reflect the latest available readings from 2025–2026 surveys.

44%
Peak Live Coral Cover
Hanauma Bay Station C — the healthiest of our three monitoring sites, with excellent water clarity and low human disturbance.
7.2%
Waikiki Reef C Bleaching
As of February 2026. Our 5% alert threshold was first crossed in November 2025. Monitoring frequency doubled. Community informed via October 2025 aquarium event.
37.5%
Lanai Lookout Baseline
New monitoring site established December 2025. Baseline live coral cover indicates a relatively undisturbed reef — excellent visibility (24 m) and low bleaching (2.3%).
27.5°C
Peak Ocean Temp (2025)
Recorded September–October 2025 at Waikiki Reef. Elevated temperatures correlate with increased bleaching at urban sites. Hanauma Bay recovered as temps cooled; Waikiki Station C did not.
1,284
Community Members (2025)
Reached through 6 outreach events across Oahu — from 14-person educator workshops to a 752-person coastal walk. We exceeded our 2025 community contact target by 28%.
85%
Endemic Marine Life
Approximately 85% of Hawaii's nearshore marine species are found nowhere else on Earth. What happens to our reefs matters globally, not just locally.
