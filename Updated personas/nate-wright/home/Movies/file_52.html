Chest-Pain Triage Protocol

A single, repeatable front-door pathway for adult chest pain, so a 12-lead EKG and a risk score happen inside a defined window on every shift. Owner Nate Wright, PA-C. Sponsor Dr. Karen Ashworth. Clinical input Dr. Phil Engstrom.

Target go-live Q1 2027

The aim

Shorten door-to-decision time and cut the variation between the day and night teams at a 320-bed Level II trauma centre.

The scaffold

The protocol doubles as the EKG-reading teaching frame for the two visiting PA students on the autumn rotation.

The discipline

Clinical numbers carry real weight. No threshold ships from an older draft, and the tracker stays in step with the working file.

The decision pathway

StepActionOwner
1Identify chest pain or anginal equivalent at arrival; immediate vitals.Triage nurse
2Order a 12-lead EKG to the agreed time-to-EKG target.Triage nurse
3Read the EKG; a STEMI activates the cath-lab line directly.Provider
4Non-STEMI: structured risk score, troponin draw, disposition.Provider
5Low-risk discharge with a documented safety-net and follow-up note.Provider

The exact time-to-EKG threshold is being finalised with Dr. Engstrom and is confirmed in writing before any deck is printed.

Stage-gate schedule

Five gates from final draft to full rollout. Each gate closes before the next opens.
