# Memory: Ryan Torres

## Personal Profile
Ryan grew up in Dearborn in an Italian-American household where his parents Mike and Linda made a life that kept the language at the table and the food on the stove. Naples to Dearborn is not a long arc by miles, but it shaped everything about how Ryan moves through a room. He speaks Italian with his parents and his grandmother and English with everyone else, switching without thinking about it.

He carries the weight of being the eldest son in a way he would not name as weight. He checks on Grandma Rose, mediates Jake and Tyler, drives Linda to appointments she could drive herself to, and would not consider not doing any of it. He is not a martyr about it. He is built for it.

He runs warm and effervescent in the room and slightly anxious in the part of his brain that does not show. The wedding stress wakes him at 4:15 AM sometimes, mind already running spreadsheets it has no business running. He has gotten better at putting his phone face-down at 10:30 PM and worse at not picking it up at 4:15 AM. He is working on a quiet personal project of being kinder to himself about the things he does not get perfect. He is not very far along. He notices, which is part of the work.

He believes that taking care of people is the main thing, and that the small daily versions of taking care of people add up to more than grand gestures ever do. He believes in showing up on time, finishing what he starts, and treating competence as a kind of respect. He applies this to patient cleanings and wedding spreadsheets and Sunday cooking equally.

His Italian-American identity is not a costume. It is the assumption underneath how he greets a guest at the door, how he insists on feeding people who stop by, how he treats Grandma Rose's word as nearly final on matters that grandmothers have authority over. The wedding is going to be unmistakably both: Italian wine toast, antipasto course before dinner, tarantella set in the reception that will scandalize half the guests and delight the rest. He is not interested in apologizing for any of it.

## Work & Projects
Ryan is a Registered Dental Hygienist at Lakewood Family Dentistry, 3150 Michigan Avenue, Dearborn, MI 48124. He started in August 2022 and is approaching four years in the role. Lakewood is a general practice with three dentists, four hygienists, and front desk and admin staff. Dr. Sarah Kim is the owner and Ryan's direct supervisor. His responsibilities are patient cleanings, X-rays, periodontal assessments, patient education, and charting. He sees 8 to 10 patients per day on a four-day workweek: Monday through Thursday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM, with Fridays off. Salary is $62,000 per year.

He has an Associate of Applied Science in Dental Hygiene from Clearwater Community College (Class of 2021) and is a licensed Registered Dental Hygienist in Michigan (License No. 6701-024758). Continuing education credits are tracked annually through the Michigan continuing-education vendor with renewal due each April.

The slow simmer in his head is dental therapy. He has been thinking about going back to school long enough that the thought is starting to feel like a plan rather than a daydream. He has compiled three accredited Michigan programs with tuition, timeline, admissions requirements, and overlap with the post-wedding year. He has not told Linda yet. He has told Emma, who told him to apply. The Notion workspace holds the comparison grid; the Webflow page is a personal landing he keeps minimally updated; the Cloudflare DNS hold runs against a future practice domain.

He volunteers at Heartland Free Dental Clinic one Saturday per month, providing cleanings to uninsured patients. He coordinates volunteer shifts and supply tracking and helps maintain the Heartland WordPress page.
