# Memory: Henry Harmon

## Personal Profile

Henry grew up in west Columbus, the youngest of three sons of Henry Harmon Sr. and Dorothy. His father ran Harmon's Barbershop on High Street for 40 years until retirement in 2017. His mother managed a school cafeteria. Henry took over the shop in 2017, renovated it, and renamed it Harmon's Heritage Cuts while preserving the spirit of the original. He carries four generations of Franklin County roots and a preacher's cadence in his everyday speech that has nothing to do with theology and everything to do with how he was raised. He is White and Anglo-American, native English, and identifies Methodist by both upbringing and active practice. Education: B.S. in Physical Education from Muskingum Valley University (2000), where he played safety on the football team for four years and was team captain his senior year, plus an OHSAA coaching certification. He grew up being told men do not cry and has spent his adult life trying to unlearn that while coaching boys who are being told the same line. He believes conditioning is character, that the chair is where his best thinking happens, and that the young men he coaches need someone who chose them back.

The organized one, manages household finances, keeps Henry on schedule, emotional anchor of the family. They bicker about his spending on players' equipment, never about anything that matters. Designated ICE contact, medical proxy, and emergency decision-maker for Henry.

Wide receiver, talented and fast; Henry is harder on him than any other player because "the coach's son doesn't get favorites." 3.4 GPA. Recruiting interest building, Division II film already circulating, Division I attention possible if his senior tape stays strong.

## Work & Projects

- **Coaching path**: Assistant defensive backs coach at Eastland High School in Whitehall (2000-2008) starting straight out of Muskingum, promoted to defensive coordinator at Eastland (2008-2014). Joined Ridgemont as assistant coach in 2014 and was promoted to head coach in 2017 when the previous head coach retired.
- **Ridgemont High School, Head Football Coach**: 12 years at Ridgemont, the last 9 as head coach. OHSAA Division III. 2 state championships (2018, 2022), playoffs 7 of 9 years. Staff: defensive coordinator Ryan Teller, offensive coordinator Brett Calloway, and 4 assistant coaches. About 60 players across varsity and JV. Henry also teaches 2 sections of P.E. at the school.
- **Harmon's Heritage Cuts**: 4-chair barbershop on High Street, Columbus's historic business district. 3 full-time barbers (including Pop Brennan), 1 part-time, plus Becky on reception and sweeping. Tuesday to Saturday, 9 AM to 6 PM, 8 PM on Fridays. Henry cuts on Tuesdays, Thursdays, and Saturdays when he can. Specialty: classic fades and hot towel shaves.
- **Grace Crossroads Methodist Church**: Elder board member. Volunteers at the church's summer youth basketball camp. Informal mentor to dozens of young men in the community.

## Health & Wellness

- Carrying about 30 extra pounds. Dr. Nathan Caldwell has been on him about weight and cholesterol for years.
- Elevated LDL cholesterol. On atorvastatin 20mg daily.
- On lisinopril 10mg daily for blood pressure.
- Left knee trouble since playing days. Torn meniscus repaired 2005. Occasional flare-ups. Knee brace during practice.
- Lower back stiffness from standing all day between the shop and the field. Karen bought him a foam roller he uses "sometimes."
- Sleeps 6 to 7 hours most nights, better in off-season, worse during game weeks.
- Annual physical with Dr. Caldwell in late January, scheduled right after football season closes so nothing gets pushed. Flu shot rolled into the same visit because Karen makes him.
- Mental health: no therapist. Talks to Pastor David Callahan when things weigh on him. The pressure of coaching, running a business, and watching his father decline takes a toll he does not always show.
- Dietary notes tied to cholesterol: Karen pushing more vegetables and less red meat. Complies at home, cheats at the shop. Trying to cut back on iced tea, working on it.

## Interests & Hobbies

- **Football, coaching and watching**: A vocation, not a hobby. Watches NFL and college on Saturdays and Sundays in season. Muskingum Valley Eagles loyalty and Big Ten culture.
- **Barbering**: Cuts hair 2 to 3 days a week even as owner. Classic fades and hot towel shaves. The chair is where he thinks.
- **Grilling and smoking**: Big Green Egg smoker in the backyard. 14-hour brisket with a dry rub he calls Harmon's Blend, recipe from his father. Hosts cookouts for the coaching staff after big wins.
- **Bass fishing**: Hoover Reservoir with Keith on off-season Saturday mornings. Cooler of iced tea, a lot of trash talk.
- **Mentoring**: Coaches at the summer basketball camp at Grace Crossroads for at-risk youth.
- **Dominoes**: Plays at the shop on slow afternoons. Considers himself unbeatable. He is not.
- **Reading**: Ohio local history and small business heritage. Proud the shop sits in High Street's historic district.
