# Memory: Jason Kim

## Personal Profile
Jason was born and raised in Columbus, Ohio, in a working-class neighborhood near the warehouse district where his father worked as a freight dispatcher. The trade industry was the family business in the lower-case sense long before it became one in the upper-case sense. American is the cultural identity, but the specific flavor of it is Columbus, Ohio, and Midwest, and that flavor shows up in the directness, the work ethic, and the assumption that you finish what you start.

English is his only language. He attended Ohio State University, finished with a bachelor's in international business, and spent seven years at National Trade Associates before opening Kim Trade Services in 2008. The decision to go on his own was less ambition than discomfort with being managed by people who had not done the work.

The values that show up everywhere in his life are reliability, courtesy, and the long view. He does what he said he would do, he treats service workers the way he would want Brandon treated, and he measures relationships in decades. The faith is private and steady: Grace Community Church on Sunday mornings before the Akron drive, more about consistency than conversion. The pride in the business is quiet. He built it from a phone and a license, and he flinches when anyone tries to make him say so out loud.

## Work & Projects
- **Firm**: Kim Trade Services, a trade consulting and compliance firm based in Columbus, Ohio.
- **Founded**: 2008, after Jason spent seven years at National Trade Associates.
- **Credentials**: US Customs Broker License #28471. Certified Trade Specialist (CTS) through the Trade Certification Academy.
- **Team of 6**: Jason as principal; Pat Delgado as office manager; Brandon Kim as junior associate in his first year; two licensed trade compliance specialists; one bookkeeper; one administrative assistant.
- **Clients**: 30 to 40 active clients, primarily mid-size manufacturers and agricultural exporters. Verticals concentrated in automotive parts, agricultural products, and textiles.
- **Services**: Trade compliance filing, tariff classification, USMCA compliance consulting, trade facilitation, duty drawback claims, and importer security filing.
- **Revenue**: Firm grosses approximately $650,000 to $750,000 per year. Jason draws a salary plus quarterly owner distributions.
- **Industry involvement**: Board member of the Columbus Trade Association. Co-founder of the Columbus Trade Mentorship Program with Ryan Montoya. Occasional speaker at trade commerce conferences.
- **Current projects**: Brandon's structured onboarding into the firm; a USMCA rule-of-origin question with an automotive-sensor client; evaluating a switch from TradeFlow Pro to BridgePort Compliance Suite for better USMCA integration.
- **Seasonality**: Q4 is the heaviest quarter as clients close out import schedules. Tariff announcements drive irregular surges any time of year.

## Preferences
- **Entertainment**: Football is the seasonal default. Buckeyes and Bengals on the big screen. Classic rock and country on Spotify during the truck commute.
- **Food and drink**: Classic American at the core. Grilled steaks, burgers, ribs, pulled pork, wings, mac and cheese. Sarah cooks home meals during the week. Helen's Sunday pot roast is the weekly culinary peak. Black coffee in the morning. Sweet iced tea in the afternoon. Bud Light or a local craft beer from Endeavor Brewing with weekend meals. Maker's Mark bourbon at celebrations and dominos nights. Moderate drinker.
- **Shopping**: Practical and local. Loyal to Columbus merchants. Invests in tools and grill equipment because they earn the weekly use. Minimum-necessary attention to everything else. Does not enjoy shopping and does not pretend to.
- **Travel**: Drives within Ohio without complaint. The road time is thinking time. Flies for two or three trade conferences a year with efficiency: early flight, hotel near the venue, back home as soon as possible. A non-fishing, non-family vacation has not happened in years.
- **Clothing**: Business casual at the office (slacks, polo, button-down). Jeans and boots on weekends. One good suit for formal trade events, worn maybe twice a year.
- **Gifts**: Food and drink. Bourbon for friends, something thoughtful for Helen, what Sarah tells him the kids want.
- **Aesthetics**: Comfortable, not curated. The house looks like a family lives there. The Silverado is dusty and dented and paid off, and he does not see the point of trading it.
- **Sensory world**: Black coffee at dawn in a quiet house. Charcoal smoke and the sizzle of fat on the grate. The specific warm-furniture scent of Helen's Akron house. Cold morning air off Lake Erie before sunrise. The I-71 stretches he could find blindfolded.
- **Comfort and decompression**: Saturday grilling is the primary reset. Sunday evening after the Akron drive is the second. Dominos nights serve as low-stakes competition decompression.
- **Pet peeves**: Unnecessary jargon, especially from consultants who use complexity to disguise shallow thinking. Tariff changes announced without adequate transition periods. Clients who ignore compliance recommendations and then call in a panic. Meetings that run long because someone did not prepare. Being late to anything. The phrase "thinking outside the box."
