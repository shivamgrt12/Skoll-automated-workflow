# Memory: Paul Rivera

## Personal Profile

Paul Raymond Rivera grew up modestly and earned his way from regional turboprops to wide-body international routes at SkyBridge Airlines over a 35-year career. The cockpit taught him everything he carries now: checklist discipline, situational awareness, and the understanding that calm under pressure is a trained response, not a personality trait. He retired in March 2023 on his own terms, healthy and sharp, and still catches himself scanning for exits when he walks into a restaurant.

Retirement has become a second career, treated with intention and a healthy respect for weather delays. He golfs four mornings a week, volunteers at the Aviation Heritage Museum, and is working through a national park list with Judith. He gives quietly to the museum, the church, and his grandchildren's college funds because he remembers what it felt like to need.

He attended Clearfield Aeronautical University (B.S. Aviation Science, 1980), holds an ATP certificate, and was type-rated on the Boeing 737, 757, 767, and 777. English is his native language; he picked up basic Spanish from international layovers. He and Judith attend Desert Springs Community Church on Sundays, more for the routine and the community than for theological conviction.

His temperament is calm, confident, and deeply present. His humor includes rather than targets. He tells stories paced well and never too long, listens better than he lets on, and is the most comfortable person in the room without needing to be the loudest. Underneath the ease there is an ache: some mornings the desert light reminds him of the cockpit view at altitude, and he feels the absence like a phantom limb. He would make the same choice again, and the ache is still there.

## Work & Projects

Retired since March 2023. 35-year career at SkyBridge Airlines: started as first officer on regional turboprops, ended as captain on international wide-body routes (777). Approximately 22,000 total flight hours. Left cleanly with no public-facing career identity in retirement; declined post-retirement endorsement requests.

Aviation Heritage Museum, Scottsdale. Volunteer docent on Tuesdays and Thursdays, 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM (when not traveling). Gives tours, talks to school groups, helps maintain the flight simulator exhibit, contributes to the docent handbook. Paul reports to the museum coordinator; the executive director knows him by name and respects the line he draws about speeches.

Travel project with Judith: national parks bucket list, 2 to 3 trips per year, planned with flight-plan thoroughness. Sedona and Flagstaff are the recurring close-to-home favorites; Red Rock Lodge is the preferred Sedona base.
