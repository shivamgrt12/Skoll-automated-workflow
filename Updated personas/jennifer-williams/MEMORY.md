# Memory: Jennifer Williams

## Personal Profile
Jennifer Avery Williams is a working poet and full-time barista who grew up in Warwick, Rhode Island, and now lives in Providence. She holds a BA in English with a creative writing concentration from Narragansett Bay College, where she graduated with honors in 2024. English is her native language and she reads Spanish at the level of four college semesters. She treats language as material with weight and texture, values community over audience, and prefers the zine culture, open mics, and small presses to any pursuit of fame. She is curious with focus, drilling slowly into language, systems of power, and how people make meaning from ordinary days. She is private about vulnerability: she will write about loneliness in a poem and read it to strangers, but rarely discusses it over coffee. She is single, not actively looking, focused on writing and financial stability, and carries a quiet pride in functioning steadily on a tight income.

## Work & Projects
Full-time barista at Alchemy Coffee Collective, an independent shop in Fox Point, Providence, where she has worked 1.5 years, going full-time after graduation. Shifts are variable, typically 6:00 AM to 2:00 PM or 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM, five days a week, posted weekly via HotSchedules, usually off Mondays and one other weekday. She reports to owner-manager Fran Delgado. As a writer she publishes poetry in small literary magazines, with four poems published in 2025 and two so far in 2026. She runs the "Marginal Notes" Substack newsletter, around 340 subscribers, and makes the quarterly zine "Thread & Bone," sold through local bookshops and Etsy. Writing income averages about $200 a month, mostly Etsy zine sales and occasional reading honorariums. She keeps an active submission database and saved a researched list of poetry magazines and deadlines to Google Drive.

## Finance
Barista wages are about $28,000 a year from hourly pay plus tips, with roughly $2,400 a year in sporadic writing income, for a monthly take-home near $2,100 after Rhode Island state tax. Monthly expenses total about $1,592: rent $725, utilities share $95, groceries $230, RIPTA bus pass $55, Visible phone $25, subsidized ACA health insurance $120, federal student loan $185, subscriptions $22, dining and coffee out $50, personal and misc $55, and a $30 zine printing fund. Student loans are $21,000 federal on an IBR plan at $185 a month, no private loans because her parents helped with tuition. She banks at Navigant Credit Union (checking and savings), keeps a Discover It Student card paid in full when possible with a credit score around 690, and carries renters insurance through Lemonade. The approval threshold for new commitments is $50.

## Health & Wellness
Jennifer is generally healthy with no chronic conditions and no allergies. She takes no prescriptions, only a daily B12 supplement, taken because she is vegetarian, and 1000 IU of Vitamin D3 daily. She is vegetarian since age 19 for ethical reasons, but not vegan, and eats eggs, dairy, and honey. Primary care is Dr. Marie Fournier at Fox Point Community Health Center, last annual visit October 2025. Her dentist is Dr. Grace Yamada at College Hill Dental, seen once a year for a cleaning, last visit January 2026. Her vision is 20/20 with no correction. She walks everywhere, bikes as primary transport besides the bus, and takes an occasional $5 drop-in yoga class at the community center; she is not a gym person. No formal mental health diagnosis; she used college counseling for a semester during a rough sophomore-year patch and is generally stable, with writing as her primary processing outlet.

## Home & Living
Jennifer rents an approximately 120 square foot room in a 3-bedroom, 1-bathroom shared house in Fox Point, Providence, RI 02906, fitting a twin bed, a desk, and a bookshelf. The kitchen and living room are shared, and there is a small backyard with a fire pit used for summer gatherings. Her desk is the command center: laptop, notebooks, a jar of pens, and a stack of zines. Her bookshelf is organized by genre, poetry on top, fiction below, nonfiction on the bottom. She keeps one pothos plant named Gertrude, fairy lights, and thrift-store art on the walls. She gets around by RIPTA bus daily and bicycle when weather permits, and owns no car.
