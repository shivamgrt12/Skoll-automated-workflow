Selected work

Poems from the counter

Short pieces on rent, shifts, and the running tallies we keep without meaning to. A few live here, most live in stapled zines and out loud at the open mic.

Lead poem, Issue 7

Fox Point Inventory

An accounting of one rented room, counted the way some people pray, badly and on time.

This is the inventory: one bed, one desk, one jar of pens, a sister an hour north who picks up on the second ring.
Read at the open mic

Closing Shift

The last hour at the shop, mop water and the register light, the small comedy of counting a drawer at midnight.

Rent is a kind of weather. It comes. You dress for it.
Short, ends quiet

What the Tip Jar Knows

A poem that keeps its own counsel. It lands soft and refuses to explain itself, which is the whole point.

Some things need their full shape. This one holds it in a handful of coins.

Thread & Bone
THREAD
& BONE
no. seven, Inventory

A quarterly poetry zine, made by hand.

Laid out on a kitchen table, printed sixty copies at a time, and priced to be affordable at the point of contact. Five dollars from the tote at a reading, eight dollars in the shops and online.

Issue Seven gathers poems and short prose on inventory, what we count and what counts us. Guest pages from friends, a found poem from a receipt, and a colophon that thanks the printer down the street.

About

Language as material, with weight and texture.

I hold a BA in English with a creative writing concentration and I have worked the bar at an independent coffee shop in Fox Point for a year and a half. The day job pays the rent, the writing pays attention.

I care about community over audience. I would rather be read by people who read than be famous, so I keep the work close to the open mics, the small presses, and the neighborhood that shows up. If something does not add up, I will say so, and I try to do the same on the page.
