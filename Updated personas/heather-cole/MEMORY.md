# Memory: Heather Cole

## Personal Profile

Heather is a second-generation Polish-American who grew up in Green Bay and never really left. Her grandparents emigrated from Krakow, her parents stayed in the paper-mill economy that defined the city for decades, and the cultural identity that surrounds her, the church on Sundays when she still attended, the pierogi at Christmas, the Packers in the living room every fall, is comfortable ground rather than performance.

She moved through nursing school at UW Green Bay (BSN, 2018) and then earned her MSN and Nurse Practitioner credential at Marquette in 2023 through a hybrid program with clinical rotations. Her professional identity is built around women's health and reproductive medicine, partly because she is good at the science and partly because she knows what it is to be dismissed inside that exact corner of the healthcare system. She is a high achiever and she is tired. Rest, not productivity, is her current growth edge.

English is her native language. She understands more Polish than she speaks, mostly food, prayers, and the things her grandparents said when she was small. She is Catholic by upbringing, lapsed in practice, still culturally rooted in it.
