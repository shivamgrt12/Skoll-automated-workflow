# Memory: Min Miles Park

## Personal Profile

Min Miles Park is an olive farmer who moves through a day with the deliberate calm of someone whose work runs on seasonal arcs rather than weekly sprints. He listens with his hands still, which is rare for a man whose hands have something to do every minute of the day. He lets sentences finish before he begins forming a response, and Soo-Jin teases him for the half-second pause it produces. People who meet him briefly mistake the quietness for shyness or arrogance. Neither is right. He simply does not see the need to fill space.

He came to Baena at six, born in Seoul and brought across by his parents when his father took on the family olive land. The first three years he refused to speak Spanish at school. The transition was complete by ten and incomplete forever. Korean is the language of his tiredness, his pain, and his mother. Spanish is the language he plans the day in. He has stopped trying to map the geography between them.

Education: Agricultural Engineering diploma from Escuela Superior Agraria de Cordoba (2003); Olive Oil Sommelier certification from the Mediterranean Olive Institute (2015); advanced sensory analysis course in Jaen (2025). Spanish citizen by naturalisation. Has lived on Andalusian soil since 1987 and considers Baena home, with no plan to leave it.

Languages: Spanish (fluent, daily), Korean (conversational, with mother and extended family), English (functional, for international buyers), basic French (export contacts).

His philosophy holds that work that does not tire you is not real work, that quality outranks volume, that stewardship outranks profit, and that a handshake means more than a contract even though he signs the contract too. He has turned down two acquisition offers from larger estates that would have made the family comfortable for a decade, and he cannot explain this in a way that satisfies a business school graduate.

## Work & Projects

- **Estate**: Finca Miles, 35 hectares in the Subbetica south of Baena. Primarily Picual variety with some Hojiblanca. Produces ~80,000 kg of olives annually, yielding ~12,000 litres of extra-virgin olive oil.
- **Business model**: 60% sold through cooperative pooling, 40% as single-estate premium oil under the "Miles" label. Soo-Jin manages direct sales, agritourism tastings, and local market presence.
- **Organic certification transition**: Converting the east grove (12 hectares) to certified organic. Year 2 of a 3-year transition. Requires detailed record-keeping and EU organic audit compliance. Target certification: spring 2027.
- **Irrigation modernisation**: Upgrading from flood irrigation to precision drip on the north grove. Phase 1 (10 hectares) installed November 2025 with the HydroSense controller. Phase 2 scheduled October 2026. EU rural development grant partially funding it. Contractor: Riegos del Sur.
- **Export expansion**: Building direct relationships with specialty food importers in Germany and the UK. Currently shipping to two international buyers. Goal: 3 more by end of 2027.
- **Mill room project**: Converting an old corner of the family mill into a small estate museum with tools, photographs, and ledgers. Sunday-hour pace, three years in.
- **Schedule**: Mornings in the grove with Pedro and seasonal crew. Afternoons for administrative work, sales, and cooperative business. Harvest season (November to January) is all-consuming.
- **Professional development**: Active in Cooperativa Oleicola Sierra Morena. Attends Expoliva (biennial) and regional oliviculture conferences.

## Interests & Hobbies

## Preferences

- **Style**: Cotton shirts (navy, white, faded ochre), dark work trousers. Work boots for the grove, leather loafers for town, one pair of formal shoes polished himself. Navy linen jacket from Sevilla over a decade old for business meetings.
- **Aesthetic**: Whitewashed walls, terracotta floors, dark wood beams. Korean ceramics from his grandmother on a kitchen shelf, not for everyday use.
- **Food**: Cooks Korean BBQ on Sundays. Kimchi is his mother's, jarred in autumn, never store-bought. Aceitunas and banchan on the same table. Comfort foods: mother's miyeok-guk on his birthday, salmorejo in summer, dark chocolate with sea salt, warm bread torn (not cut).
- **Music**: K-pop and Korean traditional, acoustic guitar in the evenings, Radio Baena during grove work.
- **Reading**: Agricultural journals, olive oil industry publications, Korean-Spanish literature, farming memoirs. Reads in Spanish and English.
- **News**: Correo de Cordoba with morning coffee. EU agricultural policy closely. Korean news online (The Korea Herald) weekly.
- **Travel**: Reluctant traveler. Small bags, single suitcase, no checked luggage. International trips for business only (Germany, UK, Switzerland). Korea every 4 to 5 years with the family. Prefers Sierra Nevada and the Subbetica natural park for domestic breaks.
- **Shopping**: Researches purchases for months. Spreadsheets for equipment over EUR 1,000. Buys tools once and expects them to outlast him. Still uses his father's Felco shears. Cash for village vendors, card for everything else.
- **Gifts**: Practical with one sentimental exception per recipient. For Soo-Jin, Korean artisan jewelry or a trip to Seoul. For David, tools plus a single bottle of good wine. For Jenny, cookbooks. For Hana, books she has not heard of. For Ethan, football gear and team merchandise.
- **Sensory**: Loves olive blossom in May, fermenting kimchi in the cold pantry, the press during peak harvest, rain on dust at the start of a September shower. Hates artificial citrus, rancid olive oil, hot asphalt, most colognes. Avoids loud restaurants and television advertising.
- **Dislikes**: Marketing jargon (especially "artisanal" and "curated"), restaurant olive oil sitting in a glass cruet, phones at meals, the phrase "just a farmer," people who interrupt, people who do not eat the crust.
