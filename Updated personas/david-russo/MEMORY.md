# Memory: David Russo

## Personal Profile
David Russo is a Boston public school kid who became a Boston public school teacher, and that fact shapes him more than anything else. He fills a room before he speaks, the laugh first, then the story, then the question, but beneath the volume runs a worrier who replays parent emails at night and turns over Grandma's care, Rachel's career, and whether a struggling kid is slipping. He is generous without naming it as generosity, distrusts performances of feeling, and would rather show up than say the right thing, a habit his father gave him.

Cultural identity sits at the center of him. The Russo line came through New York generations back into Italian-American working-class Boston, all long Sunday dinners and sauce that simmers for hours. His grandmother's life in Jacksonville rerouted the family's food memory through the South, cornbread and banana pudding and fried potatoes, so "family food" became a long affectionate argument about whose recipe is right. His Catholic, working-class union upbringing left him a moral language that talks about dignity and bread in the same breath, and he reaches for it first when something matters.

He holds a B.A. in History from Harborview University in Boston (2015) and an M.Ed. from Northfield Graduate School of Education (2017). He believes ordinary people running their own lives in solidarity is the closest thing to a sacred political idea he holds, that history is a long argument about who counted as fully human, and that his job is to hand teenagers tools rather than opinions. He believes loyalty is earned slowly and lost in an afternoon, that generosity should be quiet, and that he is more anxious and less patient than people give him credit for.

## Work & Projects
History teacher at Eastbridge High School in Boston Public Schools, seven years in, teaching AP US History and 10th Grade World History across five sections and roughly 140 students. He started at Eastbridge in 2019, after two years teaching at another Boston public school. The department chair is Dr. Patricia Vale and the principal is Dr. Kwame Asante. His salary is $78,500 (Step 7, MA+30 on the BPS scale). He is the building union rep for the Eastbridge chapter of the Boston Educators Union, mentored by Tom Moriarty; the live fight is a district proposal to cut two history positions, and the BEU contract expires in February 2027. His curriculum project is a three-week AP USH unit on American Labor History from 1870 to 1970, piloting in fall 2026. Extra duties are AP exam prep on Tuesdays and History Club on Fridays. A notable student, Elena Russell (17), is an exceptional AP student he is helping with college applications strictly through school channels.

## Health & Wellness
Mild hypertension managed with Lisinopril 10mg daily, BP around 132/84; he is 215 lbs at 5 feet 11 and wants to lose 15. Borderline high LDL at 142, managed by diet, recheck in January 2027. Seasonal allergies treated with Claritin from April through June. His PCP is Dr. Brian Callahan at Dorchester Community Health Center, and his dentist is Dr. Amy Nguyen at Ashmont Dental. He is considering therapy for work stress and Grandma anxiety but has not started. Exercise is basketball Thursdays, running Monday, Wednesday, and Saturday, plus daily dog walks; he sleeps roughly 10:30 PM to 5:15 AM. Family health he tracks: Dad has osteoarthritic knees (refusing surgery) and hypertension on Losartan 50mg; Grandma Carol has Type 2 diabetes on Metformin (A1C 7.8), uses a walker, and has had depression since Grandpa Russo passed in 2021.

## Interests & Hobbies
History is the spine: primary sources read for fun, battlefield and museum trips, an obsession with labor and immigration history and the American Revolution. Basketball is the other spine, a non-negotiable Thursday league at the Dorchester Community Center with high school friends (the team is "The Historians") and religious Celtics watching. He cooks the holiday roast for the family, learned from his mother's kitchen and his father's grill. Podcasts fill every commute and dog walk, rotating through history, labor news, and Celtics analysis. Monthly board game nights (Ticket to Ride, Catan, Azul) endure. Daily walks with Biscuit in Franklin Park count as a hobby in his head. He runs a casual two to three miles three times a week, just to move.

## Home & Living
A 2BR/1BA second-floor unit in a Dorchester triple-decker, about 900 square feet near the Ashmont T stop, rent $2,400 with the lease renewing each November 1. The landlady is Mrs. Patricia Donovan, 80, on the first floor and devoted to Biscuit. The second bedroom is a shared office: his desk a working ruin of books and papers, Rachel's a tidy island. The kitchen leans heavy on garlic powder, paprika, and hot sauce, with Biscuit's corner in the living room. He has no car; he takes the Red Line from Ashmont, walks, and borrows Dad's truck for big errands.

## Preferences
- Food and drink: omnivore with a big appetite. Family comfort food is rice and gravy, pot roast, mashed and fried potatoes, baked beans, and banana pudding, with Dad's cornbread and meatloaf; weeknights are simple pasta, stir fry, rice and beans, and quesadillas split with Rachel. A morning everything bagel with cream cheese from Donut Depot is non-negotiable, alongside a large iced coffee year-round, two a day, cream and sugar. Groceries come from Greenfield Market, Corner Market in Dudley, and the Copley Square Farmers Market on Fridays. Favorite spots are Trattoria del Sol, Pier 9 Seafood Shack for summer lobster rolls (never too much mayonnaise), and Sal's Kitchen in East Boston. Beer runs to Baystate Lager, Harbor Brewing, and seasonal Sam Adams.
- Entertainment and reading: news from The Commonwealth Herald, The Sideline Report, The Labor Dispatch, and Workers' Voice Magazine; reading is labor history, Colson Whitehead, and graphic novels like March; TV is every Celtics game, Abbott Elementary, a fourth rewatch of The Wire, and history docs.
- Music: classic rock for cooking (Tom Petty, Foo Fighters), Springsteen, and hip-hop (Nas, Kendrick).
- Style and home: school button-ups and chinos, Celtics gear and a Red Sox cap on weekends, a blazer for union meetings; he loves the broken-in over the new and stays loyal to Celtics green, Boston red, and navy.
- Shopping: he researches before buying, buys local even at a markup, takes gifts seriously (thoughtful over expensive, bookstore gift cards excepted), and splurges guilt-free only on labor memorabilia, rare books, and vintage Celtics gear.
- Travel: trips need a purpose, a battlefield, museum, monument, or relative; he packs heavy on books, prefers to drive, tolerates flying to reach family faster, and requires real morning coffee.
- Comfort and sensory: he decompresses by walking Biscuit, cooking a long-simmer sauce, or reading until the words blur; he is grounded by bread, garlic in hot oil, and the smell of a used bookstore, and bothered by chemical air fresheners (banned at home), warm drinking water, and open-plan offices. Boston in winter feels most like home.
