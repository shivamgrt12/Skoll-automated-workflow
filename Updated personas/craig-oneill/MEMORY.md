# Memory: Craig O'Neill

## Personal Profile

Craig O'Neill is a fourth-generation Irish-American fishing captain in Isafjordur, Westfjords, Iceland, owner and operator of the FV Sjonarhorn, a 22-metre stern trawler built in 2008 and owned outright. He has spent thirty-five years on the North Atlantic, twenty as captain, and holds a Master Fisherman's certificate from the Reykjavik Maritime Academy. He lives in a traditional timber-clad house overlooking the harbour with his wife Shannon Callahan, a five-minute walk to the docks. His grandfather built the family's first vessel in the 1940s. His father, Patrick O'Neill Sr., was lost at sea in a 2015 storm.

He is direct, practical, and quietly sentimental. His default off-duty mode is harbour coffee with Sean Fitzgerald at first light, a slow build of the 1:20 scale model of his grandfather's vessel in the workshop, Smithwick's on draft at Husid on Friday evenings, and Jameson neat after a successful trip or at holidays.

## Work & Projects

The FV Sjonarhorn is the operation: a 22-metre stern trawler fishing primarily cod, haddock, and redfish in the waters off the Westfjords and north Iceland. Craig holds Individual Transferable Quota allocations for all three species and runs a five-person crew with Patrick Sullivan as first mate, two experienced deckhands, and one rotating junior. Voyages are 3 to 7 days, with 15 to 20 days at sea per month during peak season.

- **2026 Fishing Season Management**: full annual operations, current focus the autumn campaign north of the Westfjords. Monitoring stock reports from the Marine Research Institute. Managing quota across species for value and sustainability.
- **Vessel Engine Refit**: main engine overhaul in the December window at the Isafjordur shipyard with Nordurverksmidja engineers in from Akureyri. Estimated cost 4,500,000 ISK. Funded from vessel reserve and operating income, no borrowing. Haulout December 7, target completion December 26.
- **Quota Optimisation and Sustainability Reporting**: catch reporting and quota utilisation documentation with the Fisheries Association. Participation in the Westfjords electronic monitoring pilot programme for bycatch reduction.
- **Professional development**: Fishing Vessel Owners' Association membership, Fisheries Conference in Reykjavik October 16, ongoing safety and navigation refreshers.
