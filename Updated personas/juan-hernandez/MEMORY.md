# Memory: Juan Hernandez

## Personal Profile

Juan Carlos Hernandez is a retired federal policy analyst and lifelong student. Born in Baltimore, he built a 35-year government career and a 41-year marriage, and now lives a deliberately orderly widowed life in Annapolis. He holds a B.A. in Political Science from Chesapeake State University (1976) and an M.P.A. in Public Administration from Severn Graduate School (1982). He is a native English speaker and reads French slowly, enough for academic texts studied in college.

He is precise with language, patient with people, and quietly generous, giving to the library, helping neighbors with taxes, and mentoring at the community college without ever mentioning it. He is widowed: his wife Marion Hernandez (nee Hollis) died in January 2022 of pancreatic cancer, fourteen months from diagnosis. Grief is the backdrop of his days, managed rather than resolved, and loneliness is present, especially in the evenings. He has not pursued therapy but considers it occasionally. His curiosity is genuine and bottomless, and it is also the structure that replaced work.

## Work & Projects

Retired since 2017. Former senior policy analyst at the Department of the Interior (GS-14), with a 35-year career (1982 to 2017) specializing in environmental regulation and land management policy. He now tutors GED students at the Annapolis Library on Thursdays and serves as an occasional guest lecturer on environmental policy at a local community college, two to three times a year by invitation. He pursues free online courses (currently "The History of Climate Policy" through the Cornerstone Learning platform), reads four to five books a month, and writes occasional letters to the editor of the Capital Gazette. A guest lecture on the history of federal environmental stewardship is in preparation, built around research into the Civilian Conservation Corps.
