# Memory: Joseph Fields

## Personal Profile

Joseph William Fields is a blue-collar tradesman who learned HVAC at 23, after four years in the Army, because his father, a mechanic who watched chain stores take his shop's business, told his sons to pick a skill that needs your hands and your presence and to get licensed. He chose right. Twenty-three years in, he is one of the most experienced technicians on his team and still gets second-guessed by customers who judge him by his work boots instead of his certifications. He handles it with professionalism and vents to his brother Kevin after.

He is White and Anglo American, born in Columbus and raised between Columbus and his mother's family in Lancaster. His education is a high school diploma plus EPA Section 608 Universal certification (certificate ID 608U-2003-44871, earned 2003), NATE (North American Technician Excellence) certification (#1182246, earned 2005), and four years of Army service (1998 to 2002) ending in an honorable discharge, after which he entered the HVAC trade. He speaks English. He is divorced from Megan since 2023 and is single and not dating.

Faith is the floor he stands on, not the flag he waves. He goes to church most Sundays, prays before meals, and believes God has a plan even when the plan looks like a crawl space full of black mold. He does not preach or debate theology; his faith is practical and gets him through Mondays. He is loyal to a fault, which is why his circle is small, and he protects his finite energy carefully.
