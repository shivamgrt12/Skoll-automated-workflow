# Memory: Tony Nguyen Sr.

## Personal Profile

Tony Michael Nguyen Sr. spent 26 years in the US State Department Foreign Service before settling in Silver Spring after retirement. American by ethnicity. The career and the marriage are detailed in Work & Projects and Key Relationships; this section is for who he is, not what he has done.

Tony moves through the world the way he moves a chess piece: slowly, deliberately, and only after the position has revealed itself. People often mistake his quiet for coldness; those who stay long enough notice the stillness is attention, not indifference. He would rather say one accurate sentence than three approximate ones, and he has the diplomat's habit of waiting for the other person to finish a thought before he begins his own. There is a quiet pride in him, though he would never frame it as such. Patience, in him, is less a virtue than a strategy he has worn so long it became character. Emotionally contained but never emotionally absent.

His patriotism is real but unsentimental, complicated by decades representing America in other countries' rooms. Each posting left a residue: a habit of formality, an ear for indirection, a respect for ceremony he carries into church and federation alike. Faith threads through his identity without dominating his conversation; he treats belief as a set of obligations rather than feelings.

In a room, he speaks least and is listened to most. Conserves himself. Prefers one real conversation over twenty pleasant ones. Builds community by connecting people, remembering what matters, following up. Trust earned from him is permanent because the earning is slow.

Core conviction: a person is the sum of what they were trusted with. Integrity is a record, not a feeling. He would tell you patience is the most underrated virtue, and admit if pressed that he has used it as a weapon as often as a kindness.

## Work & Projects

Tony retired from the US State Department Foreign Service in 2001 after 26 years, specializing in trade negotiations and multilateral diplomacy. Postings: Geneva (1978 to 1983), London (1984 to 1989), Tokyo (1990 to 1995), Brasilia (1996 to 2001, as Counselor). He settled in Silver Spring after retirement.

He founded the Mid-Atlantic Community Chess Federation (MACCF) in 2008 and has served as president since. The federation organizes the MACCF Annual Open Tournament every June at the Silver Spring Civic Center, drawing 80 to 120 participants. It manages a network of 12 chess clubs across MD, VA, and DC. The youth development program reaches 40-plus children ages 8 to 18 in after-school chess programs at 6 schools. The federation coordinates exchange programs with chess federations in Virginia, Pennsylvania, North Carolina, and Georgia. Board meetings run on the 2nd Saturday monthly at 10:00 AM at the Silver Spring Civic Center.

The memoir of his diplomatic career, drafted slowly in Google Docs and Obsidian, currently runs about 60 pages. Whether he wants it published or simply written is a question he has not answered, including to himself.

## Preferences

**Media**: NPR World Service (radio habit from diplomatic years), The Capitol Observer daily, Foreign Affairs quarterly. PBS documentaries, chess tournament broadcasts, Jeopardy at 7:00 PM, occasional Premier League (Arsenal supporter since London posting).

**Food and drink**: classic American cuisine. Breakfast: tea with milk and sugar (Lipton, never coffee) with bread and eggs, pancakes on weekends. Lunch: light, rice with stew or leftovers. Dinner: pot roast, meatloaf, or grilled fish with vegetables. Three cups of tea per day, no coffee ever. Guilty pleasure: cookies from Linda's holiday jar.

**Shopping**: Whole Foods for produce, Costco for staples. Researches before committing, distrusts impulse, satisfaction in purchases that prove durable. Loyal to brands that have earned it. Generous toward family, church, and federation; sparing on himself.

**Aesthetic**: quietly correct. Nothing flashy. Dresses a few degrees more formally than the moment requires. Prefers one excellent thing over several adequate ones. Conservative and reliable in objects as in people.

**Travel**: skilled from his foreign-service years. Now prefers shorter, purposeful trips: chess events, family visits, watching Tony Jr. compete. Arrives early. Dislikes improvisation in logistics. Treats itineraries like opening repertoires.

**Comfort**: chess board first when the weight accumulates, then walks along the creek, then the kitchen. Which composer he reaches for is a fair read of his interior weather. Linda is the deepest comfort. House kept at 73 degrees.

**Sensory**: drawn to ordered sound, the resolution of a symphony, the silence of early morning. Dislikes clamor and interruption. Loves the smell of a long-simmering pot, cut herbs, an old book opened. The sounds of grandchildren filling the house are the one beautiful exception to his preference for quiet.

**Lifestyle and conduct**: dislikes carelessness in language, being rushed, the spectacle of grievance. Limited patience for loudness mistaken for conviction. Notices when generosity runs one direction. Rarely names what he notices; the noticing changes how he allocates attention afterward.
