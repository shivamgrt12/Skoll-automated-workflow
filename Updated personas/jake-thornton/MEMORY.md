# Memory: Jake Thornton

## Personal Profile

Jake Thornton is a Certified Nurse-Midwife and founder of Jake's Table, a home-based Southern-fusion cooking class out of his East Lake kitchen in Atlanta. He grew up in Baltimore, Maryland and moved to Atlanta for nursing school; his mother Linda later relocated to Savannah after retirement. American by birth and by raising, with roots in Maryland on his mother's side and a deep food culture pulled from both his Thornton family and his husband Marcus's Johnson family in southwest Atlanta. He holds a B.S. in Nursing from Peachtree University (2018) and an M.S. in Nurse-Midwifery from Ridgeline College University (2021). He carries active CNM certification through the American Midwifery Certification Board and a Georgia RN license, which together cover both his hospital and low-risk home births. He is a trans man who carried both pregnancies; he and Marcus built their family with intention, and Jake is open about this with clients when it is relevant to their care and quietly private about it otherwise. Spiritually grounded with a strong internal compass that he never imposes on others. He believes nourishing people, by birth or by food, is the same instinct, and he is unapologetic about building two careers at once. Direct in a way that softens with humor, generous as a community builder, and unwilling to flatten either his clinical work or his cooking into a side project.

## Work & Projects

Jake works full time as a Certified Nurse-Midwife at Magnolia Women's Health in Midtown Atlanta, employed since 2021. The practice has three midwives and two OB-GYNs. He runs clinic Monday, Tuesday, and Thursday from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM, prenatal home visits on Wednesday mornings, and on-call birth rotation that flexes through the rest of the week. He delivers at Ridgeline College University Hospital Midtown where he holds privileges and attends home births for low-risk clients at a rate of one to two per quarter. His base salary is $92,000 per year. CEU requirement is 20 hours annually; the year resets each January 1, and at anchor he is at 10 of 20 logged for 2026. Collaborative physician is Dr. Keisha Williams. He also runs Jake's Table, a home-based cooking class business out of his East Lake kitchen with a student capacity of eight, plus occasional pop-ups at community venues. Classes run two to three per month on Saturday or Sunday afternoons for three hours each. Pricing is $75 per person at the home class and $65 per person at a venue class with lower overhead. Average enrollment is six to eight students. Annual revenue runs about $14,000 and is growing. Cuisines taught include Southern comfort, American fusion, and grilling. Bookings come through Calendly, Instagram DMs, and word of mouth. Supplies come from Dekalb Farmers Market, Costco, and Amazon for specialty spices. A recipe blog is in development with 12 recipes drafted in Google Docs and a soft launch that slipped from its original 2026 target, now aimed at March 1, 2027. Instagram at @jakes.table has 2,400 followers and growing steadily. He volunteers with the Atlanta Maternal Wellness Alliance focused on improving Atlanta-area maternal outcomes, with an annual board brief due each February.
