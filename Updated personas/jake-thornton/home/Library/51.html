American Fusion

Andouille Jambalaya

A one-pot study in building layers - darkened andouille fat, sweated trinity, Cajun spice bloom, and long-grain rice that drinks up everything in the pot. This is the dish that anchors the American Fusion Night class.

55 minutes
Serves 8
~$5.80 per serving
Medium
DeKalb Farmers Market ingredients

Ingredients

  • 14 oz andouille sausage, sliced into coins
  • 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 cups long-grain white rice (not parboiled)
  • 1 large onion, diced • 2 celery stalks, diced • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14 oz), drained
  • 3.5 cups chicken stock, low-sodium
  • 2 tsp Cajun seasoning blend • 1 tsp smoked paprika • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 3 scallions, thinly sliced, for serving

Method

  1. Heat oil in a heavy Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear andouille slices in a single layer, 2 minutes per side, until darkened and fat has rendered. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside. Do not drain - that fat is the foundation.
  2. Season chicken pieces with salt, pepper, and 1 tsp Cajun seasoning. Sear in the same pot in batches for 3 minutes per side until golden. Remove and set aside with the andouille.
  3. Reduce heat to medium. Add the diced onion, celery, and bell pepper to the pot. Cook for 6-8 minutes, scraping up the browned bits, until softened and starting to color. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.
  4. Add remaining Cajun seasoning and smoked paprika. Stir for 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the drained tomatoes and cook 2 minutes, stirring.
  5. Add the rice and stir to coat in the mixture, about 1 minute. Return sausage and chicken to the pot. Pour in stock and add the bay leaf. Stir once to distribute, then bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce heat to low, cover tightly, and cook for 22 minutes without lifting the lid. Remove from heat and rest, still covered, for 5 minutes before serving.
  7. Remove bay leaf. Fluff gently with a fork. Taste and adjust salt. Scatter scallions over the top and serve directly from the pot.
The rice absorption time is non-negotiable - resist the urge to lift the lid. Every time you check, you lose steam and add minutes. Set a timer and walk away.
