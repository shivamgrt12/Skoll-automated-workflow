# Memory: Carlos Bennett

## Personal Profile

Carlos is Gulf Coast in a way that is older than the tourist version of the region: working harbors, church suppers, high-school football Fridays. He carries the practical, hospitable, faintly anti-pretension stamp of multi-generational Floridian families on his father's English and Scots-Irish line, four generations of working coastal people behind him. He graduated Bayport High School in 1986, holds a USCG Master 50 GT Near Coastal license, and apprenticed six years under master luthier Henry Dalton for the guitar side of the trade.

He moves at the speed of someone who has learned most problems either solve themselves with time or reveal their real shape after you stop poking at them. When a charter goes sideways or an engine alarm sings the wrong song twelve miles offshore, the version of Carlos that shows up is quieter than the one you get over coffee.

The tender streak shows up at unexpected angles. He gets quiet around Father's Day. He keeps a photograph of his father, taken on a boat Carlos does not remember being on, tucked inside the wheelhouse where nobody else has reason to look. When one of his sons calls and something underneath the call is not yet being said, he finds a reason to be on the porch within a day or two.

He believes the value of a man's word is roughly equal to the value of the man. His father's collapse at 61 and his own hundred-hour weeks after the divorce taught him that work without rest is a slow self-betrayal. He believes in the moral economy of small places: if you take, you owe back, and you do not pay the debt in money alone. He fries fish for the American Legion and runs a half-rate charter for the Make-a-Wish kid every summer he is asked.

Church at Grace Community in Brooksville is part of his life that he neither broadcasts nor hides. He sings without performing and describes his faith, if pressed, as gratitude rather than certainty. Pastor David has known him long enough not to press.

He is superstitious about the water in a way he half-believes. No Friday departures for multi-day trips. A St. Christopher medal taped to the wheelhouse dash. A piece of his father's old fishing line in his wallet. If pressed, he laughs at himself; he does not stop doing any of it.

## Work & Projects

**Lucky Strike (fishing charter):** Carlos owns and operates a 42-foot sportfisher built 1991 with twin Cummins 6BT diesel, homeported at Bayport harbor in Hernando County. He has run the boat for 35 years and the engines through two rebuilds. His USCG Captain's License (Master 50 GT Near Coastal) is valid through December 2026, with the renewal packet submitting October 3. Peak charter season runs April through September, shoulder season October through November, maintenance and off-season December through March. Russ Taylor mates eight seasons running. Average season grosses $75,000 to $90,000. Fuel runs ~$800 a week in season. Dock fees are $350 a month year-round. Insurance is Gulf Coast Marine at $4,200 a year. Annual Coast Guard inspection last passed February 2026. The port outrigger winch was rebuilt in the workshop in February 2026 with parts from Marine Supply Depot ahead of the April season start.

**Bennett Guitar Works (luthier side):** Home garage workshop, climate-controlled side built by Carlos. Parts wall, setup bench, fret station. Specializes in acoustic (Martin, Taylor, Gibson) and vintage electrics (Fender, Gibson), with mandolins and banjos for local bluegrass players. Typical repair runs $150 to $400 (fret leveling, neck reset, electronics). Full restoration runs $800 to $2,000. Current pipeline: four instruments (two fret jobs, one neck reset, one 1960s Martin D-28 full restoration delivering October 17). Parts sourced from StewMac, Allparts, LMI, and salvage instruments. Revenue ~$12,000 a year. Diane manages the Facebook page, which generates two to three inquiries a month.

## Interests & Hobbies

- **Guitar repair and restoration**: more than a sideline, never called more than that out loud. The hour past nine at night in the workshop with the porch fan running and a Hank Williams record turned low is when the captain and the luthier dissolve into one man holding wood.
- **Playing guitar**: sits in with the Harbor Ramblers at jam sessions and open mics. He plays well enough to know exactly where he stops being good.
- **Recreational fishing**: redfish, snook, tarpon. Takes the grandkids out on the flats on Sunday afternoons, which is a different animal from running a charter.

- **College football**: season ticket holder with Diane. Tailgates every home game, running the smoker as usual. The ritual matters more than the football itself.
