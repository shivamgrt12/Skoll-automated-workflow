# Memory: John Patel

## Personal Profile
John Patel is an Indian-American emergency medicine physician living in Houston. He earned his MD from Lone Star College of Medicine in 2011, a BS in Biochemistry from Gulf Plains University, and completed his EM residency at Chesapeake Institute. He is ABEM board-certified in Emergency Medicine (2016) and holds Texas Medical Board license #N8842. He is deeply analytical, calm under extreme pressure, and values education and continuous learning above almost everything. He stays closely connected to his Indian heritage through food, music, and faith, and family traditions anchor him. He carries quiet guilt about time away from his kids, and he uses dark, gallows humor to cope with the weight of the ER.

## Work & Projects
Career timeline: completed his BS in Biochemistry in May 2007, earned his MD in May 2011, ran his EM residency from July 2011 to June 2015, and has been an attending physician at his current hospital since July 2015 (about 11 years). John is an emergency medicine attending in the Emergency Medicine department at Lone Star Medical Center-TMC, a Level 1 trauma center handling roughly 100,000 ED visits a year. He works the trauma team rotation on rotating 12-hour shifts (7A-7P days, 7P-7A nights), about 14 shifts per month, roughly half of them nights. Night differential is $85/hour versus $75/hour base (a $10/hour night premium). He precepts two 4th-year EM rotators, Sarah and James. He is preparing for the ABEM ConCert recertification exam using Hippo Education and Rosh Review, studying 2 hours per day on off days and currently scoring 68% against an 80% target, with a biweekly study group. He leads a QI initiative to cut door-to-antibiotic time for sepsis from a 68-minute mean toward under 45 minutes, and is co-authoring a point-of-care ultrasound in trauma paper with Dr. Okafor for the Journal of Academic Emergency Practice. CME requirement: 250 credits over 10 years (100 self-assessment), on track but needs recent courses logged.
