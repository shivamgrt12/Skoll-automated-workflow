# Memory: Shannon Perry

## Personal Profile
- **Full name**: Shannon Darnell Perry, called Shan by family and Coach Perry by his athletes.
- **Nicknames**: Patients sometimes say Dr. Perry out of habit; his mother calls him Baby Boy.
- **Identity**: Black and Southern, fourth-generation Birmingham, AME by birth and daily practice.
- **Family legacy**: Great-grandmother marched in the 1963 Children's Crusade; her name appears in the museum exhibit.
- **Roots**: Turned down clinic offers in Atlanta and Nashville; committed to being useful to Birmingham.
- **Temperament**: Calm that reads as distance, quiet confidence, patient with effort and short with excuses.
- **Emotional processing**: Thinks in protocols and timelines; metabolizes grief and stress physically through miles.
- **Humor**: Understatement delivered with a straight face; deeply protective of his athletes.
- **BS Exercise Science**: University of Alabama at Birmingham, 2016.
- **Doctor of Physical Therapy**: Samford University, 2019.
- **SCS certification**: Sports Clinical Specialist, American Board of Physical Therapy Specialties, 2021.
- **License**: Alabama Board of Physical Therapy, PT-4738.
- **Philosophy**: Showing up is most of the work, especially for kids without steady adults.
- **On rehabilitation**: A kind of slow moral work that asks for honesty about how hard recovery will be.
- **On faith**: The church is the place his ancestors made for him; maintaining it is his inheritance.
- **On grief**: Movement is how he metabolizes it, and he has made his peace with not being finished.

## Key Relationships
- **Brenda Perry (mother)**, 59. Retired elementary school teacher in Woodlawn; the rock of the family since James Sr. passed. Cooks Sunday dinner without fail, calls Shannon daily, has freely shared opinions about his love life. He drives over every Sunday after church.
- **Keisha Perry-Coleman (sister)**, 34. Dental hygienist in Hoover. Loud, funny, fiercely loyal. She and Shannon text constantly, and she brings the kids to his meets.
- **Marcus Coleman (brother-in-law)**, 36. Logistics manager at UPS, married to Keisha. Solid and dependable; he and Shannon watch football and grill together.
- **Jaylen Coleman**, 8, and **Nia Coleman**, 5. Keisha and Marcus's children; Nia mispronounces "Uncle Shan."
- **James Darnell Perry Sr. (father)**, deceased. Died October 17, 2023, of a heart attack at 61. Buried at Greenwood Memorial Cemetery. Postal worker for 30 years and Pop Warner coach. Shannon carries his coaching whistle in his gym bag daily.
- **Uncle Raymond Perry**, 66. James Sr.'s older brother, retired firefighter in Ensley. Stepped into a father-figure role; he and Shannon fish at Lake Purdy and he comes to most Sunday dinners.
- **Dr. Anita Lawson (clinic director)**, 48. Orthopedic surgeon and founder of Iron City Sports Medicine; Shannon's boss and mentor. Hired him out of his clinical training and gives him latitude to build the sports-rehab program his way.
- **Coach Demetrius Washington (assistant track coach)**, 27. Math teacher at Crestwood, former college sprinter at Tuskegee University. Handles distance events and meet logistics; the little brother Shannon never had, and the one person who catches his jokes in real time.
- **Pastor Leonard Hayes**, 65. Senior pastor at New Hope AME. Baptized Shannon at twelve and buried his father at sixty-one; checks in on the family.
- **Jasmine Carter (ex-girlfriend)**, 30. Pediatric nurse at Children's of Alabama. Dated 2023 to 2025; mutual, respectful breakup roughly six months ago that he has not fully metabolized. They still say hello at church.
- **Devon Mitchell (mentee and athlete)**, 17. Junior sprinter at Crestwood, the fastest kid he has coached, with Division I potential. Father is incarcerated and his grandmother is raising him; Shannon is quietly invested in his future.
- **Monica Vance**, clinic front desk at Iron City; handles scheduling and intake.

## Work & Projects
- **Primary employer**: Iron City Sports Medicine, outpatient orthopedic and sports-rehab clinic in Homewood, Alabama.
- **Role**: Sports physical therapist since 2019, caseload of 18 to 22 patients per week.
- **Specialization**: Post-surgical athlete rehab including ACL reconstructions, rotator cuff and labral repairs, and overuse injuries.
- **Clinical schedule**: Monday through Friday, 7:00 AM to 1:30 PM treatment blocks, documentation after.
- **Clinic team**: Works alongside Dr. Anita Lawson, two other PTs, three aides, and Monica at front desk.
- **Current clinical focus**: Four post-op ACL patients in return-to-sport protocols and a football prehab program rollout.
- **Coaching role**: Volunteer head coach at Crestwood High School Track and Field since 2021, AHSAA Class 6A.
- **Team size**: 28 athletes; he coaches sprints and hurdles while Demetrius Washington handles distance events.
- **Track season**: February through early June, with off-season conditioning through summer and fall.
- **Current coaching focus**: Building the 2026-2027 base-mileage program and Devon Mitchell's senior-year D1 recruiting.

## Finance
- **Annual salary**: $78,000 as a physical therapist at Iron City; coaching is unpaid volunteer work.
- **Rent**: $1,250 per month for a two-bedroom apartment in the Crestwood South neighborhood.
- **Car payment**: $385 per month on a 2022 Toyota Tacoma.
- **Retirement**: $22,000 in a 401(k) through the clinic.
- **Bank accounts**: $8,500 in savings and $2,800 in checking at Community First Bank.
- **Student loans**: $42,000 remaining on DPT loans, income-based repayment at $380 per month.
- **Church tithe**: $650 per month, non-negotiable.
- **Monthly essentials**: Groceries $300, gas $150, running gear and races $80, supplements $40, grilling about $60 in season.
- **Financial style**: Responsible but not wealthy; student loans are the anchor and the Tacoma was his one indulgence.

## Health & Wellness
- **Overall health**: Excellent; he practices what he preaches to his athletes.
- **Left knee**: Mild patellar tendinopathy from years of sprinting, managed with eccentric loading exercises.
- **Cardiac vigilance**: Father died of a heart attack at 61, so he gets an annual EKG and cholesterol panel.
- **Primary care**: Annual physical with Dr. Marcus Grant at UAB Sports Medicine, last clear late 2025.
- **Dental**: Every six months at Hometown Dental Crestwood.
- **Mental health**: Solid foundation tested by grief; father's death was the hardest thing he has faced.
- **Support system**: His mother Brenda, Uncle Ray, and Pastor Hayes; no therapist yet but open to it.
- **Running**: Five days a week, 5 to 7 miles early morning, 25 to 30 miles per week total.
- **Strength training**: Three days a week at the clinic gym on a functional program.
- **Nutrition**: High-protein and whole-food focused; chicken, fish, brown rice, sweet potatoes, eggs, Greek yogurt, and greens.
- **Hydration**: About a gallon of water daily; black coffee before the morning run, no alcohol during track season.
- **Sleep**: Roughly 10:00 PM to 5:15 AM on weekdays; blackout curtains, fan for white noise, no phone in bed.

## Interests & Hobbies
- **Running**: Oldest devotion since middle school; ran the 400m and 4x400 relay at UAB as a collegiate athlete.
- **Saturday long run**: The week's anchor at 8 to 10 miles; he ran twelve miles the morning of his father's funeral.
- **Coaching**: Became a calling around year two of what he expected to be a side commitment at Crestwood.
- **Grilling**: His father's inheritance on the same Weber grill; smokes ribs low and slow on Saturdays with regional rubs.
- **Film study**: Breaks down sprint mechanics frame by frame on Hudl, his most private analytical habit.
- **Gospel music**: Sings baritone in the men's choir at New Hope AME, the line his father and uncle hold.
- **Fishing**: Lake Purdy with Uncle Ray on occasional Saturdays; quiet and slow on purpose.
- **Reading**: Sports biographies, coaching methodology, and sports-medicine journals alongside a functional weightlifting program.
- **Family traditions**: Christmas and Watch Night at New Hope AME, MLK Day commemorations, birthday dinner at Brenda's every December 14.

## Home & Living
- **Apartment**: Two-bedroom, second floor in a quiet Crestwood South complex, about 10 minutes from the clinic.
- **Proximity**: 15 minutes from Crestwood High School and 20 minutes from Brenda's house in Woodlawn.
- **Living room**: Big-screen TV for film study and Sunday football, father's framed coaching photo, three UAB track trophies.
- **Home office**: Second bedroom with dual monitors for Hudl review and a foam-roller mobility station.
- **Kitchen**: Functional, stocked with barbecue rubs and his father's cast-iron skillet; meal-prep containers fill the fridge Sundays.
- **Patio**: Weber grill, two folding chairs, and a small cooler for Saturday smoke sessions.
- **Vehicle**: Dark gray 2022 Toyota Tacoma, crew cab, kept immaculate, with a photo of his father on the dash.
