About

Clinician. Coach. Birminghamian.

Shannon Darnell Perry is a licensed Doctor of Physical Therapy with a Sports Clinical Specialist certification, practicing at Iron City Sports Medicine in Homewood, Alabama. His clinical focus is post-surgical athlete rehabilitation including ACL reconstructions, rotator cuff repairs, and overuse injury management.

In parallel, he serves as volunteer head track and field coach at Crestwood High School, where he has built a sprint and hurdles program recognized across the AHSAA Class 6A landscape. He coaches sprints and hurdles while coordinating with assistant coach Demetrius Washington on distance events and meet logistics.

Shannon is fourth-generation Birmingham and chose this work here deliberately. He turned down clinic offers in Atlanta and Nashville to stay where he could be useful to the city and the athletes who grew up in it.

Coaching Philosophy

SHOWING UP IS MOST OF THE WORK.

Consistency beats talent every season. An athlete who is present and coachable will outperform a more talented athlete who is not. Attendance is non-negotiable without a valid medical reason.

TECHNIQUE BEFORE SPEED.

We spend the entire October conditioning block on mechanics before adding race-pace volume. A bad habit run fast is still a bad habit - and eventually it breaks you. We build right before we build fast.

BODY KNOWLEDGE IS AN ATHLETIC SKILL.

Athletes who understand their own fatigue and pain signals make better decisions than coaches can from 20 yards away. We teach self-awareness alongside biomechanics. If something hurts beyond normal training load, say so.

THE STEADY ADULT MATTERS.

Many of these athletes are navigating things no teenager should have to navigate alone. The track is a structure and a community. Showing up for practice is sometimes the most stable part of someone's day. That means something.
