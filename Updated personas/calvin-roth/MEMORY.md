# Memory: Calvin Roth

## Personal Profile

Calvin Maxwell Roth is a public defender who became one because he believes everyone deserves a real defense. Nearly four years in the system have left him relentlessly idealistic but pragmatically tired, still fighting hard for every client while no longer expecting the system to be fair. He is disciplined and composed in the courtroom and quietly chaotic at home, aware of the contrast between his meticulous case prep and the laundry piles, dishes, and three-month-old jigsaw puzzle in his apartment. He is fiercely competitive and genuinely self-doubting, a night owl who does his best thinking at 1 AM and resents the alarm at 7 AM.

He was raised in a loosely religious household in Indianapolis that had mostly stopped observing by his teens. He does not attend services and does not miss them, but an old hymn or a quiet Sunday morning still makes him pause. He calls himself culturally sentimental rather than spiritual. He is emotionally generous with the people he trusts and guarded with romantic interests, deflecting personal questions with humor on the rare date. He holds high-functioning anxiety in check through running, structure, and his sister.

He holds a B.A. in Political Science from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (2018) and a J.D. from Loyola University Chicago School of Law (2022). He is single. He is a native English speaker with conversational Spanish, enough for client intake but not fluent.

## Key Relationships

- **Naomi Roth**, younger sister, born November 7, 1998 (age 27). Social worker in Chicago, lives 20 minutes away, sees him at least weekly. His emotional anchor, the person he calls after a bad verdict, date, or run. Protective of each other almost like parents.
- **Lorraine Okafor**, supervising attorney at the office. Tough, experienced, fair, pushes him hard because she sees potential. He respects her enormously but sometimes feels the pressure exceeds what he can carry. Standing weekly one-on-one.
- **Roland Roth**, father, born January 22, 1967 (age 59). Retired postal worker in Indianapolis. Proud but does not fully understand the work, calls him "the lawyer in the family." Warm and steady. They talk by phone every other Sunday.
- **Denise Roth**, mother, born March 10, 1969 (age 57). Administrative assistant at a church office in Indianapolis. More openly emotional than Roland, worries about his stress and his singleness, sends care packages of homemade cookies and dating advice.
- **Amara Sullivan**, running partner and close friend, met through the Pacers two years ago. ER nurse in Chicago. They run together twice a week and debrief about their high-stress jobs. One of the few who does not make him perform competence.
- **Derek Yuen**, pace leader for the Lakefront Pacers, high school math teacher. Organized, encouraging, slightly nerdy. He values Derek's structure. Friendly but not close outside running.
- **Marcus Traylor**, fellow public defender hired the same year. They share an office wall and a dark sense of humor, and Marcus is who he debriefs with after tough court days. Purely platonic despite Naomi's suggestions.
- **Aunt Vivian Clark**, mother's sister, born October 15, 1963 (age 62), in Atlanta. The "cool aunt" who encouraged law school. They talk monthly, and she sends birthday money every year.

## Work & Projects

- **Role**: Public Defender, Cook County Public Defender's Office, reporting to Lorraine Okafor.
- **Tenure**: Nearly four years, started September 2022 (hits the four-year mark that month).
- **Caseload**: 35 to 45 active cases at a time, a mix of misdemeanors and felonies. Currently handling two complex felony cases heading to trial in October.
- **Hours**: Monday to Friday, officially 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM CT but regularly to 7:00 or 8:00 PM. Court typically 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM, afternoons for client meetings, prep, and filings. Frequent weekend prep during trial periods.
- **Key colleagues**: Lorraine Okafor (supervisor), Marcus Traylor (colleague), Rhonda Kim (office admin and paralegal). Judge assignments rotate.
- **PTO**: 15 days a year, 8 remaining, saving days for a trip with Naomi in October.
- **Service**: volunteers a few hours most weeks at a Chicago legal-aid clinic and sits on a Chicago Bar Association committee that runs an annual continuing-education event. Both keep him on a handful of shared club, clinic, and committee tools outside the office systems.
