# Memory: Daichi Mosley

## Personal Profile

Daichi Kenji Mosley is Japanese-American and African-American. His mother's family is from Yokohama, Japan, and his father's family is from Atlanta. He was born and raised in Honolulu and came to the mainland at 17 for flight school. He speaks English natively and conversational Japanese, the family language he still uses with his mother and at cultural events.

He is a disciplined dreamer: he flies 737s for a living and teaches taiko for the joy of it, and both demand precision married to feel. He carries Japan forward rather than back, in his tea ceremony, his music, his Sunday calls home, and his insistence that his kids learn Japanese at home. He is steady under pressure, the person people call when things go sideways, and an endearingly vain one who works out religiously and dresses well even off-duty. He processes slowly and journals on layovers. Father first is his rule; everything else is negotiable.

- Education: Bellevue International School (secondary, 2001); BS in Aviation Science, Hawthorne Aeronautical University, Daytona Beach (2005); FAA ATP Certificate; type-rated on the Boeing 737.
