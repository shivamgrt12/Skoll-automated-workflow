About the teacher

I am Daichi, a working airline pilot who has trained in kumidaiko for years and teaches at the dojo on the evenings I am home from a trip. I came up through the ensemble tradition, and I care most about timing, posture, and the listening between players. My students range from first time beginners to performers preparing for the stage.

Lessons run out of the dojo on Northeast Eighth Street. The house drums and the practice space are provided. You bring soft shoes and a willingness to make noise.

What we work on

  • Base patterns and the steady ji underneath
  • Stance, the bachi grip, and a clean strike
  • Call and answer, trading, and ensemble locking
  • Kiai, the cut, and finishing together
