What you will learn

Classroom theory is kept short. Most of the weekend is spent in the field with rescue gear, reading the snow under the guidance of a Level 3 forecaster.

1

Read the terrain

Spot avalanche slopes by angle, shape, and how they connect above you.

2

Understand the snowpack

Dig a pit, run simple stability tests, and find the weak layer that matters.

3

Plan with the advisory

Turn the daily hazard forecast into a route with a real margin of safety.

4

Companion rescue

Beacon search, probe, and strategic shoveling, practiced until it is automatic.

5

Group decisions

Make calls as a party, manage spacing, and speak up when something looks wrong.

6

Build a habit

Leave with a routine you can repeat on every tour for the rest of the winter.
