# Memory: Jeff Peterson

## Personal Profile
Jeff Peterson is a senior ski patroller and avalanche forecaster at Teton Summit Resort, and one of three certified Avalanche Level 3 forecasters on staff. He holds a BS in Geology from High Plains State University. He is fearless but not reckless, calculating risk constantly and then committing fully, with a single-task intensity that does not pretend to multitask. His body is his primary tool, and fitness is non-negotiable. His spirituality is rooted in the mountains rather than in religion; the Tetons are his church. He is a team player who believes ego gets people killed, the calm person everyone looks to when things go wrong, and understated to the point of discomfort with praise or attention. He carries the weight of the 2023 avalanche season, when he lost a colleague, and he processes it through journaling and time in the mountains rather than medication.

## Work & Projects
Senior patroller and avalanche forecaster at Teton Summit Resort. Winter season runs mid-November through mid-April, full-time. Shift is a 6:00 AM report, route open by 8:30 AM, off by 4:30 PM. The team is 28 paid patrollers, 15 volunteers, and patrol director Jake Tanner. Summer role is trail maintenance crew lead, Teton County search and rescue volunteer, and occasional climbing guide for Summit Alpine Guides.
- **Avalanche mitigation**: Co-authors the daily hazard advisory with Jake Tanner. Handles explosives work for Highway 22 mitigation and resort route opening, including the 105mm recoilless rifle. Season incident log: 3 avalanche involvements, 14 traumatic injuries, 2 helicopter evacuations. A February 14 in-bounds avalanche on Cody Bowl fully buried one skier; Jeff led the extraction and the patient survived.
- **Avy 1 public courses**: Teaches two-day field courses in the Teton Pass area through a resort partnership. Course fee is $375 per student, revenue split with the resort 60/40, max 15 students. Uses a standard gear checklist template the assistant built, now used for every outing.
- **Denali expedition planning**: Comparing guide services, Denali Mountain Guides versus Summit Peak Expeditions, for a 2027 attempt.
- **Certifications**: CPR and Wilderness First Responder recertification due in October, before the season opens; WFR renewal is $650.
- Pro deal accounts with Summit Gear, Black Diamond, Dynafit, Ortovox, and Smith Optics.

## Health & Wellness
Jeff is in peak physical condition, required for patrol, with an annual fitness test in November. Resting heart rate is 48 bpm and Garmin estimates VO2 max around 55. He runs 25 to 30 miles a week in summer and ski tours 15,000-plus vertical feet a week in winter, with strength training three times a week at Snow King Fitness focused on legs and core for patrol carries. He tore his right ACL in 2021, reconstructed and fully recovered, and wears a brace for hard skiing. He takes magnesium glycinate 400mg nightly, vitamin D 4000 IU for the long winters, and an iron supplement since he tends low. His annual pre-season physical is in October with Dr. Amy Oakes, who handles patrol physicals. He sleeps 9:00 PM to 4:45 AM during season for the early patrol report, and 10:00 PM to 6:00 AM off-season. He uses journaling and mountain time as therapy, no medication, and considered it after the 2023 avalanche season when he lost a colleague. Powder is healthy and due for a vet check with Dr. Sarah Vance at Jackson Animal Hospital.

## Home & Living
A rented 2-bedroom, 1-bath cabin of about 900 square feet, ten minutes outside Jackson toward Teton Village. The landlord is Bill Hensley, a family friend and former patroller, who charges $1,800 a month against a market rate north of $2,800. A detached converted shed stores skis, climbing rack, avalanche gear, and bikes. Powder has a dog door to the fenced backyard, essential when both Jeff and Cody are working. A 4-by-8 raised garden bed grows tomatoes and herbs only, given altitude and frost challenges. They cut and split three cords of their own firewood over October weekends, and a wood stove supplements baseboard electric heat. Starlink was installed last fall at $120 a month after years of spotty internet. The truck and Subaru both park outside; there is no garage and no smart-home system beyond a single battery doorbell camera, since Jeff prefers simple. Jeff clears the roof himself after heavy snow.

## Devices & Services
A MacBook Air at the cabin, an iPhone 15 Pro always in a waterproof case, and a Garmin inReach Mini 2 for the backcountry. A Garmin Fenix 7X for all outdoor activities. He drives a 2021 Subaru Crosstrek with studded tires in winter and avalanche gear always in the back. He uses Strava for training tracking and Gaia GPS for backcountry navigation. He uses Spotify but no video streaming services, since he reads or sleeps in the evenings.
