# Memory: Grace Hatfield

## Personal Profile
- **Heritage**: Fifth-generation Upstate New York native, Anglo-American, raised in rural Franklin County near Malone, family rooted since the 1880s.
- **Family lineage**: Railroad workers on the paternal side and farmers on the maternal side, grew up Methodist, attends church sporadically now.
- **Education**: Franklin Central High School 2014, BA Political Science and Public Policy from Pembrook College 2018, JD with honors from Thornfield 2022.
- **Gap year**: Worked as a paralegal at Franklin County Legal Services from 2018 to 2019, saving for law school tuition and gaining courtroom exposure.
- **Motivation**: Decided at 16 to pursue law after watching grandmother's neighbors lose farms through bank foreclosures and opaque legal paperwork.
- **Personality**: Sharp-minded, strategically fierce, competitive, carries dry humor, moves between courtroom and quilting table treating both with equal seriousness.
- **Status**: Single and not actively looking, one of few Franklin County-raised attorneys working rural legal aid in the region currently.

## Work & Projects
- **Employer**: Staff attorney at Keystone Legal Aid, nonprofit rural poverty and civil rights firm in Syracuse with six attorneys and three paralegals.
- **Focus areas**: Housing, public benefits, rural healthcare access, and environmental justice with eight active cases on the current caseload.
- **Lead case**: Franklin County housing class action challenging discriminatory eviction practices affecting 2,400 low-income tenants, complex federal matter years from resolution.
- **Other matters**: Water rights dispute, religious freedom case, two individual civil rights cases, and consulting on three smaller active matters.
- **Salary**: $68,000 per year after declining a $30,000 higher offer from Greystone & March LLP straight out of law school.
