# Memory: Justin Rivera

## Personal Profile

Justin Rivera grew up in Bridgeport, CT, in a working-class Puerto Rican family. His parents, Harold and Gloria Rivera, still live there. He was the kid who took things apart to see how they worked and put most of them back together. He earned a BS in Information Technology from Crestfield University in 2008, where he met DeShawn Parker, his closest friend, as a freshman roommate. He speaks English natively and Spanish conversationally (family context, not professional).

He is methodical to the point of meditation. He approaches problems like network diagrams: map the inputs, outputs, dependencies, and failure points before touching anything. This is not rigidity - it is how he manages a world that can feel noisy. Security is a worldview for him, not a job function. He checks the locks twice, manages the family passwords on a hardware key manager, and once spent a Saturday afternoon teaching his son about phishing with the intensity of a man teaching knife safety.

He is an introvert who performs extroversion at cost. He coaches Little League, manages a team at work, and shows up to Patricia's social obligations when asked. But the recharge is non-negotiable: strategy games and the occasional multiplayer session after the house goes quiet. He is funny in a way that sneaks up on you - dry, flat, delivered three seconds before the punchline lands.
