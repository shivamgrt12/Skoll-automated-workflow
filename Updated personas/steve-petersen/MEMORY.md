# Memory: Steve Petersen Jr.

## Personal Profile
- **Full name**: Steve Petersen Jr., called Steve by everyone; "Junior" only from old mill hands, never "Stevie"
- **Heritage**: Polish-American by culture; the Petersen name was Anglicized from Pietrzykowski a generation before Ellis Island
- **Upbringing**: Grew up on dumplings, sausage, and mill rhythms; his father Steve Sr. worked the steel before him
- **Character**: Proud without arrogance, sentimental beneath a gruff exterior, deeply loyal to family and union brothers
- **Demeanor**: Moves with unhurried economy from thirty-two years on his feet; wastes neither a motion nor a sentence
- **Teaching nature**: Patient and generous with students who try, but carries a short fuse for laziness or excuses
- **Faith**: Lifelong Catholic, Sunday Mass at St. Michael's is non-negotiable, Father Nowak is his spiritual anchor
- **Union identity**: A union man in his bones, quietly emotional about Pittsburgh labor history and what was lost
- **Education**: High school diploma from Southside Catholic Academy (1985), Welding Technology certificate from Monongahela Valley Technical College (1986), and Industrial Safety Instructor certification from Community College of Allegheny County (2019)
- **Core belief**: Physical work is honorable, teaching young people the worth of their hands is his second calling
- **On grief**: Carries Babs's loss daily without letting it stop him; believes discipline means showing up anyway
- **On family**: His son's sobriety is a daily miracle he protects by making room rather than making noise

## Key Relationships
- **Barbara "Babs" Petersen (late wife)**: Born 1967, died October 2022 of pancreatic cancer at 55. Married 33 years (1989 to 2022). A school secretary at Southside Catholic Academy and the organizer of the family. Her absence is the defining feature of his daily life; he still catches himself setting out two coffee mugs.
- **Linda Petersen-Chen (daughter)**: Born March 8, 1993; age 33. ICU registered nurse at Monongahela Valley Medical Center. Married to Kevin Chen. The capable, organized one who set up his phone and manages his appointments and investments. Sunday dinner every week.
- **Steve Petersen III (son)**: Born November 22, 1995; age 30. Electrician with Allegheny Valley Electrical Contractors. Sober since December 2024 after the drinking that followed his mother's death; Steve got him into a union assistance program. The relationship is rebuilding, and Steve is proud but watchful.
- **Kevin Chen (son-in-law)**: Age 35. IT consultant at Keystone Digital Solutions, married to Linda. Patient with Steve's technology questions; won him over with genuine interest in woodworking.
- **Emma Chen (granddaughter)**: Born October 3, 2019; age 6. Linda and Kevin's daughter. In dance classes; Steve attends her recitals.
- **Jack Chen (grandson)**: Born December 1, 2022; age 3. Linda and Kevin's son, who falls asleep on Steve's shoulder during the third quarter.
- **Mary Petersen (mother)**: Born January 30, 1944; age 82. Lives at Greenfield Senior Living in Mt. Lebanon. Sharp mentally, mobility declining since a 2024 hip replacement. Steve visits every Thursday; she still corrects his dumpling technique.
- **Rose Petersen-Barrett (sister)**: Born January 17, 1971; age 55. Lives in Squirrel Hill and splits their mother's care and assisted-living costs with Steve.
- **Donnie Brezinski (best friend)**: Born October 5, 1965; age 60. Retired millwright, friends since their 1986 apprenticeship. Bowling, monthly poker, and Steelers games. His wife Carol is well, an asymmetry Steve quietly feels.
- **Father Stanislaw Nowak**: Age 71. Pastor at St. Michael's, has known the family since Steve's wedding. The person Steve turns to for grief and the big questions.
- **Ray Guzman (colleague)**: Age 52. Fellow trainer at Three Rivers Trade Academy who teaches electrical work; Dominican-American, a fellow blue-collar man in a white-collar world.

## Work & Projects
- **Current role**: Vocational Trainer in Welding, Metalworking, and Industrial Safety at Three Rivers Trade Academy since 2019
- **Academy location**: Three Rivers Trade Academy in Pittsburgh's Strip District, running rolling 16-week adult certification programs
- **Teaching schedule**: Monday through Thursday, 8 AM to 3 PM, with Fridays reserved for planning and curriculum work
- **Cohort structure**: Programs serve adults 18 to 30 in welding, electrical, HVAC, plumbing, and industrial maintenance trades
- **Recent cohort**: January 2026 class of 14 completed welding certification; standout Marcus Williams placed through Steve III's referral
- **Next cycle**: October 2026 cohort start, currently between cycles and focused on curriculum and program planning work
- **Former career**: Thirty-two years as a steelworker at Allegheny River Steel Works from 1986 to 2018
- **Union membership**: USW Local 1437 member since 1986, serving on the Retiree Advisory Board since 2019
- **Board duties**: Monthly meetings on the second Wednesday, mentoring younger members, and advocating on retiree health benefits
- **Current advocacy**: Leading a letter-writing campaign against proposed increases to retiree health-insurance co-pays

## Finance
- **Pension income**: Receives $3,200 per month from the Allegheny River Steel Works plan after thirty-two years vested
- **Training salary**: Earns $42,000 per year from Three Rivers Trade Academy on a part-time teaching schedule
- **Social Security**: Not yet collecting benefits; plans to start drawing at either age 62 or 65
- **Total annual income**: Roughly $80,400 combined from pension and academy salary before Social Security begins
- **Home ownership**: 3BR/1.5BA brick row house on the South Side, purchased 1991, mortgage fully paid off 2021
- **Retirement savings**: About $125,000 across a mill 401k rollover and a Vanguard IRA that Linda set up
- **Liquid savings**: $22,000 at Pittsburgh Federal Credit Union and approximately $3,400 in his checking account
- **Monthly outlays**: Groceries $350, woodworking supplies $80 to $120, bowling $40, and $900 toward mother's care
- **Season tickets**: Steelers tickets run $2,400 per year, split evenly with his best friend Donnie Brezinski
- **Insurance**: Retiree coverage through the union supplemental to Medicare Part A, dental through the academy
- **Spending style**: Depression-era frugal by inheritance and choice, saves more than he spends, pays cash when possible

## Health & Wellness
- **General condition**: Solid health for his age but carrying the accumulated wear of decades of physical labor
- **Shoulder injury**: Chronic right rotator-cuff strain from overhead welding, managed with ibuprofen and PT exercises when flaring
- **Hearing loss**: Mild occupational loss from mill noise; wears ReSound hearing aids fitted in 2024, mostly remembers them
- **Blood pressure**: Elevated at 140/88, on lisinopril since 2023, actively working on cutting back sodium intake
- **Knee arthritis**: Both knees affected, worse in cold weather; manages with glucosamine supplements and regular stretching
- **Primary care**: Annual physical at Southside Medical Associates with Dr. Joseph Petrosky, last visit February 2026
- **Dental care**: Every six months at Mon Valley Dental Group, last visit December 2025, covered through the academy
- **Grief and mental health**: Grieving Babs but functional; completed six months of counseling at St. Michael's in 2023
- **Ongoing worry**: Steve III's recovery is a private concern; holidays and mid-October remain the hardest days each year
- **Halloween**: Hands out candy with Emma and Jack at Linda's each October 31
- **Father's Day**: Brunch with Linda and Steve III each June
- **Exercise routine**: Walks one to two miles most days, bowls weekly, fishes, and stays active in the workshop
- **Diet restriction**: Told to reduce sodium, no food allergies; gave up hard liquor in solidarity with Steve III's sobriety
- **Sleep pattern**: Roughly 10:30 PM to 6 AM, sleeps on Babs's side of the bed, occasional insomnia near October

## Interests & Hobbies
- **Woodworking**: Builds furniture, birdhouses, cutting boards, and rocking horses for grandchildren in his sacred garage workshop
- **Craft fair sales**: Sells hand-built pieces at the St. Michael's craft fair and through his Etsy shop online
- **Steelers football**: Section 527 season-ticket holder since 1991, watches every game, knows the roster back to Steel Curtain
- **Bowling league**: Tuesday nights at Liberty Lanes with the Mon Valley Rollers, averages 182, loves the camaraderie
- **Fishing trips**: Bass and walleye at Pymatuning Lake or the Allegheny River, the place his mind finally rests
- **Polish-American cooking**: Learned dumplings, sauerkraut, stuffed cabbage, and beet soup from his mother Mary's handwritten recipes
- **Union history**: Reads labor-history books and watches documentaries about the steel era, more grief than nostalgia
- **Monthly poker**: Low-stakes game with a $20 buy-in at Donnie's house with a circle of retired friends

## Home & Living
- **Property**: Brick row house on Pittsburgh's South Side, 3BR/1.5BA, purchased in 1991 with mortgage paid off 2021
- **Garage workshop**: The best room in the house with his father's 1968 workbench, hand tools, table saw, and band saw
- **Workshop details**: Pegboard-hung tools, router table, a radio tuned to WDVE, and his father's hard hat on a shelf
- **Kitchen**: Holds his mother's handwritten dumpling recipe, Babs's mother's cast-iron skillet, and a 12-cup Mr. Coffee
- **Living room**: La-Z-Boy recliner, 65-inch TV from Kevin, Steelers memorabilia, and the last photo with Babs from 2022
- **Bedroom**: Babs's side is mostly unchanged, her reading glasses still on the nightstand, a quiet memorial he keeps
- **Drive times**: Ten minutes to the academy, fifteen to St. Michael's, twenty to Linda's, twenty-five to his mother's

## Devices & Services

- 2018 Ford F-150: the workhorse for lumber, fishing gear, and grandkids; bed kept clean, cab cleaner.
- No smartwatch, fitness tracker, or tablet, by choice.

## Preferences
- **Reading**: The Mon Valley Herald daily on actual paper, woodworking magazines (Timber Craft Journal, Workshop Monthly), union newsletters, and the occasional Dale Prescott or Mark Thorne thriller; reads slowly and intentionally.
- **Music**: Classic rock and blue-collar anthems, classic country for holidays, oldies in the garage, and Christmas music from Thanksgiving to New Year's, a tradition he kept from Babs.
- **Food and drink**: Meat and potatoes at heart; Polish-American home cooking, year-round grilling, Folgers black drip (three cups before 8 AM), Iron City or Yuengling beer, and no hard liquor. Babs's chicken stew is the comfort food he keeps chasing.
- **TV**: Steelers first, then Penguins and Pirates, "Jeopardy!" at 7 PM (still, the way he watched it with Babs), History Channel, and woodworking clips.
- **Spending**: Loyal to local businesses, Giant Eagle for groceries, Home Depot and the East Carson Street hardware store for the shop; buys tools one at a time with deliberation.
- **Sensory and comfort**: Runs warm and keeps the bedroom cool, runs the TV lower than visitors expect since the hearing aids, and is restored most by a Saturday workshop morning with the radio low.
