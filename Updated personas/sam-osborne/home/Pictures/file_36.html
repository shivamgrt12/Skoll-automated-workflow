Process

How a piece is made

  1. Select and cut the stoneRough from Rio Grande Jewelry Supply or a flea-market table, cut to a cabochon and matched to a sketch.
  2. Fabricate the sterlingSheet and wire cut, formed, and soldered at the torch under the ventilation hood.
  3. Set the stoneBezel work, the slow careful part where patience earns its keep.
  4. Stamp and patinaHand stamps, oxidation, then the maker's mark goes on last.
  5. Tumble, polish, photographFinal finish and a photo for the listing before the piece ships.
Capabilities

What the bench can do

Price bands by line

Techniques

Heavy-gauge fabrication, hand stamping, overlay, bezel stone setting, oxidation and patina, and lapidary cabochon cutting. All hand work, no casting house.

Materials

Sterling silver sheet and wire, and hand-cut Southwestern stone: turquoise, agate, coral inlay, and the occasional spiny oyster. Stone sourced regionally and at the flea market.

Where it shows

Turquoise Trail Gallery in Santa Fe and Red Earth Gallery in Sedona carry the work on consignment. The studio shop holds six active listings year round.
