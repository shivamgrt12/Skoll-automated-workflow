# Memory: Sam Osborne

## Personal Profile

Sam Osborne lives on the eastern edge of Gallup, New Mexico with his daughter Kai and an Australian Shepherd named Copper. He is a senior detective with the McKinley County Sheriff's Office and the sole maker behind Osborne Metalworks, a sterling and stone studio in the converted garage. He was widowed in 2022 when Sarah died of ovarian cancer, and he is raising Kai through high school. He is plain-spoken, observant, and patient, with a dry humour delivered deadpan.

## Work & Projects

Sam has spent 22 years with the McKinley County Sheriff's Office, the last 16 as a detective in the Criminal Investigations Unit. He carries badge 1847 and drives the department-issued 2023 Chevrolet Tahoe PPV. The current caseload runs eight active files, two of them high-priority, with jurisdiction across McKinley and San Juan counties and major-crimes coordination through the state bureau. Osborne Metalworks runs out of the converted garage with a jeweller's bench, ventilation hood, torch, and tumbler. Specialties are heavy sterling cuff bracelets ($400-$800), bolo ties ($250-$500), and belt buckles ($600-$1,200). Galleries are Turquoise Trail in Santa Fe (Angela Ruiz) and Red Earth in Sedona. The Etsy shop holds six listings and averages three to five sales a month at roughly $450 each. Annual studio revenue runs about $18,000.
