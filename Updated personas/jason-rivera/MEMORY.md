# Memory: Jason Rivera

## Personal Profile

Jason was born and raised in Gaziantep, Turkey, the kebab capital, and that origin shows up in everything he does. He studied hospitality management at Marmara University in Istanbul for two years before leaving to pursue restaurant work and emigrate. He opened the original Anatolia Kebab House on Main Street in Paterson in 2012 with $40,000 scraped together from a decade of 80-hour weeks in other people's kitchens, and he still works the grill himself on Friday and Saturday nights. He is quality-obsessed (hand-selected lamb, his own ground Adana spice blend, the 90-minute drive to a halal slaughterhouse in Pennsylvania), quietly generous (the mosque food pantry every night, three refugees taught the kitchen), strategically patient (nine years before the second location), family-first traditionalist (Hatice in the house, Sunday kahvalti non-negotiable), and a community pillar known as "Jason abi" across the Paterson Turkish-American community. He is also health-avoidant about his own diabetes and blood pressure, takes the medications because Ayse fills the organizer, and does not slow down until the restaurants make him.
