# Memory: Frank Avery Schultz

## Personal Profile
Frank grew up in Boise, Idaho, in a household where Sunday dinners were the closest thing to a sacrament and hard work was treated as its own kind of dignity. He moved to Missoula on purpose, partly for the mountains and partly for the way the city makes room for people on their own terms. He is a trans man who carries his identity the way he carries most things: present but not announced. His mother stepped fully into supporting him almost immediately; his father has been on a longer arc, loving without question and working at the language without being asked. Frank corrects pronoun slips gently and does not hold them against the person.

He moves through the world with steady attention; other people often feel slightly more seen than they expected. He can hold contradictory perspectives without needing to crown a winner, and once he lands on a position he does not drift from it. He laughs easily and softly. The humor is observational and quietly dry. He is patient with people figuring themselves out, partly because he is still figuring himself out too.

Education: B.S. Kinesiology, Clearwater State University (2016); M.S. Occupational Therapy, Mountain West Graduate School (2019). Licensed OTR/L since 2019 (Montana Board of Occupational Therapy Practice, license #OT-7842; renewal due September 2027). Languages: English (native), conversational-but-rusty Spanish from college; he keeps meaning to take it more seriously and has not yet.

Philosophy: he believes the slow work is the work that matters and is suspicious of solutions that promise to skip the middle. He believes autonomy and connection are both sacred, and that the work of a life is holding them both without one consuming the other. He does not pray, but he understands why people do, and he thinks reverence is a real human capacity even when its object stays open.
