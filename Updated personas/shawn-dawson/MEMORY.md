# Memory: Shawn Dawson

## Personal Profile
- **Full name**: Shawn "Terry" Dawson, known to everyone as Terry, fourth-generation Detroit auto worker on his father's side.
- **Heritage**: Black, AME by upbringing, Baptist through his marriage to Denise, deeply rooted in Detroit's community.
- **Community standing**: Known across the neighborhood, church, and union hall, holding a chair at Local 247 for nine years.
- **Temperament**: Measured and unhurried, learned early that the loudest voice in a room is rarely the most useful.
- **Private habits**: Processes stress through late-night journaling in composition notebooks, has filled close to forty that Denise has never opened.
- **Intellectual interests**: Carries Black labor history with quiet seriousness, reads the 1967 uprising and King biographies every winter.
- **Core tension**: Holds two unreconciled convictions that the church teaches forgiveness and the union teaches accountability, owing both equally.
- **Loyalty to Detroit**: Twice offered a regional union role in Indianapolis, turned it down because the city made him and still does.
- **Education**: Associate's degree in Industrial Technology from Lakefield Community College (1994), AAW Labor Studies certificate from Briarwood (2016).

## Key Relationships
- **Denise Dawson**, born February 8, 1976 (age 50). Wife, married October 18, 1997 (29 years). School counselor at Crestwood Middle School, organized and warm, the logistical brain of the household. She plans, he executes. He is openly affectionate with her in a way his colleagues would not believe.
- **Jasmine Dawson**, born December 3, 2001 (age 24). Daughter. Marketing coordinator at Foxpoint Innovations in Chicago, independent and ambitious. Calls home every Sunday evening without fail. Terry is proud but misses her being close.
- **Marcus Dawson**, born March 22, 2005 (age 21). Son. Junior at Ashvale State University studying kinesiology, easygoing and athletic, on a partial scholarship. Terry worries he is not taking academics seriously enough.
- **Ruth Dawson**, born January 15, 1948 (age 78). Mother. Retired cafeteria worker, lives alone on Greenview Street, fiercely independent and refuses to move. Terry visits three times a week.
- **Pastor James Caldwell**, 63. Senior pastor at Greater Hope Baptist for 20 years, a mentor figure. They sometimes disagree on church modernization.
- **Darnell "Big D" Robinson**, born October 30, 1972 (age 53). Best friend. Maintenance supervisor at Irongate, has known Terry since high school, loud and loyal. They are each other's sounding board for everything.
- **Carla Simmons**, 48. Local 247 vice president, a fierce negotiator who works closely with Terry on contract talks. They respect each other but clash on strategy.
- **Raymond Okafor**, 44. Softball assistant coach and high school gym teacher. Handles practice logistics while Terry focuses on game strategy.
- **Linda Marsh**, 57. Greater Hope church secretary for 15 years, knows everything about everyone, indispensable to Terry's deacon work.
- **Dr. William Tate**, 55. Primary doctor, internal medicine at Meridian Medical Associates, Terry's physician for twelve years.

## Work & Projects
- **Current role**: Senior assembler on the chassis line at Irongate Motor Assembly Plant, working 6:00 AM to 2:30 PM Monday through Friday.
- **Career history**: Started on the trim line in 1996, promoted to chassis line over thirty years on the plant floor.
- **Union steward**: Nine-year steward for Local 247 of the Allied Auto Workers, handling grievances, disciplinary hearings, and contract negotiations.
- **Active caseload**: Manages eight to twelve grievances at any time, currently carries a high-profile wrongful-termination case for a 20-year employee.
- **Contract fight**: January 2027 contract expiration, management proposes 12% healthcare premium increase, union counters with 3% maximum.
- **Mentoring**: Mentors three younger shop stewards, maintains a professional but tense relationship with plant manager Helen Gruber during negotiations.
- **Church role**: Deacon at Greater Hope Baptist for seven years, sits on the board, visits sick congregants, coordinates community outreach, and helps plan the church's 75th anniversary celebration and the annual picnic at Rouge Park.
- **Coaching**: Coaches the Westside Wolverines in the Detroit Metro Parks League, practices Tuesday and Thursday, games Saturday mornings April through September, and organizes the end-of-season banquet.

## Finance
- **Base salary**: $72,000/year at Irongate plus typically $8,000 to $12,000 in annual overtime on the chassis line.
- **Denise's salary**: $61,000/year as a school counselor, combined household income roughly $141,000 to $145,000 per year.
- **Mortgage**: $1,450/month on the three-bedroom bungalow, owes roughly $68,000, nearing full payoff within a few years.
- **Truck payment**: $485/month on a 2024 Ford F-150, owes approximately $22,000 on the remaining loan balance.
- **College support**: Contributes $400/month to Marcus's college expenses beyond his partial scholarship at Ashvale State University.
- **Ruth's support**: Supplemental expenses for his mother run roughly $300/month, never discussed openly, it simply goes.
- **Retirement savings**: Roughly $280,000 in the union pension plan plus $45,000 in a personal IRA at current value.
- **Emergency fund**: $12,000 held at Lakeside Federal Credit Union for household emergencies and unexpected repair costs.
- **Tithing**: Tithes 10% of take-home pay to Greater Hope, cash or debit only, has refused credit cards for twenty years.

## Health & Wellness
- **Right knee**: Meniscus surgery two years ago, still stiff in cold weather, wears a brace during coaching and practice.
- **Cholesterol**: Borderline high, takes atorvastatin 20mg daily, Dr. Tate has advised him to reduce red meat intake.
- **Acid reflux**: Mild, managed with omeprazole as needed, avoids spicy food, keeps a bottle of Tums in the truck.
- **Primary physician**: Followed by Dr. William Tate at Meridian Medical Associates for ongoing care and annual physical checkups.
- **Exercise routine**: Walks two miles three times a week for cardiovascular health, with Denise holding him accountable on consistency.
- **Stress patterns**: Carries tension in his shoulders and neck, gets headaches during negotiation periods, sleeps about six hours most nights.

## Interests & Hobbies
- **Softball**: Played since age twelve, now coaches the Wolverines, keeps scoring books going back four seasons and detailed lineup spreadsheets.
- **Barbecue**: Smokes and grills on "Old Faithful," a custom smoker a plant friend welded five years ago, brisket is the specialty, hosts the annual Labor Day cookout.
- **Woodworking**: Builds birdhouses, picture frames, and small furniture in the garage workshop, meditative and private, has never sold a piece.
- **Journaling**: Writes late at night in composition notebooks, close to forty filled, never rereads them, stored in the bedroom closet.
- **Reading**: Reads labor and civil-rights history in winter, drawn to the 1967 uprising narratives and King biographies.
- **Gardening**: Tends tomatoes, peppers, and collard greens in the backyard, gives produce to neighbors throughout the growing season.
- **Dominoes**: Plays with Darnell, Pete, and Carlos, a long-standing social tradition among the group that runs deep.

## Home & Living
- **Home**: Three-bedroom brick bungalow on Greenview Street in northwest Detroit, purchased 22 years ago, nearly paid off.
- **Workshop**: Garage converted to a woodworking shop, with the smoker "Old Faithful" positioned just outside the garage door.
- **Garden**: Small but productive backyard vegetable garden with tomatoes, peppers, and collard greens, neighbors come by every August.
- **Household roles**: Denise maintains the home interior and manages decor, Terry handles all outdoor maintenance, repairs, and yard work.
- **Ruth's house**: Sits a 12-minute drive away on Greenview Street, Terry mows her lawn and checks in during growing season.

## Preferences
- Southern comfort food is the foundation: collard greens, mac and cheese, cornbread, fried catfish. Sweet potato pie is his weakness, made by Denise every holiday.
- Black coffee in the morning at 5:15 AM, sweet tea with dinner. Not much of a drinker, an occasional beer at games, never more than two.
- Shops Meijer for groceries, Kelford Hardware for the workshop, and the small grocery on Greenview for produce. Loyal to local businesses on principle.
- Practical, durable clothing: Carhartt jackets, Dickies work pants, steel-toed boots. Denise picks his church clothes. One navy suit for funerals, weddings, and arbitration.
- Listens to gospel, Motown, and old-school R&B, and Detroit sports talk radio on the commute. Watches the Tigers and Lions religiously.
- Travels little and on purpose: an annual fishing trip to a cabin near Cheboygan, weekend drives to Chicago for Jasmine, holidays in Lansing. Christmas Eve service and family gathering is the anchor of the holiday season. Hosts Mother's Day and Father's Day dinners for the family. A Charleston anniversary trip has been almost-planned for two years.
