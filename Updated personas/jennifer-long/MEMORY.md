# Memory: Jennifer Long

## Personal Profile
Jennifer Long is a pediatric occupational therapist and the sole owner of Long Pediatric Therapy in Tacoma. She holds a Master of Occupational Therapy from the University of Puget Sound, earned in 2006. She is patient and deeply attuned, reading children's cues before they can put needs into words, and observant enough to catch small changes in motor patterns that others miss. A creative problem-solver, she is always prototyping new therapy games and tools. She has learned over years of practice to be empathetic without being drained, setting healthy boundaries. She is organized and systematic, color-codes everything, and runs her life on routines. She values team-based care and is a lifelong learner who reads journals and follows the neuroscience of sensory processing.

## Work & Projects
Sole occupational therapist at Long Pediatric Therapy, which she founded in 2015 after nine years in early-intervention and school-based pediatric OT following her 2006 degree. The practice runs from a leased 900 sq ft suite in Tacoma's Proctor District at $1,800 per month. Hours are Monday to Friday, 8:30 AM to 4:30 PM, with a caseload of 22 to 25 forty-five-minute sessions per week. Clients are ages 2 to 12, primarily sensory processing disorder, fine motor delays, and handwriting. Marta Gonzalez is part-time admin. Insurance panels are Premera, Regence, Tricare, and Pacific Health Partners at roughly $135 per session; cash-pay clients are $165 per session, about 20 percent of caseload. The waitlist holds 6 families at an average 3-week wait, and Jennifer is considering hiring a second OT. Annual CE requirement is 20 hours by December, with 8 completed. A challenging case is Liam, a 4-year-old referred by Dr. Mitchell with severe sensory avoidance, making slow progress on a desensitization protocol. Therapy supply budget is $200 per month. Business insurance renews in November through Pacific Professional Insurance.
