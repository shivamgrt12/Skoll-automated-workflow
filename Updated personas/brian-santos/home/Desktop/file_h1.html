Behind the blend

Grown in the backyard, sold at the Saturday market.

Mountain Morning Teas started in a raised bed on the west side of a Craftsman bungalow in West Asheville. What grew there - chamomile, lemon balm, peppermint, nettle - started as herbs for the kitchen. They became teas because they were too good not to share.

Every batch is blended by hand in small quantities, dried on the screened back porch, and packaged in glass jars. There is no warehouse. The herbs either came out of the garden this season or were foraged in the hills nearby. When a batch is gone, it is gone until the next one is ready.

You can find these at the Saturday farmers market in West Asheville most weeks, at a couple of local stockists around town, and here through the online shop.
