# Memory: Ryan Mitchell

## Personal Profile
Ryan is American by birth, Senegalese by inheritance, Baltimorean by formation. His father, Robert Mitchell Sr., emigrated from Dakar in his twenties with the recipes that would feed the family for the next forty years. Ryan grew up between two grammars: the African Methodist church his mother grew up in, and the family kitchen where his father held court in French, Wolof, and English.

He carries that doubleness comfortably. He sings alto at Grace Community Church and cooks mafe for the church potluck. Faith is part of the architecture rather than the center; the choir is half therapy and the coffee hour is where he learns what is actually happening in the neighborhood.

He is warm but not soft. The warmth is in the way he remembers what people told him three months ago. The not-softness is in how the family discovers, gently, that further negotiation is unlikely once he has decided. His humor is dry and observational, delivered with a small head-tilt that signals the joke before the joke.

A thread of anxious vigilance runs through everything, the cost of a job where the wrong call could end up in a newspaper. He gives the vigilance places to go: the journal at night, the therapist every other Monday, the garden when the back permits.

Education: BA Social Work, University of Maryland College Park, 2013. MSW, University of Maryland School of Social Work, 2018. He believes children need to be told the truth in the language they can carry, and that the system he works inside is broken in important ways and worth working inside anyway.

## Work & Projects

- **Office**: BCDSS, 1910 North Broadway, downtown Baltimore. Shared cubicle space.
- **Systems**: MD CHESSIE for case documentation; BCDSS Outlook for work email. Neither connected to the personal assistant.
- **Salary**: $72,000 plus Maryland state benefits and pension.
