# Memory: Chris James Murray

## Personal Profile

- **DOB**: November 12, 1981
- **Heritage**: Mexican American, Catholic, shaped by his mother's kitchen through food, Spanish, family ritual, and farewell traditions.
- **Dia de los Muertos**: Annual altar setup on November 1 with family photos, marigolds, and pan de muerto, honoring family heritage and the departed.
- **Thanksgiving**: Annual family meal at home with turkey, sides, and traditional dishes.
- **Christmas**: Christmas Eve evening Mass with Father Frank followed by family gathering; Christmas Day dinner and video call with Rosa, Carlos, and the Florida family.
- **High school**: Graduated from Crestview High School in Las Vegas in 1999 before entering the construction workforce.
- **Certificates**: Hospitality Management from Southern Nevada Technical College (2005) and Event Planning from Silverado Continuing Education Center (2013).
- **Spanish fluency**: Fluent in family, food, prayer, and emotional contexts, but more hesitant in formal or professional settings.
- **Core personality**: Steady on the surface, impatient with vague answers, and tender in ways he rarely advertises to others.
- **Private tension**: Presents as someone who can always make it work, while privately resenting how often everyone assumes he will.
- **Love philosophy**: Believes love is mostly logistics: who drives, who shows up, who notices the empty fridge, who keeps promises.
- **Trust markers**: Distrusts waste, empty praise, tested confidence, and anyone who treats service workers like furniture or props.

## Work & Projects

- **Early career**: Worked construction labor in Las Vegas from 1999 to 2004, where he met his ex-husband Ernesto Morton.
- **Career pivot**: Enrolled in hospitality management after Sofia's birth in 2004, completing the certificate in 2005 for steadier hours.
- **Hotel role**: Joined the Grand Sierra Hotel housekeeping staff in 2007 and has supervised floors 12 through 18 since 2015.
- **Team scope**: Manages fourteen housekeepers with room assignments, VIP preparation, supply inventory, new-hire training, and call-out coverage.
- **Current pressure**: Training three new hires, preparing for the Q4 2026 corporate inspection, and advocating for a VIP shift differential.
- **Shift differential**: An unresolved thread he keeps polished into a business case, tired of proving harder floor work matters.
- **Event business**: Momentos by Chris is a sole proprietorship focused on Quinceaneras and milestone celebrations, planning 8 to 12 events yearly.
- **Event timeline**: Each event spans 3 to 5 months of planning, with peak seasons in early spring and fall each year.
- **Services offered**: Venue selection, decoration, vendor coordination, timeline management, and day-of coordination, with packages from $2,500 to $6,000.
- **Current clients**: Mariana Rodriguez, Camila Fuentes, and two early 2027 bookings currently in the initial planning stage.

## Finance

- **Hotel income**: Earns about $52,000 per year from the Grand Sierra Hotel, with health benefits included in the compensation package.
- **Event income**: Generates about $30,000 from Momentos by Chris events in a typical year, with seasonal variation in bookings.
- **Cookie sales**: Earns $3,000 to $4,000 annually from December Christmas cookie orders, gift boxes, and community event sales.
- **Rent**: Pays $1,650 per month for a three-bedroom apartment in Spring Valley with garage storage for event supplies.
- **Vehicle costs**: Honda Odyssey payment of $320 per month, with $140 auto insurance and $180 gas for commuting and events.
- **Household costs**: About $500 groceries, $120 family phone plan, $220 utilities, $80 church donation, and $60 for Isabella's school activities.
- **Family support**: Sends a monthly support payment to Rosa by bank transfer, a standing commitment he treats as non-negotiable.
- **Savings**: About $6,200 at Desert Palms Credit Union plus $1,800 in checking, with no debt beyond the minivan since 2022.
- **Business account**: Event business runs through a separate checking account for client deposits, vendor payments, and expense tracking.
- **Money style**: Precise and anxious, with cash envelopes for household spending, quarterly vendor price tracking, and no wasteful subscriptions.
- **Retirement gap**: Retirement savings are an unresolved problem, postponed because supporting Rosa and the kids feels non-negotiable first.

## Health & Wellness

- **General condition**: Functional but worn down by years of physical labor across construction, hotel housekeeping, and event setup.
- **Back pain**: Chronic lower back pain from lifting, bending, carts, and mattresses, managed with ibuprofen and a heating pad.
- **Knee issue**: Right knee pain after long shifts or events, managed with a compression sleeve when symptoms flare up.
- **Physical therapy**: Dr. Patricia Reeves has recommended physical therapy for the back, but Chris has not yet followed through.
- **Cholesterol**: September 2025 physical showed LDL at 155 and borderline blood pressure at 138/86, resisting statins for diet changes.
- **Dental**: Dental care overdue since February 2025, a deferred appointment he acknowledges but has not rescheduled yet.
- **Mental health**: Resilient but stretched by sole-provider pressure, supporting Rosa, divorce aftermath, and limited social life since Ernesto.
- **Therapy stance**: Has no therapist and jokes that therapy is for people with time, though the accumulated load is real.
- **Exercise**: Main activity is housekeeping floor work, supplemented by evening walks in cooler weather and kitchen dancing while cooking.
- **Sleep**: Usually sleeps early from exhaustion, with back pain sometimes waking him during the night or early morning.

## Interests & Hobbies

- **Event creativity**: Sketches decoration ideas on cleaning checklists and keeps binder pages of unseen inspiration for future Quinceanera projects.
- **Cooking**: Feels most like himself making pot roast, beef stew, cornbread, burgers, mac and cheese, casseroles, and Sunday dinners.
- **Christmas cookies**: December becomes a production season of sugar cookies, chocolate chip cookies, and gift boxes for orders and family.
- **Woodworking**: Teaching himself through YouTube videos, with half-finished garage projects including a bookshelf, planter box, and picture frame.
- **Unfinished projects**: The aspirational gap bothers him because unfinished personal work is what happens when everyone else's deadlines win.

- **Sports viewing**: Watches ESPN reruns and sports as wind-down background noise, texting running commentary with Yolanda during games.
- **Community involvement**: Helps with community association events, block parties, bake sales, and neighborhood gatherings in the apartment complex.

## Preferences

Chris likes quiet mornings before anyone is awake, strong coffee, well-made tools, punctuality, clean hotel hallways, hardware stores, serviceable clothes, earth tones, practical decor, family photos, and kitchens where he can cook. He dislikes sagging balloon arches, vendors who fail to follow up, loud restaurant music, cheap polyester tablecloths, artificial vanilla, buzzing fluorescent lights, waste, vague answers, and elaborate gratitude. He researches purchases, buys event supplies in bulk, compares vendor prices, tips service workers well, uses cash for some household categories, and cancels subscriptions quickly. Small ordinary vices: he judges other people's centerpieces too fast, buys clearance ribbon or hardware because it might become useful, and occasionally lets ESPN run late because silence would make the apartment feel too empty. He prefers driving to flying, dislikes resorts, packs efficiently, brings his own coffee, and wants someday to take the kids to the small Mexican town his grandmother came from. His music includes classic rock, country while cooking, blues, Americana, and worship music. He reads rarely for pleasure but skims ESPN, Popular Mechanics, event planning blogs, community pages, and parish updates.
