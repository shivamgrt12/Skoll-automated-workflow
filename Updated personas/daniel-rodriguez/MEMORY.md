# Memory: Daniel Rodriguez

## Personal Profile

Daniel Rodriguez is a sous chef at Linden & Rye, an upscale Southern-fusion restaurant in downtown Charleston with about thirty staff. He grew up in Houston, Texas, earned a Culinary Arts diploma from the Gulf Coast Culinary Institute in 2018, and has no college degree, which has never slowed him down. English is his native language, and he is fluent in Spanish, learned through years of study and immersion and kept alive through family and the kitchen.

He is proud of his roots without being defined by them, carrying the culture in the food, the music, and the family rhythm rather than in any performance of identity. He is restless in a productive way, always working on a new sauce, a fermentation experiment, or a sketch for a food truck concept, and the restlessness is creative energy, not anxiety. He is loyal to his crew, generous with strangers, and precise about the things that matter to him. He rents a studio apartment in the Upper King Street area and is dating Willa Chen, taking it slow because his kitchen hours make scheduling hard.

## Key Relationships

- **Willa Chen**: Girlfriend, age 27. An elementary school teacher in Charleston, met through a mutual friend at a house party. Patient about his schedule. They see each other Sundays and Monday evenings, his days off. She does not cook much, which amuses him. Early stage, good but untested by real conflict.
- **Rosa Rodriguez**: Mother, age 56. Lives in Houston with Daniel's father and works at a medical billing company. Calls Daniel every Thursday evening and sends care packages of dried chiles and spices. Proud of his career but worries he works too hard. Close in a warm, undemanding way.
- **Javier Rodriguez**: Father, age 59. A foreman at a commercial construction company in Houston. Hardworking, quiet, traditional. Supports Daniel but does not fully understand why someone would choose kitchen work, though he respects the paycheck. They talk when Rosa passes the phone.
- **Elena Rodriguez**: Sister, age 25. Lives in Austin and works as a marketing coordinator at a tech startup. Single, social, energetic. Texts Daniel constantly with memes, food photos, and Austin brunch recommendations. They are tight.
- **Chef Margaux Tillman**: Head chef and boss, age 41. Owner-chef of Linden & Rye. Exacting, respected, occasionally volatile during service. Daniel has earned her trust over two years, and she has mentioned promoting him to kitchen manager if a second location opens. A mentor, not a friend.
- **Danny Voss**: Best friend, age 29. A line cook at another Charleston restaurant. They went to culinary school together and meet for Monday evening beers. Danny is the one person Daniel bounces the food truck idea off.
- **Dr. Robert Sato**: Primary care, age 45. At Palmetto Primary Care, Charleston. Handles annual physicals and manages Daniel's asthma.

## Work & Projects

- **Role**: Sous chef at Linden & Rye, upscale Southern-fusion, downtown Charleston. Reports to Chef Margaux Tillman.
- **Prior roles**: After graduating from Gulf Coast Culinary Institute in 2018, he worked as a line cook at Southerly Table in Houston (2018 to 2020), then as a line cook at The Darling Oyster Bar in Charleston (2020 to 2022), then as a garde manger at Husk Charleston (2022 to 2024) before joining Linden & Rye.
- **Tenure**: Two years at Linden & Rye. Started as a line cook and was promoted to sous chef after ten months.
- **Schedule**: Tuesday through Saturday, 3:00 PM to midnight, often later during busy service. Sunday and Monday off.
- **Current focus**: Running the hot line during service, managing a prep team of four, and developing seasonal menu contributions. Currently testing a smoked duck taco for the spring menu; Margaux is intrigued but noncommittal. Also handling produce supplier relationships.
- **Side project**: Quietly researching food truck regulations and costs in Charleston, aiming to open a taco and torta truck within two years. Has not told Margaux. Danny knows. Keeps a business plan draft in Google Drive. Charleston requires a mobile food vendor license at about $250 per year, a fire inspection, and a commissary kitchen agreement.
- **Key work contacts**: Chef Margaux Tillman (boss), Danny Voss (friend and industry), Carlos Vega (produce supplier at Lowcountry Farms).
