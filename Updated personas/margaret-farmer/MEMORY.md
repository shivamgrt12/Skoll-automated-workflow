# Memory: Margaret Farmer

## Personal Profile

American by birth, Japanese by formation. Born in Portland, Oregon. Moved to Kyoto at age 10 with her parents, schooled in Japanese from that point. Rooting career and life in Japan was gradual, not declared; the identity feels accumulated rather than chosen.

Education: B.A. in Fine Arts (Ceramics), Kyoto Geijutsu University, 2017. Two-year apprenticeship under Toshio Saito at Shigaraki, 2017-2019.

Languages: English (native), Japanese (near-native, slight foreign-resident accent), basic French from gallery dealings. Japanese for local gallery work; English with parents, Ethan, and international galleries. Code-switching is unremarkable.

Personality: patient, deliberate, quietly stubborn. Clay does not respond to urgency; patience has become the texture of her personality. Strangers read her as quiet; collaborators read concentration. Listens before speaking; no longer obligated to fill silence.

A quiet stubbornness under the calm surprises people who underestimate her. She makes the work she wants to make. Has refused Tokyo offers conflicting with her Shibui commitment, stayed at the craft collective rather than building a more visible studio elsewhere. The career is the evidence.

Carries low-grade money anxiety since the apprenticeship years. Studio life is feast-or-famine; commissions pay slowly. Manages with disciplined budgeting learned from her mother. The household runs tighter than her gallery reputation suggests.

Cultural identity: not quite Japanese to long-time Kyoto residents, not quite American to family in Portland. Has made peace; no longer tries to bridge the gap.

Religion: loosely Christian upbringing, ethics retained without observance. Visits temples without praying, joins seasonal festivals without theological investment. The closest she has to a sacred practice is the studio at first light.

Philosophy: process is the point; materials have memory; tradition is a living practice, not a museum piece. Honouring her teacher means making her own work rather than copying his. Tells younger potters that kiln logs, glaze tests, and quiet years are the real training, and that being underestimated by people who do not know craft is sometimes useful.

## Work & Projects

- **Studio**: Independent studio at the Higashiyama Craft Collective, a shared facility with 4 potters. Individual spaces of about 25 sqm, communal gas reduction kiln, seasonal anagama wood-fire kiln used twice yearly.
- **Practice**: Wheel-thrown and hand-built vessels. Functional ware (bowls, cups, plates, vases) with occasional sculptural work. Known for wood-ash glazes and natural textures. Influenced by the Shigaraki and Arts and Crafts traditions.
- **Active projects**:
  - **"Seasonal Table" Solo Exhibition** at Shibui Gallery, Kyoto. 30-piece functional tableware collection reflecting four seasons. In production; opens autumn 2026.
  - **Galerie Terre Group Show** in Paris. 8 pieces required. Theme: "Earth and Hand." Jean-Luc coordinates five international potters; opens autumn 2026.
  - **Kyoto Craft Museum Commission**. Three large vessels for the museum's permanent collection. Prestige; prototypes underway; due end of 2026.
  - **New ash glaze series**. Wood-ash glaze line using local Kyoto mountain ash. Ongoing.
- **Sales channels**: Shibui Gallery (40% commission), Galerie Terre (50%), spring/autumn open studios, occasional online orders via the portfolio site.
- **Schedule frame**: Studio 6 days/week, Sundays usually off. Firing weeks are 3-5 day cycles with continuous kiln monitoring.
- **Kiln log**: kept since apprenticeship years. Every firing, every glaze test, every result. Closest thing she has to an autobiography of her practice. Considered whether it could become a book; in no hurry to decide.

## Interests & Hobbies

- Ceramics is no longer a hobby. Studio time, ceramics reading, and a decade of kiln logs are all the work.
- Walking: daily discipline. Temple-path loop through Higashiyama in early morning, before tourists arrive. Partly meditation, partly restoring her own scale after long studio stretches.
- Reading: ceramics journals on the work desk, essays on craft philosophy, occasional literary fiction.
- Hiking with Ethan in the Kitayama mountains on weekends. Not particularly athletic. Hikes for being above the city and long conversations that happen when two people walk side by side rather than across a table.
- Journaling: through the kiln log.

## Preferences

- **Reading**: ceramics journals (Ceramics Monthly, Studio Potter Quarterly), literary fiction, essays on craft and philosophy (William Morris, Wendell Berry). Currently reading *The Unknown Craftsman* by Soetsu Yanagi, on the bedside table two years, in no hurry to finish.
- **Music**: ambient and classical. Brian Eno, Debussy, Nils Frahm, acoustic guitar and piano for studio focus.
- **Art interests**: ceramics exhibitions and craft museums regularly. Follows contemporary American and Korean potters.
- **Aesthetic and style**: craft-Japan with a small American undertone. Well-cut linen and cotton, dark colours, leather sandals or boots properly maintained, handmade pieces from potter friends she trades with. One good kimono for formal events, one wool coat for winter. Buys quality, infrequently.
- **Travel**: prefers small towns to large cities. Japanese countryside in autumn. Specific affection for Mashiko. Twice to Korea for ceramics travel; would like to return. The long-postponed Portland trip with Daniel is for the quieter period after the autumn 2026 shows.
- **Comfort foods**: mother's beef stew, a specific way of preparing greens her mother brought from Portland decades ago. Grilled salmon (autumn favourite), fresh sourdough from the Higashiyama bakery.
- **Regular dining**: Kamo River Teahouse (weekend lunches with Ethan), Higashiyama Deli (quick lunches near the studio), The Craftsman (gastropub dinners with Mika and studio friends).
- **Cooking**: simple and seasonal. The ritual matters. Weekend cooking with Ethan is a shared pleasure.
- **Coffee and tea**: coffee daily, mid-morning and mid-afternoon, from a local roaster. Herbal tea throughout the day.
- **Shopping**: long horizon. Pays full price for clay, glaze materials, and the few good craft books she owns. Refuses full price for almost anything else. Buys clothes only when current ones wear through.
- **Gifts**: handmade where possible. A small bowl for Daniel, a specific tea cup for Carol, a piece of her own work for Ethan.
- **Sensory world**: touch-anchored. Cold weight of unworked clay, give of a properly wedged ball, slight roughness of bisqueware, cool smooth of a finished surface. Smells: wet clay, dry-mineral glaze room, pine smoke from the anagama, her mother's kitchen recreated in her own, cold mountain air on the temple path in winter. Sounds: soft hum of the electric wheel, crackle of pieces cooling, temple bells at 7 AM, Ethan's voice from the next room. Comfortable layering at home: ambient music while cooking, kitchen radio in the morning, the garden through the open window in summer. True silence makes her slightly uneasy; the studio at first light is the exception.
- **Likes**: the first hour of morning before the studio opens, the moment a firing reaches temperature, work no longer hers to control, pieces emerging from a successful firing, collectors who use what they buy, galleries that take care with display, the older women potters at the collective, a Sunday with nothing on the calendar.
- **Pet peeves**: collectors who treat handmade work as investment products, galleries that mark up too aggressively, the tone some Western visitors take on discovering ceramics is a serious discipline, "wabi-sabi" used loosely by people who have not read a book on Japanese aesthetics, "a similar one in a different colour" requests, visitors who handle pieces without asking, loud restaurants, crowded tourist craft fairs, anyone who explains Japanese culture to her in Kyoto.
