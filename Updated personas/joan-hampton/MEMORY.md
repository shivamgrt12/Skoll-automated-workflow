# Memory: Joan Hampton

## Personal Profile

Joan is an Irish citizen, born in Clifden, Co. Galway, into a family that has been in Connemara for generations. Her sense of place runs deep: the coast, the weather that changes four times before lunch, and a tight-knit community are not background to her, they are context she works within. She is calm under pressure, community-rooted, and holds a relational view of birth, treating it as a human threshold rather than a clinical procedure.

Her education is the spine of her practice. She holds a B.Sc. in Midwifery from the National University of Ireland, Galway (2015) and a Higher Diploma in Clinical Practice (Midwifery) from University College Cork (2018). Since September 2025 she has been working toward a part-time Masters in Midwifery Practice at NUIG, one module per semester. She is registered with the Nursing and Midwifery Board of Ireland (NMBI), PIN on file.

She speaks English natively, holds conversational Spanish kept up since school, and can manage basic school-level French.

## Work & Projects

- **Employer**: University Hospital Galway (UHG), HSE West, a public health system with a large maternity unit serving Galway city and county and parts of Clare and Mayo.
- **Title**: Community Midwife, permanent since 2019. Qualified 2015, worked the hospital-based labour ward 2015 to 2019 before moving to the community role.
- **Schedule pattern**: Rotational. Two weeks community (Monday to Friday home visits and clinic), one week on-call (24/7 availability, births at home and hospital). On-call weeks are unpredictable.
- **Community Birth Centre Proposal**: Lead advocate. Working with a multidisciplinary group to propose a standalone midwife-led birth centre for low-risk pregnancies. In the feasibility and consultation phase. Politically sensitive, requiring HSE and consultant buy-in. Two consultants prefer the current hospital-only model.
- **Postnatal Home Visit Pathway Redesign**: Collaborating with public health nurses to improve the postnatal visit schedule. Data-gathering phase, presenting findings to the Director of Midwifery in December 2026.
- **CPD, Advanced Neonatal Resuscitation**: Completing requirements for advanced NRP recertification, practical exam in January 2027.
- **Professional development**: Part-time Masters in Midwifery Practice at NUIG, started September 2025, one module per semester. Year 1 literature review focused on continuity of carer models, with a US comparison strand drawn from the Boston research exchange cohort.
