# Memory: Henry McMillan

## Personal Profile

Henry James McMillan is third-generation Scottish-American, raised in a Presbyterian Midwestern household where emotions were processed internally and expressed through action. His grandparents emigrated from Lanarkshire, Scotland, and the family still makes shortbread and cranachan at Christmas. He can manage about six things in Scots dialect, all food-related. His pronouns are he/him. He earned a BA in Political Science from Ridgemont University in 1992 with a teaching certificate from the same institution, then completed a Master's in Educational Leadership at Ridgemont over summers in 2005. He is unhurried, direct, and quietly principled. He treats people the way he wants his players treated: fairly, with structure, and without excuses on either side. He shows love by showing up, and he is working, slowly, on saying the things out loud that Maggie has been patient about for thirty years.

## Work & Projects

- **Status**: Active, employed full-time.
- **Title**: Head Football Coach and American Government Teacher, Westfield High School, Columbus City Schools.
- **Tenure**: 19 years as head coach at Westfield. 28 total years coaching, starting as an assistant at Ridgeview High School in 1998.
- **Early teaching years**: Taught American Government at Brookhaven High School from 1992 to 1998, the school where he had played for Bob Caldwell; he joined as a first-year teacher and moved to the Ridgeview assistant coaching role when it opened in the summer of 1998.
- **Teaching load**: Three sections of American Government (juniors and seniors), one section of Civics (sophomores). Full-time teacher and full-time coach.
- **Coaching staff**: Greg Patterson (DC), Ryan Kowalski (OC), plus three volunteer or part-time assistants for JV and position groups.
- **Program highlights**: 3 conference championships (2011, 2017, 2023). State semifinal appearances 2017 and 2023. Overall record at Westfield: 142 wins, 78 losses. Known for program integrity and player development.
- **Current challenges**: Declining enrollment in football (32 varsity players this year, down from 45 five years ago). Concussion-protocol debates with parents. District budget constraints. Henry addresses all of this by being excellent at his job and refusing to cut corners on player safety.
- **Salary**: ~$78,000 per year (teaching salary with master's stipend and coaching stipend combined).
