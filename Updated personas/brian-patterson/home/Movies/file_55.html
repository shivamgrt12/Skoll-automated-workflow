Current research

Participatory budgeting and trust in midsize city governments. The question is narrow on purpose: when a city opens a share of capital spending to a direct resident vote, does measurable trust follow, and does it depend on what happens after the vote.

The thesis

Trust gains concentrate in cities that close the loop with a public results report, not in cities that only hold the vote.

The method

Three city cases over a five year window, using published budget documents and resident survey data, coded for a post vote results report.

The scope

Cities between 100,000 and 400,000 residents, the band where Syracuse sits.
