# Memory: Brian Patterson

## Personal Profile

Brian Patterson is the first person in his family to attend a four-year university, and that fact sits quietly under everything. He carries the expectation without resentment, reading it in his mother's pride, his father's overtime, and the relatives who ask about grades at every gathering. He is quick, analytical, and comfortable arguing a position, which serves him in class and student government and occasionally reads as combative in friendships and dating. He is working on softening without dimming.

He is a senior pursuing a dual major in Political Science and Public Policy, with expected graduation in May 2027. He speaks English and conversational Spanish, learned in school and practiced occasionally. His pronouns are he/him. The family roots its identity in church community, large Christmas gatherings in Rochester, and a devotion expressed through sacrifice more than words. Brian overcommits on purpose because everything interests him and saying no feels like closing a door he might need later. The cost is sleep, occasional panic, and a calendar that would make an executive assistant weep.

- **Monthly income**: About $1,070 from the RA and tutoring roles, after taxes.
- **Monthly expenses**: Rent $525 (split 2BR with Priya), utilities $65 (split), groceries $180, phone share $30, transportation $35, subscriptions $10, coffee and dining out $80, personal and miscellaneous $40. Total about $965, leaving roughly $105.
- **Savings**: $1,800 in a Capital One savings account, slowly growing.

- **Mindset**: Tight but managed. No margin for emergencies, which stresses him quietly. Parents would help if asked, but he tries not to ask. The scholarships are the lifeline, and losing them would be catastrophic.

## Health & Wellness

- **Sickle cell trait (lifelong)**: Not sickle cell disease. He carries one copy of the sickle cell gene, generally asymptomatic but requiring awareness. He stays hydrated, avoids extreme cold without proper gear, and is cautious with intense high-altitude exercise. One episode of exercise-related muscle cramping on a high-altitude hike freshman year scared him into more caution. Annual hemoglobin checks with Dr. Adeyemi. He gets annoyed when people conflate trait with disease.
- **Stress-related tension headaches**: One to two per week during heavy academic periods, managed with ibuprofen. He recognizes the pattern of too much screen time, too little sleep, too much caffeine.
- **Sleep**: Insufficient, five to six hours most nights during the semester, caught up on weekends. He knows it is bad and does not change it.
- **Exercise**: Runs two to three times a week, moderate, two to three miles around campus. It clears his head, and he keeps intensity low out of trait awareness.
- **Mental health**: No formal diagnosis. High-achieving stress managed through movement, friends, and riding out the occasional overwhelm. Has considered campus counseling but not scheduled it.
- **Dental**: Current through the campus health plan.
- **Diet note**: No dietary restrictions or allergies. Hydration is the one health habit he keeps consistently, carrying a water bottle everywhere.
