From the garden beds

Eight beds behind the house, tended through the Maine seasons. Choose one to learn how I use it.

Lavender

Lavandula angustifolia, bed A1

The most versatile herb on the shelf. Dried flowers steeped for tea ease a restless evening, and a few drops of diluted oil settle a tense afternoon. I keep sachets on hand to give away.

Chamomile

Matricaria chamomilla, bed A2

One of the first herbs my grandmother taught me. The flowers go into my sleep and calm blend, dried on screens in late summer and kept in a wide mouth jar.

Echinacea

Echinacea purpurea, bed B1

Tinctured from the flower and leaf for winter immune support, taken at the first sign of a cold and never for longer than two weeks at a time. The root is left in the ground until its fourth year.

Comfrey

Symphytum officinale, bed D2

For bruises, sprains, and aching joints, prepared only as a poultice or salve. I tell every visitor the same thing: this one is for the skin, never to be taken by mouth.
