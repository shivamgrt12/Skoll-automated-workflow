# Memory: Joanne Lawton

## Personal Profile
Joanne Lawton is a retired registered nurse and active folk herbalist serving the Munjoy Hill community in Portland, Maine. She was born in Portland, with family roots in Maine and Massachusetts; her grandmother emigrated from England in the 1930s. She spent 38 years as an RN at Dayspring Medical Center, beginning in med-surg and finishing in geriatrics, and she earned her BSN from Suncrest University School of Nursing in 1975. Her herbal training came from her grandmother Nana Ruth and an apprenticeship with herbalist Mrs. Virginia Keene in Brunswick. She is a devout Episcopalian who sees no line between her faith and her healing, and she carries her late husband Harold "Harry" Lawton with her since his death from a stroke in September 2017. English is her native language, and she is widowed and living independently.

## Work & Projects
- **Retired from**: Dayspring Medical Center, Portland, RN for 38 years (1975 to 2013), the last 15 in geriatrics. Pension of $2,400 per month. Keeps her RN license on inactive status and informally mentors nursing students through Emily.
- **Herbalism practice (active)**: Sees three to five community members per week for energy clearings, herbal consultations, prayer sessions, and aromatherapy. Accepts donations of roughly $20 to $40 per visit, about $300 to $500 per month. Works from an apothecary room with a prayer corner, candles, herbs, oils, and healing stones, preparing teas, tinctures, poultices, and salves.
- **Church work**: Altar guild president. Organizes the annual Harvest Fair fundraiser, coordinates Thanksgiving and Christmas food drives, and visits homebound parishioners monthly.

## Health & Wellness
- **Type 2 Diabetes** (diagnosed 2008): Metformin 1000mg twice daily and Glipizide 5mg each morning. A1c last checked March 2026 at 7.4 percent, creeping up and concerning her doctor. Checks fasting glucose daily, usually running 125 to 160. Dr. Chambers is considering insulin if it does not improve; Joanne is resistant. Uses cinnamon tea and apple cider vinegar as complementary supports, which Dr. Chambers tolerates.
- **Hypertension** (diagnosed 2005): Losartan 50mg and Hydrochlorothiazide 12.5mg each morning. BP usually 135 to 142 over 80 to 86, monitored at home with Karen's cuff.
- **Osteoarthritis** (diagnosed 2018): Hands and lower back. Tylenol as needed and a heating pad. Worse in the mornings and affects her quilting and herb preparation. Researching acupuncture in Portland.
- **Mild cataracts**: Both eyes, monitored by Dr. Patterson at Kestrel Healthcare. Right-eye surgery likely within one to two years.
- **Cholesterol**: LDL 148, managing with diet; declined statins in favor of natural approaches. Takes Vitamin D 2000 IU daily.
- **Routine care**: Annual physical at Greenleaf Health, dental cleanings every six months with Dr. Sullivan, and flu and COVID vaccines each October at the church clinic.
- **Diabetic restrictions**: Watches carbs and sugar, modifies recipes with less butter and more vegetables, and drinks cinnamon tea for blood sugar.
- **Exercise**: Daily one-mile morning walk with Dot at 7 AM, plus gardening and time on her feet during herbalist sessions.
