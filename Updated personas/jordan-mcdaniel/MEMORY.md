# Memory: Jordan McDaniel

## Personal Profile

Jordan Thomas McDaniel is a Scottish-American carpenter whose family has been in North Carolina for three generations. He grew up in Garner and now lives in Raleigh. He is male, single, and casually dating after a relationship ended about eight months ago when his girlfriend Ashley moved to Charlotte for a job. He earned a high school diploma from Southgate Regional High School in 2018 and was not a bad student, just bored; his brain comes alive with a piece of wood in front of him and a problem to solve with his hands. English is his native language. He is restless on apprentice wages and quietly believes he should be further along by now, but he respects the craft too much to cut corners. He copes with that restlessness through music, work, and his dad.

## Key Relationships

- **David McDaniel** (Father, 54): Master plumber who owns a small plumbing company in Garner. Jordan's anchor. They see each other most Sundays over a home-cooked meal or a diner run. David taught him to use tools before he could ride a bike, shows love by showing up, and is quietly concerned about the drinking but does not nag.
- **Linda McDaniel** (Mother, 52): Office manager at a dental practice. Warm and talkative, worries about him living with "those boys" and not eating vegetables. Calls twice a week; he answers about half the time and feels guilty about the rest.
- **Kayla McDaniel** (Sister, 23): Dental hygienist in Raleigh. Close in a sibling way, constant mutual roasting, but the first person he would call in a real emergency. Told him once, directly, that he drinks too much; he deflected and she has not raised it since.
- **Greg Whitfield** (Boss, 48): Owner of Ridgeline Builders. Tough, fair, old-school. Took Jordan on because David asked, but Jordan earned his place through work ethic and talent. Greg does not give compliments; letting Jordan handle a finish job solo is the compliment.
- **Nate Harmon** (Roommate, 27): IT support. The organized one who made the chore chart, set up the wi-fi, and put OpenClaw on Jordan's phone. A real friend, not just a housemate. They play board games and watch hockey together.
- **Cole Stafford** (Roommate, 25): Line cook downtown. Chaotic, leaves dishes everywhere, plays music too loud, genuinely fun. He and Jordan are the ones who go out on the weekends that get messy.
- **Travis Lockhart** (Bandmate, 28): Guitar and vocals for Timber Creek and the de facto band leader. Works at a boat shop. Friends with Jordan since high school and the one who convinced him to pick up bass.

## Work & Projects

After his June 2018 high school graduation he spent about five years doing manual work before the apprenticeship: from July 2018 to August 2020 he was a general laborer and hardscape crew helper at a Garner landscaping outfit, then from September 2020 to July 2023 he worked the yard and cut desk at a Raleigh lumber and hardware supplier, where the smell of fresh-milled wood and watching the finish carpenters who came through finally pointed him toward the trade. Across those same years he picked up bass and joined Timber Creek with Travis. He started the Ridgeline carpentry apprenticeship in August 2023. Jordan is now an Apprentice Carpenter, Year 3 of 4, at Ridgeline Builders, a small residential construction and renovation firm in Raleigh owned by Greg Whitfield. He works 6:30 AM to 3:30 PM weekdays with occasional Saturday overtime on time-sensitive projects. Job sites rotate by project, assigned weekly by Greg; current work is a kitchen renovation in Wake Forest and a new deck build in Apex. He is developing finish carpentry, custom cabinetry, trim work, and framing, and Greg has been handing him more finish work lately as a sign of trust. He earns $22/hour, roughly $45,760 a year at 40 hours, bumping toward about $50,000 in good overtime years. His apprenticeship is expected to complete around April 2027, making him a journeyman. The longer goal is journeyman status, two to three years building experience, then a small custom furniture workshop of his own, not a contracting business, just a shop where he builds to his own standard.
