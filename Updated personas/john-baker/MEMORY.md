# Memory: John Baker

## Personal Profile

John Eugene Baker is a Black man from Mississippi, born in Hattiesburg, and that fact has shaped almost every layer of his life, though he would never lead with it. He carried mail for the United States Postal Service for 34 years and retired in January 2024, and he does not tell that story with apology: the job mattered, the route mattered, the people mattered. He married Doreen (nee Franklin) on October 11, 1986, and they have settled into retirement together comfortably, bickering about the thermostat and agreeing about nearly everything else.

He moves at the speed of a man who learned what hurrying costs. He listens more than he talks, picks his words the way he picks lumber, and absorbs first and reacts later, often deciding the moment did not deserve a reaction at all. He has a long fuse with a definite end: what burns through his patience is dishonesty, especially the small kind, and when he loses patience with a person he does not shout, he just stops calling. Underneath the calm runs a streak of stubbornness, a willingness to do a thing the hard way to do it properly. Faith is the quiet backbone of his week, not a conversation piece; he serves Shiloh Baptist on the building committee, tithes, and gets uncomfortable when religion turns loud or political.

Education: high school diploma, Hattiesburg High School (1982), plus USPS letter-carrier training (1990) and ongoing route-safety and hazardous-materials recertifications through retirement. Language: English. The food on his Sunday table, the gospel and blues on the workshop radio, and the rhythm of the church service connect him to a line he respects and tends.

## Work & Projects

- **Pre-USPS work (1982 to 1989)**: A string of Gulf Coast jobs after high school. Construction laborer with a Hattiesburg crew through 1984, then three years on the loading dock at a regional warehouse in Gulfport (1985 to 1987), then two seasons in an offshore-supply yard in Pascagoula before passing the postal exam and starting at the Biloxi office in October 1990.
- **Former role**: Letter carrier, USPS, Biloxi Post Office, 34 years (October 1990 to January 2024), a walking route in the Woolmarket and D'Iberville area. Retired on a federal pension plus Social Security, with no plans to work again.
- **Church building committee**: Coordinates minor repairs, painting, and landscaping at Shiloh Baptist; meets the first Saturday of each month at 8:00 AM. A November painting day is his to organize.
- **Workshop projects**: Always something on the bench; currently a set of shelves for Janelle's garage.
- **Mentoring the grandkids**: Teaches Amara, Isaiah, Jaylen, and Destiny small workshop and garden tasks when they visit.

## Health & Wellness

- **Sleep apnea (moderate, obstructive)**: Diagnosed 2020. Uses a ResMed AirSense 11 CPAP nightly with good compliance, though he hates the mask. Dr. Pham reviews the machine data annually.
- **Hypertension (Stage 1)**: Diagnosed 2015. Managed with amlodipine 5mg each morning; blood pressure stable around 134/84. Watches sodium on the doctor's recommendation, though he cheats at cookouts.
- **Mild knee osteoarthritis (both knees)**: From 34 years on a mail route, worse in damp weather. Managed with movement, OTC glucosamine, and occasional ibuprofen; no specialist.
- **Medications and supplements**: Amlodipine 5mg (morning), glucosamine chondroitin, fish oil 1200mg, Vitamin D3 1000 IU.
- **Providers**: Dr. Thanh Pham (Gulf Coast Medical Associates, annual physical in November); Dr. Linda Sellers (Biloxi Family Dental, every 6 months); Dr. Carl Henderson (Henderson Optometry, annual exam, bifocals since 2019); CPAP supplies from Coastal Medical Supply every 3 months.
- **Exercise**: Walks 30 to 40 minutes daily on the neighborhood loop at 6:30 AM, plus yard and workshop work. No known allergies.

## Interests & Hobbies

- **Woodworking**: The deepest part of him; clean gabled birdhouses, shelves, boxes, and frames. Pine for forgiveness, oak for honesty, cedar for outdoor work and the smell. He gives everything away.
- **Gardening**: Three raised beds along the back fence, tomatoes and peppers in summer, okra because his mother grew it, herbs by the back door. He walks the beds with his coffee each morning in season.
- **Fishing**: A slower hobby, a few times a year on the bayou, mostly when Curtis visits, more about the company than the catch. He keeps his father's tackle box, latch broken, held shut with a bungee cord.
- **The porch**: A daily practice more than a hobby, the rockers facing the street on purpose, where the day starts and conversations happen.

## Home & Living

- **House**: A single-story brick ranch, 3 bedrooms and 2 baths, about 1,400 square feet on a half-acre lot. A 1992 build; the roof was replaced after Hurricane Zeta (2020) and the AC in 2022. John handles most repairs himself; Coastal Plumbing is on call for what he cannot reach.
- **Workshop**: A detached garage, insulated and wired, with a table saw, band saw, drill press, and hand tools. A covered front porch holds two rockers; the fenced backyard holds the raised garden beds.
- **Vehicles**: A 2018 Chevy Colorado LT, gray, paid off, about 72,000 miles. Doreen drives a 2019 Toyota Corolla, also paid off.

## Preferences

- **Food and drink**: Folgers drip coffee, two cups black by 6 AM. Doreen cooks most nights and keeps the sodium reasonable; John grills on a charcoal Weber on weekends and makes his mother's gumbo a few times a year. Sunday and heritage food runs to collards, cornbread, smothered chicken, and peach cobbler. Restaurants: Taranto's Crawfish, Waffle House on Saturdays, Mary Mahoney's for special occasions. One or two light beers on weekends, never during the week. Snacks: peanuts, banana, crackers and cheese.
- **Music**: Gospel, blues, and classic soul, Muddy Waters, Sam Cooke, Mahalia Jackson, on a gospel station through the workshop radio. He keeps "A Change Is Gonna Come" for when the mood needs a particular repair.
- **TV and reading**: Local news morning and evening, sports (Saints, Ole Miss football, Braves baseball), cooking shows by Doreen's influence, and nature documentaries, on cable rather than streaming. Reads the Sun Herald daily and flips through woodworking magazines; not much for books.
- **Shopping and money habits**: Walmart for household, Home Depot for the workshop, Save-A-Lot for groceries. Pays cash when he can, debit otherwise, no credit cards. Buys tools slowly after thorough research, brand-loyal on boots, saw blades, and coffee, and skeptical of heavily advertised names. Does not shop online unless Janelle places the order.
- **Clothing and style**: Khakis and polos and a ball cap outdoors, jeans and a t-shirt on weekends, one pressed suit for funerals and weddings. Utility first, dignity always, nothing flashy.
- **Travel**: Prefers to drive, with the truck, a cooler, and a paper map alongside the GPS. A clean roadside motel over a fancy hotel, no cruises and no theme parks. His ideal trip is a long weekend somewhere with a porch and a body of water and not too many people.
- **Comfort**: The workshop when a day was hard, a walk when something is heavy, and comfort foods of grits, a pot of beans, pound cake, and his mother's gumbo when he wants the weight of home.
